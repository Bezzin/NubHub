# 7-Week Ultrasound: What to Expect

A 7-week ultrasound is an early pregnancy scan that can confirm your pregnancy is in the right place, check for a heartbeat, and give an early measurement of the baby. It is not part of the standard NHS schedule — most NHS trusts offer a dating scan at around 11–14 weeks — so if you are having a scan at 7 weeks, it is likely through an Early Pregnancy Unit (EPU) following bleeding or concerns, or through a private clinic. Here is what to expect.

## Why You Might Have a 7-Week Scan

A scan at 7 weeks is usually arranged for one of these reasons:

- You have had bleeding or pain and been referred to the Early Pregnancy Unit
- You have a history of ectopic pregnancy or previous miscarriage
- You conceived via IVF or assisted reproduction and have a monitoring scan scheduled
- You have booked a private reassurance scan for peace of mind

If you are having a private scan, many clinics offer early reassurance scans from around 6–7 weeks. These can be helpful if anxiety is making early pregnancy very difficult, though it is worth knowing that not everything is visible this early and some uncertainty may remain.

## What You Will See at 7 Weeks

At 7 weeks, the pregnancy is still very small — the embryo is typically around 5–10 mm, roughly the size of a blueberry. What the sonographer can see depends partly on the quality of equipment and whether the scan is transabdominal or transvaginal.

In a typical 7-week scan, you may see:

- **The gestational sac** — the fluid-filled sac in the uterus that contains the pregnancy
- **The yolk sac** — a small round structure that nourishes the embryo in early pregnancy
- **The fetal pole** — the earliest visible form of the baby, appearing as a small bright area
- **A flickering heartbeat** — visible in most pregnancies by 7 weeks, though not always

The image will look quite different from a later scan — at this stage, the embryo does not yet look like a baby. Many people find it reassuring to simply see a heartbeat; the scan makes things feel real in a way that a positive test cannot.

## What the Sonographer Checks

The sonographer's main priorities at a 7-week scan are:

- **Confirming the pregnancy is intrauterine** (inside the uterus, not ectopic)
- **Checking for a fetal heartbeat**
- **Measuring the crown-rump length (CRL)** to confirm or adjust gestational age
- **Counting the number of gestational sacs** — in case of twins or multiple pregnancy
- **Assessing the yolk sac** — its presence and appearance give information about early development

If you are at the EPU following bleeding, the sonographer will also look for any signs of miscarriage or bleeding around the pregnancy.

## What Type of Scan Is It?

At 7 weeks, many clinics use a **transvaginal ultrasound** (an internal scan using a probe inserted into the vagina). This gives a clearer image at this stage because the uterus is still low in the pelvis and the embryo is very small. It is not painful, though some people find it slightly uncomfortable.

Some clinics and hospitals use a transabdominal scan (on the tummy) first and then switch to transvaginal if the image is not clear enough. You can always ask in advance what type of scan to expect.

## Normal vs. What to Discuss With Your Clinician

At 7 weeks, the following are generally considered reassuring:

- A gestational sac measuring appropriately for dates
- A visible yolk sac
- A fetal pole with a visible heartbeat

Things that may prompt a follow-up scan or further discussion:

- **No heartbeat seen** — this does not automatically mean bad news; dates may be slightly off, or the scan may simply be a little early
- **An empty gestational sac** — the sonographer will discuss whether to rescan in 1–2 weeks
- **Measurements that do not match your dates** — sometimes this reflects a later ovulation date rather than a problem

If results are uncertain, you will usually be asked to return for a repeat scan about 1–2 weeks later. This waiting period is difficult but it is how clinicians reach a more reliable conclusion.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can you see a heartbeat at 7 weeks?

In most pregnancies, yes — a heartbeat is usually detectable by transvaginal ultrasound from around 6 to 7 weeks. If a heartbeat is not seen at 7 weeks, this does not necessarily mean the pregnancy has failed. Dates may be slightly off, or it may simply be slightly early. The sonographer will advise whether a repeat scan is needed.

### Is a 7-week scan internal or external?

At 7 weeks, a transvaginal (internal) scan often gives a clearer image because the uterus is still deep in the pelvis and the embryo is very small. Some clinics start with an abdominal scan and use transvaginal only if needed. The probe used is narrow and is covered with a protective sheath. You can ask your clinic about their approach before the appointment.

### What if there's no heartbeat at 7 weeks?

If no heartbeat is seen at 7 weeks, the most common next step is a repeat scan in 1–2 weeks. This is because gestational age based on the last menstrual period is not always accurate — ovulation may have been later than expected, making the pregnancy slightly earlier than it appears. A single inconclusive scan is rarely enough to draw a firm conclusion. The clinical team will advise you on what happens next and offer support.

Always speak to your midwife or GP if you have concerns after a scan — they can help you understand what the results mean for your individual situation.
