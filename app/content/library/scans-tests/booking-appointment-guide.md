# Your Booking Appointment: What to Expect

Your booking appointment is your first official antenatal appointment with your midwife. It usually happens between 8 and 10 weeks of pregnancy. Here's exactly what to expect.

## What Is the Booking Appointment? {#what-is-it}

The booking appointment is your first detailed midwife appointment. It's called 'booking' because it's when you're officially 'booked in' to the maternity service. Expect it to take 45-60 minutes.

## What Tests Are Done? {#tests}

Your midwife will: take blood samples (to check blood group, RhD factor, anaemia, and screen for infections like HIV, syphilis, hepatitis B); check your blood pressure; test your urine (for protein and infection); measure your height and weight to calculate your BMI; possibly offer a cervical smear if you're due.

## Questions Your Midwife Will Ask {#questions}

Your midwife will ask about: your medical history; your family medical history; your lifestyle (smoking, alcohol, diet); your mental health; any previous pregnancies; your home and work situation; your preferences for maternity care.

## What to Bring {#what-to-bring}

Bring: a list of any medications you take; your medical history if relevant; a urine sample (some clinics ask for this); a list of questions you want to ask; a partner or friend if you'd like support.

## What Happens Next? {#next-steps}

After your booking appointment, your midwife will arrange your 12-week scan (dating scan) and NT screening. You'll have regular antenatal appointments throughout your pregnancy.

## Frequently Asked Questions {#faq}

### How long does the booking appointment take?

Expect 45-60 minutes. It's the longest antenatal appointment because your midwife needs to take a full history and arrange all your care.

### Can my partner come to the booking appointment?

Yes. Partners are welcome at all antenatal appointments, though some women prefer to attend the booking appointment alone as it covers personal medical history.
