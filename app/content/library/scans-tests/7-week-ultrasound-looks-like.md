# What Does a 7-Week Ultrasound Look Like?

At 7 weeks, the ultrasound image starts to look a little more interesting. You can see a clear fetal pole, a beating heart, and the beginnings of a recognisable shape. Here's what you'll see on screen and what it all means.

## What You'll See on Screen {#screen}

At 7 weeks, your baby has grown to about 10mm — roughly the size of a blueberry. On the ultrasound screen, you'll see:

- A **dark circle** (the gestational sac) within your uterus
- A **small white ring** (the yolk sac) inside it
- A **tiny curved shape** (the fetal pole / your baby) next to the yolk sac
- A **rapid flicker** within the fetal pole — the heartbeat

The fetal pole is now big enough to have some shape. It looks like a tiny comma or a very small kidney bean. You may be able to make out a slightly larger head end and a thinner tail end, though the distinction is subtle at this stage.

## The Gestational Sac at 7 Weeks {#sac}

By 7 weeks, the gestational sac has grown to about **20-24mm** in diameter. It's still the most prominent feature on the screen — a dark, fluid-filled circle. The sac is made of amniotic fluid, which protects your baby.

The sonographer measures the sac to confirm it's growing as expected. If the sac is smaller than expected, you may be earlier than your dates suggest. If it's the right size but the fetal pole is missing, the sonographer will arrange a repeat scan.

## The Fetal Pole at 7 Weeks {#pole}

The fetal pole at 7 weeks measures about **8-11mm** (crown-rump length). It's now clearly visible as a distinct structure, not just a smudge. It looks like a tiny curved line or bean shape.

The sonographer will measure the CRL carefully — this is the most accurate way to date your pregnancy at 7 weeks, with an accuracy of about 3-5 days. The CRL is measured from the top of the head to the bottom of the rump (not the legs, which aren't developed yet).

## The Heartbeat at 7 Weeks {#heartbeat}

The heartbeat is the star of the show at 7 weeks. It appears as a **rapid flickering** within the fetal pole — much faster than your own heartbeat. At 7 weeks, the typical heart rate is **120-150 bpm**.

Seeing the heartbeat at 7 weeks is very reassuring. Once a heartbeat is confirmed, the risk of miscarriage drops significantly — to around 5% or lower at this stage. The sonographer may measure the heart rate and tell you the number, which can be fun (though don't worry if it seems fast — that's normal).

If the heartbeat is slow (below 90-100 bpm at 7 weeks), the sonographer may ask you to come back in a week to check whether it has sped up. A slow heart rate isn't always bad news, but it warrants monitoring.

## The Yolk Sac at 7 Weeks {#yolk}

The yolk sac is still visible at 7 weeks as a small white ring inside the gestational sac. It's usually about 3-5mm in diameter. The yolk sac is providing nutrition to your baby before the placenta takes over around 10-12 weeks.

The yolk sac will gradually shrink and disappear over the next few weeks. By 10-12 weeks, it's usually no longer visible. Its presence at 7 weeks is a reassuring sign of normal development.

## What a Normal 7-Week Scan Shows {#normal}

A normal 7-week scan typically shows:

- A gestational sac of about 20-24mm
- A yolk sac visible inside it
- A fetal pole measuring 8-11mm (CRL)
- A visible heartbeat at 120-150 bpm
- The pregnancy correctly located in the uterus
- Possibly early limb buds (just starting to form)

If all of these are present, the scan is very reassuring. You can breathe a sigh of relief — your pregnancy is progressing as it should.

## What You Still Won't See {#wont-see}

Even at 7 weeks, there's plenty you won't see:

- **A baby-shaped figure**: The fetal pole is still very basic — no arms, legs, or face.
- **Facial features**: Too early for eyes, nose, or mouth to be visible.
- **Movement**: The baby can't move yet at 7 weeks.
- **Gender**: Far too early.
- **Fingers and toes**: These won't be visible until about 10-11 weeks.

If you want a more recognisable image, wait until 8-10 weeks, when your baby looks more like a tiny person.

## Frequently Asked Questions {#faq}

### Can I get a photo at 7 weeks?

Most private clinics will give you at least one photo. At 7 weeks, the photo will show the gestational sac with the fetal pole inside — it won't look like a baby, but it's a keepsake.

### What if the heartbeat is slow at 7 weeks?

A slow heart rate (below 90-100 bpm) at 7 weeks can be concerning, but it's not always bad news. The heart may have only just started beating. The sonographer will arrange a repeat scan in 7-10 days to check the rate.

### Will I need a transvaginal scan at 7 weeks?

Possibly. Some clinics try abdominal first at 7 weeks, but many still use transvaginal for the clearest image. The sonographer will decide based on what they can see.
