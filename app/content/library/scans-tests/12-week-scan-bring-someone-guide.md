# 12-Week Scan: Can You Bring Someone? A UK Guide

The 12-week dating scan is a milestone moment in pregnancy, and many women want to share it with a partner, family member, or friend. Here is what you need to know about bringing someone along to your NHS or private 12-week scan.

## NHS Policy on Visitors

Most NHS hospitals allow you to bring **one person** to your 12-week scan, usually your partner. This has been the standard policy across most UK trusts, though rules tightened during the pandemic and some units have kept tighter restrictions. Always check your local hospital current policy before the appointment.

## Why Numbers Are Limited

NHS scan rooms are small and sonographers need space and quiet to concentrate. The 12-week scan includes the **combined screening test** for Down syndrome, Edwards syndrome, and Patau syndrome, which requires careful measurements. Too many people in the room can be distracting and may compromise the quality of the scan.

## Bringing Children

Most NHS hospitals **do not allow children** to attend the 12-week scan, including older siblings. This is because the scan is a medical examination, the room is not childproofed, and if a problem is detected, you need privacy to process the news. You will usually need to arrange childcare.

## Private Scans: A Different Story

Private scan clinics are generally much more relaxed about visitors. Many welcome partners, children, grandparents, and even friends. If sharing the moment with a wider group matters to you, a private 12-week scan may be worth considering alongside your NHS appointment.

## Preparing Your Partner

If your partner is attending, let them know what to expect. The scan is usually abdominal, so you will need a full bladder. The sonographer will apply gel to your tummy and move a probe across it. The first glimpse of your baby on the screen is often emotional, and it helps if your partner knows the scan also includes screening measurements that can take 15-20 minutes.

## What If They Find a Problem?

If the sonographer detects a concern, they will usually ask your partner to step out briefly while they discuss findings with you, or they may invite you both to a private room afterwards. Having someone with you for support can be invaluable if you receive unexpected news.

## Tips for the Day

- Confirm the visitor policy with your hospital the week before.
- Arrive 10-15 minutes early.
- Bring your maternity notes if you have them.
- Have your phone ready for photos of the screen (if permitted).
- Arrange childcare for older children well in advance.

## Key Takeaways

- NHS units usually allow one adult, typically your partner.
- Children are generally not permitted at NHS scans.
- Private clinics are more flexible about visitors.
- Always confirm your local hospital policy before the day.
- Having support with you can make the scan a positive shared experience.
