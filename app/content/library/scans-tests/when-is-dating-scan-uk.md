# When Is the Dating Scan in the UK

The dating scan is usually one of the first major milestones of pregnancy — the first time most parents see their baby on a screen. In the UK, the NHS offers this scan to all pregnant women, and knowing roughly when to expect it can help you plan ahead and manage the wait.

## Standard Timing in the UK {#standard-timing}

In the UK the NHS dating scan is typically offered between 10 and 14 weeks of pregnancy, with most appointments falling around 11 to 13 weeks. The exact date depends on your midwife booking appointment, local trust availability, and how your due date is initially calculated from your last period.

The scan is sometimes called the 12-week scan, though this is simply a shorthand rather than a fixed rule. The sonographer uses measurements of the baby — particularly the crown-rump length — to confirm or adjust your estimated due date. This measurement is most accurate within the 10 to 14 week window, which is why the NHS aims to complete it during this time.

Your booking appointment with a midwife (usually between 8 and 10 weeks) is what triggers the scan referral in most areas. If you book with your GP or self-refer to a midwife later than this, your dating scan may be booked at shorter notice, sometimes closer to 13 or 14 weeks.

## What Can Affect Your Date {#affects-date}

Several things can shift your appointment earlier or later. If your cycles are irregular, your midwife may book the scan toward the later end of the window to be sure the baby is big enough to measure accurately. Local waiting times and clinic capacity also play a part, so some people are seen at 11 weeks and others closer to 14.

If you have had bleeding or pain in early pregnancy and are referred for an early reassurance scan through the Early Pregnancy Unit (EPU), that is a separate scan from your dating scan and does not replace it. You will still be offered the NHS dating scan in the normal window even if you have had a prior early scan.

Some women choose to book a private early pregnancy scan between 6 and 10 weeks for reassurance. Again, this does not replace the NHS dating scan — the NHS will still schedule yours in the usual timeframe.

## What Happens at the Dating Scan {#what-happens}

The sonographer will apply gel to your abdomen and use an ultrasound probe to view the baby. In some cases, particularly earlier in the window, a transvaginal (internal) scan may give a clearer image. You will be asked if you consent to this before it is done.

The scan typically takes around 20 to 30 minutes. The sonographer will check the baby's measurements, confirm a heartbeat, and check for any obvious structural concerns at this stage. You will usually receive a printout of the scan image to take home.

If you are in an area where combined screening for Down's syndrome, Edwards' syndrome, and Patau's syndrome is offered at this appointment, the sonographer will also measure the nuchal translucency (the fluid at the back of the baby's neck). This is part of the NHS screening programme and you can choose whether to have it.

## If Your Scan Is Delayed {#if-delayed}

If you have not heard about your dating scan by around 10 weeks and have already had your booking appointment, contact your midwife or the maternity unit to check the referral has been received. Scans can occasionally fall through administrative gaps, particularly if you booked with a new GP practice or moved areas during early pregnancy.

If you pass 14 weeks without a scan, let your midwife know so alternative arrangements can be made. Dating scans can technically be done after 14 weeks but the measurements become progressively less reliable for due date calculation.

## Frequently Asked Questions {#faq}

### Is 12 weeks the latest I can have the dating scan?

No — most NHS trusts aim to complete it by 14 weeks, as later measurements are less accurate for dating. Twelve weeks is simply the most common time, not a deadline.

### What if I am not sure of my dates?

Your midwife will usually book toward the later end of the window and use the scan to confirm timing. The crown-rump length measurement is more reliable than period dates for working out how far along you are.

### Can I ask for an earlier appointment?

You can ask, but earlier slots are usually reserved for clinical concerns such as bleeding or pain. If you have those symptoms, contact your midwife or GP rather than waiting for a routine appointment.
