# 7-Week Private Scan: What You Can Expect to See

A private scan at 7 weeks is one of the earliest points at which you can get meaningful reassurance about your pregnancy. Here is a detailed look at what a sonographer can typically see at this stage and what to expect from the appointment.

## Why Have a Scan at 7 Weeks?

At 7 weeks, the embryo is still tiny (around 10mm, the size of a blueberry), but key milestones are visible on ultrasound. Many women book a 7-week scan for reassurance after IVF, after a previous loss, or to confirm the pregnancy is progressing after experiencing bleeding.

## What the Sonographer Sees

- **Gestational sac**: A fluid-filled sac in the uterus, confirming the pregnancy is in the right place.
- **Yolk sac**: A small circular structure inside the gestational sac that nourishes the embryo before the placenta forms.
- **Fetal pole**: The first sign of the developing baby, appearing as a small shape near the yolk sac.
- **Heartbeat**: From around 6-7 weeks, a flickering heartbeat may be visible. A confirmed heartbeat at 7 weeks is a very positive sign.
- **Crown-rump length (CRL)**: The sonographer may measure the baby from head to bottom to confirm your due date.

## Transvaginal vs Abdominal Scan

At 7 weeks, a **transvaginal scan** is often used because it gives a clearer, closer view of the tiny embryo. Some clinics may try an abdominal scan first, but if the view is poor, they will switch to transvaginal. The internal scan is not painful, though it can feel a little undignified.

## What If Nothing Is Seen?

If your dates are slightly off, you may be earlier than 7 weeks, and only the gestational sac will be visible. Most reputable clinics offer a free rescan one to two weeks later in this situation. It does not necessarily mean the pregnancy has failed.

## What You Take Home

Most private clinics give you printed scan photos at 7 weeks. The image may show the gestational sac and a small blob with a visible flicker if the heartbeat is detected. Some clinics offer a short video clip on a USB stick.

## Questions to Ask

- Can you confirm a heartbeat?
- Is the pregnancy in the correct location?
- Does the size match my dates?
- What happens if the scan is inconclusive?

## Preparing for Your Scan

You may be asked to arrive with a partially full bladder for an abdominal scan, though this is less critical for a transvaginal scan. Wear comfortable clothing, and bring your partner or a friend if the clinic allows.

## Key Takeaways

- At 7 weeks, you can usually see the sac, yolk sac, fetal pole, and heartbeat.
- A transvaginal scan often gives the clearest view.
- If nothing is seen, a rescan in a week or two is normal and not a cause for panic.
- A confirmed heartbeat at 7 weeks significantly lowers miscarriage risk.
- Bring someone for support and check the clinic rescan policy.
