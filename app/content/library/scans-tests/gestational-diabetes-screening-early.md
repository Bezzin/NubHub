# Gestational Diabetes Screening in Pregnancy

Who is offered gestational diabetes screening in the UK, how the OGTT works, and what early screening means for your care.

## Who Is Offered Screening and When

Gestational diabetes is a form of diabetes that develops in pregnancy and usually resolves after birth. In the UK, screening is not offered to everyone. Instead, your midwife assesses your risk factors at booking using a checklist. You will be offered an oral glucose tolerance test (OGTT) at 24 to 28 weeks if you have a body mass index above 30, a previous baby weighing 4.5 kg or more, a family history of diabetes, a family origin with a high prevalence of diabetes, or polycystic ovary syndrome. If you had gestational diabetes before, you will be offered early screening soon after booking and again at 24 to 28 weeks.

## How the Oral Glucose Tolerance Test Works

The OGTT measures how well your body handles a sugar load. You will be asked to fast overnight, then attend a clinic where a blood sample is taken. You then drink a glucose solution and have a second blood sample taken two hours later. A fasting result above 5.6 mmol/L or a two-hour result above 7.8 mmol/L indicates gestational diabetes. If diagnosed, you will be supported with blood glucose monitoring, diet and lifestyle advice, and, if needed, medication such as metformin or insulin. Most people manage with diet alone, and extra growth scans will be arranged in later pregnancy.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I be tested for gestational diabetes before 24 weeks?**

If you had gestational diabetes in a previous pregnancy, you will be offered early screening at booking or soon after. For others, routine screening is at 24 to 28 weeks when the hormonal changes that cause gestational diabetes peak.

**What happens if I am diagnosed with gestational diabetes?**

You will be shown how to check your blood sugar at home and given dietary advice. If lifestyle changes do not control your levels, you may be prescribed metformin tablets or insulin. Extra scans will check your baby growth.

**Can I reduce my risk of gestational diabetes before pregnancy?**

A balanced diet, regular activity and achieving a healthy weight before conception can lower risk. However, some risk factors such as family history and ethnicity cannot be changed, which is why screening is offered to those at higher risk.
