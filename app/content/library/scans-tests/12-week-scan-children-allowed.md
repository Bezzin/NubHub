# 12-Week Scan: Are Children Allowed? A UK Guide

Many parents wonder whether they can bring their older children to the 12-week scan to share the excitement of seeing their new sibling on screen. The answer in most NHS hospitals is no, but there are nuances and alternatives worth knowing.

## The Short Answer

Most NHS hospitals in the UK **do not allow children** to attend the 12-week dating scan. This includes toddlers, older siblings, and even well-behaved older children. The policy applies across the majority of trusts, though a small number may be more flexible.

## Why Children Are Not Allowed

There are several reasons for this policy:
- **Medical environment**: The scan room is a clinical space with sensitive, expensive equipment and cables that are not childproofed.
- **Concentration**: The sonographer needs to take precise measurements for dating and combined screening. Distractions can affect accuracy.
- **Privacy and news**: If the scan reveals a problem such as a missed miscarriage or a high-risk screening result, you need a private, calm space to process the information without managing a child reactions.
- **Space**: Scan rooms are small and simply cannot safely accommodate extra people.

## Are There Exceptions?

A small number of NHS trusts may allow older, quiet children if there is genuinely no childcare available, but this is rare and must be agreed in advance. Do not assume you can bring a child on the day, as you may be turned away.

## Private Clinics: The Alternative

Many private scan clinics actively welcome children at 12-week scans. Some even offer sibling-focused packages with extra photos and a relaxed atmosphere. If it is important to you that your older child sees the scan, booking a private appointment is usually the most reliable option.

## Tips if You Want to Involve Your Child

- Book a private scan specifically advertised as child-friendly.
- Call ahead to confirm the clinic policy on children.
- Bring snacks and a toy to keep your child occupied during any waiting.
- Explain what will happen in advance so they know what to expect.
- Consider showing them photos from your NHS scan afterwards if they cannot attend.

## Childcare Arrangements for NHS Scans

Arrange childcare well in advance of your NHS appointment. If your partner is attending the scan, consider asking a grandparent or friend to watch your child, or look into whether your hospital has a creche (most do not). Book the appointment for a time that works with school or nursery if possible.

## What to Do on the Day

If you arrive with a child and the hospital does not allow them in, you may have to reschedule. Always confirm the policy by phone the week before your scan to avoid disappointment.

## Key Takeaways

- Most NHS hospitals do not allow children at the 12-week scan.
- The reasons are safety, concentration, privacy, and space.
- A few trusts may make exceptions if pre-arranged, but this is rare.
- Private clinics are generally child-friendly and a good alternative.
- Always confirm the policy in advance and arrange childcare as a backup.
