# Slow Heartbeat at 6 Weeks: What's Normal?

A slow heartbeat at 6 weeks can be worrying, but it may be completely normal. Here's what you need to know.

## Normal Heart Rate at 6 Weeks

- 6 weeks: 90-110 bpm is typical
- The heart has just started beating, so a slower rate is normal
- The rate increases rapidly over the next few weeks

## When to Be Concerned

A heart rate below 80 bpm at 6+ weeks may be concerning. However:
- It could be too early and the heart is just starting
- The measurement may be inaccurate
- A follow-up scan usually shows a normal rate

## FAQ

### What's a normal heart rate at 6 weeks?

At 6 weeks, a fetal heart rate of 90-110 bpm is normal. The heart has just started beating so it's slower than later in pregnancy.

### Can a slow heartbeat at 6 weeks improve?

Yes. Many babies with a slow heartbeat at 6 weeks have a normal heart rate by 8 weeks. A follow-up scan is usually arranged to check progress.
