# 8-Week Private Scan: What You Can Expect to See

An 8-week private scan is a popular choice for early reassurance. At this gestation, the embryo has developed further, and there is usually plenty to see on the ultrasound screen.

## What Has Developed by 8 Weeks?

By 8 weeks, your baby is around 16mm long - roughly the size of a raspberry. The heartbeat is typically strong and easier to detect than at 7 weeks, and the basic body structure is starting to form, although it still looks more like a tiny tadpole than a baby.

## What the Sonographer Sees

- **Gestational and yolk sacs**: Both should be clearly visible inside the uterus.
- **Fetal pole with a clear heartbeat**: A strong, flickering heartbeat is usually easy to detect at 8 weeks, often visible at 150-170 beats per minute.
- **Crown-rump length (CRL)**: The sonographer measures the baby to confirm gestational age and your estimated due date.
- **Limb buds**: Tiny buds that will become arms and legs may be visible, though they are very small at this stage.
- **Single or multiple pregnancy**: The scan can confirm whether you are carrying one baby, twins, or more.

## Abdominal vs Transvaginal

At 8 weeks, some clinics may be able to use an abdominal ultrasound, particularly if you are slim and your bladder is full. However, many still prefer a transvaginal scan for the clearest image. The sonographer will choose the best approach.

## Why Choose 8 Weeks for a Scan?

By 8 weeks, the miscarriage risk drops markedly once a heartbeat is confirmed. This makes it an ideal time for reassurance if you have been anxious, have had a previous loss, or have experienced bleeding. It is also early enough to rule out an ectopic pregnancy.

## What You Receive

You will typically get two or three printed photos showing the baby measurement and heartbeat. Many clinics also offer a digital image or short video. You will be given a written report with the CRL, heartbeat rate, and due date.

## What If Something Looks Wrong?

If the sonographer cannot find a heartbeat or the sac looks empty, they will usually arrange a follow-up scan in 7-14 days rather than diagnosing a miscarriage immediately, as dates can be wrong. If there is a clear problem, they will refer you to your NHS Early Pregnancy Unit.

## Preparing for the Appointment

Drink water so your bladder is moderately full if an abdominal scan is planned. Arrive 10 minutes early to fill in forms. Bring your partner if you wish, and have questions ready about what you are seeing on the screen.

## Key Takeaways

- At 8 weeks, a heartbeat is usually clear and reassuring.
- The scan confirms gestational age, location, and whether it is a multiple pregnancy.
- Transvaginal scans are common but abdominal may work at 8 weeks.
- If results are unclear, a rescan is normal and not automatically bad news.
- You will usually leave with photos, a report, and a due date estimate.
