# Can You Bring Someone to Your 12-Week Scan?

Your 12-week scan is one of the most emotional moments of early pregnancy — the first time you'll see your baby. It's natural to want to share it with someone. But hospital policies on who you can bring vary, so it's worth checking in advance.

## Can I Bring My Partner? {#partner}

Yes — virtually all NHS hospitals allow you to bring **one adult** into the scan room, and this is usually your partner. This is the most common arrangement across the UK, and it's a lovely moment to share. Your partner will be able to sit or stand next to you and watch the scan on the screen.

Some hospitals allow your partner to be there for the whole scan, while others may ask them to wait outside for the first few minutes if the sonographer needs to check something clinically before sharing the images. If everything looks healthy, your partner will usually be called in straight away.

## Can I Bring a Friend or Family Member? {#friend}

If your partner can't make it, or if you'd prefer to bring someone else, most hospitals are happy for you to bring a friend, your mum, your sister, or another supportive adult instead. The key point is that most NHS trusts limit the scan room to **one additional adult** at a time.

If you want to bring more than one person, you'll need to check with your hospital. Some hospitals are more flexible, especially if the scan room is large, but many have strict one-person policies due to space and infection control. It's always best to ring the ultrasound department beforehand to ask.

## Can I Bring Children? {#children}

This is where it gets more complicated. Policies on children vary widely between hospitals:

- **Some hospitals allow children** of any age to come into the scan room.
- **Some hospitals allow children but only if another adult is there to supervise them**, so they don't want the child's other parent sitting in if it means the child is unsupervised.
- **Some hospitals don't allow children at all** in the scan room.

The reason for the restriction is that young children can get bored and restless during a 20-minute scan, which can distract the sonographer. The sonographer needs to concentrate fully — they're checking your baby's development and measuring for chromosomal conditions, which requires focus.

If you want to bring an older sibling, ring the hospital in advance to ask about their policy. If they say no, don't be upset — it's a common rule. Consider arranging childcare instead.

## Why Do Hospitals Have Restrictions? {#why}

Hospitals have restrictions for several sensible reasons:

- **Space**: Scan rooms are small and already full of equipment. Too many people create a cramped, uncomfortable environment.
- **Concentration**: The sonographer needs to focus on clinical measurements. Distractions could lead to missed findings.
- **Privacy**: Other patients are being scanned nearby. Too many visitors in the department can compromise privacy.
- **Infection control**: More people means more potential for infection, which matters in a hospital setting.
- **Emergencies**: If something is found during the scan, the sonographer needs space to discuss it sensitively with you.

## What About Private Scans? {#private}

Private scan clinics tend to be more flexible about bringing people. Many allow you to bring your partner and children, and some even let you bring a small group. Private clinics often have larger rooms and a more relaxed atmosphere, as they're designed for the experience as well as the clinical assessment.

If bringing your children to the scan is really important to you, a private clinic may be the better option. Prices for a 12-week private scan start at around £60.

## Tips for Bringing Someone {#tips}

- **Check the policy first**: Ring the ultrasound department or check your hospital's website before the day.
- **Bring one person, not a crowd**: Stick to the hospital's limit. If you want more people, ask in advance.
- **Prepare children**: If children are allowed, bring snacks and a small toy to keep them occupied.
- **Don't forget childcare**: If children aren't allowed, arrange childcare early — scans are hard to reschedule.
- **Ask about photos**: If your partner can't come, ask if you can take a video call during the scan — some hospitals allow this, some don't.

## Frequently Asked Questions {#faq}

### Can I bring my mum instead of my partner?

Yes. Most hospitals allow you to bring any one adult you choose — it doesn't have to be your partner. Just let the receptionist know who you've brought.

### Can I video call my partner during the scan?

Some hospitals allow this, but many don't due to privacy concerns about other patients. Ask the sonographer before you start filming — never assume it's OK.

### Can I bring both my partner and my mum?

Some hospitals allow two adults, but many don't. Ring ahead to check. If they say no, respect the policy.
