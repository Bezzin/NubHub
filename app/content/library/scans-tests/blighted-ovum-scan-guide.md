# Blighted Ovum: What the Scan Shows

A blighted ovum (also called an anembryonic pregnancy) is when a gestational sac develops but no embryo forms inside it.

## How It's Diagnosed

A blighted ovum is diagnosed when:
- The gestational sac is large enough that an embryo should be visible
- No fetal pole or yolk sac is seen
- Follow-up scans confirm no development

## FAQ

### How is a blighted ovum confirmed?

It's confirmed through repeat ultrasounds. The sac must be large enough (typically 20-25mm) with no visible embryo.

### Can a blighted ovum be misdiagnosed?

Yes, especially if dates are wrong. That's why doctors always arrange a follow-up scan before making a final diagnosis.
