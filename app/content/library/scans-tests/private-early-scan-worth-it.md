# Private Early Scan: Is It Worth It?

Whether a private early scan is worth it really comes down to what you are hoping to get from it. On the NHS, your first routine scan is usually the dating scan at around 11 to 14 weeks, which can feel like a long wait if you are anxious or have had a difficult time before. A private early scan can offer earlier reassurance, but it is an optional, paid extra rather than a medical necessity. For many people the peace of mind is worth the cost; for others, the NHS pathway is perfectly enough.

## Pros Of A Private Early Scan

- Reassurance: seeing a heartbeat can ease anxiety, especially after a previous loss or fertility treatment
- It happens earlier than the routine NHS dating scan
- You usually get more relaxed time with the sonographer
- Many clinics include printed photos or a short video

## Things To Weigh Up

- Cost: typically around £60 to £200 in the UK, depending on the clinic and location
- Timing: before about 6 to 7 weeks it may be too early to see a heartbeat, which can sometimes cause more worry rather than less
- A reassurance scan is not a substitute for the NHS dating and screening scans, which check dates, growth and certain conditions
- Results are a snapshot in time and cannot guarantee how a pregnancy will progress

## What To Look For In A Clinic

If you decide to book privately, choose a clinic with qualified, registered sonographers and clear information about what the scan does and does not include. A reputable clinic will explain that an early reassurance scan is not the same as the detailed NHS screening scans. If anything unexpected is found, they should advise you to contact your GP, midwife or your local early pregnancy unit. If you have heavy bleeding, severe one-sided pain or feel faint at any point, seek urgent medical help rather than waiting for a booked scan.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does a private early scan cost?

Private early pregnancy scans usually cost around £60 to £200 in the UK, depending on the clinic, the type of scan, and your location. It is worth checking exactly what is included, such as photos or a written report.

### When is the best time for a private scan?

Around 7 to 8 weeks is often a good time for an early reassurance scan, as a heartbeat can usually be seen and the pregnancy located. Before 6 to 7 weeks it may be too soon to see much, which can occasionally add to anxiety.

### Does a private scan replace my NHS scans?

No. A private reassurance scan does not replace the NHS dating scan or the later screening and anomaly scans, which check your dates, your baby's growth and certain conditions. Continue with all your NHS appointments.

Always speak to your midwife or GP for advice tailored to your circumstances.
