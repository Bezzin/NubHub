# Pregnancy After IVF: Coping With the Anxiety of Finally Being Pregnant

The journey to get here was long, expensive, and emotionally exhausting. Now you're finally pregnant — but instead of pure joy, you feel terrified. This is completely normal.

## Table of Contents

2. [Managing the Anxiety](#section-2)

## How IVF Anxiety Differs {#section-1}

IVF mums often experience higher anxiety because:
- You've invested enormous emotional and financial resources
- You may have had failed cycles before
- You've been focused on getting pregnant for so long that being pregnant feels surreal
- You may have PGT-A results that add pressure
- Each scan feels like a pass/fail test

The anxiety is valid. After everything you've been through, it's natural to be protective of this pregnancy.

## Managing the Anxiety {#section-2}

- Limit scan photo analysis and forum scrolling
- Tell your midwife about your IVF journey so they can offer extra support
- Consider counselling if anxiety is overwhelming
- Take it one day at a time — each day pregnant is a victory
- Connect with other IVF mums who understand

## FAQ {#faq}

### Is it normal to feel anxious after IVF?

Yes. IVF mums often experience higher pregnancy anxiety because of the long journey to get there. This is completely normal and valid.

### Will the anxiety ever go away?

It typically eases after the first trimester when miscarriage risk drops. Many IVF mums find the 20-week scan is a turning point.
