# Fear of Miscarriage: How to Cope

Fear of miscarriage is one of the most common anxieties in early pregnancy, and it can cast a shadow over what should be an exciting time. Understanding the facts, learning to manage anxious thoughts, and knowing where to turn can help you cope during weeks 0-12.

## Understanding the real statistics {#section-1}

The overall miscarriage rate is around one in five pregnancies, but this includes many very early losses. By the time a heartbeat is seen on scan, the risk drops significantly. After a confirmed heartbeat at 8 weeks, the risk of loss is generally under two percent. Most miscarriages are caused by chromosomal issues and are not preventable. Knowing the facts can help counter catastrophic thoughts.

## Managing anxious thoughts about miscarriage {#section-2}

Recognise anxious thoughts as thoughts, not predictions. Avoid repeatedly checking symptoms, as they naturally fluctuate. Limit exposure to loss stories online. Talk to your midwife about your fears rather than holding them in. If you have had a previous loss, ask about additional support or early scans. Professional support via NHS Talking Therapies can help if fear dominates your days.

## FAQ {#faq}

### What is the actual miscarriage rate in early pregnancy?

Around 10-20 percent of confirmed pregnancies end in miscarriage, with most occurring before 12 weeks.

### Does stress or exercise cause miscarriage?

No. Everyday stress, exercise, lifting, and working do not cause miscarriage. Most losses are due to chromosomal factors.

### How do I stop constantly worrying about miscarriage?

Professional support, grounding techniques, limiting online searches, and talking to your midwife can all help reduce persistent fear.
