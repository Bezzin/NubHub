# Coping with an Unplanned Pregnancy

An unplanned pregnancy can feel like the ground has vanished beneath you. Whether it's shock, fear, anger, or numbness — whatever you're feeling is valid. This is a big moment, and you deserve clear information and compassionate support, not pressure.

## First, breathe
You do not have to decide anything today. The urgency you feel is often emotional, not practical. Unless there's a medical reason to act quickly, give yourself time to process before making any decision about the pregnancy.

## Your options
You have three paths, and each is legitimate:
- **Continuing the pregnancy and parenting.** Support exists — financial, practical, and emotional.
- **Continuing the pregnancy and placing the baby for adoption.** This is a valid, caring choice with professional support.
- **Ending the pregnancy (abortion).** Legal and accessible in the UK through NHS services.

Information on all three is available from Brook, BPAS, MSI Reproductive Choices, and the Family Planning Association. A neutral options counsellor can help you think, not decide for you.

## Processing the emotions
Unplanned pregnancy can bring grief (for the future you expected), anger (at circumstances or a partner), fear (about money, judgement, your body), and even relief mixed with guilt. All of these can coexist. You are not a bad person for having complicated feelings.

## Practical steps
- **Confirm the pregnancy.** A test and, if you wish, a scan at a sexual health clinic or GP.
- **Talk to someone safe.** A trusted friend, partner, or counsellor — or a helpline if you'd rather stay anonymous.
- **Write it down.** Listing your fears and supports can clarify what feels right.
- **Avoid pressure.** People with strong views may push their opinion. Your body, your choice.

## If you're continuing the pregnancy
Tell your GP to access antenatal care. If the pregnancy was unplanned, you may struggle with positive feelings for a while — that's normal and doesn't predict your bond with your baby.

## Looking after yourself
Sleep, eat, hydrate, and avoid using alcohol or substances to cope. If you feel hopeless, panicked, or unable to cope, contact your GP, 111, or a crisis line the same day.

## A reminder
There is no single right reaction to an unplanned pregnancy. Whatever you choose, you deserve support without judgment.
