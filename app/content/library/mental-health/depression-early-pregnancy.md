# Depression in Early Pregnancy: Signs to Watch

Low mood in early pregnancy is often dismissed as hormones or tiredness, but persistent sadness, numbness, or hopelessness can be signs of antenatal depression. Recognising it early means you can get support sooner, which is better for you and your baby.

## Signs of depression in early pregnancy {#section-1}

Watch for persistent low mood lasting more than two weeks, loss of interest in things you usually enjoy, feeling numb or disconnected from the pregnancy, changes in appetite, difficulty concentrating, excessive guilt, and thoughts that you are a bad mother or would be better off not here. If you notice these, speak to your GP or midwife.

## How depression differs from normal pregnancy mood changes {#section-2}

Mood swings in pregnancy come and go and often relate to specific triggers. Depression is more constant, lasts longer, and affects your ability to function. It can make you feel detached from your baby or pregnancy in a way that ordinary hormonal shifts do not. If you are unsure, ask a professional to assess you.

## FAQ {#faq}

### Is depression in pregnancy the same as postnatal depression?

No. Antenatal depression occurs during pregnancy and is just as common as postnatal depression, though it is less often discussed.

### Can depression in early pregnancy affect my baby?

Untreated depression can affect bonding and self-care, which is why early support matters. Treatment helps you and your baby.

### What treatment is safe for depression in pregnancy?

Talking therapies like CBT are first-line. Some antidepressants are considered safe in pregnancy. Your GP can advise on options.
