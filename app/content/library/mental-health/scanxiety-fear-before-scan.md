# Scanxiety: The Fear Before Every Scan

Scanxiety is the term many expectant parents use for the intense fear that builds before a pregnancy scan. Whether it is your dating scan at 12 weeks or an early reassurance scan, the wait can be agonising. This page explains why scanxiety happens and offers practical ways to cope on the day.

## Why scans trigger such intense anxiety {#section-1}

Scans are moments of truth. In seconds, you may learn whether your pregnancy is progressing as hoped. For anyone who has had a previous loss or a difficult scan, the association between the scan room and bad news can be powerful. Even first-time mothers feel scanxiety because the scan confirms viability in a way symptoms cannot. The combination of high stakes and lack of control makes scans uniquely stressful.

## Coping strategies for scan day {#section-2}

Plan something comforting for after the scan, regardless of outcome. Bring someone supportive. Tell the sonographer you are anxious so they can explain what they see. Ask for the screen to be explained as you go. If results are not immediate, ask when and how you will hear. Avoid Googling in the waiting room. If scanxiety is severe, ask your midwife about a referral to perinatal mental health support before the scan.

## FAQ {#faq}

### Is scanxiety normal?

Yes. Fear before scans is extremely common, especially in the first trimester and after previous loss.

### What if the sonographer seems concerned during the scan?

Sonographers often concentrate quietly. If you are worried, ask them to explain what they are seeing. You can ask for a follow-up scan if needed.

### How can I reduce anxiety before the 12-week scan?

Prepare questions, bring support, plan a treat afterwards, and avoid symptom-checking online in the days before.
