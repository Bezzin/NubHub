# Setting Boundaries During Pregnancy

Pregnancy tends to invite opinions, questions, and physical intrusions you never asked for. Suddenly everyone has advice on what you should eat, how you should birth, and whether you're "too small" or "too big." Setting boundaries isn't selfish — it's a protective skill, and pregnancy is an excellent time to practise it.

## Why boundaries matter now
Your energy, body, and emotional bandwidth are all under extra demand. Without boundaries, you risk burnout, resentment, and anxiety. With them, you protect the space you need to rest, process, and prepare for your baby.

## Common boundary areas in pregnancy
- **Unsolicited advice.** "You really should have a natural birth." A boundary: "Thanks, I've got a plan I'm comfortable with."
- **Bump touching.** Physical autonomy still applies. "I'd rather people didn't touch my bump, thanks."
- **Medical commentary.** Opinions on your scans, weight, or feeding choices. "I'm keeping medical details private for now."
- **Visits and social demands.** "We'd love a quiet weekend — let's catch up after the baby arrives."

## How to set a boundary kindly
1. **Be clear and brief.** Long explanations invite debate.
2. **Use "I" statements.** "I need some quiet time" rather than "You're too much."
3. **Expect some pushback.** People used to your old patterns may resist. Hold firm without over-explaining.
4. **Follow through.** If a boundary is crossed, act on it — end the visit, change the topic, or leave.

## When boundaries feel impossible
If you grew up people-pleasing, saying no can feel physically uncomfortable. That discomfort is not a sign you're wrong — it's a sign the pattern is old. Start small, notice that nothing catastrophic happens, and build from there.

## Boundaries are a form of care
Far from pushing people away, clear boundaries protect relationships from resentment. They tell others how to love you well.

## A reminder
You are not obligated to host everyone's excitement, opinions, or hands. Your body, your pregnancy, your terms.
