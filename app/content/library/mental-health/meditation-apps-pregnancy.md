# Best Meditation Apps for Pregnancy

Meditation apps can be a convenient way to manage pregnancy anxiety, improve sleep, and feel more connected to your body. Here we compare popular options available in the UK, including free NHS-recommended tools and subscription apps with pregnancy-specific content.

## Table of Contents

2. [How to choose the right app for you](#section-2)
3. [FAQ](#faq)

## Top meditation apps for pregnancy {#section-1}

Calm offers guided meditations and sleep stories, with some pregnancy content. Headspace has structured courses for beginners and anxiety. Expectful is designed specifically for fertility, pregnancy, and postpartum. Insight Timer is free with thousands of tracks. NHS-approved apps such as NHS Moodzone audio are free and ad-free. Compare pricing, as some require subscriptions.

## How to choose the right app for you {#section-2}

Consider your main need: anxiety, sleep, or connection with your baby. Try free trials before subscribing. Check whether content is pregnancy-specific. Read reviews from other expectant mothers. Avoid apps that make medical claims. If you have severe anxiety, use apps alongside professional support, not as a replacement.

## FAQ {#faq}

### Are meditation apps safe in pregnancy?

Yes. Guided meditation and breathing exercises are safe throughout pregnancy.

### Is there a free pregnancy meditation app?

Insight Timer and NHS Moodzone are free. Expectful offers a free trial. Some NHS areas provide free access to meditation apps via prescription.

### Can meditation apps replace therapy?

No. Apps are helpful for mild anxiety and daily maintenance but are not a substitute for professional treatment of moderate to severe symptoms.
