# Lemon as a Morning Sickness Remedy

Lemon is one of the simplest and most surprisingly effective natural remedies for morning sickness. This page explains why it helps and how to use it safely in pregnancy.

## Why Lemon Helps Nausea

The sharp, clean scent of lemon is thought to interrupt nausea signals, and many women find that simply smelling a freshly cut lemon eases a wave of sickness. Lemon also contains mild compounds that may help neutralise stomach acid. Some small studies of aromatherapy in pregnancy found that lemon essential oil scent reduced nausea scores, though more research is needed. The advantage of lemon is that it is cheap, safe and easy to keep to hand, whether as fruit, juice or in drinks.

## Simple Ways to Use Lemon

Keep a fresh lemon sliced and ready to smell when nausea strikes. Add a squeeze of lemon to water or herbal tea, which can make plain fluids more appealing if you are struggling to drink. Lemon in hot water first thing in the morning is a gentle way to start the day. Avoid very sugary lemon drinks, as sugar can worsen energy dips. A few drops of lemon juice on a salad or cold meal can also make plain foods more appetising when little else appeals.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is lemon safe in pregnancy?**

Yes, lemon as fruit or juice in normal food and drink amounts is safe. Avoid concentrated supplements without advice.

**Q: Does smelling lemon really help?**

Many women find the scent eases nausea quickly, and some small studies support lemon aromatherapy for pregnancy nausea.

**Q: What is the best way to use lemon for nausea?**

Keep a sliced lemon ready to smell, and add a squeeze to water or tea to make fluids easier to drink.
