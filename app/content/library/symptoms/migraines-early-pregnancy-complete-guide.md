# Migraines in Early Pregnancy: Complete Guide

Migraines can improve, worsen, or appear for the first time in pregnancy. Here's what you need to know.

## Table of Contents

1. [How Pregnancy Affects Migraines](#s1)
2. [Safe Management](#s2)
3. [FAQ](#faq)

## How Pregnancy Affects Migraines {#s1}

About 50-80% of women who had migraines before pregnancy find they improve in the second and third trimesters. However, in the first trimester, migraines may worsen due to hormonal changes.

## Safe Management {#s2}

Paracetamol is safe. Avoid triptans unless prescribed by a specialist. Anti-sickness medications like prochlorperazine may help. Rest, hydration, and avoiding triggers (certain foods, stress, lack of sleep) are key.

## FAQ {#faq}

### Can I take my migraine medication in pregnancy?

Some migraine medications are not safe in pregnancy. Talk to your GP about pregnancy-safe alternatives. Never stop prescribed medication without consulting your doctor first.

### Will my migraines get worse in pregnancy?

For most women, migraines improve after the first trimester. However, some women experience worse migraines in early pregnancy due to oestrogen fluctuations.
