# Light Spotting vs Heavy Bleeding in Early Pregnancy

One of the most common questions midwives hear is whether the bleeding a woman has noticed is spotting or something more. The distinction matters because it guides what action you should take. This guide explains the difference clearly so you can describe your symptoms accurately and know when to seek help.

## How to Tell the Difference

Spotting is light enough that you see it on toilet paper or as a few drops on underwear. You do not need a pad. Heavy bleeding is anything that requires a sanitary pad, especially if you are soaking one within an hour. The colour also helps - brown and pink are usually lighter and older, while bright red is fresh and more concerning. Clots and tissue are always worth reporting.

## What Each Might Mean

Light spotting is most often implantation bleeding, cervical irritation, or hormonal changes - all common and usually benign. Heavy bleeding can signal a threatened miscarriage, an ectopic pregnancy, or a subchorionic bleed. It does not always mean pregnancy loss, but it does need same-day assessment. When in doubt, call your early pregnancy unit and describe exactly what you are seeing.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do I need a pad to know if it is heavy bleeding?**

If you are using a pad and it is collecting blood rather than just protecting your underwear, treat it as bleeding, not spotting, and contact your midwife.

**Can spotting turn into heavy bleeding?**

Yes, occasionally. If your spotting increases, turns bright red, or you pass clots, contact your early pregnancy unit the same day.

**Is mild cramping with spotting normal?**

Mild period-like cramps with light spotting can be normal as your womb stretches. Severe or one-sided pain with bleeding needs urgent assessment.
