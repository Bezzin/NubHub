# How to Cope with Morning Sickness at Work

Managing morning sickness while holding down a job can be one of the hardest parts of early pregnancy. You may not want to announce your pregnancy yet, but nausea does not conveniently stay at home. With a few practical strategies, you can get through the working day more comfortably.

## To Tell or Not to Tell {#to-tell-or-not-to-tell}

You are not legally required to tell your employer before 15 weeks, but if nausea is affecting your work, sharing the news early with your manager or HR can make life easier. They can arrange flexible hours, allow more breaks, or adjust your duties. If you prefer to wait, you can ask for changes such as flexible start times without disclosing the reason, though pregnancy-specific protections apply once you tell them.

## Desk Strategies for Nausea {#desk-strategies-for-nausea}

Keep plain snacks such as crackers, dry biscuits or rice cakes in your drawer and nibble every hour or two. Ginger sweets, mints or lemon drops can take the edge off nausea. Sip water, ginger tea or diluted juice steadily through the day. Avoid strong-smelling lunches; cold, bland foods such as a plain sandwich, rice salad or crackers with cheese are often better tolerated than hot meals. Keep a small emergency kit with mints, wet wipes and a spare top in case of sudden nausea.

## Managing Meetings and Commutes {#managing-meetings-and-commutes}

Sit near the door in meetings so you can slip out quietly if needed. Let a trusted colleague know, if you can, so they can cover for you. For commutes, try travelling outside peak times, sitting down, keeping a window open, and carrying a snack and water. If smells on public transport trigger nausea, a dab of a tolerable scent such as lemon on a tissue can help mask others.

## Knowing Your Rights {#knowing-your-rights}

Pregnant employees in the UK have the right to a risk assessment, reasonable adjustments, and protection from discrimination. If your work involves heavy lifting, chemicals or long shifts, your employer should adjust your duties once they know you are pregnant. Keep a record of how symptoms affect you and discuss adjustments early rather than struggling in silence.

## FAQ {#faq}

### Do I have to tell my boss I am pregnant early?

No, you can wait until 15 weeks. But if symptoms affect your work, telling them earlier allows adjustments and protects you from discrimination.

### What if I am sick during a meeting?

Excuse yourself calmly. Most people assume a stomach bug. If you have told someone you trust, they can cover for you discreetly.

### Can I take time off for morning sickness?

Yes. Pregnancy-related illness is protected, and you should not be penalised for it. Record it separately from ordinary sickness so it is clear it is pregnancy-related.
