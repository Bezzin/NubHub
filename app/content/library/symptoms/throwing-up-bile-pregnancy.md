# Throwing Up Bile in Pregnancy: What It Means & What to Do

You're already feeling rotten, and now you're vomiting bright yellow or green liquid. It looks alarming, but throwing up bile in pregnancy is actually very common - especially when you've been vomiting a lot. Here's what it means and what you should do.

## What Is Bile? {#what-is-bile}

Bile is a **yellow-green fluid** produced by your liver and stored in your gallbladder. It helps digest fats in your small intestine. When you vomit on an empty stomach, or when vomiting is severe, bile can come up instead of food.

Bile has a distinctive:
- **Colour**: Yellow, yellow-green, or green
- **Taste**: Bitter or sour
- **Consistency**: Thin and watery

Seeing bile in your vomit is alarming but usually not dangerous on its own. It simply means your stomach is empty and your body is bringing up digestive fluids from further down.

## Why You're Vomiting Bile {#why-bile}

There are several reasons you might vomit bile in pregnancy:

### Empty Stomach
The most common reason. When your stomach is empty (because you can't keep food down, or you haven't eaten), there's nothing to bring up except digestive fluids. Bile from your small intestine can reflux back up.

### Severe or Frequent Vomiting
If you've been vomiting multiple times, your stomach has been emptied of food. Subsequent vomits may contain only bile, stomach acid, and mucus.

### Hyperemesis Gravidarum (HG)
Women with HG often vomit bile because they can't keep any food down. The stomach is perpetually empty, so any vomiting brings up bile. Read our [HG guide](/symptoms/hyperemesis-gravidarum-guide).

### Delayed Gastric Emptying
Pregnancy hormones (particularly progesterone) slow digestion. Food stays in your stomach longer, and bile can reflux back up. This is why you might vomit bile even hours after eating.

### Gastroesophageal Reflux
Pregnancy hormones relax the valve between your stomach and oesophagus. Bile, along with stomach acid, can flow upward, causing a bitter taste and sometimes vomiting.

## Is It Dangerous? {#dangerous}

### Usually Not

Vomiting bile itself is **not dangerous**. It's a sign your stomach is empty, not that something is wrong with your pregnancy. The bile is your own digestive fluid - it won't harm you to bring it up.

### When It Can Be a Problem

Vomiting bile becomes concerning when:

- **It's happening frequently** - 5+ times per day
- **You can't keep any food or water down** - risk of dehydration
- **It's accompanied by severe pain** - could indicate other issues
- **There's blood in the bile** - see below
- **You're losing weight**
- **You feel dehydrated** - dark urine, dizziness, dry mouth

### The Real Risk: Dehydration

The danger isn't the bile itself - it's that you can't keep food or fluids down. This leads to dehydration, which is dangerous in pregnancy. If you're vomiting bile and can't keep water down for 12+ hours, seek medical help.

## What the Colour Means {#colour}

### Yellow or Yellow-Green
- **Normal bile colour**
- Means your stomach is empty
- Usually not concerning on its own

### Green
- **Bile that's more concentrated**
- Still normal - just means your gallbladder has released more bile
- Common with repeated vomiting

### Brown or Coffee-Ground
- **Could indicate old blood** in your stomach
- **Seek medical advice** - this needs investigation
- Can be caused by severe vomiting irritating the stomach lining

### Red
- **Fresh blood** - this is an emergency
- **Seek immediate medical attention**
- Can be caused by severe vomiting tearing the oesophagus (Mallory-Weiss tear)

### Clear
- Usually stomach acid and mucus
- Normal for repeated vomiting

## How to Stop Vomiting Bile {#how-to-stop}

### Keep Your Stomach from Being Empty
- **Eat small amounts frequently** - every 1-2 hours
- **Keep dry crackers by your bed** - eat one before you get up
- **Eat a bedtime snack** - so your stomach isn't empty overnight
- **Try bland foods** - plain toast, rice, crackers, bananas

### Stay Hydrated
- **Sip fluids constantly** - even tiny amounts help
- **Try ice chips and ice lollies**
- **Use oral rehydration salts** (Dioralyte)
- **Cold fluids** are often easier to tolerate

### Anti-Nausea Strategies
- **Ginger** (if you can tolerate it)
- **Acupressure wristbands** (Sea-Bands)
- **Rest** - fatigue worsens nausea
- **Avoid triggers** - smells, certain foods
- **Fresh air**

### Medication
If you're vomiting bile frequently, you likely need **prescription medication**:
- **First-line**: Cyclizine, promethazine, or prochlorperazine
- **Second-line**: Ondansetron, metoclopramide
- **Combination therapy** is common

Read our guide to [anti-sickness medication in pregnancy](/symptoms/anti-sickness-medication-pregnancy).

### If You Can't Keep Tablets Down
- **Dissolvable tablets** (ondansetron ondispers, prochlorperazine buccal)
- **Suppositories** (promethazine)
- **IV medication** (in hospital)

## When to Seek Help {#when-help}

### Contact Your GP If:
- You're vomiting bile 3+ times per day
- You can't keep food down
- You're losing weight
- You feel dizzy, weak, or dehydrated
- Your urine is dark

### Go to Hospital If:
- You can't keep any water down for 12+ hours
- You have no urine for 8+ hours
- You're vomiting blood (red or brown)
- You have severe abdominal pain
- You feel confused or very drowsy
- You're dehydrated (sunken eyes, dry mouth, dizziness)

### When It's an Emergency
Call 999 or go to A&E if:
- You're vomiting **red blood**
- You have **severe abdominal pain**
- You're **confused or can't stay awake**
- You've **fainted**

## Frequently Asked Questions {#faq}

### Is throwing up bile normal in pregnancy?

Vomiting bile (yellow or green fluid) is common in pregnancy, especially if you've been vomiting a lot or on an empty stomach. It's not dangerous in itself, but if you're vomiting bile frequently and can't keep food or water down, you need medical treatment.

### What colour is bile vomit?

Bile is typically yellow, yellow-green, or green. If your vomit is brown (coffee-ground) or red, this could indicate blood - seek medical attention.

### Why am I vomiting bile if I haven't eaten?

When your stomach is empty, there's nothing to bring up except digestive fluids. Bile from your small intestine can reflux back up, especially when pregnancy hormones slow digestion and relax the valve between your stomach and oesophagus.

### Should I go to hospital if I'm vomiting bile?

Go to hospital if you can't keep any water down for 12+ hours, have no urine for 8+ hours, are vomiting blood, or feel dizzy, confused, or very unwell. Vomiting bile alone isn't an emergency, but dehydration from not keeping fluids down is.

### Will vomiting bile harm my baby?

Vomiting bile itself won't harm your baby. The concern is dehydration and malnutrition from not being able to keep food and fluids down. Getting treatment for the underlying nausea and vomiting protects both you and your baby.
