# When Does Morning Sickness End?

The end of morning sickness is a milestone many pregnant women look forward to. While there is no single day it stops for everyone, there is a clear typical pattern, and recognising the signs that nausea is easing can be reassuring during a difficult phase.

## When Does Morning Sickness Usually End? {#when-does-morning-sickness-usually-end}

For most women, morning sickness ends between weeks 12 and 14 of pregnancy, as the first trimester draws to a close. This coincides with the stabilising of hCG and other hormone levels. Some women find symptoms disappear almost overnight, while others notice a gradual fading over a week or two. A small number continue to feel nausea into the second trimester, and an even smaller number have it throughout pregnancy.

## Signs It Is Easing {#signs-it-is-easing}

You may notice you can tolerate a wider range of foods again, that smells no longer trigger nausea, and that you have more energy. The gap between bouts of nausea lengthens, and you may go a full day without feeling sick for the first time in weeks. Appetite often returns, though some food aversions linger for a while longer. These are all positive signs that the worst is over.

## What If It Does Not End? {#what-if-it-does-not-end}

If you reach 14 or 16 weeks and nausea is still significant, speak to your midwife. A minority of women have a longer course, and there may be safe anti-sickness options that can help. It is also worth checking for other causes, such as acid reflux or food intolerances, which can mimic ongoing morning sickness and are treated differently.

## When to Seek Help Before It Ends {#when-to-seek-help-before-it-ends}

If at any point you cannot keep fluids down for 12 to 24 hours, are losing weight, feel dizzy or faint, or notice very dark urine, contact your GP or midwife. These can be signs of dehydration or hyperemesis gravidarum, which needs prompt treatment. Do not wait for it to end on its own if symptoms are severe.

## FAQ {#faq}

### Can morning sickness end then come back?

It can, particularly if triggered by tiredness, certain foods, or stress. A brief return is common and usually settles, but a full recurrence later may have another cause worth checking.

### Does the sudden end of morning sickness mean miscarriage?

Not necessarily. Symptoms often fade naturally around the end of the first trimester. However, if all pregnancy symptoms vanish suddenly very early, contact your midwife for reassurance or an early scan.

### Is it normal to still feel sick at 15 weeks?

A minority of women do. It often continues to improve over the following weeks, but mention it to your midwife so they can offer support or check for other causes.
