# Left-Sided Pain in Early Pregnancy: Causes and Care

Pain focused on the lower left side of your abdomen in early pregnancy can feel alarming, especially if it is sharp or persistent. In most cases, the cause is benign - but because left-sided pain can occasionally signal an ectopic pregnancy, it is worth understanding what is normal and what is not. This guide walks you through the most likely culprits.

## Common Benign Causes on the Left

Round ligament pain is a frequent cause - as your womb grows, the ligament on the left can stretch and spasm, causing a sharp twinge. If you ovulated from your left ovary, you may have a corpus luteum cyst there, which can ache. Constipation, very common in pregnancy thanks to hormonal slowing of your gut, often builds up on the left side where the bowel descends. All of these are harmless.

## When Left-Sided Pain Needs Urgent Attention

Seek same-day care if the pain is severe, worsening, or paired with vaginal bleeding, shoulder tip pain, dizziness, or fainting. An ectopic pregnancy can implant in the left fallopian tube, and a ruptured tube is a medical emergency. Your early pregnancy unit will perform a scan and blood tests to check the location of the pregnancy and rule out anything serious.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is left-sided cramping at 5 weeks normal?**

Mild cramps are common as your womb and ligaments adjust. If they are severe or accompanied by bleeding, call your midwife.

**Can constipation cause left-sided pain in pregnancy?**

Yes. The descending colon runs down the left side, and constipation is very common in pregnancy, often causing localised discomfort.

**When is left-sided pain an emergency?**

If it is severe, you have bleeding, shoulder tip pain, or feel dizzy or faint, call 999 - these can indicate an ectopic pregnancy.
