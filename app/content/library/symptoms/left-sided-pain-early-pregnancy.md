# Left-Sided Pain in Early Pregnancy: Causes and Care

Pain focused on the lower left side of your abdomen in early pregnancy can feel alarming, especially if it is sharp or persistent. In most cases, the cause is benign — but because left-sided pain can occasionally signal an ectopic pregnancy, it is worth understanding what is normal and what is not. This guide walks you through the most likely culprits and tells you clearly when to seek help.

## Common Benign Causes on the Left

The majority of left-sided pain in early pregnancy has a straightforward, harmless explanation. Your body is going through rapid physical changes, and many of them cause noticeable discomfort.

**Round ligament pain:** The round ligaments support the womb on both sides. As your uterus grows — even in the first trimester — these ligaments stretch and can spasm, causing a sharp, sudden twinge or a dull aching sensation. It is more common on the right, but the left is affected too. The pain often comes on with sudden movement, coughing, or rolling over in bed.

**Corpus luteum cyst:** After ovulation, the follicle that released your egg becomes a corpus luteum — a temporary structure that produces progesterone to support the pregnancy in its early weeks. If you ovulated from your left ovary, the corpus luteum sits on the left side and can cause a dull, achy sensation there. This is completely normal and usually resolves by the end of the first trimester as the placenta takes over progesterone production.

**Constipation and gas:** Progesterone slows down your digestive system significantly in early pregnancy, which leads to constipation and trapped wind in many women. The descending colon runs down the left side of the abdomen, so constipation frequently causes localised discomfort on the left. Increasing your fluid intake, eating fibre-rich foods, and gentle movement can all help.

**Implantation and uterine stretching:** In very early pregnancy, the womb begins growing and the lining adjusts to support the embryo. This can cause mild cramps that feel similar to period pain, and they may be more noticeable on one side depending on where the embryo has implanted.

## Less Common Causes Worth Knowing {#less-common-causes}

**Ovarian cyst:** Small ovarian cysts are relatively common in pregnancy and are usually harmless. If a cyst is present on the left ovary, it can cause a persistent dull ache or occasional sharp pain. Most resolve on their own, but your midwife may recommend monitoring with a scan if you are having ongoing discomfort.

**Symphysis pubis dysfunction (SPD):** While more associated with pelvic pain as pregnancy progresses, some women experience pelvic girdle pain that begins early and can be felt across the lower abdomen and into the left or right side.

**Urinary tract infection (UTI):** UTIs are more common in pregnancy, and while they typically cause discomfort on urination and lower abdominal pressure, some women feel referred pain on one side. If you have any urinary symptoms alongside left-sided pain, contact your GP or midwife.

## When Left-Sided Pain Needs Urgent Attention

Seek same-day care — from your early pregnancy unit, GP, or by calling 111 — if:

- The pain is severe, worsening, or does not ease with rest
- You have any vaginal bleeding alongside the pain
- You have shoulder tip pain (a referred pain felt at the tip of the shoulder, caused by internal bleeding irritating the diaphragm)
- You feel dizzy, faint, or unwell

**Call 999 immediately** if the pain is sudden and severe, especially if combined with shoulder tip pain, dizziness, or fainting. These are warning signs of a ruptured ectopic pregnancy, which is a life-threatening emergency. An ectopic pregnancy can implant in the left fallopian tube and, if the tube ruptures, causes internal bleeding. It requires emergency surgery.

An ectopic pregnancy is rare, but it cannot be ruled out without a scan and blood tests. If you have any doubt, err on the side of caution and seek assessment. Your early pregnancy unit will scan you to confirm the location of the pregnancy and check that everything is where it should be.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is left-sided cramping at 5 weeks normal?

Mild cramps on either side are common as your womb and ligaments adjust to early pregnancy. If they are mild, brief, and not accompanied by bleeding, they are most likely benign. If they are severe, persistent, or come with bleeding, contact your midwife or early pregnancy unit.

### Can constipation cause left-sided pain in pregnancy?

Yes. The descending colon runs down the left side of the abdomen, and constipation is very common in pregnancy due to the effect of progesterone on the gut. Staying hydrated, eating fibre, and gentle walking can all help relieve it.

### When is left-sided pain an emergency?

If the pain is severe, you have vaginal bleeding, shoulder tip pain, or you feel dizzy or faint, call 999 immediately. These symptoms can indicate a ruptured ectopic pregnancy, which is a medical emergency requiring urgent treatment.
