# Acupressure Bands for Morning Sickness

Acupressure bands are soft wristbands that press on the P6 point, long used for travel sickness and now widely tried by pregnant women in the UK. This page explains how they work, how to fit them and what to expect.

## How Acupressure Targets Nausea

The P6 or Neiguan point sits on the inner wrist, roughly three finger-widths below the crease. Stimulating it is believed to influence the nerve pathways that trigger nausea. Some clinical trials show modest benefit for pregnancy nausea, while others find little effect, so results genuinely vary between women. The appeal of bands is that they are drug-free, reusable and inexpensive, making them a low-risk option worth trying alongside other strategies. Many UK pharmacies stock them without prescription.

## Fitting and Wearing the Bands

Place the plastic stud on the inside of each wrist, three finger-widths down from the wrist crease, between the two tendons you can feel when you flex your hand. Wear both bands for best results, and keep them on through the times of day you feel worst. Some women find them more useful for mild nausea than for severe vomiting. They can be worn overnight, and unlike medication there is no concern about dose. If you see no improvement after a few days, try a different remedy rather than relying on the bands alone.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How quickly do acupressure bands work?**

Some women feel relief within minutes, while others notice no real difference. Give them a fair trial of a few days before deciding.

**Q: Can I wear the bands all day?**

Yes, they are safe for continuous wear. Take them off if the skin becomes sore or irritated.

**Q: Do acupressure bands help severe vomiting?**

They tend to help mild to moderate nausea more than severe vomiting. If you cannot keep fluids down, contact your GP or midwife promptly.
