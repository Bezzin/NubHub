# Fresh Air for Morning Sickness

Stuffy rooms and lingering smells can make pregnancy nausea noticeably worse. A simple walk or an open window is often overlooked but can genuinely help. This page explains how to use fresh air as a practical remedy.

## Why Air Quality Matters

Pregnancy heightens your sense of smell, which means odours that were once unnoticeable can become powerful nausea triggers. Cooking smells, perfumes, stale air and cleaning products all build up indoors. Fresh air dilutes these odours and gives your senses a break. A short walk outdoors also provides gentle movement, which can aid digestion and reduce the lethargy that makes nausea feel harder to cope with. It is a simple, free and low-risk strategy.

## Practical Fresh Air Tips

Open windows when cooking and for a few minutes each morning. Step outside for a short walk, even just around the block, when nausea builds. Avoid walking in very hot or humid weather if that worsens symptoms. If going outside is difficult, sit near an open window and breathe slowly. Ask family members to avoid strong perfumes or cooking strong-smelling foods when you are struggling. Small changes to your environment can make a measurable difference.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Does fresh air really help morning sickness?**

Yes, by diluting odours and giving you a break from stuffy, smelly environments that can trigger nausea.

**Q: How long should I go outside for?**

Even five to ten minutes can help. A short gentle walk is ideal if you feel up to it.

**Q: What if I cannot go outside?**

Sit by an open window and breathe slowly. Even brief ventilation of your room can reduce nausea triggers.
