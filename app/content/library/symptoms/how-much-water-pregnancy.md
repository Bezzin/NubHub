# How Much Water Should You Drink in Pregnancy?

Staying well hydrated in pregnancy supports your increased blood volume, helps form amniotic fluid, aids digestion and can even ease some common complaints such as constipation and headaches. But how much should you actually drink, and what counts towards your daily total?

## How Much Water Do You Need? {#how-much-water-do-you-need}

The general advice for pregnant women in the UK is to drink around 8 to 10 glasses of fluid a day, roughly 1.6 to 2 litres. This includes water, milk, diluted juices, herbal teas and the water content of foods such as soup and fruit. You may need more in hot weather, during exercise, or if you are losing fluid through vomiting. Let thirst and the colour of your urine guide you; pale yellow is ideal.

## Why Hydration Matters in Pregnancy {#why-hydration-matters-in-pregnancy}

Your blood volume increases by up to 50 percent during pregnancy, and water is essential for this expansion. Water also helps form amniotic fluid, supports kidney function as your body processes extra waste, and aids digestion by preventing constipation. Mild dehydration can contribute to headaches, dizziness and tiredness, so consistent fluid intake through the day is better than drinking large amounts occasionally.

## Tips When Water Makes You Nauseous {#tips-when-water-makes-you-nauseous}

If plain water turns your stomach, there are plenty of alternatives. Try sparkling water with a slice of lemon, diluted fruit juice, herbal teas such as ginger or peppermint, or cold fruit infusions. Sipping small amounts frequently is often better tolerated than large glasses. Ice lollies made from diluted juice can help, and water-rich foods such as watermelon, cucumber and oranges contribute to your fluid intake too.

## Signs You May Need More Fluid {#signs-you-may-need-more-fluid}

Watch for dark yellow urine, infrequent trips to the toilet, headaches, dizziness or dry lips. These can all signal dehydration. If you are vomiting and unable to keep fluids down for more than 12 to 24 hours, contact your midwife or GP, as severe dehydration in pregnancy may need assessment and sometimes intravenous fluids.

## FAQ {#faq}

### Can I drink sparkling water in pregnancy?

Yes, sparkling water is safe and can be a good option if plain water feels unappealing. Just be mindful that very fizzy drinks may worsen bloating for some women.

### Do caffeinated drinks count towards fluid intake?

They contribute some fluid, but caffeine should be limited to 200mg a day. Water, milk and herbal teas are better choices for everyday hydration.

### Is it possible to drink too much water?

Excessive water intake can dilute sodium levels, but this is rare with normal drinking habits. Let thirst be your guide and avoid forcing very large quantities beyond your needs.
