# Protein Snacks for Morning Sickness

Protein is often overlooked in morning sickness advice, yet many women find that including protein at each snack stabilises blood sugar and reduces nausea more effectively than carbohydrates alone. This page explains why and gives easy ideas.

## Why Protein Eases Nausea

Protein slows digestion and helps keep blood sugar steady, which reduces the dips that trigger nausea. Some small studies suggest protein-rich meals are associated with fewer symptoms than carbohydrate-heavy ones. Protein also helps with satiety, meaning you feel less of the emptiness that can bring on a wave of nausea. The trick is to combine a little protein with carbohydrate at each snack rather than eating large protein meals, which can feel heavy.

## Easy Protein Snack Ideas

Try a cheese and cracker pair, a hard-boiled egg, a small handful of nuts, yogurt with seeds, or hummus with breadsticks. If smells are a problem, cold protein snacks such as cheese or yogurt tend to be more tolerable than hot meat. Keep snacks simple and ready to grab, as preparation time can worsen nausea. Aim for around 5 to 10g of protein per snack, spread through the day alongside the small-meal approach.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Why does protein help morning sickness?**

It stabilises blood sugar and slows digestion, both of which reduce the nausea triggered by empty-stomach dips.

**Q: What if meat smells make me feel ill?**

Choose cold protein snacks such as cheese, yogurt or nuts, which have little odour and are often better tolerated.

**Q: How much protein should each snack contain?**

Around 5 to 10g is plenty. The aim is steady intake rather than large amounts at one sitting.
