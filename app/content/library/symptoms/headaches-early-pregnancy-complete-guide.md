# Headaches in Early Pregnancy: Complete Guide

Headaches are one of the most common early pregnancy symptoms, affecting up to 40% of pregnant women.

## Table of Contents

1. [Causes](#s1)
2. [Safe Treatment](#s2)
3. [FAQ](#faq)

## Causes {#s1}

Headaches in early pregnancy are caused by: hormonal changes, increased blood volume, dehydration, low blood sugar, stress, caffeine withdrawal, and poor sleep. Most are tension headaches.

## Safe Treatment {#s2}

Paracetamol is generally safe in pregnancy (follow dosage instructions). Avoid ibuprofen. Other remedies: drink water, rest in a dark room, use a cold compress, eat regularly, and try relaxation techniques.

## FAQ {#faq}

### Can I take paracetamol for headaches in pregnancy?

Yes, paracetamol is considered safe in pregnancy at the recommended dose. Avoid ibuprofen and aspirin unless prescribed.

### When are headaches dangerous in pregnancy?

Severe headaches that don't respond to paracetamol, especially with visual disturbances or upper abdominal pain, need urgent assessment for pre-eclampsia. This is more common after 20 weeks.
