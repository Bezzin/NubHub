# Weight Loss in First Trimester: Is It Normal?

Stepping on the scales and seeing a lower number? Losing a little weight in the first trimester is more common than you might think. Here's when it's normal and when to contact your midwife.

## Why Weight Loss Happens {#why-loss}

Weight loss in the first 12 weeks is usually down to:

- **Morning sickness**: Nausea and vomiting reduce how much you eat and keep down.
- **Loss of appetite**: Hormones suppress hunger and food becomes unappealing.
- **Food aversions**: Favourite meals suddenly seem revolting.
- **Heightened smell**: Strong food odours put you off eating.
- **Changes in digestion**: Slower digestion and bloating can reduce intake.
- **Lifestyle changes**: You may be exercising less or eating differently.

For most women, this kind of weight loss is mild and temporary.

## How Much Weight Loss Is Normal? {#how-much}

Losing up to around 2 to 3 kilograms (about 4 to 7 pounds) in the first trimester is generally considered acceptable, particularly if you started pregnancy at a healthy weight or above. Your baby is still very small and has modest nutritional needs at this stage.

What matters more than the number on the scales is how you feel. If you're staying hydrated, keeping some food down and feeling reasonably well, a small weight dip is usually fine.

## When to Worry {#when-worry}

Contact your GP or midwife promptly if:

- You're losing weight quickly (more than 5% of your pre-pregnancy weight)
- You can't keep any food or fluids down for 24 hours
- You feel dizzy, faint or weak
- Your urine is dark and you're weeing infrequently
- You're vomiting several times a day

These could indicate hyperemesis gravidarum or dehydration, which need medical treatment. Severe sickness is not something you just have to put up with.

## How to Maintain Your Weight {#how-maintain}

If you're struggling to eat, focus on:

- **Small, frequent meals** of bland, easy-to-digest foods
- **Calorie-dense snacks** like nuts, cheese or smoothies if tolerated
- **Cold or room-temperature foods** that smell less
- **Staying hydrated** with water, herbal teas or squash
- **Eating whatever you can keep down** — calories in are better than none
- **Taking a pregnancy vitamin** to fill nutritional gaps

## Frequently Asked Questions {#faq}

### Is losing weight in the first trimester harmful to my baby?

In most cases, no. Your baby is tiny in the first trimester and needs very few extra calories. As long as you're staying hydrated and keeping some food down, a small weight loss is usually safe.

### Should I try to gain weight back quickly?

No. Forcing large meals when you feel sick will often make nausea worse. Focus on small, frequent portions and let your appetite recover naturally as you approach the second trimester.

### When will my appetite and weight return to normal?

For most women, nausea and appetite improve around weeks 12 to 14, and weight gain typically begins steadily in the second trimester.
