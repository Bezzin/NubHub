# Nail Changes in Pregnancy: Complete Guide

Pregnancy hormones can affect your nails as well as your skin and hair. Some people find their nails grow faster and feel stronger, while others notice them becoming softer, more brittle, or prone to splitting and peeling. Both directions are common and almost always harmless, and your nails will usually settle back to how they were within a few months of giving birth.

## Common Changes {#s1}

Many people notice their nails grow faster during pregnancy and look thicker or healthier than usual. Others experience the opposite: nails that feel soft, bend easily, develop ridges, or chip and peel at the tips. You might also see small grooves or changes in colour. All of these variations are within the range of normal, and it is even possible to notice different changes in different pregnancies.

These changes affect the nail you can see, which is already made of non-living tissue, so they are cosmetic rather than a sign of anything wrong with you or your baby.

## Why It Happens {#s2}

The main driver is the surge in pregnancy hormones, particularly oestrogen, alongside increased blood flow and a faster metabolism. For some people this speeds up nail growth and makes nails feel stronger. For others, the same hormonal shifts and the extra demands pregnancy places on the body leave nails feeling more fragile. Everyday factors such as frequent hand-washing, household chemicals, and using gel or acrylic products can add to dryness and brittleness. Because the changes are linked to pregnancy hormones, they are temporary.

## Looking After Your Nails

You do not need to do anything special, but a few simple habits can help if your nails feel weak. Keep them fairly short to reduce snagging and splitting, moisturise your hands and cuticles regularly, and wear gloves for washing up and cleaning to protect them from water and chemicals. A balanced diet that includes protein, iron, and your usual pregnancy supplements supports healthy nails along with the rest of your body. Be gentle with nail tools and avoid harsh removers where you can.

## When to Mention It to a Midwife or GP

Nail changes on their own are rarely a cause for concern. It is worth mentioning to your midwife or GP if your nails become very brittle alongside symptoms such as extreme tiredness, breathlessness, or unusual paleness, as this can sometimes be linked to low iron. You should also seek advice if a nail becomes painful, red, swollen, or is leaking pus, as this may be an infection that needs treatment, or if you notice a new dark streak or change in nail colour that you are unsure about.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Will my nails go back to normal after pregnancy?

Yes. Nail changes during pregnancy are temporary and driven by hormones. Most people find their nails gradually return to their usual texture and growth rate within a few months of giving birth.

### Are brittle nails in pregnancy a sign of a problem?

Usually not. Brittle, soft, or peeling nails are a common and harmless effect of pregnancy hormones and everyday wear. If brittleness comes with tiredness, breathlessness, or paleness, mention it to your midwife so they can check whether low iron might be playing a part.

### Is it safe to have manicures or use nail products in pregnancy?

Occasional manicures are generally considered fine. Use products in a well-ventilated space, avoid breathing in fumes, and let salon staff know you are pregnant. If you have any specific concerns about a treatment, check with your midwife or GP.

For advice tailored to your circumstances, speak to your midwife or GP.
