# Cervical Bleeding in Pregnancy: Causes and Reassurance

Your cervix goes through enormous changes during pregnancy - it becomes softer, engorged with blood, and more sensitive than usual. That means even gentle contact can cause light bleeding. Cervical bleeding is one of the most common and least worrying causes of spotting in pregnancy. This guide explains why it happens and what to do about it.

## Why Your Cervix Bleeds More in Pregnancy

Pregnancy hormones increase blood flow to your cervix, making it engorged and delicate. The medical term is cervical ectropion, where the softer inner cells spread to the outer cervix. These cells bleed easily when touched - by a penis during sex, a speculum during an exam, or even a bowel movement. The result is usually light pink or brown spotting that settles within a day.

## Managing Cervical Bleeding

If you notice light spotting after sex or an internal exam, it is usually cervical in origin and nothing to worry about. Avoid intercourse until the bleeding has fully settled, and mention it to your midwife at your next appointment. If the bleeding becomes heavy, bright red, or is accompanied by pain, contact your early pregnancy unit to rule out other causes.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is bleeding after sex always cervical?**

Often, but not always. Your midwife can examine your cervix to confirm. If bleeding is heavy or painful, further investigation is needed.

**Will cervical ectropion affect my pregnancy?**

No. It is a normal hormonal change and does not harm your baby or increase miscarriage risk.

**Should I avoid sex if I have cervical bleeding?**

Until the bleeding settles, yes. Once it has stopped and your midwife is happy, you can resume normal activity.
