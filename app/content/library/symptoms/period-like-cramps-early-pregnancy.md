# Period-Like Cramps in Early Pregnancy: Normal or Not?

Feeling period-like cramps when you are newly pregnant is one of the most common - and most anxiety-inducing - symptoms. The good news is that mild cramping is usually a sign that your womb is doing exactly what it should: stretching and growing to accommodate your baby. This guide explains what is normal and what warrants a call to your midwife.

## Why You Feel Cramps in Early Pregnancy

From the moment of implantation, your womb starts to change. The muscle thickens, the ligaments stretch, and hormones relax your pelvic joints. All of this can feel like a dull period ache or mild cramps low in your abdomen. You might also feel twinges when you stand up quickly, cough, or roll over in bed. These are normal and usually settle with rest and a warm pack.

## When Cramping Needs Checking

Mild, bilateral cramps that come and go are usually fine. Contact your midwife or early pregnancy unit if cramps are severe, one-sided, or accompanied by bleeding, fever, or back pain. Severe cramping with bleeding can signal a miscarriage or ectopic pregnancy, and prompt assessment brings clarity. Most women with mild cramping go on to have healthy pregnancies, so try not to jump to the worst conclusion.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How long do early pregnancy cramps last?**

They can come and go throughout the first trimester as your womb grows. Each episode usually lasts a few minutes to a few hours.

**Are cramps at 6 weeks a sign of miscarriage?**

Not on their own. Mild cramps without bleeding are very common. If cramps are severe or paired with bleeding, contact your midwife.

**Can I take painkillers for pregnancy cramps?**

Paracetamol is generally safe in pregnancy. Avoid ibuprofen and other NSAIDs in the first trimester unless advised by your midwife.
