# Peppermint Tea for Morning Sickness

Peppermint tea is a popular, gentle option that some pregnant women find calms their stomach when nausea strikes. This page covers how it may help, how to use it safely, and a few important points to be aware of.

## How Peppermint Soothes the Stomach

Peppermint contains menthol, a compound thought to relax the smooth muscle of the digestive tract and reduce the spasms that contribute to nausea. Many women find the cool, mild aroma easier to tolerate during pregnancy than stronger food smells — which is relevant, because strong scents are one of the most common nausea triggers in the first trimester. Sipping a warm drink in itself can also be settling, helping to slow breathing and encourage a moment of rest.

Scientific evidence specifically supporting peppermint for pregnancy nausea is limited. Most of what we know comes from general research into peppermint and digestive discomfort, or from women's self-reported experience. That said, peppermint tea made from dried leaves or standard teabags is caffeine-free, widely available, and generally considered safe in moderate amounts during pregnancy — making it a reasonable first-line home remedy for mild nausea.

## Who Peppermint Tea Might Not Suit

Peppermint is not ideal for everyone during pregnancy. The menthol in peppermint relaxes the lower oesophageal sphincter — the valve between the stomach and oesophagus — which can worsen acid reflux and heartburn. If you already experience reflux (very common in the second and third trimesters), peppermint tea may make it worse rather than better.

If heartburn is an issue for you, ginger tea is a well-regarded alternative with a different mechanism of action that does not worsen reflux. Speak to your midwife or GP if you are unsure which is more appropriate for your situation.

## Brewing and Safety Tips

Using peppermint tea safely during pregnancy is largely a matter of moderation and preparation:

- **Use one teabag per cup** and steep for three to five minutes. Standard strength is fine; very strong infusions are unnecessarily concentrated.
- **Drink it warm rather than very hot.** Very hot drinks of any kind are not recommended in large amounts during pregnancy.
- **Limit yourself to a few cups a day.** Moderate amounts from teabags are considered low risk; drinking very large quantities of any herbal tea is not advisable.
- **Avoid peppermint essential oil entirely.** Oil is far more concentrated than tea and is not recommended during pregnancy.
- **Try it after meals.** A cup after eating may help with the post-meal nausea that many women experience in the first trimester, as well as supporting digestion.

## Other Remedies to Try Alongside Peppermint

Peppermint tea works best as part of a broader approach to managing morning sickness. Other approaches supported by general guidance include:

- **Ginger.** Ginger tea, crystallised ginger, or ginger biscuits are frequently recommended for pregnancy nausea and are well tolerated by many women.
- **Small, frequent meals.** An empty stomach often worsens nausea. Eating small amounts regularly — including before getting out of bed in the morning — can reduce symptoms.
- **Cold foods.** Some women find cold or room-temperature food more tolerable than hot food, as it produces less smell.
- **Hydration.** Sipping water regularly throughout the day helps prevent dehydration, which can worsen nausea.

If nausea is severe — particularly if you cannot keep fluids down, are losing weight, or feel faint — contact your midwife or GP promptly. Hyperemesis gravidarum (HG), a severe form of pregnancy sickness, requires medical treatment and is not something to manage with home remedies alone.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is peppermint tea safe in the first trimester?

Peppermint tea made from standard teabags in moderate amounts is widely considered safe throughout pregnancy, including the first trimester. Avoid concentrated peppermint preparations such as essential oils, and limit yourself to a few cups per day. If you have any specific concerns, ask your midwife at your next appointment.

### Can peppermint tea cause heartburn in pregnancy?

Yes, for some women. Peppermint relaxes the valve between the stomach and oesophagus, which can allow stomach acid to travel upward. If you notice heartburn or reflux worsening after drinking peppermint tea, switch to ginger or chamomile tea, which do not have this effect. Heartburn is already very common in pregnancy, particularly from the second trimester onward.

### When is the best time to drink peppermint tea for nausea?

Many women find a cup after meals helpful for the post-eating nausea that is common in early pregnancy. Sipping it slowly when nausea strikes — rather than drinking it quickly — may also be more effective. Warm rather than hot works best for most people. If morning nausea is your main issue, keeping a flask by the bed and sipping before getting up is worth trying.
