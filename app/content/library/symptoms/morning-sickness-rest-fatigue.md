# Rest and Fatigue Management for Morning Sickness

Tiredness and morning sickness feed each other, so managing fatigue is a genuinely important part of reducing nausea. This page explains why rest matters and how to build it into your day.

## Why Fatigue Worsens Nausea

Pregnancy fatigue is real and powerful, particularly in the first trimester. When you are exhausted, your body is less able to cope with nausea, and the nausea itself makes sleep harder, creating a difficult cycle. Studies of pregnant women consistently show that poor sleep and high fatigue are linked to more severe nausea symptoms. Rest does not cure morning sickness, but it lowers the threshold at which nausea becomes overwhelming, which makes everything else easier to manage.

## Building Rest Into Your Day

Aim for an earlier bedtime and a short nap in the afternoon if you can. Sit down during tasks that you would normally do standing, and accept offers of help with cooking and chores. If nausea is worst in the morning, prepare for it the night before by having snacks and water ready by your bed. Gentle activity such as a short walk can improve sleep quality, but avoid pushing yourself. Treat rest as a treatment in its own right, not a luxury.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Does tiredness make morning sickness worse?**

Yes, fatigue lowers your ability to cope with nausea and is linked to more severe symptoms.

**Q: How much rest do I need in early pregnancy?**

Most women benefit from an earlier night and a daytime nap or rest period. Listen to your body.

**Q: What if I cannot sleep because of nausea?**

Try resting upright with a snack nearby. If sleep remains very difficult, speak to your midwife about additional support.
