# Dry Crackers for Morning Sickness

Dry crackers are probably the most famous morning sickness remedy, and they remain a sensible first option for many pregnant women. This page explains why they help and how to get the most from them.

## Why Crackers Help

Plain dry crackers are bland, low in odour and easy to digest, making them gentle on a sensitive stomach. They absorb excess stomach acid and provide a small amount of carbohydrate that can stabilise blood sugar. Their real strength is convenience, as you can keep them beside the bed and eat one before you even sit up. Many women find that starting the day with a cracker reduces the sudden nausea that hits on an empty stomach first thing in the morning.

## Getting the Most from Crackers

Keep a small pack by your bedside and eat one or two slowly before getting up, then wait a few minutes before moving. Choose plain varieties without strong flavourings or high salt. Salted crackers can help if you have been vomiting, as they replace a little sodium, but plain are usually best for everyday nausea. Pair them with a sip of water rather than a large drink, which can overfill the stomach. Crackers are a useful tool but work best alongside other strategies rather than alone.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Which crackers are best for morning sickness?**

Plain, unsalted or lightly salted crackers are usually best. Avoid strongly flavoured or heavily seasoned varieties.

**Q: When should I eat crackers?**

Eat one or two before getting out of bed and keep some with you for when nausea strikes through the day.

**Q: Can I eat too many crackers?**

They are safe, but variety matters. Use them as a tool alongside other foods and remedies rather than relying on them exclusively.
