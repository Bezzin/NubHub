# Heightened Smell and Nausea: How to Cope

When every smell sets off your nausea, daily life becomes a real challenge. Here's how to cope when a heightened sense of smell is triggering sickness in early pregnancy.

## Why Smells Trigger Nausea {#why}

The link between smell and nausea in pregnancy is well documented:

- **Hormone-driven hyperosmia**: Rising hCG and oestrogen sharpen your sense of smell.
- **Brain wiring**: The brain's smell and nausea centres are closely linked, so strong scents directly trigger the sick reflex.
- **Evolutionary protection**: Heightened smell may have evolved to help pregnant women avoid unsafe foods.
- **Morning sickness amplifier**: Smell sensitivity often makes existing nausea worse.
- **Trigger chaining**: One whiff of a trigger food can set off a vomiting episode that lasts hours.

## Worst Offender Smells {#worst}

The smells most likely to trigger nausea include:

- **Cooking meat, fish and eggs**
- **Garlic, onion and spicy foods**
- **Coffee** (even the smell of brewing)
- **Cigarette smoke**
- **Strong perfumes and air fresheners**
- **Bin and food waste**
- **Public transport and enclosed spaces**
- **Pet litter and animal smells**

## Practical Coping Strategies {#cope}

- **Cook with windows open**: Ventilation is your best friend.
- **Let others cook**: Ask your partner or housemates to handle meal prep.
- **Eat cold or room-temperature foods**: They release far fewer odours.
- **Carry a scent lifesaver**: A tissue with lemon, ginger or peppermint oil to sniff when triggers appear.
- **Switch to unscented products**: Choose fragrance-free toiletries, washing powder and cleaning products.
- **Avoid the kitchen after cooking**: Lingering smells can be enough to set you off.
- **Keep bins empty and clean**: Take out rubbish daily.
- **Try plain, bland foods**: Crackers, toast, rice and bananas are less smelly and easier to keep down.
- **Use extractor fans**: Ventilate whenever you cook or clean.
- **Sip ginger or peppermint tea**: Both can soothe nausea and mask other smells.

## When to Get Medical Help {#when-help}

If smell-triggered nausea is severe, contact your GP or midwife if:

- You can't keep any food or fluids down for 24 hours
- You're vomiting multiple times daily
- You're losing weight
- You feel dizzy, weak or dehydrated
- Your urine is dark and you're weeing less

These could be signs of hyperemesis gravidarum, which may need anti-sickness medication and sometimes hospital treatment for fluids.

## Frequently Asked Questions {#faq}

### Can smell-triggered nausea be treated?

Yes. If coping strategies aren't enough, your GP can prescribe safe anti-sickness medication such as cyclizine, promethazine or ondansetron.

### When does smell-triggered nausea ease?

For most women, it improves around weeks 12 to 14 as hormone levels stabilise. Some remain sensitive throughout pregnancy.

### Is it safe to use essential oils in pregnancy?

Some oils, like ginger, peppermint and lemon, are generally considered safe in small amounts. Avoid clary sage, rosemary and others not recommended in pregnancy. Check with your midwife first.
