# Is No Morning Sickness Normal?

About 20-30% of women have no morning sickness. Is this normal, or should you be worried?

## Table of Contents

1. [Why Some Women Don't Get Morning Sickness](#s1)
2. [Is It a Problem?](#s2)
3. [FAQ](#faq)

## Why Some Women Don't Get Morning Sickness {#s1}

- Lower hCG levels (still normal)
- Different hormone sensitivity
- Genetics
- Diet and lifestyle factors
- Some women are just lucky

## Is It a Problem? {#s2}

No. Having no morning sickness doesn't increase miscarriage risk. In fact, some studies suggest the opposite — but many healthy pregnancies have no nausea at all.

## FAQ {#faq}

### Is it normal to have no morning sickness?

Yes. About 20-30% of women have no morning sickness and go on to have healthy pregnancies. Not having nausea is not a sign of miscarriage.
