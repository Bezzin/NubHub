# Implantation Bleeding: What It Looks Like vs Your Period

Implantation bleeding can be one of the earliest signs of pregnancy. Here's how to recognise it and tell it apart from your period.

## What Is Implantation Bleeding? {#what-is-it}

Implantation bleeding occurs when the fertilised egg attaches to the lining of the uterus, typically 10-14 days after conception. It's one of the earliest signs of pregnancy, affecting approximately 25% of women.

## What Does It Look Like? {#what-looks-like}

Implantation bleeding is typically:
- **Light pink or brown** in colour (not bright red)
- **Very light** - just a few spots, not enough to fill a pad
- **Not accompanied by clots**
- **Painless** or with very mild cramping

## Implantation Bleeding vs Period {#vs-period}

| Feature | Implantation Bleeding | Period |
|---------|----------------------|--------|
| **Colour** | Pink or brown | Bright red (starts) |
| **Flow** | Very light spotting | Moderate to heavy |
| **Duration** | 1-3 days | 3-7 days |
| **Clots** | No | Yes, often |
| **Cramping** | Mild or none | Moderate to severe |
| **Timing** | 10-14 days after conception | 28 days after last period |

## How Long Does It Last? {#how-long}

Implantation bleeding typically lasts **1-3 days**. It's usually just light spotting. If bleeding is heavier or lasts longer, it may not be implantation bleeding.

## When to Worry {#when-to-worry)

Contact your GP or midwife if:
- Bleeding is heavy (soaking a pad)
- Bleeding is bright red
- You have severe cramping
- Bleeding lasts more than a few days
- You feel dizzy or faint

## Frequently Asked Questions {#faq}

### How do I know if it's implantation bleeding or my period?

Implantation bleeding is much lighter than a period, usually pink or brown (not red), lasts only 1-3 days, and doesn't have clots. If you're unsure, take a pregnancy test.

### Can implantation bleeding be heavy?

No. Implantation bleeding should be very light spotting. If you're experiencing heavy bleeding, it's more likely your period or a different type of bleeding that should be checked by a doctor.

Can I have implantation bleeding and still be pregnant?

Yes! Implantation bleeding is actually a sign of pregnancy. It occurs when the embryo attaches to your uterus. If you have light spotting around 10-14 days after conception, take a pregnancy test.
