# Red Bleeding in Early Pregnancy: When to Call 999

Bright red bleeding in early pregnancy is more concerning than brown or pink spotting. While it does not always mean the worst, it does warrant prompt attention. This guide helps you recognise the red-flag symptoms that need a 999 call and the ones that can wait for a midwife phone call.

## Red-Flag Symptoms That Need 999

Call 999 immediately if you experience heavy bleeding that soaks a sanitary pad in under an hour, severe or one-sided abdominal pain, shoulder tip pain, dizziness or fainting, a racing heartbeat, or fever with chills. These symptoms can indicate an ectopic pregnancy or a ruptured ovarian cyst, both of which are medical emergencies. Do not drive yourself - get someone to take you or call an ambulance.

## When to Call Your Midwife Instead

If the bleeding is lighter - more like a period but not soaking pads - and you have mild cramping, call your early pregnancy unit or midwife the same day. They will usually invite you in for a scan and blood tests. Many women with red bleeding go on to have healthy pregnancies, especially if a heartbeat is detected. The key is not to ignore it; prompt assessment brings reassurance and, rarely, life-saving treatment.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can red bleeding be normal in early pregnancy?**

Some women have a light bleed around the time their period was due and still have a healthy pregnancy. However, all red bleeding should be assessed by a midwife.

**What does shoulder tip pain mean?**

Shoulder tip pain, especially with dizziness, can mean internal bleeding from an ectopic pregnancy. Call 999 immediately.

**Should I go to A&E or call my midwife first?**

If you have heavy bleeding with pain or dizziness, go straight to A&E or call 999. If bleeding is lighter and you feel well, call your early pregnancy unit.
