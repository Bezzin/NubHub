# Melasma (Pregnancy Mask) in Pregnancy

Melasma causes brown or grey-brown patches on your face during pregnancy. It's also called chloasma or 'the pregnancy mask.'

## Table of Contents

1. [What Causes It](#s1)
2. [Prevention and Treatment](#s2)
3. [FAQ](#faq)

## What Causes It {#s1}

Pregnancy hormones increase melanin production, causing darker patches on the forehead, cheeks, nose, and upper lip. Sun exposure makes it worse.

## Prevention and Treatment {#s2}

Wear SPF 30+ daily. Avoid direct sun. Use a pregnancy-safe vitamin C serum. Most melasma fades after birth, though it may take months.

## FAQ {#faq}

### Will melasma go away after pregnancy?

For most women, yes. Melasma usually fades within a few months after birth. However, it may return in subsequent pregnancies or with sun exposure. Daily sunscreen is essential.
