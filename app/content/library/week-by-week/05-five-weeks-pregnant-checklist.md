# 5 Weeks Pregnant Checklist

At 5 weeks pregnant your hormones are shifting fast and early symptoms may start to appear. This is the perfect time to build healthy habits and prepare for your upcoming booking appointment. Use this 5 weeks pregnant checklist to stay organised.

## Table of Contents
- [Section 1](#section-1)
- [Section 2](#section-2)
- [FAQ](#faq)

## Section 1: Track Your Early Pregnancy Symptoms

- Start a daily symptom diary noting nausea, fatigue, breast tenderness, and mood changes.
- Record what eases your symptoms, such as ginger tea or small frequent meals, so you can spot patterns.
- Note any bleeding or sharp pain separately and contact your GP if it occurs.
- Download a pregnancy app or use a notebook, whichever is easier to keep up with.

## Section 2: Prepare for Your Booking Appointment

- Gather your medical history including any chronic conditions and previous pregnancies.
- List medications, supplements, and allergies you currently have.
- Note your family medical history, particularly genetic conditions or congenital disorders.
- Write down any questions you want to ask the midwife so you do not forget them on the day.
- Check whether your partner or a support person can attend the appointment with you.

Here are the most important points to remember from this guide:

- Track Your Early Pregnancy Symptoms: Start a daily symptom diary noting nausea, fatigue, breast tenderness, and mood changes.
- Prepare for Your Booking Appointment: Gather your medical history including any chronic conditions and previous pregnancies.

Take each step at your own pace and reach out to your midwife whenever you need reassurance. Every pregnancy is unique, so trust your instincts and keep a note of anything that feels unusual.

## FAQ

**Is morning sickness normal at 5 weeks?**

Yes. Nausea often begins around weeks 5 to 6 due to rising hCG levels. Eating small bland meals and staying hydrated can help. If you cannot keep any fluids down, speak to your GP.

**Can I exercise at 5 weeks pregnant?**

Yes, gentle exercise such as walking, swimming, and pregnancy yoga is encouraged. Avoid contact sports and activities with a high risk of falling.

**Should I avoid any skincare products at 5 weeks?**

Check labels for retinoids and salicylic acid, which are best avoided in pregnancy. When in doubt, ask your pharmacist or midwife for pregnancy-safe alternatives.
