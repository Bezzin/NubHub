# Top Tips for 10 Weeks Pregnant

Reaching 10 weeks of pregnancy is an exciting milestone. Whether you are experiencing early symptoms or feeling relatively normal, these practical tips will help you navigate this stage with confidence and care for your changing body.

## Looking After Your Body

At 10 weeks, your body is working hard to support your developing baby. Prioritise rest and aim for seven to nine hours of sleep each night. If you are experiencing fatigue, short daytime naps can help. Staying hydrated is essential, so drink plenty of water throughout the day, aiming for at least eight glasses.

Focus on a balanced diet rich in folate, iron, calcium, and protein. Leafy greens, fortified cereals, lean meats, and dairy products all support your growing baby. If you are struggling with nausea, try eating small, frequent meals and keeping plain snacks like crackers by your bedside. Ginger tea and acupressure wristbands may also ease morning sickness.

Gentle exercise such as walking, swimming, or prenatal yoga can boost your energy and mood. Aim for around 150 minutes of moderate activity per week, but listen to your body and slow down if you feel tired. Start taking a daily prenatal vitamin containing 400 micrograms of folic acid if you have not already, and book your antenatal appointments early.

## Looking After Your Mind

Pregnancy can bring a rollercoaster of emotions, especially in the early weeks. It is completely normal to feel anxious, excited, or overwhelmed, sometimes all at once. Acknowledging your feelings is an important first step in looking after your mental health.

Talk to your partner, a trusted friend, or a family member about how you are feeling. Sharing your worries can lighten the load and help you feel less isolated. If anxiety is affecting your daily life, speak to your GP or midwife, who can offer support and, if needed, refer you to a perinatal mental health team.

Consider journaling your pregnancy journey, practising mindfulness, or trying guided relaxation exercises. Limit your exposure to stressful news and avoid comparing your experience to others on social media. Every pregnancy is unique, and what matters most is that you feel informed, supported, and cared for during these precious weeks.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How can I manage morning sickness at 10 weeks?

Eating small, frequent meals, staying hydrated, and avoiding strong smells can help. Ginger, vitamin B6 supplements, and acupressure bands may also provide relief. If your sickness is severe, speak to your midwife or GP.

### Should I have told people I am pregnant by 10 weeks?

There is no right or wrong time to share your news. Some people wait until after their first scan at around 12 weeks, while others tell close family earlier. Choose what feels right for you and your partner.

### What appointments should I book at 10 weeks?

Contact your GP or midwife to book your first antenatal appointment, known as the booking appointment. This usually happens between 8 and 12 weeks and includes health checks and a discussion of your care plan.
