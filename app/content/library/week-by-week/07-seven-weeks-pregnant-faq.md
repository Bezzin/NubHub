# 7 Weeks Pregnant: Your FAQs Answered

At 7 weeks pregnant you are well into the first trimester, and it is completely normal to have a long list of questions. The short answer to the most common ones: yes, extreme tiredness is normal and often peaks around now; food aversions and nausea are expected; paracetamol is generally considered safe at the recommended dose; and symptoms that come and go from one day to the next are nothing to worry about on their own. Below we go through what most people want to know at week 7, what is normal, and when it is sensible to seek advice.

## What is happening at 7 weeks?

Your baby is growing rapidly, roughly the size of a blueberry, and your body is working hard to build the placenta that will nourish your pregnancy. Levels of hormones such as hCG and progesterone are climbing, which is why so many early pregnancy symptoms feel strongest around this point. You may notice tender breasts, a heightened sense of smell, more frequent trips to the loo, mild cramping, and changes in appetite. Most people do not look pregnant yet, even though they may feel very different.

Symptoms vary enormously from person to person and from day to day. Having strong symptoms is normal, and so is having mild or barely noticeable ones. The presence or absence of symptoms is not a reliable sign of how the pregnancy is progressing.

## Coping with tiredness and nausea

First-trimester fatigue can feel overwhelming. Rest whenever you can, go to bed earlier, and accept help with chores where possible. Gentle movement, fresh air, and staying hydrated can help your energy levels.

For nausea, many people find it easier to eat small amounts often rather than large meals, and to keep plain snacks such as crackers nearby. Sipping fluids regularly matters, especially if you are being sick. If you cannot keep food or fluids down at all, are losing weight, or feel very unwell, contact your GP or midwife, as this may be a treatable condition called hyperemesis gravidarum.

## When to seek advice

Most week 7 symptoms are normal, but contact your GP, midwife, or early pregnancy unit if you have heavy bleeding, severe or one-sided tummy pain, pain in the tip of your shoulder, feel faint or dizzy, or have a high temperature. Call 999 or go to A&E if you have very heavy bleeding, severe pain, or feel like you might collapse, as these can be signs of a problem that needs urgent assessment.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is extreme tiredness normal at 7 weeks?

Yes. At 7 weeks, fatigue is often at its worst because your body is making the placenta and your hormone levels are surging. Rest as much as you can, and mention it to your midwife if exhaustion is severe or paired with breathlessness, which can sometimes point to low iron.

### Can I take paracetamol at 7 weeks?

Paracetamol is generally considered the first-choice painkiller in pregnancy when taken at the recommended dose for the shortest time needed. Avoid ibuprofen and other anti-inflammatory painkillers in pregnancy unless a doctor advises otherwise. Check with a pharmacist or GP before taking any other medicine.

### Is it normal for symptoms to come and go at 7 weeks?

Yes. It is very common for early pregnancy symptoms to fluctuate from day to day, and a quieter day does not mean anything is wrong. If symptoms disappear suddenly alongside bleeding or pain, contact your early pregnancy unit for reassurance.

For advice tailored to your circumstances, speak to your midwife or GP.
