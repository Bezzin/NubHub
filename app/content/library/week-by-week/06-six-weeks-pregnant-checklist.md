# 6 Weeks Pregnant Checklist

At 6 weeks pregnant your baby's heart is beginning to beat and symptoms may intensify. This checklist helps you manage early pregnancy discomforts while preparing for the weeks ahead.

## Section 1: Manage Early Pregnancy Discomforts

- Keep plain snacks like crackers by your bed to eat before getting up each morning.
- Sip water throughout the day rather than large glasses at once to ease nausea.
- Wear a supportive, non-wired bra to reduce breast tenderness.
- Prioritise rest and aim for an extra hour of sleep at night if fatigue is heavy.
- Try acupressure wristbands or ginger supplements for nausea, after checking with your pharmacist.

## Section 2: Understand Early Scans and When They Happen

- The NHS dating scan typically takes place between 11 and 14 weeks, not at 6 weeks.
- An early scan may be offered if you have bleeding, pain, or a history of miscarriage or ectopic pregnancy.
- Private early reassurance scans are available from around 6 to 7 weeks if you want peace of mind.
- Write down the date of your booking appointment so you can plan scans around it.

## FAQ

**Can I hear my baby's heartbeat at 6 weeks?**

A heartbeat can sometimes be detected on an internal ultrasound from around 6 weeks. An abdominal scan may not yet pick it up, which is normal and not a cause for concern.

**Why am I so tired at 6 weeks pregnant?**

Fatigue is driven by rising progesterone and the energy demands of early fetal development. Rest when you can, eat iron-rich foods, and accept help from family and friends.

**Is spotting normal at 6 weeks?**

Light spotting can occur after implantation or cervical changes, but any bleeding should be reported to your GP or early pregnancy unit to rule out complications.
