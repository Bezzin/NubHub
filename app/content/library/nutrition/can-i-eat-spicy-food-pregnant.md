# Can I Eat Spicy Food When Pregnant?

Can you eat spicy food when pregnant? The short answer: **yes — spicy food is safe during pregnancy**. It does not harm your baby and is not on the NHS foods-to-avoid list. The main thing to be aware of is that spicy food can worsen heartburn and indigestion, which are already very common in pregnancy, especially in the second and third trimesters.

## Is Spicy Food Safe in Pregnancy? {#is-it-safe}

Spicy food — whether that is a curry, chilli, hot sauce, or heavily spiced dishes — poses no direct risk to your baby. The capsaicin and other compounds that make food taste hot do not cross the placenta or cause any known harm to fetal development.

Unlike some foods in pregnancy, spicy food has no concerns around listeria, toxoplasmosis, salmonella, or vitamin A toxicity. The safety question is really about your own comfort rather than any risk to your baby.

That said, pregnancy hormones — particularly progesterone — relax the valve between your stomach and oesophagus. This means stomach acid can travel upwards more easily, causing heartburn and acid reflux. Spicy food can trigger or worsen this. If you are already struggling with heartburn, you may want to moderate how much spicy food you eat, particularly in the evenings or when lying down.

## NHS Guidance on Spicy Food {#nhs-guidance}

The NHS does not list spicy food as something to avoid in pregnancy. The NHS foods-to-avoid list focuses on foods with a risk of harmful bacteria or parasites (such as certain soft cheeses, raw meat, and liver), or foods with high levels of certain nutrients (like vitamin A from liver). Spicy food does not fall into any of these categories.

The NHS does advise that heartburn is common in pregnancy and that eating smaller meals, avoiding fatty or spicy food close to bedtime, and not lying down immediately after eating can all help manage symptoms. If heartburn is severe or persistent, speak to your midwife or GP — they can advise on safe antacids to use during pregnancy.

## Practical Tips for Eating Spicy Food Comfortably {#how-to-eat-safely}

If you love spicy food and want to continue enjoying it during pregnancy, a few adjustments can help you stay comfortable:

- **Eat earlier in the day.** Heartburn tends to be worse at night. Enjoying spicy food at lunch rather than dinner can make a real difference.
- **Sit upright after eating.** Staying upright for at least an hour after a meal helps keep stomach acid where it belongs.
- **Eat smaller portions.** A large spicy meal is more likely to cause discomfort than a smaller amount of the same dish.
- **Stay hydrated.** Drinking water alongside a spicy meal can help ease the burn.
- **Notice your own triggers.** Every pregnancy is different. Some women find their tolerance for spice increases; others find they become more sensitive. Listen to your body.
- **Pair spicy food with cooling accompaniments.** Yoghurt-based raita, plain rice, or bread can buffer the spice and reduce irritation.

If you experience vomiting alongside spicy food, it is worth keeping in mind that morning sickness — particularly during the first trimester — can already make certain foods feel unbearable. There is no nutritional requirement to eat spicy food in pregnancy, so if it makes you feel unwell, it is absolutely fine to avoid it for now.

## What About Old Wives' Tales? {#old-wives-tales}

You may have heard that eating spicy food can bring on labour. There is no reliable clinical evidence to support this. While some people anecdotally report that a spicy meal seemed to trigger contractions, there is no established mechanism by which capsaicin induces labour. Spicy food is safe to eat throughout your entire pregnancy, including the final weeks.

Similarly, some people believe spicy food during pregnancy affects the baby's temperament or causes colic after birth. There is no credible evidence for either claim. What your baby experiences through amniotic fluid is flavour compounds, not heat — and this may actually help them develop food preferences later on.

## Frequently Asked Questions {#faq}

### Can spicy food cause a miscarriage?

No. There is no evidence that eating spicy food causes miscarriage at any stage of pregnancy. Spicy food is not on the NHS list of foods to avoid in pregnancy, and it has no known effect on pregnancy viability or fetal development.

### Will spicy food give my baby an upset stomach?

Your baby does not digest food the way you do. In the womb, your baby swallows amniotic fluid, which carries flavour molecules from the foods you eat — but not heat or irritation. Eating spicy food does not cause discomfort for your baby, though it may well cause heartburn or digestive discomfort for you.

### I'm getting terrible heartburn from spicy food — what can I do?

Heartburn in pregnancy is very common and can be aggravated by spicy food, fatty food, large meals, and lying down after eating. Try smaller meals earlier in the day, avoid trigger foods in the evening, and prop your head up slightly at night. If heartburn is persistent or affecting your sleep and wellbeing, speak to your midwife or GP — they can recommend antacids that are safe to use in pregnancy.
