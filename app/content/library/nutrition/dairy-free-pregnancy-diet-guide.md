# Dairy-Free Pregnancy Diet: A Complete Guide

Avoiding dairy during pregnancy, whether due to allergy, intolerance, or personal choice, does not mean missing out on key nutrients. This guide shows you how to meet your calcium, protein, and vitamin needs with dairy-free alternatives and balanced meals.

## Key Nutrients to Monitor Without Dairy

Calcium is the main nutrient to watch on a dairy-free pregnancy diet, as it supports your baby bones and teeth. Fortified plant milks, such as soya or oat, often contain similar calcium levels to cow milk. Other good sources include tofu set with calcium, leafy green vegetables, almonds, and sesame seeds. Protein can be sourced from eggs, fish, legumes, nuts, and seeds. Vitamin D helps your body absorb calcium, so a daily supplement is recommended. Iodine, often supplied by dairy, can be obtained from seaweed, fish, or a supplement if needed.

## Dairy-Free Pregnancy Meal Ideas

For breakfast, try overnight oats with fortified soya milk and berries, or avocado on wholemeal toast. Lunch could be a lentil and vegetable soup with wholegrain bread, or a chicken and quinoa salad. Dinner ideas include baked salmon with roasted vegetables and rice, or a chickpea and spinach curry. Snacks such as nut butter on rice cakes, fortified yoghurt alternatives with fruit, and a handful of walnuts provide steady energy. Always check that plant milks and yoghurts are unsweetened and fortified with calcium for the best nutritional benefit.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How can I get enough calcium on a dairy-free pregnancy diet?

Use calcium-fortified plant milks, tofu, leafy greens, almonds, and sesame seeds, and take a vitamin D supplement to aid absorption.

### Is soya safe during a dairy-free pregnancy?

Moderate soya consumption is generally considered safe in pregnancy and provides valuable protein and calcium.

### Do I need an iodine supplement if I do not eat dairy?

Possibly, since dairy is a major iodine source; discuss your intake with your midwife or a dietitian.
