## Is Chicken Safe in Pregnancy? {#is-it-safe}

Chicken is a poultry that is safe to eat during pregnancy when properly prepared and stored.

The main concerns with food in pregnancy are listeria (a bacteria that can cause serious illness), toxoplasmosis (a parasite), salmonella (a bacteria), and vitamin A toxicity.

## NHS Guidance on Chicken {#nhs-guidance}

The NHS advises that yes - cook thoroughly (no pink, steaming hot). Always follow NHS food safety guidance during pregnancy.

## How to Eat Chicken Safely {#how-to-eat-safely}

Chicken is safe to eat during pregnancy. Follow general food hygiene: check use-by dates, store at correct temperature, and prepare cleanly.

## Frequently Asked Questions {#faq}

### What if I accidentally ate chicken while pregnant?

Don't panic. The risk of infection is low. Watch for symptoms like fever, nausea, vomiting, or diarrhoea. If you feel unwell, contact your GP or call 111.

### Is chicken safe in the first trimester?

The same rules apply throughout pregnancy. Yes - cook thoroughly (no pink, steaming hot)
