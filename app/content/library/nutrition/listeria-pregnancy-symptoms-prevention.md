# Listeria Pregnancy Symptoms Prevention

Listeria in pregnancy — symptoms, risks, and how to prevent listeria infection.

## Overview

Listeria is a foodborne bacteria that can cause serious illness in pregnancy. Here's how to prevent it.

## FAQ

### Listeria (listeriosis) is a bacterial infection from contaminated food. In pregnancy, it can cause miscarriage, stillbirth, or serious illness in newborns. Symptoms include: fever, muscle aches, nausea, diarrhoea. Prevention: avoid soft mould-ripened cheeses, unpasteurised dairy, deli meats (unless heated), and pre-prepared salads past their use-by date.

How common is listeria in pregnancy?

### Listeria is rare in the UK — about 1 in 25,000 pregnancies. However, pregnant women are 13 times more likely to get it than the general population. Prevention through food safety is key.
