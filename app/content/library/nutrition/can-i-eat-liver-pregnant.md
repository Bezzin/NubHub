## Is Liver Safe in Pregnancy? {#is-it-safe}

Liver is a organ meat that should be avoided during pregnancy due to the risk of food poisoning or other complications.

The main concerns with food in pregnancy are listeria (a bacteria that can cause serious illness), toxoplasmosis (a parasite), salmonella (a bacteria), and vitamin A toxicity.

## NHS Guidance on Liver {#nhs-guidance}

The NHS advises that no - avoid liver (high vitamin a can harm baby). Always follow NHS food safety guidance during pregnancy.

## How to Eat Liver Safely {#how-to-eat-safely}

Liver should be avoided during pregnancy. If you want a similar taste, try safer alternatives like hard cheeses, pasteurised products, or thoroughly cooked options.

## Frequently Asked Questions {#faq}

### What if I accidentally ate liver while pregnant?

Don't panic. The risk of infection is low. Watch for symptoms like fever, nausea, vomiting, or diarrhoea. If you feel unwell, contact your GP or call 111.

### Is liver safe in the first trimester?

The same rules apply throughout pregnancy. No - avoid liver (high vitamin A can harm baby)
