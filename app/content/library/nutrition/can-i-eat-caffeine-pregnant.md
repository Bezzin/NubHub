## Is Caffeine Safe in Pregnancy? {#is-it-safe}

Caffeine is a drinks that is safe to eat during pregnancy when properly prepared and stored.

The main concerns with food in pregnancy are listeria (a bacteria that can cause serious illness), toxoplasmosis (a parasite), salmonella (a bacteria), and vitamin A toxicity.

## NHS Guidance on Caffeine {#nhs-guidance}

The NHS advises that yes - limit to 200mg per day. Always follow NHS food safety guidance during pregnancy.

## How to Eat Caffeine Safely {#how-to-eat-safely}

Caffeine is safe to eat during pregnancy. Follow general food hygiene: check use-by dates, store at correct temperature, and prepare cleanly.

## Frequently Asked Questions {#faq}

### What if I accidentally ate caffeine while pregnant?

Don't panic. The risk of infection is low. Watch for symptoms like fever, nausea, vomiting, or diarrhoea. If you feel unwell, contact your GP or call 111.

### Is caffeine safe in the first trimester?

The same rules apply throughout pregnancy. Yes - limit to 200mg per day
