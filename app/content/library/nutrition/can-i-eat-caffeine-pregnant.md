# Can I Eat Caffeine When Pregnant?

Can you consume caffeine when pregnant? The short answer is: **yes, but keep it under 200mg per day**. Caffeine is found in coffee, tea, energy drinks, cola, and even chocolate. You don't need to cut it out entirely, but staying within the NHS limit is important throughout pregnancy.

## Is Caffeine Safe in Pregnancy? {#is-it-safe}

Caffeine crosses the placenta, and because your baby cannot metabolise it the same way you can, high intakes over a prolonged period may affect development. The main reason for the limit is not an acute single-dose risk but rather consistent high intake across pregnancy.

Most everyday sources of caffeine are perfectly fine if you track your intake. A single cup of filter coffee contains roughly 140mg, a mug of instant coffee around 100mg, a mug of tea around 75mg, and a can of cola around 40mg. Dark chocolate also contributes a small amount. The key is totalling everything across the day, not focusing on any single source.

## NHS Guidance on Caffeine in Pregnancy {#nhs-guidance}

The NHS advises a maximum of **200mg of caffeine per day** during pregnancy. This recommendation comes from reviews of available evidence and is broadly consistent with guidance from other UK health bodies.

You do not need to cut caffeine out completely. If you normally drink several coffees a day, switching to one or two cups, or swapping some to decaffeinated, will usually bring you comfortably under the limit. Herbal teas are generally low in caffeine, though some herbs carry their own cautions — peppermint and ginger are widely considered fine, but limit liquorice root tea.

If you are finding it hard to cut back on caffeinated drinks due to fatigue, your midwife can offer practical suggestions during your antenatal appointments.

## How to Stay Within the Safe Limit {#how-to-eat-safely}

- **Swap one coffee for decaf.** Decaffeinated coffee and tea taste very similar and contain only trace caffeine.
- **Count cola and energy drinks.** Cola contains around 40mg per can; energy drinks can contain much more and are best avoided in pregnancy due to their high caffeine and other additives.
- **Check chocolate portions.** A small square of dark chocolate contains only a few milligrams, so it rarely pushes you over the limit on its own.
- **Read labels on soft drinks.** Many fizzy drinks contain caffeine. Check the label if you are unsure.
- **Spread intake through the day.** Having one moderate source of caffeine at a time is fine. Avoid very large single doses.

If you accidentally have a day slightly over 200mg, there is no need to panic. The limit is about consistent daily intake across the whole pregnancy rather than a single accidental excess.

## Frequently Asked Questions {#faq}

### What if I accidentally had too much caffeine one day during pregnancy?

A one-off day over the 200mg limit is very unlikely to cause harm. The guidance is about regular daily intake over time. If you are worried, mention it to your midwife at your next appointment for reassurance.

### Does decaffeinated coffee contain any caffeine?

Decaffeinated drinks are not completely caffeine-free — they usually contain a very small amount (around 2–5mg per cup), which is negligible. You can count them as essentially caffeine-free for the purposes of tracking your daily limit.

### Is caffeine safe in the first trimester?

Yes, within the 200mg daily limit. Some women choose to reduce caffeine in the first trimester because strong coffee smells can worsen nausea — but there is no requirement to cut it out completely at any stage of pregnancy.
