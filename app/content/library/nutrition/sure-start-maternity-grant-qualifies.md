# Sure Start Maternity Grant: Who Qualifies?

The Sure Start Maternity Grant is a £500 one-off payment to help with the costs of a new baby.

Having a baby is expensive, and if you are on a low income or receiving certain benefits, the Sure Start Maternity Grant can provide a welcome boost. It is a one-off, tax-free payment of £500 that you do not have to pay back. It is intended to help with the costs of buying things for your new baby — from clothing and bedding to a pram or feeding equipment.

## Who Qualifies

You can apply for the Sure Start Maternity Grant if you meet both of the following conditions:

**1. You are receiving at least one of these qualifying benefits:**
- Universal Credit
- Income Support
- Income-related Employment and Support Allowance (ESA)
- Income-based Jobseeker's Allowance (JSA)
- Child Tax Credit (at a rate higher than the family element)
- Working Tax Credit that includes a disability or severe disability element
- Pension Credit

**2. You do not already have children under 16 in your household** — unless you are expecting a multiple birth (twins, triplets, or more). If you already have a child or children, you would only qualify if this pregnancy results in a multiple birth.

If you are expecting twins or triplets and you already have children, you can still claim because a multiple birth automatically qualifies regardless of existing children.

## How to Claim

You claim using form **SF100 (Sure Start Maternity Grant)**. You can get this form:
- From your local Jobcentre Plus
- By calling the Social Fund on the number listed on the GOV.UK website
- From some antenatal services

**When to claim:** You can make your claim from **11 weeks before your due date** (that is, from around 29 weeks pregnant) up until your baby is **6 months old**. Do not leave it too late — the deadline of six months after birth is firm.

**What you need:** When you submit the form, you will need a declaration signed by your midwife, health visitor, or GP confirming your pregnancy or the birth of your baby.

The grant is paid into your bank account and does not need to be repaid. It does not affect most other benefits you may be receiving.

## What Can You Spend It On?

The grant has no restrictions on how you spend it. Most families put it towards essentials such as:
- A pram, pushchair, or travel system
- A cot, Moses basket, or bedside crib
- Baby clothing and bedding
- Nappies and feeding equipment (bottles, a breast pump)
- Car seat

You are free to use it however best suits your family's needs.

## Other Financial Support in Pregnancy

The Sure Start Maternity Grant is just one of several forms of financial support available to pregnant women in the UK. Depending on your circumstances, you may also be entitled to:

- **Healthy Start vouchers** — for fruit, vegetables, milk, and vitamins if you are pregnant and on certain benefits.
- **Free NHS prescriptions and dental treatment** — available to all pregnant women and for one year after birth, via a Maternity Exemption Certificate (MATEX).
- **Statutory Maternity Pay (SMP) or Maternity Allowance** — depending on whether you are employed or self-employed.
- **Universal Credit childcare element** — once your child is born, to help with childcare costs.

It is worth checking your eligibility for all of these through GOV.UK or speaking to your midwife, who can point you to local support.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much is the Sure Start Maternity Grant?

The grant is £500 per pregnancy (not per baby, even for a multiple birth). It is paid as a one-off lump sum into your bank account and does not need to be repaid. It is also tax-free and does not count as income for benefit calculations.

### Can I get the Sure Start Maternity Grant for my second baby?

Only if this pregnancy results in a multiple birth (twins, triplets, or more). If you are having a single baby and you already have children under 16 living with you, you will not qualify. The grant is designed for families having their first child or expanding their family through a multiple birth.

### When should I apply for the Sure Start Maternity Grant?

You can apply from 11 weeks before your due date (around 29 weeks pregnant) until your baby is 6 months old. Applying during pregnancy means the money arrives before your baby does, giving you time to buy essentials. Contact your local Jobcentre Plus or check GOV.UK for the current SF100 form.

Always speak to your midwife or GP for personalised advice about financial support during pregnancy, and check GOV.UK for the most up-to-date eligibility rules.
