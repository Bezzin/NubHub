# Iron Rich Foods Pregnancy Complete

Complete list of iron-rich foods for pregnancy. How to boost your iron intake naturally.

## Overview

Iron is essential in pregnancy. Here's how to get enough from your diet.

## FAQ

### Iron-rich foods: red meat (lean beef, lamb), poultry (chicken, turkey), fish (salmon, tuna), legumes (lentils, chickpeas, beans), leafy greens (spinach, kale), dried fruit (apricots, raisins), fortified cereals, and seeds (pumpkin, sesame). Pair iron-rich foods with vitamin C (orange juice, peppers) to boost absorption.

How much iron do I need in pregnancy?

### The NHS recommends 27mg of iron per day in pregnancy. Your midwife will check your iron levels at the booking appointment and at 28 weeks. If you're low, they'll recommend supplements.
