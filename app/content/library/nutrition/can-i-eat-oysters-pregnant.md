## Is Oysters Safe in Pregnancy? {#is-it-safe}

Oysters is a shellfish that should be avoided during pregnancy due to the risk of food poisoning or other complications.

The main concerns with food in pregnancy are listeria (a bacteria that can cause serious illness), toxoplasmosis (a parasite), salmonella (a bacteria), and vitamin A toxicity.

## NHS Guidance on Oysters {#nhs-guidance}

The NHS advises that no - avoid raw oysters in pregnancy. Always follow NHS food safety guidance during pregnancy.

## How to Eat Oysters Safely {#how-to-eat-safely}

Oysters should be avoided during pregnancy. If you want a similar taste, try safer alternatives like hard cheeses, pasteurised products, or thoroughly cooked options.

## Frequently Asked Questions {#faq}

### What if I accidentally ate oysters while pregnant?

Don't panic. The risk of infection is low. Watch for symptoms like fever, nausea, vomiting, or diarrhoea. If you feel unwell, contact your GP or call 111.

### Is oysters safe in the first trimester?

The same rules apply throughout pregnancy. No - avoid raw oysters in pregnancy
