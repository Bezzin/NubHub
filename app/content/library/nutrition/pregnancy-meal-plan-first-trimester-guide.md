# Pregnancy Meal Plan First Trimester: A Simple Weekly Guide

The first trimester brings excitement, exhaustion, and often morning sickness. Eating well can feel challenging, but a simple meal plan helps you manage nausea while giving your baby the nutrients needed for early development.

## First Trimester Nutritional Priorities

During the first 12 weeks, folate is critical for reducing the risk of neural tube defects. Iron supports your expanding blood volume, while vitamin C helps your body absorb that iron. Bland, easy-to-digest foods are often best when nausea strikes. Focus on small, frequent meals that include wholegrains, lean protein, dairy or fortified alternatives, and plenty of fruit and vegetables. Staying hydrated is equally important, especially if you are vomiting. If smells trigger nausea, try cold foods, which tend to be less aromatic than hot dishes.

## Sample First Trimester Meal Plan

Breakfast options include porridge with berries, wholemeal toast with scrambled eggs, or a banana and spinach smoothie. For lunch, try a chicken and avocado wrap, lentil soup with wholegrain bread, or a quinoa salad with roasted vegetables. Dinner could be grilled salmon with sweet potato, a vegetable stir-fry with tofu, or pasta with tomato sauce and a side salad. Snacks such as yoghurt, nuts, crackers, and fruit keep energy levels steady. Adjust portion sizes to your appetite and keep ginger tea on hand for nausea relief.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What should I eat in the first trimester if I have nausea?

Opt for bland, easy-to-digest foods like porridge, toast, crackers, and plain rice, eaten in small amounts throughout the day.

### Do I need extra calories in the first trimester?

Most women do not need additional calories in the first trimester; focus on nutrient quality rather than quantity.

### Is it safe to drink coffee in the first trimester?

Up to 200mg of caffeine per day is generally considered safe, roughly equivalent to one to two cups of instant coffee.
