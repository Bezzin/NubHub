# Vitamin C in Pregnancy

Vitamin C is important for your immune system and helps your body absorb iron from food.

## Table of Contents

1. [Benefits](#s1)
2. [Food Sources](#s2)
3. [FAQ](#faq)

## Benefits {#s1}

Vitamin C supports: immune function, iron absorption, collagen formation, and tissue repair. It's safe in pregnancy at normal doses.

## Food Sources {#s2}

Rich sources: citrus fruits, strawberries, kiwi, bell peppers, broccoli, and tomatoes. Most prenatal vitamins include vitamin C.

## FAQ {#faq}

### How much vitamin C do I need in pregnancy?

The NHS recommends around 50mg/day, which is easily obtained from a balanced diet. Most prenatal vitamins contain 80-100mg.
