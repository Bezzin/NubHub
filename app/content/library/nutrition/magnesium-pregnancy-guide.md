# Magnesium in Pregnancy: Benefits and Safety

Magnesium is important in pregnancy for muscle function, sleep, and preventing leg cramps.

## Table of Contents

1. [Benefits](#s1)
2. [Safety](#s2)
3. [FAQ](#faq)

## Benefits {#s1}

Magnesium may help with: leg cramps, sleep quality, anxiety reduction, and blood pressure regulation. The NHS recommends 300mg/day in pregnancy.

## Safety {#s2}

Magnesium from food is always safe. Supplements are generally safe but talk to your GP before taking high doses. Avoid magnesium oxide which can cause diarrhoea.

## FAQ {#faq}

### Can magnesium help pregnancy leg cramps?

Yes. Studies suggest magnesium supplements can reduce the frequency and severity of leg cramps in pregnancy. 300mg per day is the typical recommendation.
