# Food Poisoning Pregnancy Complete Guide

Food poisoning during pregnancy — types, symptoms, when to get help, and prevention.

## Overview

Food poisoning in pregnancy can be more serious than usual. Here's your complete guide.

## FAQ

### Common types: salmonella (raw eggs/meat), listeria (soft cheese/deli meats), campylobacter (undercooked poultry), and E. coli (undercooked meat). Symptoms: diarrhoea, vomiting, fever, cramps. Treatment: stay hydrated, contact GP if severe. Prevention: follow NHS food safety guidelines.

What should I do if I get food poisoning while pregnant?

### Stay hydrated with water and oral rehydration salts. Contact your GP if: symptoms are severe, you have a fever, you can't keep fluids down, or symptoms last more than 48 hours. Don't take anti-diarrhoea medication without consulting your GP.
