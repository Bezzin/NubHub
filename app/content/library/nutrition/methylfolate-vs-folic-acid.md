# Methylfolate vs Folic Acid: Which Is Better?

Folic acid is the standard pregnancy supplement, but methylfolate is the active form. Here's how they compare.

## Table of Contents

1. [Folic Acid](#s1)
2. [Methylfolate](#s2)
3. [FAQ](#faq)

## Folic Acid {#s1}

Folic acid is the synthetic form of folate used in most pregnancy vitamins. It's well-studied and NHS-recommended at 400mcg daily. The body converts it to active folate.

## Methylfolate {#s2}

Methylfolate (5-MTHF) is the active form that doesn't need conversion. Some women have MTHFR gene variants that reduce folic acid conversion. Methylfolate may be better for these women.

## FAQ {#faq}

### Should I take methylfolate instead of folic acid?

For most women, standard folic acid is fine and NHS-recommended. If you have MTHFR variants or prefer the active form, methylfolate is a reasonable alternative. Discuss with your GP.
