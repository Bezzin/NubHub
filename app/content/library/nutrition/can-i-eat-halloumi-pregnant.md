## Is Halloumi Safe in Pregnancy? {#is-it-safe}

Halloumi is a semi-hard cheese that is safe to eat during pregnancy when properly prepared and stored.

The main concerns with food in pregnancy are listeria (a bacteria that can cause serious illness), toxoplasmosis (a parasite), salmonella (a bacteria), and vitamin A toxicity.

## NHS Guidance on Halloumi {#nhs-guidance}

The NHS advises that yes - safe if made from pasteurised milk. Always follow NHS food safety guidance during pregnancy.

## How to Eat Halloumi Safely {#how-to-eat-safely}

Halloumi is safe to eat during pregnancy. Follow general food hygiene: check use-by dates, store at correct temperature, and prepare cleanly.

## Frequently Asked Questions {#faq}

### What if I accidentally ate halloumi while pregnant?

Don't panic. The risk of infection is low. Watch for symptoms like fever, nausea, vomiting, or diarrhoea. If you feel unwell, contact your GP or call 111.

### Is halloumi safe in the first trimester?

The same rules apply throughout pregnancy. Yes - safe if made from pasteurised milk
