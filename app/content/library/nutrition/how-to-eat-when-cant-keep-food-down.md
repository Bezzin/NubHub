# How to Eat When You Cannot Keep Food Down in Pregnancy

Nausea and vomiting in pregnancy can make eating feel impossible. Whether you are dealing with typical morning sickness or hyperemesis gravidarum, this guide offers practical strategies to help you stay nourished and hydrated when keeping food down is a struggle.

## Strategies for Eating With Severe Nausea

When you cannot keep food down, the goal is to eat whatever you can tolerate, even if it is not perfectly balanced. Try small, frequent bites of bland foods such as crackers, dry toast, plain rice, or bananas. Cold foods often smell less and are easier to manage than hot meals. Keep snacks by your bed and eat a little before getting up. Ginger, whether in tea, biscuits, or sweets, may help reduce nausea. Avoid spicy, greasy, or strongly scented foods, and steer clear of triggers that make symptoms worse. If plain water is hard to keep down, try sucking ice cubes or taking small sips of flat lemonade.

## When to Seek Medical Help

If you are vomiting repeatedly, unable to keep any food or fluids down for 24 hours, losing weight, or feeling dizzy and weak, contact your midwife or doctor promptly. You may have hyperemesis gravidarum, a severe form of pregnancy sickness that can require medication, intravenous fluids, or hospital treatment. Do not struggle alone, as effective treatments are available. Your healthcare team can prescribe anti-sickness medication that is safe in pregnancy and help you develop a plan to maintain adequate nutrition. Early support makes a significant difference to your comfort and your baby wellbeing.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What can I eat if I cannot keep any food down in pregnancy?

Try bland, easy-to-digest foods like crackers, toast, plain rice, and bananas in very small amounts throughout the day.

### When should I see a doctor for pregnancy vomiting?

Seek help if you cannot keep fluids down for 24 hours, feel dizzy, lose weight, or vomit repeatedly throughout the day.

### Does ginger help with severe pregnancy nausea?

Ginger may help reduce mild to moderate nausea for some women, though it is less effective for hyperemesis gravidarum.
