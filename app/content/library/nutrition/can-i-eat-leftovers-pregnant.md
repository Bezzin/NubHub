# Can I Eat Leftovers Pregnant?

Yes, you can eat leftovers when you're pregnant — but food hygiene matters more than ever during pregnancy because your immune system is naturally less effective, making you more vulnerable to food poisoning. The good news is that following a few straightforward rules means leftovers can be a convenient and nutritious part of your diet throughout all three trimesters.

## Why Food Safety Matters More in Pregnancy

During pregnancy, your immune system is deliberately suppressed so that your body doesn't reject the baby. This means bacteria that a non-pregnant person might shake off can make you much more unwell. Food poisoning from bacteria such as listeria, salmonella, or campylobacter can cause serious illness in pregnancy, and in rare cases can lead to complications including preterm labour or pregnancy loss.

This doesn't mean you need to avoid leftovers — it means you need to handle and reheat them properly. The risks come from food that has been left out too long, stored incorrectly, or not reheated to a high enough temperature.

## How to Store Leftovers Safely During Pregnancy

**Cool food quickly.** Don't leave cooked food sitting out at room temperature for more than two hours before refrigerating it. Hot food should ideally be cooled within an hour and then placed in the fridge or freezer.

**Use airtight containers.** Store leftovers in clean, sealed containers to prevent cross-contamination with other foods in the fridge.

**Keep the fridge at the right temperature.** Your fridge should be set below 5°C. Many people's fridges run warmer than they realise — it's worth checking with a fridge thermometer.

**Use leftovers within two days.** Most cooked leftovers are safe to eat within two days of cooking. If you are not going to eat them within that time, freeze them promptly.

**Label leftovers.** If you freeze cooked food, label it with the date so you know how long it has been stored. Most cooked foods can be frozen safely for up to three months.

## How to Reheat Leftovers Safely

**Reheat until piping hot throughout.** This is the most important rule. Leftovers must reach a core temperature of at least 70°C for two minutes to kill harmful bacteria. In practice, this means the food should be steaming hot all the way through — not just warm on the outside.

**Stir when reheating.** Microwaves can heat food unevenly, leaving cold spots where bacteria can survive. Stir the food partway through heating and check that there are no cold patches before eating.

**Reheat once only.** Reheat leftovers only once. Each cooling and reheating cycle gives bacteria more opportunity to multiply. If you have a large batch of leftovers, take out only what you plan to eat and reheat that portion.

**Rice deserves extra caution.** Cooked rice can harbour a bacterium called Bacillus cereus, which can survive cooking and multiply rapidly if rice is left at room temperature. Cool and refrigerate leftover rice within an hour of cooking, and always reheat it thoroughly. Avoid rice that has been left out overnight.

## Leftovers to Be Cautious About

Most cooked leftovers are fine when stored and reheated correctly. However, a few foods are worth extra care during pregnancy:

- **Rice and grains** — cool quickly, store promptly, and reheat thoroughly (see above)
- **Shellfish and fish** — best eaten freshly cooked; if reheating, ensure the flesh is piping hot throughout
- **Eggs** — reheated egg dishes should be fully set and hot through; avoid reheating dishes containing soft or undercooked egg
- **Meat dishes** — always reheat until the juices run clear and the meat is steaming throughout
- **Prepared salads and cold dishes** — these should not be reheated; if they contain ingredients like cooked chicken or fish, eat within a day of making and keep refrigerated

## Eating Out and Takeaway Leftovers

The same rules apply to restaurant leftovers and takeaway food brought home. Refrigerate promptly, use within a day or two, and reheat thoroughly. Avoid leaving takeaway food on the counter to cool for hours before storing it.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is it safe to eat cold leftovers from the fridge when pregnant?

Cold leftovers that have been properly stored are generally safe for most foods, as long as they were refrigerated within two hours of cooking and you are eating them within two days. However, reheating is advisable for meat, fish, poultry, and rice — eating these foods cold increases the risk of bacterial illness, even when they have been correctly stored.

### How long can I keep leftovers in the fridge when pregnant?

Most cooked leftovers should be eaten within two days of cooking. After that, the risk of bacterial growth increases. If you cannot eat them in time, freeze them on the day of cooking rather than letting them sit in the fridge until they are borderline.

### Can I eat leftover rice when pregnant?

Yes, provided it was cooled and refrigerated within an hour of cooking, stored in the fridge, and is reheated until piping hot throughout. Never eat rice that was left out at room temperature overnight — this is a known cause of food poisoning even outside of pregnancy.

Speak to your midwife or GP if you have any concerns about food safety or your diet during pregnancy.
