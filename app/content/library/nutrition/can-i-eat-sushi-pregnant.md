# Can I Eat Sushi When Pregnant?

Can you eat sushi when pregnant? The short answer: **Caution - vegetarian or fully cooked sushi is OK; avoid raw fish**.

## Is Sushi Safe in Pregnancy? {#is-it-safe}

Sushi is a fish and rice that requires caution during pregnancy - it can be eaten in some forms but not others.

The main concerns with food in pregnancy are listeria (a bacteria that can cause serious illness), toxoplasmosis (a parasite), salmonella (a bacteria), and vitamin A toxicity.

## NHS Guidance on Sushi {#nhs-guidance}

The NHS advises that caution - vegetarian or fully cooked sushi is ok; avoid raw fish. Always follow NHS food safety guidance during pregnancy.

## How to Eat Sushi Safely {#how-to-eat-safely}

Check the specific form of sushi - some varieties are safe, others should be avoided. When in doubt, cook it thoroughly until steaming hot.

## Frequently Asked Questions {#faq}

### What if I accidentally ate sushi while pregnant?

Don't panic. The risk of infection is low. Watch for symptoms like fever, nausea, vomiting, or diarrhoea. If you feel unwell, contact your GP or call 111.

### Is sushi safe in the first trimester?

The same rules apply throughout pregnancy. Caution - vegetarian or fully cooked sushi is OK; avoid raw fish
