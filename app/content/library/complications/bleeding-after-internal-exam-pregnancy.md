# Bleeding After an Internal Exam in Pregnancy

Internal examinations, whether a speculum exam, a cervical check, or a manual pelvic exam, are sometimes needed during early pregnancy. Because the cervix is softer and more blood-rich in pregnancy, light spotting afterwards is very common and rarely a cause for concern.

## Why the Cervix Bleeds Easily in Pregnancy

Pregnancy hormones increase blood flow to the cervix and vaginal walls, making the tissue fragile. Even gentle contact from a speculum, a swab, or a gloved finger can release a few drops of blood. This is sometimes called a friable cervix and is a normal physiological change, not a sign of damage. The spotting is usually light, pink or brown, and stops within a day.

## Caring for Yourself After an Exam

Use a panty liner rather than a tampon to monitor the bleeding, and avoid sex, swimming, or vigorous activity for 24 to 48 hours to let the cervix settle. If the bleeding becomes heavy, smells offensive, or you develop a fever, contact your maternity unit as these can indicate infection. If you have rhesus-negative blood, staff may give you an anti-D injection depending on the situation and gestation.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### 1. Is it normal to bleed after a speculum exam?

Yes, light spotting is common because the pregnant cervix is delicate and well-supplied with blood.

### 2. Can an internal exam cause miscarriage?

No. A gentle exam does not affect the pregnancy inside the uterus.

### 3. How long should I wait before having sex again?

Wait until the spotting has stopped and ideally 48 hours, or as advised by your clinician.
