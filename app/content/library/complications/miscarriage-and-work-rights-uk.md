# Miscarriage and Work: Your Rights in the UK

After a miscarriage, you have employment rights in the UK. Here's what you're entitled to.

## Your Rights

If your miscarriage happens before 24 weeks, you're entitled to: sick leave (as normal), statutory sick pay if eligible, and reasonable time off. If your miscarriage happens after 24 weeks, it's classified as stillbirth and you're entitled to full maternity leave and pay.

## What to Do

Tell your employer what's happened. You don't need to go into detail. Ask for sick leave. Your employer should be supportive. If they're not, contact ACAS.

## FAQ

### Can I get time off work after a miscarriage?

Yes. Before 24 weeks, you can take sick leave as normal. After 24 weeks, you're entitled to maternity leave. Talk to your employer and GP.

### Am I entitled to pay after a miscarriage?

Before 24 weeks, you may get Statutory Sick Pay (£109.40/week for up to 28 weeks). After 24 weeks, you're entitled to Statutory Maternity Pay.
