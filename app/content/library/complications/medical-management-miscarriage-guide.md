# Medical Management of Miscarriage: What It Involves

Medical management uses medication (mifepristone and misoprostol) to help the body pass the pregnancy tissue. It's one of three options for managing miscarriage.

## What to Expect

You'll take mifepristone first, then misoprostol 24-48 hours later. The misoprostol causes cramping and bleeding as the tissue passes. This is similar to a heavy period. Pain relief is provided. The process usually takes 1-2 days. Bleeding continues for 1-3 weeks.

## FAQ

### Is medical management painful?

You'll experience cramping similar to a period, which can be strong. You'll be offered pain relief including paracetamol, ibuprofen (if not pregnant), and stronger painkillers if needed. Many women manage at home.

### How long does bleeding last after medical management?

Bleeding typically lasts 1-3 weeks. It should get lighter over time. If bleeding is very heavy (soaking a pad in an hour), contact your healthcare provider.
