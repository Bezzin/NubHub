# Chemical Pregnancy: What Is It?

A chemical pregnancy is a very early pregnancy loss that occurs shortly after implantation, usually around the time your period is due. It is called a chemical pregnancy because it is detected by the chemical reaction of a pregnancy test but is too early to be seen on an ultrasound. Though common, it is rarely talked about, leaving many women confused and upset.

## Signs and How It Is Diagnosed

The typical pattern is a positive pregnancy test followed by a period-like bleed a few days later, and a subsequent negative test. Sometimes hCG blood tests show low, falling levels. Clinically, a chemical pregnancy is a form of early miscarriage, but because it happens so early, bleeding is usually similar to a normal period and does not usually require medical management. Your doctor may check your hCG has returned to zero to confirm the loss is complete.

## Causes and Trying Again

Most chemical pregnancies are caused by chromosomal abnormalities in the embryo and are not related to anything you did or did not do. They are extremely common, accounting for a significant proportion of all conceptions, though many go unnoticed without early testing. Having one chemical pregnancy does not mean you will not go on to have a healthy pregnancy; in fact, fertility is often normal afterwards. You can usually try again as soon as you feel ready, emotionally and physically.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### 1. Is a chemical pregnancy the same as a miscarriage?

It is a type of very early miscarriage, occurring before a pregnancy can be seen on ultrasound.

### 2. Can I try again straight after a chemical pregnancy?

Yes, most women can try again immediately, though waiting for one normal period helps with dating.

### 3. Does having one mean something is wrong with me?

No. Most are caused by random chromosomal issues and do not indicate a fertility problem.
