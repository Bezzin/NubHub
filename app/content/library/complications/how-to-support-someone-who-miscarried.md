# How to Support Someone Who Has Had a Miscarriage

When someone you care about has a miscarriage, it's hard to know what to say. Here's how to help.

## What to Say

- 'I'm so sorry'
- 'I'm here for you'
- 'Take all the time you need'
- 'Your feelings are valid'
- 'Would you like to talk about it?'

## What NOT to Say

- 'At least you can get pregnant'
- 'It wasn't meant to be'
- 'You can always try again'
- 'At least it was early'
- 'Everything happens for a reason'

These well-meaning comments can be hurtful.

## Practical Support

Bring food, help with chores, check in regularly (not just once), remember important dates (due date, loss date), and follow their lead on what they need.

## FAQ

### What should I say to someone who miscarried?

Keep it simple: 'I'm so sorry, I'm here for you.' Avoid platitudes like 'it wasn't meant to be' or 'at least it was early.' Listen more than you talk.

### How long should I check in after a miscarriage?

Don't just check in once. Keep checking in for weeks and months afterwards. Remember the due date — it can be a particularly hard day.
