# Empty Sac at 6 Weeks: What Next?

Being told the gestational sac is empty at a 6-week scan is an anxious moment, but it is also one of the most common reasons for a follow-up appointment rather than a definitive diagnosis. At 6 weeks, a pregnancy can simply be too early to see an embryo or heartbeat, especially if your dates are slightly out.

## Why the Sac May Appear Empty at 6 Weeks

At 6 weeks, the embryo is only a few millimetres long and a heartbeat may not yet be visible, particularly if ovulation occurred later than expected or your cycle is irregular. A transvaginal scan before 6 weeks 5 days often shows a sac without a yolk sac or fetal pole. This is why most units will not diagnose a failed pregnancy at this stage and will instead invite you back for a repeat scan, usually 7 to 14 days later, to allow time for growth.

## What Happens at the Follow-Up Scan

The repeat scan gives a clearer picture. If the embryo and heartbeat are now visible, the pregnancy is progressing. If the sac remains empty and has grown, or if measurements confirm the gestational sac is large enough that an embryo should be seen, the diagnosis may be a missed miscarriage or blighted ovum. You will be supported through your options: expectant management, medical management, or surgical management, with no rush to decide.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### 1. Can an empty sac at 6 weeks turn out to be a healthy pregnancy?

Yes, especially if your dates could be off or you ovulated late. A follow-up scan often shows normal development.

### 2. How long will I have to wait for the second scan?

Usually 7 to 14 days, to allow enough time for an embryo to become visible if the pregnancy is ongoing.

### 3. What size should the sac be before an embryo is expected?

Guidelines suggest that if the mean sac diameter is 25mm or more with no embryo, a failed pregnancy is likely.
