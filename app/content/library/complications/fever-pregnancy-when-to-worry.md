# Fever Pregnancy When To Worry

Fever during pregnancy — when it's concerning, what to do, and safe treatment.

## Overview

A fever in pregnancy can be concerning. Here's when to worry and what to do.

## FAQ

### A normal body temperature is 36-37.2C. A fever is 38C or above. In early pregnancy, high fever may increase the risk of certain birth defects. If your temperature is 38C+, contact your GP. Safe treatment: paracetamol, cool fluids, light clothing. Avoid ibuprofen.

Can a fever harm my baby in early pregnancy?

### A high fever (39C+) in early pregnancy may slightly increase the risk of certain birth defects. However, most women with fever in pregnancy have healthy babies. Contact your GP if your temperature is 38C+.
