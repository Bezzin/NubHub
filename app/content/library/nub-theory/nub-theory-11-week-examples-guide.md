# Nub Theory: 11-Week Scan Examples

At 11 weeks, nub theory is possible but less accurate (approximately 70%). Here's what to look for.

## Table of Contents

1. [What 11-Week Nubs Look Like](#s1)
2. [Limitations](#s2)
3. [FAQ](#faq)

## What 11-Week Nubs Look Like {#s1}

At 11 weeks, both boy and girl nubs may look similar — slightly raised but not clearly differentiated. Boy nubs may show a slight upward angle, girl nubs may be starting to flatten.

## Limitations {#s2}

Accuracy is only 70% at 11 weeks. Many nubs are ambiguous. It's best to wait until 12+ weeks for a more reliable reading.

## FAQ {#faq}

### Should I try nub theory at 11 weeks?

You can try, but accuracy is only 70%. If you get a clear reading (obviously up or obviously flat), it's more reliable. Ambiguous readings at 11 weeks should be rechecked at 12+ weeks.
