## Why We Share Real Stories {#why-share}

Real stories help set realistic expectations. Some mums had predictions that matched, others did not, and many had scans that were simply too unclear to read. Reading a mix reminds us that nub theory is speculation, not certainty, and that every pregnancy is unique.

Understanding these basics helps you approach each appointment with realistic expectations and clearer questions for your sonographer or midwife.

## A Mix of Outcomes {#mix-outcomes}

Among the stories shared, predictions ranged from spot on to completely wrong. One mum had a boy fork reading that turned out to be a girl. Another had a flat nub read as a girl that was confirmed. Several mums had images too unclear for any reading. The takeaway is that outcomes vary widely and no single experience defines how reliable nub theory is.

Understanding these basics helps you approach each appointment with realistic expectations and clearer questions for your sonographer or midwife.

## FAQ {#faq}

### Can I submit my own story?

Many communities welcome shared experiences. Always be honest about whether your prediction matched.

### Do most mums find nub theory accurate?

Experiences are mixed. Some swear by it, others were misled. Treat it as fun, not fact.

### Should stories influence my expectations?

Read them for interest, not for certainty. Your own scan results are what matter.
