# Ramzi Theory Explained: Does Placenta Position Predict Gender?

Ramzi theory is one of the most talked-about early gender prediction methods. It claims to determine your baby's sex from just 6 weeks by looking at which side of your uterus the placenta is on. But does it actually work?

## What Is Ramzi Theory? {#what-is-ramzi}

Ramzi theory (named after Dr. Saad Ramzi Ismail) is a gender prediction method that uses the position of the placenta on an early ultrasound to determine fetal sex. According to the theory, if the placenta is on the right side of the uterus, you're having a boy; if it's on the left, you're having a girl.

The theory claims to work from as early as 6 weeks, making it one of the earliest gender prediction methods available.

## How Ramzi Theory Supposedly Works {#how-ramzi-works}

The claimed methodology:

1. **Get a transverse ultrasound** (a cross-sectional view) at 6–8 weeks
2. **Identify the placenta location** — which side of the uterus it's attached to
3. **Right side = boy** (according to the theory)
4. **Left side = girl** (according to the theory)

The theory is based on the claim that male and female embryos implant on different sides of the uterus due to differences in metabolism or chromosome influence.

## Does Ramzi Theory Work? {#does-ramzi-work}

**The short answer is no — Ramzi theory has not been scientifically validated.**

Despite Dr. Ramzi's claim of 97% accuracy, no independent peer-reviewed study has replicated these results. A 2014 analysis found that Ramzi theory performed no better than chance (approximately 50%).

Key problems with Ramzi theory:
- The biological mechanism (sex-determined implantation) has not been demonstrated
- Placenta position is not consistently identifiable at 6–8 weeks
- Many ultrasounds at this stage are transvaginal, which has different orientation from abdominal scans
- The original study has not been published in a peer-reviewed journal

## Ramzi Theory Accuracy {#ramzi-accuracy}

| Claimed Accuracy | Real-World Accuracy |
|-----------------|---------------------|
| 97% (Ramzi's claim) | ~50% (chance) |

Independent testing has found Ramzi theory to be no more accurate than flipping a coin.

## How to Use Ramzi Theory {#how-to-use-ramzi}

If you want to try Ramzi theory for fun:

1. You need an ultrasound from 6–8 weeks
2. The image must be a transverse view (cross-section), not a profile
3. Identify which side of the uterus the placenta is on
4. Right = boy, left = girl
5. **Remember**: This is just for fun. The accuracy is approximately 50%.

## Frequently Asked Questions {#faq}

### Is Ramzi theory accurate?

Ramzi theory claims 97% accuracy, but this has never been validated by independent research. Real-world testing suggests the accuracy is approximately 50% — no better than guessing. Nub theory, by contrast, has been validated at 80–95% accuracy in multiple peer-reviewed studies.

### Can I use Ramzi theory from my 7-week scan?

You can try, but the accuracy will be approximately 50%. Ramzi theory has not been validated scientifically, and placenta position at 7 weeks is not a reliable indicator of fetal sex.

### Should I trust Ramzi theory or nub theory?

Nub theory is significantly more reliable. It has scientific backing and accuracy rates of 80–95% at 12+ weeks. Ramzi theory has no scientific validation and accuracy rates equivalent to guessing. If you want a real prediction, use nub theory.

## Frequently Asked Questions {#faq}

### Why is Ramzi theory so popular if it doesn't work?

Ramzi theory is popular because it promises very early gender prediction (6 weeks) and claims high accuracy (97%). The combination of early timing and a seemingly scientific methodology makes it appealing. Unfortunately, the claims haven't held up to scientific scrutiny.

### Can Ramzi theory be confirmed at 20 weeks?

The only way to confirm your baby's gender definitively is through NIPT (99%+), the 20-week anatomy scan (95–99%), or diagnostic tests (100%). Ramzi theory cannot be 'confirmed' because it's not a validated method.
