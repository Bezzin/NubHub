## What Is a Girl Stack {#girl-stack}

A girl stack refers to the appearance of the genital tubercle pointing upward in a short, stacked shape rather than a long angled line. In nub theory, this stacked look is read as a possible early sign of a girl. The term comes from the way the developing nub appears to sit in layers above the body line, creating a small bump rather than a pronounced point. It is one of the most talked about visual cues in early gender prediction communities, but it is not a medical confirmation.

Understanding these basics helps you approach each appointment with realistic expectations and clearer questions for your sonographer or midwife.

## How to Spot a Girl Stack on Your Scan {#spot-girl-stack}

To see a possible girl stack, your scan image needs to be a clear side profile taken between roughly 11 and 14 weeks. Look at the angle between the genital tubercle and the spine. A girl stack typically shows the nub sitting almost flat or slightly raised with a short stacked appearance, rather than angled sharply upward by 30 degrees or more. Lighting and image quality matter, so avoid trying to read grainy or front facing photos.

Understanding these basics helps you approach each appointment with realistic expectations and clearer questions for your sonographer or midwife.

## FAQ {#faq}

### How accurate is the girl stack sign?

It can be a reasonable early indicator after 12 weeks, but accuracy varies widely by image quality and the person reading it. It is never a guarantee of sex.

### Can I see a girl stack before 11 weeks?

Before 11 weeks the nub looks similar in boys and girls, so any reading is unreliable that early.

### Is a girl stack the same as three lines?

They are related. The three line sign is a later, more reliable girl indicator seen after about 15 weeks. A girl stack is an earlier, softer clue.
