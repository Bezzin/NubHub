# What If There Is No Nub Visible on Scan

Pregnancy brings countless questions, and understanding what each scan and sign means can help you feel more prepared. This guide breaks down the essentials in plain language so you know what to expect and what to ask your care team.

## Why the Nub Might Not Be Visible {#why-not-visible}

The most common reason the nub is not visible is simply scan angle. If your baby was not in a clear side profile, or was moving during the capture, the genital area will not show in a readable way. Other causes include the baby being too small, image quality being low, or the scan being taken too early before the nub has developed a clear angle.

Understanding these basics helps you approach each appointment with realistic expectations and clearer questions for your sonographer or midwife.

## What It Means for Gender Prediction {#meaning-for-prediction}

An unreadable scan means no reliable nub theory prediction can be made from it, and that is completely normal. It tells you nothing about your baby's sex. Some images simply are not suitable, and it is better to wait for a clearer scan than to guess from a blurry photo.

Understanding these basics helps you approach each appointment with realistic expectations and clearer questions for your sonographer or midwife.

## FAQ {#faq}

### Does no visible nub mean something is wrong?

No. It usually just means the scan angle was not right. It has no bearing on your baby's health or sex.

### Can I get another scan to try again?

Yes. A private scan later in pregnancy will give a clearer view, and sex can be confirmed after about 16 weeks.

### Should I pay someone to read an unclear image?

Be cautious. If the image is not a clear side profile, no reader can make a reliable call.
