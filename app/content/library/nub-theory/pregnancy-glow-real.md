# Is the Pregnancy Glow Real?

The pregnancy glow is one of the most famous pregnancy symptoms, and yes, it has a real biological basis. Increased blood volume and hormonal shifts can give skin a brighter, rosier appearance. Not everyone experiences it, but for many it is a genuine and noticeable change.

## What Causes the Glow {#what-causes-the-glow}

During pregnancy, blood volume rises by up to 50 percent to support the baby. This increased circulation brings more blood to the skin, especially the face, giving a flushed, radiant look. Hormones such as oestrogen also boost oil production, which can make skin appear more luminous.

## When the Glow Appears {#when-the-glow-appears}

Most people notice the glow in the second trimester, when hormones stabilise and morning sickness eases. For some it starts earlier. The glow usually fades after birth as hormones and blood volume return to normal levels.

## FAQ {#faq}

### Does everyone get the pregnancy glow?

No. Hormonal changes affect everyone differently. Some people get dry skin, acne, or pigmentation instead of a glow. All of these are normal.

### Can I enhance the glow?

Gentle skincare, hydration, rest, and a balanced diet support healthy skin. Avoid harsh treatments during pregnancy and check with your midwife before new products.

### Is the glow a sign of baby gender?

No. The old wives' tale that a glow means a girl and dull skin means a boy has no scientific basis. The glow comes from hormones and circulation, not the baby's sex.
