# Night Sweats in Early Pregnancy

Waking up drenched in sweat during early pregnancy is surprisingly common. Hormonal surges, a higher core temperature, and increased blood flow all contribute. Night sweats can be uncomfortable but are usually a normal part of early pregnancy.

## Why Night Sweats Happen {#why-night-sweats-happen}

Rising progesterone raises core body temperature and triggers the body's cooling system, leading to sweating. Oestrogen fluctuations also affect temperature regulation. Increased blood volume and a faster metabolic rate add to the effect, particularly at night when the body tries to cool down.

## Managing Night Sweats {#managing-night-sweats}

Wear lightweight, breathable sleepwear in natural fibres, keep the bedroom cool, use layered bedding so you can adjust, and stay hydrated through the day. A fan by the bed and a cool shower before sleep can also help.

## FAQ {#faq}

### Are night sweats normal in pregnancy?

Yes, especially in the first trimester. Hormone-driven temperature changes are the usual cause.

### When should I worry about night sweats?

If sweating comes with fever, chills, pain, or a rash, contact your GP. Otherwise, night sweats alone are usually benign.

### How long do pregnancy night sweats last?

They often ease in the second trimester as hormones settle. Some people experience them throughout pregnancy, and they can also appear postpartum.
