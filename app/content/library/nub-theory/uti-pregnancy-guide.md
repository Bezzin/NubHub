# UTI in Pregnancy Symptoms and Treatment

Urinary tract infections are more common in pregnancy due to hormonal and physical changes that slow urine flow and alter the urinary tract. Prompt diagnosis and treatment are important because untreated UTIs can lead to kidney infections and pregnancy complications.

## Why UTIs Are Common in Pregnancy {#why-utis-are-common-in-pregnancy}

Progesterone relaxes the muscles of the urinary tract, slowing urine flow. The growing uterus presses on the bladder. These changes allow bacteria to multiply more easily. Glucose in urine during pregnancy also feeds bacteria.

## Symptoms and Treatment {#symptoms-and-treatment}

Symptoms include burning on urination, frequent urges, cloudy or smelly urine, and lower abdominal pain. Treatment is with pregnancy-safe antibiotics prescribed by a GP. Left untreated, a UTI can progress to a kidney infection, so always report symptoms.

## FAQ {#faq}

### Can a UTI harm my baby?

An untreated UTI can increase the risk of preterm birth and low birth weight. Prompt treatment with safe antibiotics protects both you and your baby.

### How is a UTI diagnosed in pregnancy?

A simple urine sample is tested. Routine antenatal appointments include urine checks to catch infections early.

### Can I prevent UTIs in pregnancy?

Drink plenty of water, wipe front to back, empty the bladder fully, and urinate after sex. These steps reduce risk significantly.
