# Nub Theory at Dating Scan vs NT Scan

If you are interested in nub theory, one of the first questions you will probably ask is: which scan should I use? The dating scan and the NT scan both fall in the first trimester, but they are performed at slightly different times, for different purposes, and produce images of varying quality for nub reading. Understanding the differences will help you get the most out of whichever images you have.

## What Is Nub Theory?

Nub theory is based on the observation that between approximately 11 and 14 weeks of pregnancy, the genital tubercle — sometimes called the nub — sits at an angle that differs between male and female babies. In boys, the nub tends to angle upward at more than 30 degrees relative to the spine. In girls, it typically remains parallel to the spine or angles very slightly upward.

The theory is not a medical diagnostic tool and is not used by NHS sonographers to determine sex. It is something many parents explore for fun using their scan images, sometimes with the help of experienced nub readers in communities like Nub Hub. Accuracy improves significantly with gestational age and image quality — a clear 13-week profile image will always give a more meaningful reading than a blurry 10-week one.

## The Dating Scan and Nub Theory {#dating-scan}

The dating scan is offered by the NHS between roughly 10 and 14 weeks of pregnancy, though in practice most trusts aim to perform it between 11 and 13 weeks and six days. Its primary purpose is to confirm your due date, check for a heartbeat, look at the number of babies, and carry out some initial checks on the pregnancy.

Because the dating scan often falls within the nub theory window, many parents attempt to read the nub from these images. However, there are a few limitations to be aware of:

- **Earlier gestational age** — if your dating scan is performed closer to 11 weeks, the nub may not have developed a clearly readable angle yet. At 11 weeks, the genital tubercle is still relatively undeveloped and the difference in angle between male and female fetuses is subtler.
- **Variable image quality** — dating scans are not routinely performed with nub reading in mind. The sonographer focuses on measurements and checks, and a clear side profile may not always be captured.
- **Fetal position** — the baby may not be lying in a position that gives a readable side profile view. Sonographers will move on once they have the measurements they need.

Despite these limitations, some dating scan images are excellent for nub reading, particularly if they happen at 12 weeks or later and the baby cooperates with a clear side-on position.

## The NT Scan and Nub Theory {#nt-scan}

The NT (nuchal translucency) scan, which is part of the combined first trimester screening test for chromosomal conditions such as Down's syndrome, is typically performed between 11 and 14 weeks. In many NHS trusts, this is done at the same appointment as the dating scan. However, because the NT scan requires a very clear side profile of the baby's head and neck to measure the fluid at the back of the neck, the images it produces are often better suited to nub reading than a purely dating-focused scan.

Key advantages for nub reading:

- **The NT measurement requires a true mid-sagittal side profile** — this is exactly the view needed for nub theory. If you have a clear NT scan image, it is likely to be your best nub photo.
- **It is typically performed at 12 to 13 weeks** — this gestational age is within the optimal nub theory window, when the angle difference between male and female babies is most pronounced.
- **The baby needs to be in the right position** — sonographers performing NT scans will wait or encourage the baby to move until they achieve the correct profile view, which often means the nub is also visible in the image.

## Key Differences Between the Two Scans {#key-differences}

| | Dating Scan | NT Scan |
|---|---|---|
| Primary purpose | Confirm dates, count babies, check heartbeat | Screen for chromosomal conditions |
| Gestational age | Often 10–14 weeks | Usually 11–14 weeks |
| Image type needed | Various views | True mid-sagittal profile |
| Usefulness for nub theory | Variable — depends on timing and position | Often better, as profile view is required |
| NHS availability | Offered to all | Offered as part of combined screening |

## Getting a Good Nub Image

The most useful nub images are side-on profiles where you can clearly see the spine running horizontally and the nub projecting from the bottom of the torso. If you are having both scans at the same appointment, you may receive several images — look for the one where the baby is lying completely on its side with the spine visible from top to bottom.

Private early pregnancy scans can also be used for nub reading. Many private clinics that offer 12-week or early reassurance scans will capture side profile images, and some are specifically experienced in nub reading. If you are booking privately with nub theory in mind, mention this when booking so they know to try to capture an appropriate view.

Remember that no image, however clear, gives a confirmed gender result at this stage. Nub theory is an educated guess — a bit of fun during the long wait before the 20-week anomaly scan.

## FAQ {#faq}

### Which scan is better for nub theory?

Usually the NT scan image, because it requires a true mid-sagittal side profile — the exact view needed for nub reading — and is typically performed at the optimal 12 to 14 week window. That said, some dating scan images are equally clear, especially if performed at 12 weeks or beyond. Use whichever image gives the clearest side profile with the spine fully visible.

### Can I use both scans?

Yes. If you have images from both appointments, compare them. Sometimes one will be much clearer than the other. Comparing two readings from different images can be reassuring if they agree, though neither is a confirmed result.

### Does the NHS tell you the nub angle?

No. NHS sonographers do not predict sex from the nub and will not comment on its angle. Any reading is done informally — by you, by other parents in online communities, or by experienced nub readers.

### At what week is nub theory most reliable?

Nub theory readings are generally considered most meaningful from around 12 weeks onwards. Before 11 weeks, the angle is very difficult to assess reliably. At 13 to 14 weeks, the difference between male and female angles is at its clearest on a good quality image.
