# Vaginal Discharge in Early Pregnancy

An increase in vaginal discharge is one of the earliest and most normal signs of pregnancy. Known as leucorrhoea, this thin, milky discharge helps protect the vagina from infection. Knowing what is normal helps you spot when to seek advice.

## What Is Normal Discharge {#what-is-normal-discharge}

Normal pregnancy discharge is thin, milky white or clear, and mild-smelling. It increases due to higher oestrogen and greater blood flow to the vaginal area. It is your body's way of keeping the birth canal clean and protected.

## When to Seek Help {#when-to-seek-help}

See your GP or midwife if discharge becomes green, yellow, thick and clumpy, foul-smelling, or is accompanied by itching, soreness, or pain. These can signal infections like thrush, bacterial vaginosis, or STIs, most of which are treatable in pregnancy.

## FAQ {#faq}

### Is more discharge normal in pregnancy?

Yes. Increased discharge is common and usually harmless. It typically continues throughout pregnancy.

### When is discharge a concern?

If it changes colour, smell, or texture, or comes with itching or pain, contact your midwife. Bleeding or watery leakage also needs urgent review.

### Can I use panty liners in pregnancy?

Yes, unscented panty liners are fine. Avoid douching and perfumed products, which can irritate and upset the vaginal balance.
