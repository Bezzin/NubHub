# When Does Linea Nigra Appear?

Linea nigra is the dark vertical line that can appear on the belly during pregnancy. It is completely normal and caused by hormonal changes that increase pigmentation. For most people it appears in the second trimester and fades after birth.

## What Is Linea Nigra {#what-is-linea-nigra}

Linea nigra is a line of increased pigmentation running from the belly button down to the pubic bone, and sometimes upward toward the ribs. It is actually always present as a faint line called the linea alba, but pregnancy hormones make it darken and become visible.

## When It Appears and Fades {#when-it-appears-and-fades}

Most people notice linea nigra around weeks 18 to 24, though some see it earlier. It tends to darken as pregnancy progresses. After birth it usually fades over several months as hormone levels drop. Sun exposure can make it darker, so sun protection helps.

## FAQ {#faq}

### Is linea nigra harmful?

No. It is a harmless, normal pregnancy change caused by melanin-stimulating hormones. It needs no treatment.

### Does linea nigra predict baby gender?

No. There is an old myth that a long line means a boy, but there is no evidence for this. The line is purely hormonal.

### Will it go away completely?

For most people it fades significantly within months of birth. Some retain a faint line, especially with subsequent pregnancies.
