# When Does Linea Nigra Appear?

Linea nigra is the dark vertical line that can appear on the belly during pregnancy. It is completely normal and caused by hormonal changes that increase pigmentation. For most people it appears in the second trimester and fades after birth. Here is everything you need to know about when to expect it, why it happens, and how long it lasts.

## What Is Linea Nigra {#what-is-linea-nigra}

Linea nigra is a line of increased pigmentation running from the belly button down to the pubic bone, and sometimes upward toward the ribs. It is actually always present as a faint line called the linea alba, but pregnancy hormones make it darken and become visible.

The line can vary in width, length, and darkness from person to person. Some people notice a thin, barely visible stripe while others develop a more prominent dark band. Both are completely normal. It is generally between one and two centimetres wide and can run the full length of the abdomen.

People with naturally darker skin tones tend to notice linea nigra more easily and may find it is more prominent. Those with lighter skin can also develop it, though the contrast may be less pronounced.

## When It Appears and Fades {#when-it-appears-and-fades}

Most people notice linea nigra around weeks 18 to 24, though some see it earlier — even in the first trimester. It tends to darken gradually as pregnancy progresses and hormone levels rise. It is particularly common in the second and third trimesters.

Not everyone develops linea nigra, and the absence of it does not indicate anything is wrong with your pregnancy. It is simply a variation in how different bodies respond to pregnancy hormones.

After birth it usually begins to fade over several months as hormone levels drop. For many people it lightens significantly within three to six months postpartum. However, some people retain a faint line, particularly after subsequent pregnancies. Sun exposure can make it darker, both during pregnancy and in the months after, so protecting your skin from direct sun can help it fade more quickly.

## Why Does It Happen {#why-does-it-happen}

Linea nigra appears because pregnancy hormones — particularly melanocyte-stimulating hormone (MSH) — encourage cells called melanocytes to produce more melanin, the pigment that gives skin its colour. This increased melanin production affects areas of the skin that already have more pigment, including the linea alba (the connective tissue line that runs down the centre of the abdomen), the nipples, the areolae, and sometimes the face (where it is called melasma or the "mask of pregnancy").

The same hormonal process that creates linea nigra is responsible for other skin changes you may notice during pregnancy, such as darkening of freckles and moles, or a general deepening of skin tone in certain areas. None of these changes are harmful.

## How to Care for Your Skin {#how-to-care-for-your-skin}

There is no need to treat linea nigra — it is a normal part of pregnancy and will fade on its own. However, if you want to minimise its appearance:

- **Use sunscreen**: Applying SPF 30 or above to your abdomen when it is exposed to sunlight can prevent the line from darkening further. Sun exposure is one of the most significant factors in how dark linea nigra becomes.
- **Moisturise regularly**: Keeping the skin moisturised does not prevent linea nigra, but it can help your skin feel comfortable as it stretches.
- **Avoid bleaching products**: Do not use skin-lightening or bleaching creams on linea nigra during pregnancy. Many contain ingredients that are not safe to use when pregnant.

After birth, continuing to use sunscreen on the area will help it fade. For most people, patience is all that is needed — the line will gradually lighten without any intervention.

Speak to your midwife or GP if you have concerns about any skin changes during pregnancy.

## Frequently Asked Questions {#faq}

### Is linea nigra harmful?

No. It is a harmless, normal pregnancy change caused by melanin-stimulating hormones. It needs no treatment and does not affect your baby. It will fade naturally after birth as your hormone levels return to their pre-pregnancy state.

### Does linea nigra predict baby gender?

No. There is an old wives' tale that suggests the length or darkness of the line can predict whether you are having a boy or a girl, but there is no scientific evidence to support this. The line is purely the result of hormonal changes and has nothing to do with your baby's sex.

### Will it go away completely?

For most people it fades significantly within months of birth. Some people retain a faint line, especially after subsequent pregnancies or if they have naturally darker skin. Protecting the area from sun exposure during and after pregnancy will help it fade as much as possible.
