# Pregnancy Jealousy Among Friends

Jealousy in friendship is one of the most common — and most hidden — emotions around pregnancy. A friend announces, and something dark flickers: why her and not me? Or you're pregnant and a friend pulls away, and you suspect envy. It's uncomfortable to talk about, but naming it is the first step to handling it well.

## Jealousy is normal, not shameful
Evolutionary and social psychology recognises envy as a near-universal human emotion, especially around major life milestones like pregnancy. Feeling it doesn't make you a bad friend. Acting on it unkindly does — but the feeling itself is just information about what you long for.

## If you're the one feeling jealous
- **Name it privately.** "I'm jealous, and I also love my friend." Both are true.
- **Don't punish them.** Withdrawing silently, making snide comments, or minimising their news turns feeling into harm.
- **Investigate the jealousy.** Is it about the baby, the timing, the attention, the life stage? Understanding it loosens its grip.
- **Take space if needed.** "I'm so happy for you. I'm also working through some feelings — bear with me."
- **Get support elsewhere.** Talk to a partner, counsellor, or forum where you can be honest without hurting your friend.

## If you suspect a friend is jealous of you
- **Don't call it out directly.** "Are you jealous?" rarely helps.
- **Be sensitive with pregnancy talk.** Not everyone needs every update.
- **Protect the friendship's core.** Prioritise shared interests and history.
- **Give grace.** If they're distant, assume pain, not malice.
- **Don't shrink your joy.** Being considerate doesn't mean hiding your happiness.

## When jealousy damages the friendship
If a friend becomes consistently undermining — minimising your news, competing, or making cutting remarks — you can address it: "I've noticed things feel strained since I announced. Can we talk about it?" If it persists, a pause may be healthier than a slow deterioration.

## Comparison is the thief
Pregnancy timelines are arbitrary. A friend's pregnancy doesn't delay yours. Easier said than felt — but worth reminding yourself when the comparison spiral starts.

## A reminder
Jealousy doesn't end friendships; unspoken resentment does. Honest, kind communication — even when awkward — is what protects relationships through this season.
