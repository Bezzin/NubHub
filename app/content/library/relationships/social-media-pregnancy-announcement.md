# Social Media Pregnancy Announcement: When Is It Safe?

Announcing your pregnancy on social media is a popular way to share your exciting news, but deciding when it is safe and appropriate to post can feel daunting. Here is some guidance to help you make the right choice for you.

2. [Tips for a Thoughtful Announcement](#section-2)

## When Is the Right Time to Post? {#section-1}

The question of when to announce your pregnancy on social media does not have a single answer, but the most common approach is to wait until after the twelve-week scan. By this point, the risk of miscarriage has dropped significantly, and most couples feel more confident sharing their news publicly. The twelve-week scan also provides a photo many people like to include in their announcement.

Some couples choose to wait even longer, perhaps until they know the sex or until the bump is showing, while others announce earlier to close friends via private messages. Ultimately, the decision is yours. Consider how you would feel if you had to share difficult news later, and whether you would want the support of your social media community or prefer privacy. There is no wrong choice, only what feels right for you.

## Tips for a Thoughtful Announcement {#section-2}

When you are ready to post, think about what you want to share and with whom. Consider adjusting your privacy settings to limit the post to close friends and family if you are not comfortable with a fully public announcement. Choose a photo or message that reflects your personality, whether that is a scan picture, a pair of baby shoes, or a humorous caption.

Be mindful that once something is on social media, it is hard to control. Avoid posting sensitive details like your exact due date or hospital, and think about who might see the post through shares and tags. If you have not told certain family members or colleagues yet, make sure your post will not reach them first. A little planning ensures your announcement is joyful and stress-free.

## FAQ {#faq}

### Is it safe to announce pregnancy on social media before twelve weeks?

Many choose to wait until twelve weeks, but it is a personal decision. Consider your comfort with sharing difficult news publicly if the pregnancy does not progress.

### Should I post a scan photo?

Scan photos are a popular choice and usually fine to share. Some hospitals have policies about photography during the scan, so check beforehand and always respect the sonographer's guidance.

### What if someone announces my pregnancy before me?

This can be upsetting. To prevent it, tell close family and friends before posting publicly and ask them not to share the news. If it happens, communicate your feelings calmly and set clear boundaries going forward.
