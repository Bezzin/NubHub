# Partner's Guide to Pregnancy Scans

Pregnancy scans are some of the most exciting moments of the first trimester, and as a partner, your presence can make them even more special. Here is everything you need to know about what scans involve and how to make the most of each appointment.

## What Scans Happen in the First Trimester {#section-1}

The first trimester typically includes two key scans on the NHS. The early dating scan, offered between eleven and fourteen weeks, confirms your due date, checks the baby's heartbeat, and can detect some early abnormalities. This scan may also include the combined screening test for chromosomal conditions such as Down's syndrome, which involves a blood test and a special measurement called the nuchal translucency.

Some couples have an earlier private scan, sometimes called a viability or reassurance scan, around six to eight weeks. This can confirm the pregnancy is in the right place and detect a heartbeat, though it is not routinely offered on the NHS unless there are concerns such as bleeding or pain. Understanding what each scan involves helps you prepare and know what questions to ask.

## How to Make the Most of Scan Appointments {#section-2}

Attending scans is one of the most rewarding parts of pregnancy for partners. It is your first chance to see your baby and hear the heartbeat, a moment many parents describe as when the pregnancy suddenly feels real. To make the most of each appointment, arrive early to allow time for parking and paperwork. Bring a list of questions for the sonographer, as appointments can feel rushed and it is easy to forget what you wanted to ask.

Some hospitals allow you to purchase scan photos, so bring a few pounds just in case. Be emotionally prepared, as scans can bring up unexpected feelings, especially the first one. If results raise concerns, stay calm and supportive, and ask for clarification about next steps. Your presence at these appointments is invaluable, not just for your partner but for your own connection to the pregnancy.

## FAQ {#faq}

### Can I attend every scan with my partner?

Most NHS trusts welcome partners at scans, though policies can vary, especially during flu season or other restrictions. Check with your local hospital in advance.

### What if the scan shows something concerning?

Stay as calm as possible and ask the sonographer to explain the findings clearly. You may be referred for a follow-up scan or further tests. Your support during this time is crucial.

### Can we find out the sex at the first scan?

The dating scan is not designed to determine sex, and at this stage it is too early for reliable identification. If you want to know, this is usually possible at the twenty-week anomaly scan.
