# Should You Announce Pregnancy Before 12 Weeks?

The twelve-week mark is traditionally seen as the safe time to announce a pregnancy, but the decision is not always straightforward. Here is a balanced look at the reasons to wait and the reasons you might choose to share your news earlier.

## Why Twelve Weeks Is the Traditional Milestone {#section-1}

The twelve-week mark has long been considered the safest time to announce a pregnancy because the risk of miscarriage drops significantly after this point. By twelve weeks, the baby's vital organs have formed, the heartbeat is strong and regular, and the first scan has confirmed that the pregnancy is viable. For many couples, this milestone provides the reassurance needed to share the news with confidence.

Waiting until twelve weeks also means that if the pregnancy does not progress, you can grieve privately without having to explain the situation to everyone who knew. This is the primary reason the twelve-week rule became so widespread, and it remains a valid and thoughtful choice for many families today.

## Reasons You Might Announce Earlier {#section-2}

There are good reasons to announce before twelve weeks. The first trimester can be physically and emotionally challenging, and having a support network who knows what you are going through can make a real difference. If you are experiencing severe morning sickness, needing time off work, or dealing with complications, telling people early means they can offer practical and emotional support when you need it most.

Some couples also feel that sharing the journey from the beginning is more authentic to who they are. They would rather include their loved ones in the full experience, including any difficulties, than present a polished announcement later. There is no right or wrong answer, only what is right for you. Consider your circumstances, your support network, and how you would handle sharing difficult news if it became necessary.

## FAQ {#faq}

### What is the miscarriage risk before twelve weeks?

The risk of miscarriage is highest in the first trimester, decreasing significantly after twelve weeks. However, most pregnancies progress normally, and the risk at any given point is lower than many people assume.

### Should I tell my employer before twelve weeks?

If your symptoms affect your work or you need accommodations, you may need to tell your employer earlier. You are not legally required to inform them until fifteen weeks before your due date, but earlier disclosure can help you get the support you need.

### Is it wrong to want to keep the pregnancy private?

Not at all. Privacy is a perfectly valid choice. Some people prefer to keep the news between themselves and their partner until they feel ready to share, and that is completely fine.
