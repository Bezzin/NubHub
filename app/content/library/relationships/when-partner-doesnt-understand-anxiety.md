# When Your Partner Doesn't Understand Your Pregnancy Anxiety

Your partner means well, but they don't get it. Here's how to communicate your anxiety and get the support you need.

## Table of Contents

1. [Why Partners Don't Understand](#section-1)
2. [How to Communicate](#section-2)
3. [FAQ](#faq)

## Why Partners Don't Understand {#section-1}

- They're not experiencing the physical symptoms
- They may not have the same hormonal changes
- They may think anxiety is 'just worrying'
- They may feel helpless and shut down

## How to Communicate {#section-2}

- Use 'I feel' statements rather than 'you don't'
- Explain that pregnancy anxiety is hormonal and real
- Tell them specifically what would help (hugs, listening, not fixing)
- Share articles or resources with them
- Consider attending a midwife appointment together

## FAQ {#faq}

### How do I explain pregnancy anxiety to my partner?

Use specific examples. Say 'I feel anxious when...' rather than 'You don't understand.' Tell them exactly what would help — whether that's listening, hugs, or coming to appointments.

### What if my partner dismisses my anxiety?

This is common but hurtful. Try sharing resources about perinatal mental health. If they still dismiss it, lean on your midwife, friends, or a therapist for support.
