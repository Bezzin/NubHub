# How to Involve Your Partner in Pregnancy

Pregnancy can sometimes feel like it is happening to one person, but there are many ways to make it a shared journey. Here is how to involve your partner from the first trimester onwards so you both feel connected and engaged.

## Involving Your Partner from the Start {#section-1}

Pregnancy can sometimes feel like a solo journey for the pregnant person, but there are many ways to bring your partner along from the very beginning. Start by sharing everything you learn. Forward articles, mention symptoms you are reading about, and talk openly about what is happening each week. Apps like Nub Hub that track pregnancy week by week are great for giving both of you a window into what is developing and what to expect.

Invite your partner to attend every appointment possible, from the booking visit to every scan. Even if they cannot attend in person, call them during the appointment or send photos immediately afterwards. Make decisions together, whether it is choosing a midwife, deciding on screening tests, or talking about birth preferences. Being part of the decision-making process helps your partner feel like an equal participant rather than a bystander.

## Keeping Them Connected as Pregnancy Progresses {#section-2}

As the pregnancy advances, find new ways to keep your partner engaged. Suggest they talk or sing to the bump, as babies can hear voices from around twenty weeks, though this is more relevant later in pregnancy. In the first trimester, you can still build the habit by having your partner place their hand on your belly during quiet moments and talking about the future together.

Encourage your partner to read about pregnancy, birth, and newborn care. Suggest attending antenatal classes together when the time comes. Start a shared journal or photo album documenting the journey, and let your partner contribute their own thoughts and pictures. The more involved your partner feels during pregnancy, the more confident and connected they will be when the baby arrives.

## FAQ {#faq}

### What if my partner does not seem interested?

Some partners need time to engage. Keep sharing information gently and invite them to appointments. As the pregnancy becomes more visible and scans happen, most partners start to feel more connected.

### Can my partner come to midwife appointments?

Yes, in most cases partners are welcome. It is always worth checking with your midwife or hospital in advance, especially for early booking appointments.

### Should we start buying things together?

Involving your partner in choosing and purchasing baby items is a great way to build excitement. Start with small purchases like a vest or a pregnancy book and build from there.
