# Pregnant and Alone: Where to Get Support

Feeling alone in pregnancy is more common than you think. Here's where to find support in the UK.

## Table of Contents

1. [Where to Find Support](#section-1)
2. [Building a Network](#section-2)
3. [FAQ](#faq)

## Where to Find Support {#section-1}

- **Your midwife**: They're your first port of call for all support
- **GP**: For mental health and practical concerns
- **Netmums / Mumsnet**: Online communities of other mums
- **NCT**: Antenatal classes are a great way to meet other mums
- **Home-Start**: Volunteer support for families
- **Citizens Advice**: For financial and housing support
- ** Gingerbread**: Charity for single-parent families

## Building a Network {#section-2}

- Join local pregnancy groups on Facebook
- Attend antenatal classes
- Connect with other pregnant women at your GP surgery
- Consider online pregnancy communities

## FAQ {#faq}

### What support is available for pregnant women alone?

The NHS provides free maternity care. Charities like Gingerbread (single parents), Home-Start (volunteer support), and NCT (antenatal classes) offer additional support. You're entitled to maternity pay, benefits, and free prescriptions.

### Can I get a support person for my birth?

Yes. You can bring anyone to your birth — a friend, family member, or doula. Some areas offer volunteer doulas for women without support.
