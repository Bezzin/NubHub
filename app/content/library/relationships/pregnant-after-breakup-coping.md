# Pregnant After a Breakup: How to Cope

Going through a breakup while pregnant is emotionally and practically challenging. Here's how to cope.

## Table of Contents

1. [Processing the Breakup](#section-1)
2. [Practical Steps](#section-2)
3. [FAQ](#faq)

## Processing the Breakup {#section-1}

- Allow yourself to grieve the relationship
- Focus on the baby — they're your priority now
- Don't make major decisions while emotional
- Lean on friends and family for support
- Consider counselling to process the emotions

## Practical Steps {#section-2}

- Inform your midwife about the situation
- Sort out finances and housing
- If the ex-partner is the co-parent, consider a parenting plan
- Know your legal rights regarding child support
- Build your support network

## FAQ {#faq}

### Can I co-parent with my ex while pregnant?

Yes. If the ex is the biological parent, you can start discussing a co-parenting plan. Consider mediation if communication is difficult. Focus on what's best for the baby.

### What if my ex doesn't want to be involved?

You can't force involvement. Focus on building your own support network. Your child is entitled to financial support from their other parent — contact CMS for advice.
