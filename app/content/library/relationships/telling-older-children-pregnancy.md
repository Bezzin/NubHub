# Telling Older Children About Your Pregnancy

Breaking the news of a new sibling to older children requires thought. Here's how to do it age-appropriately.

## Table of Contents

1. [For Toddlers (1-3 years)](#section-1)
2. [For Older Children (4-10 years)](#section-2)
3. [FAQ](#faq)

## For Toddlers (1-3 years) {#section-1}

- Keep it simple: 'There's a baby growing in mummy's tummy'
- Use picture books about new siblings
- Don't tell them too early (they don't understand time)
- Show them scan photos
- Reassure them they're still loved

## For Older Children (4-10 years) {#section-2}

- Explain that a baby is growing
- Answer questions honestly but simply
- Involve them in preparation (choosing names, decorating)
- Read books about becoming a big sibling
- Reassure them about their role in the family

## FAQ {#faq}

### When should I tell my toddler I'm pregnant?

Wait until you're showing or past the first trimester. Toddlers don't understand waiting — if you tell them at 6 weeks they'll expect a baby tomorrow. Around 12-16 weeks is usually best.

### How do I handle jealousy from older children?

Involve them in the pregnancy from the start. Let them feel the bump, help choose things for the baby, and talk about their important role as a big sibling.
