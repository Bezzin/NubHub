# What Partners Need to Know About Early Pregnancy

If your partner is pregnant, you might feel unsure about what is happening and how best to help. This guide covers the key things partners need to know about early pregnancy, from common symptoms to emotional changes and practical ways to provide support.

## Understanding the Physical Symptoms {#section-1}

Early pregnancy brings a wave of physical changes that your partner may not have expected. Morning sickness, which can strike at any time of day, affects up to eighty percent of pregnant women and usually peaks around weeks six to ten. Fatigue can be overwhelming, often described as similar to running on empty. Other common symptoms include sore breasts, heightened sense of smell, food aversions, bloating, and frequent urination.

As a partner, knowing these symptoms are coming helps you respond with empathy rather than confusion. When she says she is too tired to go out or cannot face certain foods, it is not a personal rejection. It is her body adjusting to an enormous internal task. Patience and understanding are your most valuable tools.

## Navigating Emotional Changes Together {#section-2}

Hormonal shifts in early pregnancy can cause mood swings that rival any you have seen before. Your partner might feel elated one minute and tearful the next, often without an obvious trigger. Anxiety about miscarriage, the health of the baby, and the reality of becoming a parent is also common and entirely normal.

Your role is to be a steady, reassuring presence. Listen without judgement, validate her feelings, and avoid dismissing her worries with phrases like, "I am sure it will be fine." Instead, offer to attend appointments together, read up on what is happening, and remind her that you are in this as a team. Small gestures, like taking over dinner or running a bath, can make an enormous difference.

## FAQ {#faq}

### Can my partner take medication for morning sickness?

Some medications are safe in pregnancy, but always check with a GP or midwife first. Never give her over-the-counter remedies without professional advice.

### How long do early pregnancy symptoms last?

Most symptoms ease around twelve to fourteen weeks as the second trimester begins, though every pregnancy is different. Some symptoms, like fatigue, may persist throughout.

### Is it safe to have sex during early pregnancy?

Yes, sex is safe in a normal pregnancy and will not harm the baby. However, your partner may not feel up to it due to nausea and fatigue, so communication is key.
