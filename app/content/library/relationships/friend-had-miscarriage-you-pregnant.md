# When a Friend Has Had a Miscarriage and You're Pregnant

Your friend has lost a pregnancy, and you are carrying one. The contrast is agonising — for both of you. You may feel guilty for being pregnant, terrified of saying the wrong thing, and unsure how to be a friend when your body is a daily reminder of their loss.

## Understanding miscarriage grief
Miscarriage is a real loss, often unacknowledged publicly. Your friend is grieving a baby, a future, and often a sense of safety in pregnancy. Their grief may resurface with your milestones — your scan, your bump, your baby's movements. This isn't envy; it's loss being mirrored.

## How to tell them you're pregnant
- **Tell them privately, ideally in writing.** A text gives them space to react without an audience.
- **Acknowledge the difficulty.** "I'm pregnant, and I know this may be really hard to hear. No need to reply."
- **Don't cushion it with platitudes.** Avoid "everything happens for a reason" or "at least you know you can get pregnant."
- **Respect their response.** Silence, tears, or a brief "congratulations" are all valid.

## Being a friend through your pregnancy
- **Let them choose their level of involvement.** Some want to celebrate with you; others need to step back.
- **Don't hide your pregnancy, but don't centre it with them.** Talk about the rest of your friendship.
- **Mark their loss.** Don't pretend it didn't happen because you're pregnant. Saying "I'm thinking of you today" on hard dates matters.
- **Avoid complaining about pregnancy symptoms.** They would endure it all for their baby.

## Managing your guilt
Guilt is nearly universal here, but it's not helpful to either of you. You didn't cause their loss, and your joy doesn't steal from their grief. Both can exist. If guilt is overwhelming, talk it through with a counsellor or your partner.

## If the friendship changes
Some friendships strain under this weight. That doesn't make either of you a bad friend. Keep the door open: "I miss you, and I understand if you need space. I'm here whenever you're ready."

## A reminder
You can't remove their pain, but you can refuse to abandon them through it. Sensitivity, honesty, and patience are the best gifts you can offer.
