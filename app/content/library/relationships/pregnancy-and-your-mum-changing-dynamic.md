# Pregnancy and Your Mum: The Changing Dynamic

Becoming a mum yourself changes your relationship with your own mum. Here's how to navigate this transition.

## Table of Contents

1. [The Shift](#section-1)
2. [Managing the Relationship](#section-2)
3. [FAQ](#faq)

## The Shift {#section-1}

- Your mum may become more involved (or want to be)
- She may offer lots of unsolicited advice
- You may feel closer or more distant
- She may compare your pregnancy to hers
- Boundary-setting becomes important

## Managing the Relationship {#section-2}

- Set boundaries early (what advice you do/don't want)
- Acknowledge her excitement — she's becoming a grandmother
- Share what you're comfortable sharing
- Remember her advice comes from love, even if it's annoying
- Your pregnancy, your choices

## FAQ {#faq}

### My mum keeps giving me unwanted pregnancy advice. What do I do?

Set boundaries gently. Say 'I know you're trying to help, but I've got advice from my midwife and I'm following their guidance.' Acknowledge her love but stick to your choices.

### Is it normal to feel closer to my mum during pregnancy?

Yes. Many women feel a new connection with their own mum during pregnancy. You're joining the 'motherhood club' and may understand her in a new way.
