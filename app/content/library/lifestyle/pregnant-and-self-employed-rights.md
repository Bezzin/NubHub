# Pregnant and Self-Employed: Your Rights

If you're self-employed and pregnant, you have different rights and options. Here's what you need to know.

## Maternity Allowance

Self-employed women can claim Maternity Allowance (MA) instead of Statutory Maternity Pay. MA pays £172.48/week for 39 weeks (or 90% of earnings if lower). You need to have paid National Insurance for 26 of the 66 weeks before your due date.

## Other Support

You may also be entitled to: Universal Credit, Healthy Start vouchers, free prescriptions and dental care, and the Sure Start Maternity Grant if on benefits.

## FAQ

### Can I get maternity pay if self-employed?

Yes. Self-employed women can claim Maternity Allowance (£172.48/week for 39 weeks) instead of Statutory Maternity Pay. You need 26 weeks of NI contributions in the 66 weeks before your due date.

### Do I have to stop working when self-employed and pregnant?

No. You can work for as long as you want. There's no legal requirement to stop. However, take into account your health and energy levels. Plan for time off after the birth.
