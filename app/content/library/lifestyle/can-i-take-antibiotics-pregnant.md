# Can I Take Antibiotics When Pregnant?

Can you take antibiotics when pregnant? Here's what you need to know.

## Quick Answer

**Antibiotics in pregnancy:** Some antibiotics are safe in pregnancy (e.g., penicillins, cephalosporins). Others should be avoided (e.g., tetracyclines). Always tell your doctor you're pregnant when prescribed antibiotics.

## Is Antibiotics Safe in Pregnancy? {#is-it-safe}

Some antibiotics are safe in pregnancy (e.g., penicillins, cephalosporins). Others should be avoided (e.g., tetracyclines). Always tell your doctor you're pregnant when prescribed antibiotics.

Always consult your GP, midwife, or pharmacist before taking ANY medication during pregnancy. This includes over-the-counter medicines, herbal remedies, and supplements.

## NHS Guidance {#nhs-guidance}

The NHS advises that you should always check with a healthcare professional before taking medication in pregnancy. Some medicines can harm your baby, while others are perfectly safe.

## Alternatives {#alternatives}

If antibiotics is not recommended, ask your GP or pharmacist about pregnancy-safe alternatives. There are often alternative treatments that are safe during pregnancy.

## Frequently Asked Questions {#faq}

### What if I already took antibiotics before knowing I was pregnant?

Don't panic. Taking a small amount of most medications before you knew you were pregnant is unlikely to cause harm. Contact your GP or midwife for reassurance and mention it at your booking appointment.

### Can I buy antibiotics over the counter while pregnant?

Always tell the pharmacist you're pregnant before buying any medication. They can advise whether it's safe and suggest alternatives if needed.
