# Compression Socks for Pregnancy Travel

Compression socks reduce swelling and DVT risk during travel in pregnancy.

## Table of Contents

1. [Do You Need Them?](#s1)
2. [Choosing Socks](#s2)
3. [FAQ](#faq)

## Do You Need Them? {#s1}

For flights over 4 hours, compression socks are recommended in pregnancy. They're also helpful for long car or train journeys. Look for graduated compression of 15-20mmHg.

## Choosing Socks {#s2}

- Medical-grade graduated compression
- Correct size (measure your ankle and calf)
- Comfortable but snug
- Put them on before you start travelling

## FAQ {#faq}

### Are compression socks safe in pregnancy?

Yes. Graduated compression socks are safe and recommended for long-distance travel in pregnancy. They reduce swelling and blood clot risk. Look for 15-20mmHg compression.
