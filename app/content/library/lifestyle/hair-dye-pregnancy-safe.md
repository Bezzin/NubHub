# Hair Dye in Pregnancy: Is It Safe?

## How Hair Dye Works {#section-1}

Hair dye contains chemicals that penetrate the hair shaft or coat the surface to change colour. Permanent dyes use stronger chemicals, while semi-permanent and temporary dyes are gentler. Some women worry that these chemicals could be absorbed through the scalp and reach the baby. The amount absorbed through the skin during normal hair dye use is very small, which is why most guidance considers occasional use to be low risk.

## What the Guidance Says {#section-2}

The NHS and most UK health professionals consider hair dye safe to use during pregnancy, particularly after the first 12 weeks. Many women choose to wait until the second trimester as a precaution, since the first trimester is a critical period of development. If you work in a salon and are exposed to hair dye regularly, good ventilation is important to minimise inhalation of fumes.

## Reducing Your Risk {#section-3}

If you decide to colour your hair, there are steps you can take to minimise exposure. Consider highlights or balayage, where dye does not touch the scalp. Use semi-permanent or vegetable-based dyes, which tend to be gentler. Always do a patch test first, as pregnancy hormones can make your skin more sensitive. Apply dye in a well-ventilated room and leave it on for the minimum recommended time.

## Alternatives and Natural Options {#section-4}

If you prefer to avoid chemical dyes altogether, there are natural alternatives. Henna is a popular choice, though check the ingredients as some products marketed as henna contain other chemicals. Vegetable-based dyes and tinted shampoos offer a temporary colour refresh without permanent chemicals. Some women simply embrace their natural colour during pregnancy, knowing they can return to their usual routine after birth.

## FAQ {#faq}

### Can I dye my hair in the first trimester?

Most guidance considers hair dye low risk throughout pregnancy, but many women wait until after 12 weeks as a precaution. Speak to your midwife if you are concerned.

### Are highlights safer than all-over colour?

Highlights involve less scalp contact, which reduces chemical absorption. They are often considered a lower-risk option during pregnancy.

### Is henna safe during pregnancy?

Pure henna is generally considered safe, but check labels as some products contain added chemicals. Always do a patch test first, as pregnancy can increase skin sensitivity.
