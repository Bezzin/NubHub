# Can I Take Antidepressants When Pregnant?

Can you take antidepressants when pregnant? Here's what you need to know.

## Quick Answer

**Antidepressants in pregnancy:** Some antidepressants are safe in pregnancy. Sertraline is often considered the safest SSRI. NEVER stop antidepressants suddenly - always consult your GP.

## Is Antidepressants Safe in Pregnancy? {#is-it-safe}

Some antidepressants are safe in pregnancy. Sertraline is often considered the safest SSRI. NEVER stop antidepressants suddenly - always consult your GP.

Always consult your GP, midwife, or pharmacist before taking ANY medication during pregnancy. This includes over-the-counter medicines, herbal remedies, and supplements.

## NHS Guidance {#nhs-guidance}

The NHS advises that you should always check with a healthcare professional before taking medication in pregnancy. Some medicines can harm your baby, while others are perfectly safe.

## Alternatives {#alternatives}

If antidepressants is not recommended, ask your GP or pharmacist about pregnancy-safe alternatives. There are often alternative treatments that are safe during pregnancy.

## Frequently Asked Questions {#faq}

### What if I already took antidepressants before knowing I was pregnant?

Don't panic. Taking a small amount of most medications before you knew you were pregnant is unlikely to cause harm. Contact your GP or midwife for reassurance and mention it at your booking appointment.

### Can I buy antidepressants over the counter while pregnant?

Always tell the pharmacist you're pregnant before buying any medication. They can advise whether it's safe and suggest alternatives if needed.
