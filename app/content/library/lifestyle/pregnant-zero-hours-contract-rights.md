# Pregnant on a Zero-Hours Contract: Your Rights

Being on a zero-hours contract doesn't mean you have no rights when pregnant. Here's what you're entitled to.

## Your Rights

You still have: the right to a risk assessment, protection from discrimination, the right to maternity leave, and potential entitlement to Statutory Maternity Pay (if you meet the earnings and length of service requirements).

## Maternity Pay

If you don't qualify for SMP (due to low earnings or short service), you may qualify for Maternity Allowance. Contact the Jobcentre Plus to claim.

## FAQ

### Can I get maternity pay on a zero-hours contract?

Yes, if you meet the requirements. You need 26 weeks of service by the 15th week before your due date and average earnings of at least £123/week. If not, claim Maternity Allowance.

### Can my employer stop giving me shifts because I'm pregnant?

No. That's discrimination. Your employer cannot reduce your shifts or treat you differently because you're pregnant. If this happens, contact ACAS.
