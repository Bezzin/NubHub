# Exercise in Pregnancy: What's Safe

Exercise is generally safe and beneficial during pregnancy. Here's what you need to know about exercising safely in the first trimester.

## NHS Guidelines {#nhs-guidelines}

The NHS recommends at least 150 minutes of moderate exercise per week during pregnancy. This can be: walking; swimming; pregnancy yoga; Pilates; low-impact aerobics. If you exercised before pregnancy, you can usually continue at a similar level.

## Safe Exercises (First Trimester) {#safe}

Safe exercises include: walking; swimming; cycling (stationary bike is safest); pregnancy yoga; Pilates; light weights; low-impact aerobics; gentle stretching. Always listen to your body and stop if you feel unwell.

## Exercises to Avoid {#avoid}

Avoid: contact sports; scuba diving; exercises on your back (after 16 weeks); high-impact sports; horse riding; gymnastics; skiing; hot yoga (overheating risk). If you have any pregnancy complications, consult your midwife before exercising.

## Modifying Your Workout {#modify}

In the first trimester: reduce intensity if you feel nauseous; drink plenty of water; avoid overheating; eat before exercise (low blood sugar worsens nausea); stop if you feel dizzy; listen to your body.

## When to Stop Exercising {#stop}

Stop exercising and contact your midwife if: you experience bleeding; severe pain; dizziness; calf pain; shortness of breath; contractions; or your waters break.

## Frequently Asked Questions {#faq}

### Can I run in early pregnancy?

Yes, if you were a runner before pregnancy, you can usually continue. However, slow down if you feel nauseous or tired. Listen to your body. If you weren't a runner before, start with walking instead.

### Can I do HIIT or CrossFit while pregnant?

If you did these before pregnancy, you can often continue with modifications. Reduce intensity, avoid overheating, and always listen to your body. If you're new to exercise, start gentler.
