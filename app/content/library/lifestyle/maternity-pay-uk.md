# Maternity Pay UK: Complete Guide

Understanding maternity pay in the UK can be confusing. Here's a clear breakdown of what you're entitled to and how to claim it.

## Table of Contents

1. [Statutory Maternity Pay (SMP)](#smp)
2. [Maternity Allowance](#ma)

4. [Additional Support](#additional)

## Statutory Maternity Pay (SMP) {#smp}

SMP is paid by your employer. You get: 90% of average weekly earnings for the first 6 weeks; £172.48/week (or 90% of earnings if lower) for the next 33 weeks. Total: 39 weeks. To qualify, you must have worked for your employer for 26+ weeks by the 15th week before your due date.

## Maternity Allowance {#ma}

If you don't qualify for SMP (e.g., self-employed or recently changed jobs), you may get Maternity Allowance. This pays £172.48/week or 90% of earnings (whichever is lower) for 39 weeks. Claim through the Jobcentre Plus.

## When to Claim {#when-claim}

Claim SMP through your employer at least 15 weeks before your due date. Claim Maternity Allowance as soon as you can - you can claim from 26 weeks pregnant.

## Additional Support {#additional}

You may also be entitled to: Healthy Start vouchers (free food and milk); Sure Start Maternity Grant (£500 one-off payment if on benefits); Universal Credit; free prescriptions and dental care; tax credits.

## Frequently Asked Questions {#faq}

### How much is maternity pay in the UK?

SMP is 90% of your average earnings for the first 6 weeks, then £172.48/week (or 90% if lower) for 33 more weeks. Total 39 weeks. Maternity Allowance is £172.48/week for 39 weeks if you don't qualify for SMP.

### When should I tell work about maternity leave?

You must tell your employer at least 15 weeks before your due date. Give them your MAT B1 certificate (from your midwife at 20 weeks) to claim SMP.
