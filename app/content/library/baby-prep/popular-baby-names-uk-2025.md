# Popular Baby Names UK 2025

Baby name trends shift each year, influenced by culture, royalty, and social media. Whether you want a name that is on-trend or one that stands apart from the crowd, here is a look at popular baby names in the UK for 2025, based on ONS data and emerging trends.

## Top girls' names in 2025 {top-girls-names}

Olivia, Amelia, and Isla have consistently topped the UK charts in recent years, and they remain strong in 2025. Rising names include Maeve, Elara, and Ivy, reflecting interest in short, vowel-rich names. Nature-inspired names like Willow, Hazel, and Florence continue to climb. Vintage revivals such as Arabella and Penelope are also popular.

## Top boys' names in 2025 {top-boys-names}

Muhammad, Noah, and Oliver remain at or near the top for boys. Rising names include Arthur, Leo, and Finlay, reflecting a love of traditional but soft-sounding names. Short names like Jax, Rex, and Kit are trending, as are surname-as-first-name choices like Hudson and Mason. Alfie and Freddie remain popular across the UK.

## Emerging trends {trends}

Several trends shape 2025 naming. Short names (Isla, Leo, Ava) are popular for ease and modernity. Vintage and revival names (Arthur, Florence, Elsie) continue to appeal. Gender-neutral names like Riley, Rowan, and Quinn are rising. Pop culture, from TV shows to influencers, continues to shape choices, while sustainability-inspired names (Willow, River, Sage) reflect broader values.

## How to use this list {how-to-use}

Use this list for inspiration, not restriction. If you want a unique name, check the ONS top 100 to avoid accidental popularity. If you love a popular name, consider a less common middle name. Remember that popularity varies by region and year, so a name in the top 10 nationally may be rare locally. Trust your instincts and choose a name that feels right for your family.

## FAQ {#faq}

### Where can I find official UK baby name data?

The ONS publishes annual baby name statistics for England and Wales, usually released in autumn. Scotland and Northern Ireland publish their own data.

### Are gender-neutral names becoming more popular?

Yes, names like Riley, Rowan, and Quinn are rising in the UK, reflecting broader cultural shifts.

### How often do baby name trends change?

Trends shift gradually, but top names like Olivia and Muhammad have stayed popular for years. Pop culture can spark faster changes.
