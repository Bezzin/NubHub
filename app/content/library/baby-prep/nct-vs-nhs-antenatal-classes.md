# NCT vs NHS Antenatal Classes

NHS and NCT are the two most common routes to antenatal education in the UK. Both have strengths, and many parents take both. This comparison helps you decide which fits your budget, learning style, and social needs.

## Cost comparison {cost-comparison}

NHS antenatal classes are free at the point of use. NCT classes are fee-paying, typically 200 to 300 pounds per couple with concessions available. If budget is tight, NHS classes cover the essentials at no cost. If you can afford it, NCT adds depth and a smaller group experience. Some parents use NHS classes as a baseline and NCT for the social network.

## Class size and format {class-size}

NHS classes are often larger and may be held in community centres or hospitals with 10-20 attendees. NCT groups are small, usually 6-8 couples, allowing more discussion and personal attention. NCT sessions are longer (15-20 hours) compared with NHS sessions (often 6-12 hours total). Both include partners, though NHS availability for partners varies by area.

## Content and teaching style {content}

NHS classes cover labour, pain relief, feeding, and baby care, led by midwives or health visitors. NCT covers similar topics but in more depth, with more discussion, practical activities, and emphasis on informed choice and postnatal wellbeing. NCT also places strong focus on partner involvement and building confidence through shared learning.

## Social benefits {social-benefits}

NCT's small, local groups are famous for creating lasting friendships. Many parents meet their closest parent friends through NCT. NHS classes can also spark connections, but larger, rotating groups make it harder. If a local support network matters to you, NCT is often the stronger route.

## FAQ {#faq}

### Can I do both NHS and NCT classes?

Yes, many parents do. They complement each other well and you get both free essentials and a social group.

### Are NHS classes as good as NCT?

NHS classes are excellent for factual content. NCT adds depth, partner focus, and social connection that many parents value.

### Which should I book first?

Book NCT early as popular courses fill up fast. NHS classes can be booked via your midwife around 28 weeks.
