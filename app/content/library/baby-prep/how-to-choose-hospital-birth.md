# How to Choose a Hospital for Birth

Choosing where to give birth is one of the biggest decisions in pregnancy. Most people in the UK give birth in an NHS hospital maternity unit, but not all hospitals offer the same experience. Here is how to compare your options and make an informed choice.

## Understand your options {understand-options}

In the UK you can usually choose between an obstetric-led labour ward, a midwife-led unit (MLU) within or beside a hospital, or a home birth. Your suitability depends on whether your pregnancy is low or higher risk. Your midwife will discuss options at your booking appointment and again around 28-36 weeks.

## Research local hospitals {research-hospitals}

Use the NHS Maternity Choices tool and the CQC (Care Quality Commission) website to check ratings for local hospitals. Look at their latest maternity inspection reports. The CQC rates services as outstanding, good, requires improvement, or inadequate. Also check intervention rates, such as caesarean and induction rates, which some hospitals publish.

## Book a maternity tour {maternity-tour}

Most NHS hospitals offer free maternity tours or open days. This is your chance to see the labour ward, midwife-led unit, and postnatal rooms. Pay attention to the facilities: birthing pools, partner staying overnight, visiting policies, and how the rooms feel. Come with questions prepared.

## Questions to ask on your tour {questions-to-ask}

Ask about their caesarean rate, induction rate, access to birthing pools, whether midwives provide continuity of carer, policies on partners staying overnight, pain relief options, and what happens if you need to transfer from MLU to labour ward. Also ask about postnatal support and breastfeeding help.

## FAQ {#faq}

### Can I choose any hospital in the UK?

You have a legal right to choose your maternity provider, but practical factors like distance and availability matter. Most people choose their nearest suitable unit.

### What if my nearest hospital has a poor CQC rating?

You can request to give birth at a different hospital. Discuss with your midwife, who can help arrange a transfer of care.

### Do I need to book a hospital tour in advance?

Yes, tours are popular and limited. Contact your chosen hospital's maternity unit early, ideally in the second trimester.
