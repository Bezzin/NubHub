# Second-Hand Baby Equipment: What's Safe

Buying second-hand can save hundreds of pounds. Here's what's safe to buy used and what to buy new.

## Safe to Buy Second-Hand

- Prams and pushchairs (check brakes, harness, and frame)
- Cots and cot beds (check they meet current safety standards)
- Baby clothes (wash before use)
- Toys (check for loose parts)
- Changing bags
- Baby monitors
- High chairs

## Buy New

- Car seats (hidden damage from accidents may not be visible)
- Mattresses (link to SIDS risk with old mattresses)
- Bottle teats (wear and tear)
- Breast pump parts that contact milk

## FAQ

### Can I buy a second-hand car seat?

It's not recommended. A car seat that's been in an accident may have hidden damage. If you must buy second-hand, only buy from someone you trust who can confirm it's never been in an accident. Check the expiry date — car seats expire.

### Is a second-hand mattress safe for a baby?

No. The NHS recommends a new mattress for each baby due to the link between old mattresses and SIDS. Buy a new mattress even if the cot is second-hand.
