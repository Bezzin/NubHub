# Baby Registry UK: How It Works

A baby registry — sometimes called a baby gift list or baby wish list — is a curated list of items you would like for your new baby that friends and family can shop from. While registries have long been common in the United States, they have grown steadily in popularity across the UK over the past several years, and many parents now find them a genuinely useful way to coordinate gifts and avoid ending up with three sets of muslin squares and no car seat.

## How a Baby Registry Works

You create a list of items you want or need, hosted on a registry platform or retailer website. You then share a link with family and friends — often via a baby shower invitation, WhatsApp group, or email. People can browse the list, choose an item within their budget, and either buy it directly through the platform or mark it as purchased so others know it has been claimed.

Some platforms allow contributors to pool money towards a single larger item (such as a pram or nursery furniture). Others focus on direct purchasing. Most send you notifications when something is bought so you know what to expect.

**Popular UK baby registry options include:**

- **John Lewis** — widely trusted in the UK; items purchased in-store or online; a reliable choice for friends and family less comfortable with newer platforms
- **Amazon** — large product range and convenient delivery; lets you add almost anything on Amazon to one list
- **Mamas & Papas** — good for nursery furniture and prams; dedicated baby retailer
- **Babylist** — US-originated but works well in the UK; the key advantage is that you can add items from any website to a single list, not just one retailer
- **My Gift List / Prezola** — UK-based platforms that let you combine items from multiple retailers and also include cash funds for experiences or bigger purchases

## What to Include on Your Baby Registry

The most useful registries mix essential big-ticket items with everyday consumables at various price points, so guests at all budget levels can find something suitable.

**Essentials — higher cost:**
- Car seat (always buy new; never second-hand for safety reasons)
- Pram or pushchair
- Next-to-me crib or Moses basket
- Crib mattress (new — important for safe sleep)
- Baby monitor

**Everyday essentials — lower cost:**
- Nappies and wipes (in multiple sizes — newborn nappies are only used for a short time)
- Muslins (you will want many more than you think)
- Fitted crib sheets
- Baby grows and sleepsuits in mixed sizes — include 0-3 months and 3-6 months, as newborn sizing is quickly outgrown
- Changing bag
- Swaddle blankets

**Useful extras:**
- Bouncy chair or rocker
- White noise machine
- Nursing pillow
- Baby bath or bath support
- Bottles and steriliser (useful even if you plan to breastfeed)

**Cash fund options:**
If there are larger items you would prefer to choose yourselves — nursery furniture, a specific pram, or a contribution towards childcare — a cash fund option on platforms like Prezola or Babylist lets people contribute to these without you committing to a particular product before the baby arrives.

## Tips for Setting Up Your Registry

- **Open it at around 20 weeks** — early enough to share with people planning baby shower gifts, but late enough that you have had your anomaly scan and feel more settled.
- **Include a range of price points** so that close family, distant relatives, and colleagues can all find something appropriate.
- **Be specific** — include links to the exact product you want rather than a generic category, especially for safety items like car seats where model and compatibility matter.
- **Don't over-edit yourself** — it can feel awkward to list items, but guests genuinely prefer knowing what you need to guessing.
- **Update it as things are purchased** — on most platforms this happens automatically, but check occasionally.

## FAQ

### Are baby registries common in the UK?

They are becoming increasingly common. While the concept was traditionally associated with the United States, many UK parents now use registries — particularly through John Lewis, Amazon, and multi-retailer platforms like Babylist. Baby showers have also become much more popular in the UK, which has helped normalise the registry concept.

### What should I put on my baby registry?

Focus on practical items you would actually buy for yourself: a car seat, pram, crib, mattress, clothes in various sizes, nappies, muslins, and feeding equipment. Include a spread of price points — some items under £20, some mid-range, and one or two higher-cost "group gift" items for people who want to contribute together. Avoid listing too many luxury items that you would not miss if you did not receive them.

### Is it rude to share a baby registry?

Not in the UK in 2024. Most guests actively prefer having a list to shopping blind. The key is to share it as a helpful suggestion rather than a demand — include it as a gentle note in your baby shower invite or share it when people ask what you need.

Always speak to your midwife or health visitor if you have questions about which baby equipment is safest for your situation.
