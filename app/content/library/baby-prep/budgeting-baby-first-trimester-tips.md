# Budgeting for Baby: First Trimester Tips

The first trimester is a great time to start financial planning for your baby. Here's what to do now.

## Table of Contents

1. [Start a Baby Fund](#s1)
2. [What to Research](#s2)
3. [FAQ](#faq)

## Start a Baby Fund {#s1}

Open a separate savings account for baby expenses. Even £20-50/month adds up. Set up a standing order so it's automatic.

## What to Research {#s2}

Look into: maternity pay entitlement, childcare costs in your area, essential baby items, second-hand options, and benefits you might qualify for.

## FAQ {#faq}

### When should I start buying baby things?

Start researching in the first trimester but wait until the second trimester to start buying. This gives you time to compare prices and find second-hand bargains. Don't leave it too late though!
