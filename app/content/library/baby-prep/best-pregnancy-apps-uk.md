# Best Pregnancy Apps UK Reviews

Pregnancy apps can track your baby's development, remind you of appointments, and offer daily tips. With so many options, it helps to know which are most accurate, useful, and aligned with UK maternity care. Here are reviews of the best pregnancy apps for UK parents.

## NHS-approved apps {nhs-apps}

The NHS Pregnancy and Baby Guide is a free, ad-free app with trusted content aligned to UK maternity care. It covers week-by-week development, appointment reminders, and what to expect at each stage. Because it is NHS-backed, the medical information reflects UK guidelines rather than US practice, which can differ on screening, appointments, and terminology.

## Popular week-by-week trackers {trackers}

Ovia Pregnancy Tracker and BabyCentre Pregnancy are popular for detailed daily updates, kick counters, and forums. They include images, size comparisons, and symptom tracking. BabyCentre is widely used in the UK and has an active community. Note that these apps are US-owned, so some medical content reflects US practice; cross-reference with NHS guidance.

## Apps for birth prep and wellbeing {wellbeing}

The Positive Birth Company app offers hypnobirthing-style tools, affirmations, and birth prep videos. Headspace and Calm have pregnancy-specific meditation packs for relaxation and sleep. Expectful is a dedicated mental health app for fertility, pregnancy, and postpartum, though it is US-based. These complement rather than replace NHS care.

## What to look for in a pregnancy app {what-to-look}

Choose apps that are evidence-based, ad-light, and privacy-respecting. Check that medical content aligns with UK guidance, especially on screening tests, medications, and birth options. Avoid apps that push premium features or use fear-based messaging. A good app should inform and reassure, not add stress.

## FAQ {#faq}

### Is the NHS pregnancy app free?

Yes, the NHS Pregnancy and Baby Guide is free, ad-free, and aligned with UK maternity guidelines.

### Are US pregnancy apps accurate for UK parents?

Mostly for development milestones, but medical content can differ. Always cross-reference with NHS.uk or your midwife.

### Do pregnancy apps sell my data?

Some free apps do. Check the privacy policy, especially if you are sharing sensitive health information.
