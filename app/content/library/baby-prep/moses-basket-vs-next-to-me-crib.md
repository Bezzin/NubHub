# Moses Basket vs Next-to-Me Crib: Which Is Better?

Choosing where your newborn will sleep is a big decision. Here's how moses baskets and next-to-me cribs compare.

The Lullaby Trust and NHS recommend that your baby sleeps in the same room as you for at least the first six months, in their own separate sleep space. Both a moses basket and a next-to-me (bedside) crib fulfil this requirement — but they work quite differently in practice. The right choice depends on your budget, your bed height, how you plan to feed, and how long you want the sleeping space to last.

## Moses Basket

A moses basket is a woven basket with handles, fitted with a firm mattress. It sits in a separate stand on the floor beside your bed.

**Pros:**
- Lightweight and portable — you can carry it from room to room, which is useful during the day so your baby naps near you wherever you are in the house.
- Generally cheaper than next-to-me cribs, with many available second-hand.
- Traditional and widely available — stands, dressings, and replacement mattresses are easy to find.
- The enclosed, cosy shape can feel reassuring to some newborns.

**Cons:**
- Baby outgrows it quickly — most babies are too large by around three to four months, or sooner if they are on the bigger side.
- You need to sit up and lean over to pick up or settle your baby in the night, which can be harder if you have had a caesarean section or stitches.
- The stand adds to the overall cost, and some stands are less stable than others — check for a wide, secure base.
- Less useful once your baby moves on from it, though some families use them for daytime naps into the second half of the first year.

**Safety note:** If you are buying a second-hand moses basket, always buy a new mattress. The mattress should be firm, flat, and fit the basket snugly with no gaps at the sides.

## Next-to-Me Crib

A next-to-me crib (sometimes called a bedside crib or co-sleeper crib) attaches or sits flush against your bed, with one side that can be lowered or removed. Your baby sleeps at the same level as you, within arm's reach.

**Pros:**
- Night feeds become significantly easier — you can reach your baby without getting out of bed, which many parents find transformative, especially in the early weeks.
- Particularly helpful after a caesarean section or a difficult birth, when getting in and out of bed repeatedly is painful.
- Lasts longer than a moses basket — most next-to-me cribs accommodate babies up to around five or six months.
- The open side means you can comfort your baby with a hand or a gentle shush without fully picking them up every time.
- Some models convert to a standalone crib or have rocking functions.

**Cons:**
- More expensive than moses baskets, particularly well-known branded models.
- Bulkier and less portable — you will not easily carry it room to room.
- Compatibility with your bed matters: the crib needs to adjust to the height of your mattress and sit flush without a gap. Check adjustability carefully before buying, especially if you have a divan, a high-sided bed frame, or a low platform bed.
- Takes up more floor space than a moses basket on its stand.

## Which Should You Choose?

There is no single right answer. If budget is a priority and you move around the house a lot during the day, a moses basket is a practical choice. If night feeds and easy access are your priority — especially if you are planning to breastfeed or recovering from a surgical birth — a next-to-me crib is likely to make your nights easier.

Some families use both: a next-to-me crib for night-time in the bedroom and a moses basket for daytime naps downstairs. Others borrow or buy a moses basket second-hand for a few months and then move to a cot.

Whatever you choose, always follow safe sleep guidance: firm, flat mattress; no pillows, duvets, or bumpers in the sleep space; baby always placed on their back to sleep.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is a next-to-me crib better than a moses basket?

For most parents, particularly those who are breastfeeding or recovering from a difficult birth, the side-access design of a next-to-me crib makes night feeds significantly easier and less disruptive. However, moses baskets are cheaper, more portable, and perfectly safe. Both options meet NHS safe sleep guidelines when used correctly with a firm, well-fitting mattress.

### How long does a baby use a moses basket?

Most babies use a moses basket for around three to four months before they outgrow it, though some larger babies may reach the weight or size limit sooner. Next-to-me cribs typically last until around five or six months. After this, most babies move into a full-size cot in their own room — though the NHS recommends keeping them in your room until at least six months old.

### Can I use a moses basket and a next-to-me crib?

Yes — many parents use both, with the next-to-me crib in the bedroom for nights and a moses basket downstairs for daytime naps. This gives you the best of both: easy night access and daytime portability. If budget is a concern, a moses basket alone is sufficient; a next-to-me crib alone is also a perfectly complete solution.

Always speak to your midwife or health visitor for personalised advice on safe sleep for your baby.
