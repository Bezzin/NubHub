# Right-Sided Pain in Early Pregnancy: Causes and Care

Pain on the lower right side in early pregnancy has a slightly longer list of possible causes than the left, because your appendix also lives on that side. In most cases the cause is benign, but right-sided pain warrants a careful check. This guide explains the common causes and the red flags you should never ignore.

## Benign Causes on the Right

Round ligament pain can affect the right side just as easily as the left - a sharp, brief twinge when you stand or roll over. A corpus luteum cyst on your right ovary can cause a dull ache. Constipation and trapped wind often collect on the right side of your bowel. All of these are normal in early pregnancy and usually ease with rest, hydration, and gentle movement.

## When Right-Sided Pain Needs Urgent Checks

Right-sided pain has two extra red flags: appendicitis and ectopic pregnancy. Appendicitis in pregnancy can present as pain near your belly button that moves to the lower right, with nausea and fever. An ectopic pregnancy in the right fallopian tube causes severe pain, bleeding, and dizziness. If you have severe or worsening pain, fever, bleeding, or shoulder tip pain, go to A&E or call 999.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can appendicitis happen during early pregnancy?**

Yes. Pregnancy does not protect you from appendicitis. If you have right-sided pain with nausea and fever, seek urgent care.

**Is right-sided twinge at 7 weeks normal?**

A brief twinge is likely round ligament pain. If it is severe, persistent, or paired with bleeding, contact your early pregnancy unit.

**How is right-sided pain assessed in pregnancy?**

Your midwife will examine you and arrange a scan and blood tests to rule out ectopic pregnancy and appendicitis.
