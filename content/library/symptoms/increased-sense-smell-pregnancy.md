# Increased Sense of Smell in Pregnancy

Suddenly able to smell things from miles away? An increased sense of smell (hyperosmia) is a classic early pregnancy sign. Here's why it happens and how to cope.

## Why Your Sense of Smell Heightens {#why}

A heightened sense of smell is one of the earliest pregnancy signs, often noticeable before a missed period:

- **hCG and oestrogen**: Rising hormones are thought to sharpen smell perception.
- **Evolutionary protection**: A heightened sense of smell may help pregnant women avoid spoiled or harmful foods.
- **Oestrogen effect**: Even small increases in oestrogen can dramatically affect olfactory sensitivity.
- **Nausea link**: Heightened smell often triggers or worsens morning sickness.

Exactly why this happens isn't fully understood, but it's very real — studies confirm pregnant women have increased smell sensitivity.

## Common Smell Triggers {#triggers}

Smells that often become unbearable in early pregnancy include:

- **Cooking meat** and fish
- **Garlic, onion and spices**
- **Coffee** (suddenly unappealing to many)
- **Cigarette smoke**
- **Perfume and strongly scented products**
- **Pet odours**
- **Bin and food waste**
- **Public transport smells**

## How to Cope With Smell Sensitivity {#cope}

- **Open windows**: Fresh air dilutes strong odours quickly.
- **Ask others to cook**: If kitchen smells are unbearable, let someone else prepare meals.
- **Choose cold foods**: Cold or room-temperature dishes release fewer odours.
- **Wash with unscented products**: Switch to fragrance-free toiletries and washing powder.
- **Carry a pleasant scent**: A hanky with a drop of lemon or peppermint oil can mask triggers.
- **Avoid known triggers**: Step away from smoke, strong perfume and pungent foods.
- **Keep bins empty**: Take out rubbish frequently to avoid build-up.
- **Use extractor fans**: Ventilate when cooking or cleaning.

## When to Seek Help {#when-help}

A heightened sense of smell itself isn't harmful. But talk to your GP or midwife if:

- Smell sensitivity is causing severe nausea or vomiting
- You can't keep food or fluids down
- You're losing weight or becoming dehydrated
- Smells are making daily life impossible

This could indicate hyperemesis gravidarum, which needs medical treatment.

## Frequently Asked Questions {#faq}

### Is a heightened sense of smell a sign of pregnancy?

Yes. Hyperosmia is one of the earliest and most common pregnancy signs, often starting before a missed period.

### When does smell sensitivity start in pregnancy?

Many women notice it from around week 5 or 6, when hCG levels begin rising rapidly. It often peaks between weeks 6 and 10.

### How long does the heightened sense of smell last?

For most women it eases after the first trimester, around weeks 12 to 14. Some women remain sensitive throughout pregnancy.
