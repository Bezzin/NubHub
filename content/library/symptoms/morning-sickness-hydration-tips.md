# Hydration Tips for Morning Sickness

Staying hydrated is one of the most important things you can do when morning sickness strikes, but it is also one of the hardest. This page explains why fluids matter and how to get enough when drinking feels impossible.

## Why Hydration Matters

Vomiting and poor fluid intake can quickly lead to dehydration, which in turn worsens nausea and can become a serious medical issue. Even mild dehydration can make you feel dizzier, more tired and more nauseous. In pregnancy your fluid needs are higher, so the gap between what you need and what you can manage can widen quickly. Recognising the signs of dehydration, such as dark urine, dizziness and a dry mouth, helps you know when to seek advice from your GP or midwife.

## Practical Ways to Stay Hydrated

Sip small amounts frequently rather than drinking a full glass at once, which can trigger vomiting. Try cold or room-temperature water, ice chips, diluted squash, herbal teas or clear soups. Some women find fizzy water easier to tolerate than still. If water itself is a trigger, try sucking ice cubes or eating water-rich fruits such as melon. An oral rehydration solution from a pharmacy is useful if you have been vomiting. If you cannot keep any fluids down for more than 12 hours, contact your GP or midwife promptly.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How much should I drink with morning sickness?**

Aim for around 1.5 to 2 litres a day, taken in small frequent sips rather than large amounts at once.

**Q: What if water makes me feel sick?**

Try ice chips, diluted squash, herbal tea, fizzy water or water-rich fruits such as melon.

**Q: When should I seek help for dehydration?**

If you cannot keep any fluids down for over 12 hours, or notice dark urine and dizziness, contact your GP or midwife promptly.
