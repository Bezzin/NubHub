# No Symptoms at 8 Weeks: What It Means

By 8 weeks, many women are in the thick of first-trimester symptoms — nausea, exhaustion, tender breasts, and frequent urination are all common. But some women reach eight weeks with little or nothing to show for it symptom-wise, and that can feel unsettling. If you are wondering whether having no symptoms at 8 weeks is a cause for concern, the honest answer is: usually not — but it depends on your individual situation.

## Is It Normal to Have No Symptoms at 8 Weeks? {#s1}

Yes, it can be entirely normal. Pregnancy symptoms are driven largely by rising hCG (human chorionic gonadotropin) and progesterone levels, and these vary considerably from person to person and pregnancy to pregnancy. Some women experience very little nausea, minimal fatigue, and barely any breast changes throughout their entire first trimester — and go on to have completely healthy pregnancies.

It is also worth knowing that symptoms are not a reliable indicator of pregnancy health. A woman with severe morning sickness is not having a "better" pregnancy than one who feels fine. The presence or absence of symptoms does not tell you whether your baby is developing normally.

If you have never had strong symptoms, and you have had a scan showing a heartbeat, the absence of symptoms is very unlikely to be cause for concern.

## The Important Distinction: Never Having Symptoms vs. Symptoms Disappearing {#s2}

This is the key question to ask yourself at 8 weeks:

**Have you never really had symptoms?** If your pregnancy has felt relatively symptom-light from the beginning, this is much less concerning. Some women simply don't get strong symptoms, and that is a normal variation.

**Did you have symptoms that have now suddenly stopped?** A noticeable, sudden disappearance of symptoms that were previously present is worth mentioning to your midwife. This is not always a sign of a problem — symptoms can naturally ease and fluctuate — but a sudden loss of all symptoms can occasionally accompany a missed miscarriage. Most of the time it doesn't, but it is worth a call or a scan for peace of mind.

If you are unsure which category you fall into, trust your instincts. You know your body. If something feels different or wrong, it is always reasonable to seek reassurance.

## When to Contact Your Midwife or GP {#s2}

Contact your midwife or call NHS 111 if:

- You had clear symptoms (nausea, tender breasts, fatigue) and they have all stopped suddenly and completely
- You are experiencing pain — particularly one-sided pain low in your abdomen — alongside a reduction in symptoms (this combination can occasionally indicate an ectopic pregnancy, which requires urgent attention)
- You have bleeding alongside a loss of symptoms
- You are feeling anxious enough that it is significantly affecting your daily life — your midwife can arrange an early reassurance scan

You do not need symptoms to feel confident your pregnancy is progressing. A scan is the only reliable way to check.

## What Can a Scan Tell You?

An early scan — whether through the NHS if you have a clinical concern, or privately as a reassurance scan — can confirm:

- Whether a gestational sac is present and correctly located in the uterus
- Whether a fetal pole (the early baby) is visible
- Whether a heartbeat is detectable (usually visible from around 6 to 7 weeks)

If you are 8 weeks pregnant and haven't had a scan yet, your NHS 12-week dating scan will be the first opportunity in the standard pathway. If you are concerned and want reassurance sooner, many private early pregnancy clinics offer reassurance scans from around £80 to £150, and do not require a GP referral.

## Symptoms That Come and Go

It is very common for symptoms to fluctuate during the first trimester — feeling dreadful one day and almost fine the next. This does not mean anything is wrong. Nausea in particular tends to follow an uneven pattern, influenced by what you have eaten, how much sleep you have had, time of day, and other factors. A good day is not a bad sign.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is no symptoms at 8 weeks normal?

It can be. Some women experience minimal pregnancy symptoms throughout the first trimester and have completely healthy pregnancies. The presence of symptoms is not a reliable indicator of whether a pregnancy is progressing well. If you have had a scan showing a heartbeat, no symptoms is very unlikely to indicate a problem. If you have not yet had a scan and are worried, speak to your midwife about arranging one.

### I felt sick last week but feel fine today — should I worry?

Symptoms fluctuating from day to day, or even within the same day, is very normal in early pregnancy. A single good day does not mean something has gone wrong. If symptoms disappear entirely for several days and you previously had strong symptoms, a check-in with your midwife is a reasonable step — but one good day is not cause for alarm.

### Can I have no symptoms and still have a healthy pregnancy?

Yes. Many women with symptom-light first trimesters go on to have entirely healthy pregnancies and babies. Symptoms are not a measure of how well your pregnancy is going. They reflect individual variation in hormone levels and sensitivity, not the health of your baby.

Speak to your midwife or GP if you are concerned about your symptoms — or lack of them — at any stage of pregnancy.
