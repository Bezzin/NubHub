# Fainting in Pregnancy: When to Worry

Fainting during pregnancy can be frightening, but it's not uncommon. Here's why it happens, what to do if you feel faint, and when to seek urgent medical help.

## Why Fainting Happens in Pregnancy {#why}

Fainting (syncope) in pregnancy is usually linked to changes in your cardiovascular system:

- **Low blood pressure**: Progesterone relaxes blood vessels, which can drop your blood pressure, especially when you stand up.
- **Low blood sugar**: Not eating enough due to nausea can cause hypoglycaemia.
- **Dehydration**: Vomiting and reduced fluid intake reduce blood volume.
- **Standing for too long**: Blood can pool in your legs, reducing flow to the brain.
- **Hot environments**: Heat causes blood vessels to widen, lowering pressure further.
- **Anaemia**: Low iron levels reduce oxygen delivery to the brain.

## What to Do If You Feel Faint {#what-to-do}

If you feel lightheaded or think you might faint:

1. **Sit or lie down immediately** — don't try to push through it
2. **Lower your head** between your knees if sitting, or lie on your left side
3. **Loosen tight clothing** around your neck and waist
4. **Get fresh air** — open a window or step outside
5. **Sip water** once you feel steady enough
6. **Eat something** if you think low blood sugar is the cause

Don't try to drive or operate machinery if you feel faint.

## When Fainting Is Concerning {#when-concerning}

Seek urgent medical advice — call 111 or your GP — if fainting is:

- **Repeated** or happening often
- **With chest pain**, palpitations or shortness of breath
- **With blurred vision** or a severe headache
- **After a fall** that caused injury, especially to your abdomen
- **With heavy bleeding** or abdominal pain

If you actually lose consciousness, even briefly, contact your midwife or GP the same day to rule out underlying causes such as anaemia, low blood pressure or heart issues.

## How to Prevent Fainting {#prevent}

- **Stand up slowly** from sitting or lying positions
- **Eat little and often** to keep blood sugar stable
- **Stay well hydrated** — aim for 6 to 8 drinks a day
- **Avoid standing for long periods**
- **Stay cool** in hot weather and avoid hot, stuffy rooms
- **Wear support tights** if advised, to help blood return from your legs
- **Rise slowly in the morning** — sit on the bed edge before standing

## Frequently Asked Questions {#faq}

### Is fainting normal in early pregnancy?

Fainting and near-fainting are fairly common thanks to low blood pressure and hormone changes. However, fully losing consciousness should always be discussed with your midwife or GP.

### Can fainting harm my baby?

A brief faint is unlikely to harm your baby. The bigger concern is injury from a fall. If you've fainted and hit your abdomen, contact your midwife or call 111 straight away.

### Should I call 111 if I faint in pregnancy?

Yes, if you fully lose consciousness, faint repeatedly, or have chest pain, bleeding or a fall, call 111 or seek urgent care. It's always better to be checked.
