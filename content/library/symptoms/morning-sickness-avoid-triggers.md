# Avoiding Morning Sickness Triggers

Much of managing morning sickness is avoiding the things that set it off. This page helps you identify your personal triggers and reduce your exposure to them.

- [Common Triggers to Watch For](#common-triggers-to-watch-for)

## Common Triggers to Watch For

Typical triggers include strong cooking smells, perfumes, cleaning products, stale air, very hot environments, flashing screens, strong flavours and going too long without eating. Every woman is different, so keeping a simple diary for a few days can help you spot your own patterns. Note what you were doing, eating or smelling just before nausea struck. Once you know your triggers, you can plan around them rather than being caught off guard. Many women are surprised by how specific their triggers are.

## Practical Ways to Reduce Exposure

Cook with the windows open or ask someone else to cook when possible. Switch to unscented toiletries and cleaning products. Avoid hot, crowded places when you can. Eat before you get hungry to prevent empty-stomach nausea. If a particular food smell is a problem, choose cold meals or prepare food in advance when you feel well. Small adjustments to your daily routine can significantly reduce how often and how severely nausea hits.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What are the most common morning sickness triggers?**

Strong smells, hot environments, going too long without food and certain perfumes or cleaning products are very common.

**Q: Should I keep a nausea diary?**

Yes, a few days of noting what preceded each wave of nausea can reveal your personal triggers clearly.

**Q: Can I avoid all triggers?**

Not entirely, but reducing exposure to the main ones can make a real difference to how often you feel sick.
