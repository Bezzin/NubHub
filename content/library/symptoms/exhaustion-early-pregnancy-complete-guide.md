# Extreme Tiredness in Early Pregnancy: Complete Guide

Extreme tiredness is one of the most common and debilitating first trimester symptoms. You're not being lazy — your body is literally building a human.

## Table of Contents

1. [Why You're So Tired](#s1)
2. [How to Cope](#s2)
3. [FAQ](#faq)

## Why You're So Tired {#s1}

Your body is: producing massive amounts of progesterone (which causes sleepiness), creating a new organ (the placenta), increasing blood volume by 50%, and supporting rapid cell division. No wonder you're exhausted.

## How to Cope {#s2}

Nap when you can. Go to bed earlier. Cut back on non-essential activities. Ask for help. Eat iron-rich foods. Stay hydrated. Accept that this is temporary — energy usually returns in the second trimester.

## FAQ {#faq}

### When does pregnancy exhaustion end?

For most women, extreme tiredness eases around weeks 12-14 as the placenta takes over hormone production. The second trimester is often called the 'honeymoon period' — enjoy it!

### Is it normal to need a nap every day in early pregnancy?

Yes. Many women need a daily nap in the first trimester. Your body is working incredibly hard. Listen to it and rest when you can.
