# Cramping in Early Pregnancy: Is It Normal?

Cramping in early pregnancy is very common and usually nothing to worry about. Here's how to tell when it's normal and when to seek help.

## Is Cramping Normal? {#is-it-normal}

Yes! Cramping in early pregnancy is very common. As your uterus begins to grow and your body adjusts to pregnancy, mild cramping is completely normal. Most women experience it at some point in the first trimester.

## What Causes Cramping? {#causes)

Common causes of normal cramping include:
- **Uterus expanding**: Your uterus is growing to accommodate the baby
- **Implantation**: When the embryo attaches to the uterine wall (around weeks 4-5)
- **Hormonal changes**: Pregnancy hormones cause ligaments to soften
- **Gas and bloating**: Digestion slows in pregnancy, causing gas pain
- **Round ligament pain**: As the uterus grows, supporting ligaments stretch

## When Cramping Is Concerning {#when-concerning}

Seek medical help if cramping is:
- **Severe** (doubling-you-over pain)
- **One-sided** (could indicate ectopic pregnancy)
- **Accompanied by heavy bleeding**
- **Accompanied by fever**
- **Accompanied by shoulder pain** (ectopic sign)

## How to Ease Cramping {#how-to-ease}

- Rest and relax
- Use a warm (not hot) water bottle
- Stay hydrated
- Try gentle stretching
- Take paracetamol if needed (safe in pregnancy)

## Frequently Asked Questions {#faq}

### Is cramping at 6 weeks normal?

Yes. Mild cramping at 6 weeks is very common as your uterus begins to grow. However, if cramping is severe, one-sided, or accompanied by bleeding, contact your GP immediately.

### When should I worry about cramping?

Worry if cramping is severe (not relieved by paracetamol), one-sided, or accompanied by heavy bleeding, fever, or shoulder pain. These could be signs of ectopic pregnancy or miscarriage.

Is period-like cramping normal in early pregnancy?

Yes. Many women describe early pregnancy cramping as similar to period cramps. This is usually caused by the uterus expanding and hormonal changes. It's very common and usually nothing to worry about.
