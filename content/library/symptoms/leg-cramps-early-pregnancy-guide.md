# Leg Cramps in Early Pregnancy

Leg cramps can occur in early pregnancy, though they're more common later. Here's what helps.

## Table of Contents

1. [Causes](#s1)
2. [Prevention and Relief](#s2)
3. [FAQ](#faq)

## Causes {#s1}

Changes in blood circulation, increased weight, magnesium deficiency, dehydration, and inactivity. Cramps often happen at night.

## Prevention and Relief {#s2}

Stretch your calves regularly, stay hydrated, try magnesium supplements (ask your GP), gentle exercise, and massage the cramping muscle.

## FAQ {#faq}

### Can I take magnesium for leg cramps in pregnancy?

Yes, magnesium is generally safe in pregnancy and may help reduce leg cramps. The NHS recommends 300mg/day. Talk to your GP before starting supplements.
