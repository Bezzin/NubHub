# Body Temperature Changes in Early Pregnancy

Feeling warmer than usual is a common early pregnancy symptom. Here's why it happens.

## Table of Contents

1. [Why You Feel Hotter](#s1)
2. [When to Worry](#s2)
3. [FAQ](#faq)

## Why You Feel Hotter {#s1}

Your basal body temperature rises after ovulation and stays elevated in early pregnancy due to increased progesterone. Your blood volume also increases, making you feel warmer.

## When to Worry {#s2}

A body temperature above 38C (fever) is not normal and needs medical attention, as high fever in early pregnancy can affect development. Feeling warm is fine; having a fever is not.

## FAQ {#faq}

### Is it normal to feel hot in early pregnancy?

Yes. Progesterone raises your body temperature and increased blood volume makes you feel warmer. This is normal. However, an actual fever (38C+) needs medical attention.

### Can a fever harm my baby in early pregnancy?

A high fever in early pregnancy may increase the risk of certain birth defects. If you have a fever above 38C, contact your GP. Paracetamol can help reduce fever safely.
