# No Symptoms at 7 Weeks Pregnant - Is Baby OK?

Seven weeks pregnant with no symptoms is one of the most common reasons women search for reassurance online. This is the week when symptoms are "supposed" to be in full swing - so feeling fine can feel worrying. Let's look at what's really going on.

## Is No Symptoms at 7 Weeks Normal? {#is-normal}

Yes - no symptoms at 7 weeks is normal, though it's less common than at 5 or 6 weeks. At this point, morning sickness has usually begun for most women who will experience it. However, a significant minority of women - **around 20%** - still have no noticeable symptoms at 7 weeks, and the majority of these go on to have healthy pregnancies.

The key question is whether you've never had symptoms (normal) or whether you had symptoms that have disappeared (worth investigating).

### If You've Never Had Symptoms

If you've felt fine throughout, this is likely just how your body responds to pregnancy hormones. You may be one of the 20-30% of women who never develop significant symptoms.

### If Your Symptoms Have Disappeared

If you previously had nausea, breast tenderness, or fatigue and they've vanished over a day or two, this is different from never having had them. Sudden symptom loss - especially with bleeding or cramping - warrants a call to your GP or early pregnancy unit.

## What's Happening with Your Baby at 7 Weeks {#baby-development}

Even with no symptoms, your baby is developing rapidly:

- **Size**: About 10mm - the size of a blueberry
- **Heartbeat**: Strong and regular, around 150-170 bpm (visible on scan)
- **Brain**: Developing rapidly, with new brain cells forming at a rate of 100 per minute
- **Limbs**: Arm and leg buds are growing and beginning to look less like paddles
- **Heart**: Now divided into left and right chambers
- **Digestive system**: Stomach and intestines beginning to form
- **Kidneys**: Beginning to develop
- **Facial features**: Eyes, nostrils, and mouth becoming more defined

This extraordinary development is happening regardless of whether you feel pregnant.

## Why You Might Not Have Symptoms {#why-no}

### Lower hCG Levels

hCG levels at 7 weeks can range from 7,650 to 229,000 mIU/mL. If your levels are at the lower end of the normal range, you may not experience nausea. Lower doesn't mean "too low" - it just means not high enough to trigger symptoms in your body.

### Hormone Insensitivity

Some women's bodies simply don't react strongly to pregnancy hormones. You may have perfectly healthy hCG, progesterone, and oestrogen levels but not experience the symptom cascade that other women do.

### Genetic Factors

Research has shown that morning sickness has a genetic component. If your mother or sister had little or no morning sickness, you're more likely to be the same.

### Later Implantation

If your embryo implanted a few days later than average, your hormone timeline is shifted. At 7 weeks by dates, your hormone levels may be more like a typical 6-week pregnancy, and symptoms may arrive in the next week.

### Being Further Along Than You Think

Conversely, some women who track based on ovulation rather than last period may actually be at a different gestational age than they think. An early scan can confirm dates.

## The Difference Between No Symptoms and Lost Symptoms {#difference}

This distinction is important:

### Never Had Symptoms
- **Common and normal** at 7 weeks
- Usually indicates lower hormone sensitivity
- Miscarriage risk is only very slightly elevated
- No need to worry unless there's bleeding or pain

### Had Symptoms That Disappeared
- **Less common and worth investigating**
- Can be normal (symptoms fluctuate), but can also indicate a problem
- If accompanied by bleeding, cramping, or sudden loss of breast tenderness, contact your GP
- An early scan can provide reassurance

If you're in the second category, read our guide on [symptoms disappearing at 9 weeks](/symptoms/symptoms-disappearing-9-weeks) - the principles apply at 7 weeks too.

## How to Get Reassurance at 7 Weeks {#reassurance}

### Early Reassurance Scan

At 7 weeks, a transvaginal scan can usually detect:
- A gestational sac
- A yolk sac
- A fetal pole
- A heartbeat

Seeing a heartbeat at 7 weeks reduces miscarriage risk to around 5% - a powerful reassurance. Private clinics offer scans from around £50-100. The NHS may scan early if you have bleeding, pain, or a history of loss.

### hCG Blood Tests

Two blood tests 48 hours apart can check that hCG is rising appropriately. It should roughly double in 48 hours. This can provide reassurance before a scan is possible.

### Progesterone Levels

A blood test can also check progesterone levels. A level above 25 ng/mL generally indicates a viable pregnancy.

### Wait for Your Booking Appointment

If you have no pain or bleeding, you may choose to wait for your NHS booking appointment (typically 8-12 weeks). At this appointment, your midwife will arrange your 12-week scan and begin your antenatal care.

## When to Contact Your Midwife {#when-contact}

Contact your GP, midwife, or NHS 111 if you experience:

- **Vaginal bleeding** - any amount, but especially red or heavy
- **Abdominal pain** - especially severe or one-sided (possible ectopic)
- **Shoulder pain** - can be a sign of ectopic pregnancy
- **Sudden disappearance of symptoms** that were present, alongside bleeding or pain
- **Dizziness or feeling faint**
- **Painful urination** (possible UTI, common in pregnancy)
- **Fever** (38°C or above)

## Frequently Asked Questions {#faq}

### Is it normal to have no symptoms at 7 weeks?

Yes. While 7 weeks is when many women have symptoms, around 20% have little to none. If you've never had symptoms and have no bleeding or pain, this is usually normal.

### Should I have a scan at 7 weeks if I have no symptoms?

A scan at 7 weeks can detect a heartbeat, which is very reassuring. If you're anxious, a private early reassurance scan is an option. The NHS may offer a scan if you have bleeding, pain, or a history of loss.

### Does no symptoms at 7 weeks mean miscarriage?

No. Most women with no symptoms at 7 weeks have healthy pregnancies. Miscarriage risk is only very slightly elevated compared to women with symptoms, and a scan showing a heartbeat provides far more useful information.

### I had nausea at 6 weeks but nothing at 7 weeks - should I worry?

Symptoms can fluctuate. Having a good day after feeling sick doesn't necessarily mean something is wrong. However, if all symptoms have completely disappeared and you're also experiencing bleeding or cramping, contact your GP.

### When should I tell people I'm pregnant if I have no symptoms?

This is a personal decision. Many people wait until after the 12-week scan. Having no symptoms doesn't change this timing - it's more about what feels right for you and your partner.
