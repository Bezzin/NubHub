# Cold Foods for Morning Sickness

When smells trigger nausea, cold foods can be a surprisingly effective option. This page explains why they help and which cold meals and snacks are worth keeping to hand.

## Why Cold Foods Are Easier

Heat intensifies the aroma of food, and strong smells are one of the most common nausea triggers in pregnancy. Cold foods give off much less odour, making them easier to face when you feel unwell. They also require no cooking, which removes the time spent near a hot stove where smells are strongest. Many women find they can tolerate a cold sandwich or salad when a hot meal would be impossible. Cold foods are also quicker to prepare, reducing the window of nausea during preparation.

## Good Cold Food Options

Try sandwiches with mild fillings such as cheese or chicken, salads with a simple dressing, cold pasta, yogurt with fruit, or crackers with a slice of cheese. Smoothies blended with yogurt and banana can be filling and low in odour. Avoid very strong cheeses or fishy fillings. Keeping a few cold options ready in the fridge means you can eat without having to prepare anything when nausea is at its worst, which makes a real difference.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Why do cold foods help morning sickness?**

They give off less aroma than hot foods, which reduces a common nausea trigger.

**Q: What cold foods are good to keep ready?**

Sandwiches, salads, yogurt, cold pasta and smoothies are all easy, low-odour options.

**Q: Are cold foods safe in pregnancy?**

Yes, as long as you follow standard pregnancy food safety, such as avoiding unpasteurised cheese and certain deli meats.
