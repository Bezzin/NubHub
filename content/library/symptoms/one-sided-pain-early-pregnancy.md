# One-Sided Pain in Early Pregnancy: What It Means

Feeling pain on one side of your lower abdomen in early pregnancy can be unsettling. In many cases, it is a normal part of your body adjusting to pregnancy. However, one-sided pain is also a classic warning sign of an ectopic pregnancy, so it always deserves attention. This guide helps you work out what is likely causing your pain and when to act.

## Benign Causes of One-Sided Pain

As your womb grows, the ligaments that support it stretch, causing sharp or aching pains on one or both sides - this is called round ligament pain. You may also feel a twinge from the ovary that released the egg, known as ovulation pain or a corpus luteum cyst. These are common, harmless, and usually settle with rest. Constipation and bloating, both frequent in early pregnancy, can also cause localised discomfort.

## When One-Sided Pain Is a Red Flag

Call your early pregnancy unit or 999 if the pain is severe, persistent, or accompanied by bleeding, shoulder tip pain, dizziness, or fainting. These are the classic signs of an ectopic pregnancy, where the embryo implants outside the womb - usually in a fallopian tube. Ectopic pregnancies are not viable and can be life-threatening if the tube ruptures. Prompt assessment with a scan and blood tests is essential.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is mild one-sided pain normal at 6 weeks?**

Yes, mild twinges are common as ligaments stretch and hormones change. If it is severe or paired with bleeding, seek help.

**How do I know if it is round ligament pain?**

Round ligament pain is usually sharp, brief, and triggered by movement like standing up quickly. It eases with rest.

**When should I go to A&E for one-sided pain?**

Go to A&E or call 999 if the pain is severe, you have bleeding, shoulder tip pain, dizziness, or you feel faint.
