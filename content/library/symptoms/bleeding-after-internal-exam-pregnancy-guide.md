# Bleeding After an Internal Exam in Pregnancy

Internal exams - whether for a smear test, a cervical check, or a midwife assessment - can cause light spotting in pregnancy. Your cervix is softer and more vascular than usual, so even gentle contact can release a small amount of blood. This guide explains what is normal and when to pick up the phone.

## What Happens During an Internal Exam

An internal exam involves a speculum or gloved fingers examining your cervix and vagina. In pregnancy, the increased blood flow means the tissues are delicate. A swab, a cervical smear, or even a gentle palpation can cause a tiny blood vessel to leak. The result is usually pink or brown spotting within a few hours of the exam.

## Caring for Yourself Afterwards

Use a panty liner to monitor the amount and colour. The spotting should settle within a day. Avoid intercourse and tampons until the bleeding has stopped. If the spotting turns to bright red bleeding, you pass clots, or you develop pain or fever, call your midwife or early pregnancy unit the same day.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is spotting after a cervical smear normal in pregnancy?**

Yes. Your cervix is more sensitive, so light spotting after a smear is common and usually settles within 24 hours.

**Can an internal exam cause a miscarriage?**

No. A gentle internal exam does not affect your pregnancy or harm your baby.

**Should I cancel my smear test if I am pregnant?**

Smears are usually postponed until after pregnancy, but if you have one, do not worry - light spotting afterwards is normal.
