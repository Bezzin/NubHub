# Hair Changes in Early Pregnancy: Complete Guide

Pregnancy hormones can dramatically change your hair. Here's what to expect.

## Table of Contents

1. [Thicker Hair](#s1)
2. [Other Changes](#s2)
3. [FAQ](#faq)

## Thicker Hair {#s1}

Oestrogen extends the hair growth phase, meaning less hair falls out. This is why many pregnant women notice thicker, fuller hair by the end of the first trimester.

## Other Changes {#s2}

Some women notice their hair becomes shinier, changes texture (straight becomes curly or vice versa), or grows faster. These changes are temporary and caused by hormones.

## FAQ {#faq}

### Will my hair go back to normal after pregnancy?

Yes. About 3 months after birth, the hair that didn't fall out during pregnancy will shed. This is called postpartum hair loss and is normal. Your hair will return to its pre-pregnancy state.
