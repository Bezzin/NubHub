# Vegan Pregnancy Diet: Is It Safe?

A vegan pregnancy can be entirely safe and healthy, but it requires more careful planning than a diet that includes animal products. The nutrients most at risk on a vegan diet, vitamin B12, iron, omega-3, iodine and calcium, are all obtainable from plant sources or supplements with the right approach. Here is what to focus on.

## Vitamin B12 Is Non-Negotiable {#vitamin-b12-is-non-negotiable}

Vitamin B12 is found naturally only in animal products, so vegans must get it from fortified foods or supplements. During pregnancy, low B12 can affect your baby nervous system development. Choose fortified plant milks, fortified nutritional yeast and breakfast cereals, and take a reliable B12 supplement. Do not rely on unfortified plant foods for B12, as they contain none.

## Iron and Zinc on a Vegan Diet {#iron-and-zinc-on-a-vegan-diet}

Vegan iron sources include lentils, tofu, quinoa, fortified cereals and leafy greens. Pair them with vitamin C-rich foods to maximise absorption, and keep tea and coffee away from meals. Zinc comes from pumpkin seeds, chickpeas, cashews and fortified cereals. Soaking and sprouting legumes and grains can reduce phytates that otherwise reduce mineral absorption.

## Calcium and Iodine {#calcium-and-iodine}

Many plant milks are fortified with calcium, but check the label as not all are. Tofu set with calcium, fortified cereals and leafy greens help. Iodine is often overlooked and is important for your baby brain development. Use iodised salt if available, or include a small amount of seaweed occasionally, but avoid kelp supplements which can provide excessive amounts.

## Omega-3 and Supplements {#omega-3-and-supplements}

Plant omega-3 from flaxseed, chia and walnuts is less efficient than the DHA from fish. A pregnancy-safe algae DHA supplement is the most reliable option for vegans. Alongside this, take 400mcg folic acid and 10mcg vitamin D, and consider a pregnancy multivitamin designed for vegans that includes B12, iodine and choline.

## FAQ {#faq}

### Is a vegan pregnancy safe without supplements?

A vegan pregnancy without B12 and DHA supplementation is risky. Plan your diet carefully and speak to your midwife or a dietitian about appropriate supplements.

### Can I get enough protein as a vegan in pregnancy?

Yes. Lentils, beans, tofu, tempeh, quinoa and nuts provide ample protein. Eat a variety across the day and include a source at each meal.

### Should I switch from vegan during pregnancy?

There is no need to abandon a vegan diet if it is well planned, but some women choose to reintroduce foods for convenience. This is a personal decision best made with professional advice.
