# Montgomery Glands in Pregnancy

Those small bumps on your areolas are Montgomery glands, and they become more prominent during pregnancy.

## Table of Contents

1. [What Are Montgomery Glands?](#s1)
2. [When They Appear](#s2)
3. [FAQ](#faq)

## What Are Montgomery Glands? {#s1}

Montgomery glands (or Montgomery tubercles) are small sebaceous glands on the areola that secrete an oily substance to lubricate and protect the nipple. They become more visible during pregnancy.

## When They Appear {#s2}

Many women notice Montgomery glands in early pregnancy as one of the first signs. They look like small pimples on the areola and are completely normal.

## FAQ {#faq}

### Are Montgomery glands a sign of pregnancy?

They can be. Enlarged Montgomery glands are an early pregnancy sign for some women. However, they can also be visible outside pregnancy, so they're not a definitive sign.
