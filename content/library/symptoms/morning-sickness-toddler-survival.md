# Morning Sickness and a Toddler: Survival Guide

Coping with morning sickness is hard enough on its own, but doing it while chasing a toddler around the house is a particular kind of exhausting. When you cannot just rest on demand, survival becomes about realistic shortcuts, low-effort meals, and leaning on every bit of help available.

## Lower Your Standards {#lower-your-standards}

This is a season for getting through, not for thriving. A day where the children are fed, safe and loved is a successful day, even if the house is a mess, the laundry is piled up, and dinner is toast. Give yourself permission to do the minimum and let non-essentials slide. Your toddler will not remember a week of simple meals and extra screen time, but they will benefit from a less stressed parent.

## Low-Effort Food for Both of You {#low-effort-food-for-both-of-you}

Stock up on easy snacks that work for both you and your toddler: crackers, bread, cheese sticks, fruit, yoghurt, rice cakes and plain biscuits. Batch-cook simple meals such as pasta, soup or rice dishes when you feel well and freeze portions. Accept that some days dinner will be cereal or a sandwich, and that is fine. Keep a snack box at toddler height so they can help themselves to safe options while you rest.

## Rest When You Can {#rest-when-you-can}

Lie down whenever your toddler naps or has quiet time, even if you do not sleep. Set up activities they can do beside you on the sofa, such as sticker books, drawing or a favourite programme. Use a stair gate to create a safe play area so you can rest nearby without constant chasing. If someone offers help, say yes immediately, whether it is a playdate, a cooked meal or taking your toddler to the park.

## Protecting Your Milk Supply If Breastfeeding {#protecting-your-milk-supply-if-breastfeeding}

If you are still breastfeeding your toddler, nausea can make feeding sessions tough. Stay well hydrated, snack before and after feeds, and try positions that let you lie back. Some women find breastfeeding triggers nausea more strongly; if so, shortening feeds or distracting your toddler with a snack or drink alongside can help. Speak to your midwife if nausea is severe, as medication can make a big difference.

## FAQ {#faq}

### Is morning sickness worse with a second baby?

It can be, as you cannot rest as much, but the severity itself is not necessarily greater. Some women find it easier the second time, others worse.

### How do I explain feeling sick to my toddler?

Keep it simple: Mummy tummy feels a bit poorly and needs to rest. Toddlers often accept this and can be surprisingly gentle and patient.

### Should I wean my toddler during pregnancy?

Not necessarily, unless you want to or there are medical reasons. Continue if it is working for you both, but do seek support if nausea makes feeding very difficult.
