# How Much Weight Should You Gain in First Trimester?

Wondering how much weight you should be putting on in the first 12 weeks? First trimester weight gain is usually modest. Here's what's normal, what affects it, and when to talk to your midwife.

## How Much Weight Gain Is Normal? {#how-much}

Most women gain very little weight in the first trimester — typically between 0.5 and 2 kilograms (1 to 4 pounds). Some women gain nothing at all, and others actually lose a little due to nausea and reduced appetite. This is all perfectly normal.

The bulk of pregnancy weight gain happens in the second and third trimesters, when your baby grows rapidly. In the first 12 weeks, your baby is still tiny, weighing only a few grams by the end of week 12.

## What Affects First Trimester Weight Gain {#what-affects}

Several factors influence how much you gain early on:

- **Morning sickness**: Nausea and vomiting often reduce weight gain or cause mild loss.
- **Appetite changes**: Hormones can suppress or increase hunger.
- **Starting weight**: Women with a lower pre-pregnancy BMI may gain more quickly.
- **Fluid retention**: Hormonal changes can cause mild bloating and water weight.
- **Multiple pregnancy**: Twins or more mean faster weight gain.
- **Diet and lifestyle**: Eating patterns and activity levels play a role.

## Weight Gain by Pre-Pregnancy BMI {#by-bmi}

General guidance for total pregnancy weight gain, based on your pre-pregnancy BMI:

- **Underweight (BMI under 18.5)**: 12.5 to 18 kg over the whole pregnancy
- **Healthy weight (BMI 18.5 to 24.9)**: 11.5 to 16 kg
- **Overweight (BMI 25 to 29.9)**: 7 to 11.5 kg
- **Obese (BMI 30 or above)**: 5 to 9 kg

In the first trimester, the gain is typically minimal — around 0.5 to 2 kg for most women regardless of starting BMI. Your midwife will track your weight at antenatal appointments and advise what's right for you.

## When to Talk to Your Midwife {#when-talk}

Speak to your midwife or GP if:

- You're gaining weight very rapidly (more than expected)
- You're losing weight and can't keep food down
- You feel dizzy, weak or dehydrated
- You're worried about your weight for any reason

Your midwife is there to support you, not judge you. Honest conversations help them give you the best care.

## Frequently Asked Questions {#faq}

### Is it normal to not gain weight in the first trimester?

Yes. Many women gain little or no weight in the first 12 weeks, especially with morning sickness. Your baby is tiny at this stage and needs only a small amount of extra nutrition.

### Can you gain too much weight in early pregnancy?

Rapid weight gain in the first trimester is uncommon but can happen. If you're concerned, speak to your midwife — they can advise on healthy eating without unnecessary restriction.

### When does most pregnancy weight gain happen?

Most weight gain occurs in the second and third trimesters, when your baby, placenta and amniotic fluid grow significantly. Expect to gain around 0.4 kg per week in the second trimester if you started at a healthy weight.
