# Linea Nigra in Pregnancy: Complete Guide

Linea nigra is the dark vertical line that appears on your belly during pregnancy. Here's everything you need to know.

## Table of Contents

1. [What Is Linea Nigra?](#s1)
2. [When It Appears](#s2)
3. [FAQ](#faq)

## What Is Linea Nigra? {#s1}

Linea nigra (Latin for 'black line') is a darkened line running from your belly button to your pubic bone. It's caused by increased melanin production due to pregnancy hormones.

## When It Appears {#s2}

Linea nigra typically appears around weeks 10-12, though some women notice it earlier or later. It's more visible in women with darker skin.

## FAQ {#faq}

### Will linea nigra go away after birth?

Yes. Linea nigra fades after pregnancy, usually within a few months. It may not disappear completely in all women, especially those with darker skin.

### Does linea nigra predict gender?

No. The old wives' tale that linea nigra going only to the belly button means girl and past it means boy is a myth. Linea nigra is caused by hormones, not gender.
