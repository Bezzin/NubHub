# No Symptoms at 5 Weeks Pregnant - Is That Normal?

You've had a positive pregnancy test, you're five weeks pregnant, and you feel... completely normal. No nausea, no sore breasts, no exhaustion. If you've been frantically Googling "no symptoms at 5 weeks," take a breath. You're in very good company.

## Is No Symptoms at 5 Weeks Normal? {#is-normal}

Yes, it is completely normal to have no symptoms at 5 weeks. In fact, **most women don't have significant symptoms at this stage.** Morning sickness typically doesn't begin until week 6, and many women don't feel anything until weeks 7-8.

At 5 weeks, your hCG levels are still relatively low. They've only been rising for about a week since implantation. The hormone levels needed to trigger noticeable symptoms haven't been reached yet for many women.

### What's Typical at 5 Weeks

Some women do experience mild symptoms at 5 weeks, including:
- Mild fatigue
- Slight breast tenderness
- Heightened smell
- Frequent urination
- Mild cramping (as the uterus begins to grow)
- Light spotting (implantation bleeding)

But many women feel absolutely nothing - and both are normal.

## Why You Might Not Feel Pregnant Yet {#why-not}

Several factors explain the absence of symptoms at 5 weeks:

### Low Hormone Levels

At 5 weeks, hCG is typically between 18 and 7,340 mIU/mL - a huge range. Symptoms are more likely at higher levels. Your hCG may simply not be high enough yet to cause nausea or other symptoms.

### Late Implantation

If the embryo implanted a little later (which is normal and common), your hormone rise is delayed by a few days. This means symptoms will start a bit later too.

### Individual Hormone Sensitivity

Some women are less sensitive to hormonal changes. Your body may be processing hCG, progesterone, and oestrogen without triggering the symptom cascade that other women experience.

### Your Body Has Done This Before

Women who've been pregnant before may notice symptoms earlier because they know what to look for. First-time mums often miss subtle early signs.

## When Symptoms Usually Start {#when-start}

Here's the typical timeline for when common symptoms appear:

| Symptom | Typical Onset |
|---------|--------------|
| Fatigue | Week 5-6 |
| Breast tenderness | Week 5-6 (can be earlier) |
| Nausea/morning sickness | Week 6-7 |
| Heightened smell | Week 6-7 |
| Food aversions | Week 6-8 |
| Frequent urination | Week 6-8 |
| Mood swings | Week 6-8 |

As you can see, 5 weeks is right at the very beginning of the symptom window. Most symptoms haven't even started yet.

## What's Happening Inside at 5 Weeks {#inside}

Even if you feel nothing, there's a lot going on:

- **The embryo is the size of a sesame seed** - about 2mm long
- **The neural tube** (which will become the brain and spinal cord) is forming
- **The heart is beginning to develop** and may start beating this week
- **The placenta is starting to form**
- **The amniotic sac** is developing around the embryo
- **hCG is being produced** and is detectable in your urine (which is why your test was positive)

Your body is doing extraordinary work, even if you can't feel it.

## When to Seek Reassurance {#when-reassurance}

While no symptoms at 5 weeks is normal, contact your GP or midwife if:

- **You have bleeding** that's heavier than spotting
- **You have severe pain**, especially one-sided
- **You have a history of ectopic pregnancy** and are experiencing any pain
- **You have a history of recurrent miscarriage** and want early monitoring
- **You feel generally unwell** with fever or other concerning signs

### When a Scan Can Help

At 5 weeks, a scan may not show much - the embryo is too small. Most early pregnancy units prefer to scan at 6-7 weeks when a heartbeat can be detected. If you're worried, your GP can arrange blood tests to check hCG levels are rising appropriately.

## How to Handle the Wait {#how-to-wait}

The period between a positive test and feeling pregnant can be an anxious limbo. Here are some tips:

- **Don't symptom-spot obsessively**: It can drive you mad and doesn't predict pregnancy health
- **Start taking folic acid** (400mcg daily, or 5mg if prescribed) if you haven't already
- **Book your midwife appointment**: Contact your GP to get the referral process started
- **Eat well and rest**: Even without symptoms, your body is working hard
- **Avoid alcohol and smoking**: Even before symptoms appear, these can affect development
- **Share the news selectively**: Tell your partner or a close friend, but you may want to wait before wider announcements
- **Limit Google**: The internet will tell you both that no symptoms is fine AND that it means miscarriage. Both can't be true - trust that no symptoms at 5 weeks is normal

## Frequently Asked Questions {#faq}

### Should I be worried if I have no symptoms at 5 weeks?

No. Most women don't have significant symptoms at 5 weeks. Morning sickness typically starts at week 6. The absence of symptoms at this stage doesn't indicate a problem with your pregnancy.

### I had symptoms at 4 weeks but they've gone at 5 weeks - is that OK?

Mild symptoms like breast tenderness can fluctuate in the early weeks. Hormones rise in waves, not steadily, so symptoms can come and go. However, if all symptoms have completely disappeared along with bleeding or cramping, contact your GP.

### Can I get a scan at 5 weeks?

Most early pregnancy units won't scan before 6-7 weeks because the embryo is too small to see clearly. If you have pain or bleeding, they may scan to rule out ectopic pregnancy. Otherwise, it's best to wait until at least 6 weeks.

### Does no symptoms at 5 weeks mean low hCG?

Not necessarily. Your hCG could be perfectly normal and rising as it should, but just not high enough to cause symptoms yet. The only way to know your hCG level is through a blood test, which your GP can arrange if there's cause for concern.

### I'm 5 weeks and feel fine - should I enjoy it?

Yes! If you feel fine, enjoy it. Eat well, rest, and take care of yourself. Symptoms may arrive in the next week or two, or they may never arrive. Either way, you're pregnant and that's wonderful.
