# Acne in Early Pregnancy

Noticed a breakout now you're pregnant? Acne in early pregnancy is common thanks to hormonal changes. Here's why it happens, what's safe to use, and how to care for your skin.

## Why Pregnancy Causes Acne {#why}

Pregnancy acne is driven largely by hormones:

- **Androgens rise**: Pregnancy increases androgen hormones, which boost sebum (oil) production in your skin.
- **More oil = more spots**: Excess sebum clogs pores, leading to blackheads, whiteheads and inflamed spots.
- **Hormone surges**: The first trimester sees the steepest hormone increases, making breakouts common.
- **Stress and fatigue**: Poor sleep and stress can worsen acne.
- **Diet changes**: Cravings for sugary or refined foods may contribute.

Breakouts often appear on the chin, jawline and cheeks, and can range from mild to quite severe.

## Safe Treatments for Pregnancy Acne {#safe-treatments}

Some acne treatments are safe in pregnancy:

- **Gentle cleansers**: Wash twice daily with a mild, fragrance-free cleanser.
- **Benzoyl peroxide**: Often considered safe in low concentrations — check with your pharmacist.
- **Glycolic acid**: A mild exfoliant that's generally pregnancy-safe.
- **Niacinamide**: Helps reduce inflammation and redness without risk.
- **Oil-free moisturiser**: Keeps skin hydrated without clogging pores.
- **Salicylic acid (low strength)**: Small amounts in over-the-counter products are often considered safe, but check with your pharmacist.

## Treatments to Avoid {#avoid}

Some acne treatments are **not safe** in pregnancy:

- **Retinoids** (isotretinoin, tretinoin, adapalene) — linked to birth defects
- **High-dose salicylic acid** peels or strong concentrations
- **Doxycycline** and other tetracycline antibiotics
- **Hormonal treatments** like spironolactone
- **Chemical peels** with strong acids

Always check with your GP, midwife or pharmacist before using any new acne product in pregnancy.

## Skincare Tips for Pregnancy Breakouts {#tips}

- **Cleanse twice daily** with a gentle, non-abrasive cleanser
- **Don't over-scrub**: Harsh scrubbing irritates skin and worsens acne
- **Use oil-free, non-comedogenic products**: Look for these labels on moisturisers and makeup
- **Avoid touching your face**: Hands transfer bacteria and oil
- **Change pillowcases regularly**: They harbour oil and bacteria
- **Eat a balanced diet**: Limit sugary, processed foods that may worsen breakouts
- **Stay hydrated**: Water helps skin stay healthy
- **Be patient**: Pregnancy acne often improves as hormones settle in the second trimester

## Frequently Asked Questions {#faq}

### Is acne a sign of early pregnancy?

Yes. Hormonal breakouts, especially around the chin and jaw, can be an early pregnancy sign. They're not diagnostic on their own, though.

### When does pregnancy acne start?

Many women notice breakouts in the first few weeks of pregnancy as hormones surge. It often peaks in the first trimester.

### Will pregnancy acne go away?

For most women, acne improves in the second trimester as hormone levels stabilise. Some women experience breakouts throughout pregnancy.
