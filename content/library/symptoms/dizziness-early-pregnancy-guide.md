# Dizziness in Early Pregnancy: Complete Guide

Feeling lightheaded or woozy in the first trimester? Dizziness is a common early pregnancy symptom. Here's a complete guide to why it happens, how to manage it, and when to get help.

## Why Dizziness Happens {#why}

Dizziness in early pregnancy is usually caused by your body adjusting to pregnancy:

- **Hormonal changes**: Progesterone relaxes blood vessels, which can lower blood pressure and reduce blood flow to the brain.
- **Lower blood sugar**: Nausea and reduced eating can cause blood sugar dips.
- **Increased blood volume**: Your body is producing more blood, which can take time to adjust to.
- **Dehydration**: Vomiting and not drinking enough can leave you dehydrated.
- **Anaemia**: A drop in iron levels can cause dizziness and tiredness.

## Common Triggers {#triggers}

You might feel dizzy when:

- You stand up quickly from sitting or lying down
- You've gone too long without eating
- You're in a hot, stuffy room
- You're dehydrated
- You've been standing for a long time
- You get up too quickly in the morning

## How to Manage Dizziness {#manage}

Try these steps to reduce and cope with dizziness:

- **Stand up slowly**: Take your time going from sitting to standing.
- **Eat regularly**: Small, frequent meals keep blood sugar steady.
- **Stay hydrated**: Sip water throughout the day, especially if you've been sick.
- **Avoid hot environments**: Step outside for fresh air if a room feels stuffy.
- **Sit or lie down when dizzy**: Lowering your head between your knees or lying on your left side helps blood flow.
- **Wear layers**: Overheating makes dizziness worse, so dress in layers you can remove.
- **Don't skip meals**: Even a cracker helps prevent blood sugar dips.

## When to Seek Help {#when-help}

Contact your GP or midwife, or call 111, if dizziness is:

- **Severe** or persistent
- **Accompanied by fainting**
- **With blurred vision or a headache**
- **With chest pain or palpitations**
- **With bleeding or abdominal pain**

These could be signs of anaemia, low blood pressure, dehydration or something more serious.

## Frequently Asked Questions {#faq}

### Is dizziness a sign of early pregnancy?

Yes. Dizziness can be an early pregnancy symptom due to hormonal changes and lowered blood pressure. Some women notice it before they even test positive.

### When does dizziness in pregnancy usually start?

It can begin as early as week 4 to 6 and is often most noticeable in the first trimester as your body adjusts to pregnancy hormones.

### Should I lie down when I feel dizzy?

Yes. Lying on your left side improves blood flow back to your heart and brain. If you can't lie down, sit down and lower your head between your knees.
