# Pregnancy-Safe Herbal Teas: Complete List

Herbal teas can be a comforting drink in pregnancy, but not all are safe. Here's what's OK and what to avoid.

## Safe Teas (in moderation)

- Peppermint tea (may help nausea)
- Ginger tea (excellent for morning sickness)
- Rooibos tea (caffeine-free)
- Fruit teas (strawberry, raspberry, etc.)
- Chamomile tea (in moderation — 1-2 cups/day)

## Avoid

- Licorice tea (may affect baby's development)
- Sage tea (may stimulate contractions)
- Parsley tea (may stimulate contractions)
- Pennyroyal tea (toxic in pregnancy)
- Yarrow tea

## FAQ

### Is ginger tea safe in pregnancy?

Yes. Ginger tea is one of the safest and most effective teas for morning sickness. Up to 3-4 cups per day is safe. Limit ginger supplements to 1g/day.

### Is chamomile tea safe in pregnancy?

In moderation (1-2 cups/day), chamomile is generally considered safe. Some sources recommend avoiding it entirely in pregnancy due to limited safety data. When in doubt, ask your midwife.
