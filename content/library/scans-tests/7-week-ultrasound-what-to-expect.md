# 7-Week Ultrasound: What to Expect

A 7-week ultrasound is an early pregnancy scan that can confirm your pregnancy, check the heartbeat, and measure the baby. Here's what to expect.

## What You'll See at 7 Weeks

At 7 weeks, the ultrasound will show:
- The gestational sac
- The yolk sac
- The fetal pole
- Possibly a heartbeat (visible from around 6-7 weeks)
- The baby measuring approximately 10mm

## What the Sonographer Checks

The sonographer will:
- Confirm the pregnancy is in the uterus (rule out ectopic)
- Check for a heartbeat
- Measure the CRL (crown-rump length) to confirm gestational age
- Check the number of babies (singleton or twins)

## FAQ

### Can you see a heartbeat at 7 weeks?

Yes, in most cases a heartbeat is visible at 7 weeks. The heart rate is typically 120-160 bpm. If no heartbeat is seen, it may be too early or the dates may be off.

### Is a 7-week scan internal or external?

At 7 weeks, the scan may be transvaginal (internal) for better visibility, as the baby is still very small. Some clinics use abdominal ultrasound.

### What if there's no heartbeat at 7 weeks?

If there's no heartbeat at 7 weeks, the sonographer may ask you to return in 1-2 weeks for a repeat scan. It could be that the dates are wrong or it's simply too early.
