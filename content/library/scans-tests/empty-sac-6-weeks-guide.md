# Empty Sac at 6 Weeks: What Happens Next?

Finding out your gestational sac is empty at 6 weeks is worrying, but it may just be too early. Here's what you need to know.

## Why the Sac Might Be Empty at 6 Weeks

- It's simply too early - the fetal pole hasn't developed yet
- Your dates may be wrong
- In some cases, it may indicate a blighted ovum

## What Happens Next

You'll be asked to return for a repeat scan in 1-2 weeks. In many cases, the follow-up scan shows a fetal pole and heartbeat.

## FAQ

### Is an empty sac at 6 weeks normal?

Yes, it can be completely normal. At 6 weeks, it may simply be too early to see the fetal pole. A follow-up scan at 7-8 weeks usually shows development.

### What is a blighted ovum?

A blighted ovum is when the sac develops but no embryo forms. It's only diagnosed after follow-up scans confirm no development.
