# Bmi And Pregnancy What Need Know

BMI in pregnancy — what you need to know, what happens if your BMI is high or low, and your care options.

## Overview

Your BMI is calculated at your booking appointment. Here's what it means for your pregnancy.

## FAQ

### A BMI of 18.5-24.9 is considered normal. A BMI of 25-29.9 is overweight, and 30+ is obese. A high BMI may mean extra monitoring, a higher risk of complications (gestational diabetes, pre-eclampsia), and possibly a recommendation for aspirin. A low BMI may also need monitoring.

Will a high BMI affect my pregnancy care?

### If your BMI is 30+, you may be offered: extra growth scans, gestational diabetes screening, aspirin to prevent pre-eclampsia, and a consultation about birth options. Your midwife will create a personalised care plan.
