# Blood Tests in Early Pregnancy: What Gets Checked

A plain-English guide to the routine blood tests offered in early pregnancy in the UK, what each one looks for, and when to expect results.

## Which Blood Tests Are Offered in Early Pregnancy?

At your booking appointment, usually between 8 and 12 weeks, your midwife will offer a set of routine blood tests. These check your full blood count, blood group and Rhesus (RhD) factor, iron and haemoglobin levels, rubella immunity, syphilis and HIV status, and hepatitis B. Depending on your background and family history you may also be offered sickle cell and thalassaemia screening and a thyroid function test. None of these tests are compulsory, but they are strongly recommended because they pick up conditions that can affect you and your baby long before symptoms appear.

## Understanding Your Results

Most results come back within a week. Your midwife will flag anything outside the normal range and explain what it means for your care. A low haemoglobin level points to anaemia and may lead to a recommendation for iron supplements or dietary changes. Being Rhesus negative means you will be offered Anti-D injections to protect future pregnancies. Positive infection screening does not mean your pregnancy is in danger, but it does allow your team to plan treatment that dramatically reduces the risk of passing the infection to your baby. Ask for copies of your results so you can track changes across trimesters.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do I have to have all the blood tests in early pregnancy?**

No, every test is optional. Your midwife will explain what each one is for and you can accept or decline individually. Declining a test does not affect your antenatal care, but it may mean a condition goes undetected until it causes symptoms.

**Will the blood tests tell me if my baby is healthy?**

The routine booking bloods check your health rather than your baby's directly. They screen for infections and conditions that could affect the pregnancy. Combined with the 12-week scan, they give a fuller picture of how your pregnancy is progressing.

**Can I request extra blood tests privately?**

Yes. Many private clinics offer additional hormone panels such as hCG and progesterone tracking, thyroid antibodies, and vitamin D levels. These can be useful if you have had previous losses or are managing a known condition.
