# Can hCG Levels Predict Twins?

Whether high or fast-rising hCG can hint at a twin pregnancy before scan, and why only an ultrasound can confirm it.

## The Link Between hCG and Twin Pregnancies

Twin and higher-order multiples often produce more hCG because two placentas (or one large shared placenta) generate more of the hormone. Studies show that twin pregnancies have, on average, higher hCG levels at the same gestational age than singleton pregnancies. This is why some people with twins report stronger early symptoms, darker test lines, and earlier nausea. However, the overlap between singleton and twin ranges is enormous. A high hCG can simply be a healthy singleton, and a lower hCG can still be twins.

## Why Only a Scan Can Confirm Twins

No hCG number, however high, can diagnose twins. The only reliable method is ultrasound, which can show two gestational sacs, two fetal poles, and two heartbeats from around 6 to 7 weeks. Even a very early scan can miss a twin that is hiding behind its sibling, so a repeat scan at 7 to 8 weeks is sometimes needed. If you are curious about twins because of strong symptoms or a family history, ask for an early dating scan rather than relying on blood tests, which can be misleading and anxiety-inducing.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What hCG level suggests twins?**

There is no single number. Twin pregnancies average higher hCG, but the normal ranges overlap so much that no threshold can predict twins. Only a scan can confirm.

**Do higher hCG levels mean stronger pregnancy symptoms?**

Generally yes. Higher hCG is associated with more nausea and fatigue, which is why twin pregnancies often cause stronger symptoms. However, severe symptoms can also occur in singleton pregnancies.

**Can I find out I am having twins from a blood test?**

No. A blood test can show hCG is high, but cannot distinguish one baby from two. An ultrasound from around 6 to 7 weeks is the only way to confirm a multiple pregnancy.
