# Private Early Scan: Is It Worth It? A UK Guide

The wait between a positive pregnancy test and your first NHS scan at 12 weeks can feel endless. A private early scan, typically offered from 6 or 7 weeks, promises reassurance in that gap - but is it worth the money?

## What a Private Early Scan Involves

A private early scan usually takes place between 6 and 11 weeks. The sonographer will perform either a transvaginal or abdominal ultrasound to check for the pregnancy sac, a yolk sac, a fetal pole, and, from around 6-7 weeks, a heartbeat. The appointment typically lasts 15-30 minutes and includes printed photos.

## The Case For: Why It Might Be Worth It

If you have had a previous miscarriage, experienced bleeding or pain, or are simply anxious, seeing a heartbeat at 7 or 8 weeks can provide enormous relief. Research shows that once a heartbeat is confirmed at 8 weeks, the risk of miscarriage drops significantly. A private scan also lets you confirm the pregnancy is in the right place (ruling out ectopic pregnancy) and check whether you are carrying multiples.

## The Case Against: When It May Not Be Worth It

Before 6 weeks, there is often little to see, and you may be told to come back a week later - effectively paying twice. Even at 7 weeks, a heartbeat can be hard to detect if your dates are slightly off. If you are low-risk and not anxious, waiting for the NHS 12-week scan is perfectly reasonable and saves you money.

## Cost in the UK

Private early scans typically cost between 40 and 90, depending on the clinic and location. Some packages include a follow-up scan if it is too early to see anything.

## Questions to Ask Before Booking

- What is the earliest gestation the clinic will scan?
- Is the sonographer fully qualified and HCPC-registered?
- What happens if nothing can be seen - is a free rescan included?
- Can I bring my partner?
- Will the clinic contact my NHS midwife if a problem is found?

## What If They Find a Problem?

Reputable private clinics will refer you straight to your local NHS Early Pregnancy Unit if they detect a miscarriage, ectopic pregnancy, or other concern. They should not attempt to manage clinical problems themselves.

## Making Your Decision

There is no right or wrong answer. If reassurance will help you sleep at night and the cost is manageable, a private early scan can be a positive experience. If you are relaxed about waiting, the NHS 12-week scan will give you the same key information for free.

## Key Takeaways

- Private early scans run from around 6-11 weeks and cost 40-90.
- They are most valuable after a previous loss or if you have symptoms.
- Before 7 weeks, results can be inconclusive and a rescan may be needed.
- Always choose a clinic with qualified, registered sonographers.
- For low-risk pregnancies, the NHS 12-week scan is usually sufficient.
