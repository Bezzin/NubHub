# What Does an 8-Week Ultrasound Look Like?

By 8 weeks, your baby has grown significantly and the ultrasound image is much more interesting. You'll see a recognisable baby shape, a strong heartbeat, and possibly even some movement. Here's what to expect.

## What You'll See on Screen {#screen}

At 8 weeks, your baby is about 15-20mm long — roughly the size of a raspberry. On the ultrasound screen, you'll see:

- The **gestational sac**, now larger at about 27-30mm
- The **yolk sac**, still visible but starting to shrink
- Your **baby**, looking like a tiny curved bean with a large head and smaller body
- The **heartbeat**, a strong rapid flicker at 150-170 bpm
- **Limb buds**, small protrusions where arms and legs are forming
- Possibly **movement** — tiny twitches as the baby shifts

The image is much more recognisable than at 6 or 7 weeks. You can clearly see a baby shape, and most parents feel a real emotional connection at this stage.

## Your Baby's Shape at 8 Weeks {#shape}

At 8 weeks, your baby looks like a tiny curved creature — often described as looking like a **gummy bear, a kidney bean, or a comma**. The head is disproportionately large compared to the body, which is normal at this stage. The brain is developing rapidly, causing the head to grow faster than the rest of the body.

You may be able to see:

- **A large head**: Making up about half of the baby's total length
- **A curved spine**: Running from the head down to the tail
- **Limb buds**: Small buds where arms and legs are forming, starting to show signs of joints
- **A tail**: Yes, human embryos have a small tail at this stage! It will disappear as the baby grows.
- **The beginnings of facial features**: The eyes are forming, and the tip of the nose may be visible

## The Heartbeat at 8 Weeks {#heartbeat}

The heartbeat at 8 weeks is strong and easily visible. It appears as a **rapid flicker** in the chest area, beating at about **150-170 bpm**. This is near the peak heart rate for pregnancy — the rate will gradually slow down over the coming weeks.

The heartbeat is usually detectable on an abdominal scan at 8 weeks (though transvaginal is still clearer for some). Seeing the strong, steady flicker is one of the most reassuring moments of early pregnancy. The miscarriage risk drops to about 3-5% once a healthy heartbeat is confirmed at 8 weeks.

## Movement at 8 Weeks {#movement}

At 8 weeks, your baby can move, and you might see tiny movements on the scan. These look like small **spontaneous twitches** — the baby may shift position, wiggle a limb bud, or turn slightly. The movements aren't coordinated like later movements will be, but they're there.

You won't feel any of this movement — it's far too early for that. Most women don't feel their baby move until 16-24 weeks. But seeing it on screen is magical.

## What the Sonographer Measures {#measures}

At 8 weeks, the sonographer will:

- **Measure the crown-rump length (CRL)**: At 15-20mm, the CRL is accurate to about 5 days for dating.
- **Check the heartbeat**: Confirm it's present and measure the rate.
- **Check the gestational sac and yolk sac**: Ensure they're the right size.
- **Assess overall development**: Look at the baby's shape and growth.
- **Count the babies**: Twins are clearly visible by 8 weeks.
- **Confirm uterine location**: Rule out ectopic pregnancy.

## What a Normal 8-Week Scan Shows {#normal}

A normal 8-week scan typically shows:

- A gestational sac of about 27-30mm
- A yolk sac still present
- A fetal pole (baby) measuring 15-20mm CRL
- A strong heartbeat at 150-170 bpm
- Limb buds visible
- The pregnancy correctly located in the uterus
- Possibly some movement

If all of these are present, the scan is very reassuring. Your pregnancy is progressing well.

## How It Differs from 6 and 7 Weeks {#differences}

The 8-week scan is significantly more rewarding than scans at 6 or 7 weeks:

| Feature | 6 weeks | 7 weeks | 8 weeks |
|---------|---------|---------|---------|
| Baby size | 4-5mm | 8-11mm | 15-20mm |
| Shape | Smudge | Tiny comma | Baby-shaped bean |
| Heartbeat | Maybe | Usually yes | Strong and clear |
| Limb buds | No | Starting | Clearly visible |
| Movement | No | No | Possibly |
| Photo quality | Basic | Better | Good |
| Reassurance level | Low-moderate | Moderate-high | High |

If you're deciding when to book a private early scan, 8 weeks offers the best balance of early reassurance and a recognisable image.

## Frequently Asked Questions {#faq}

### Can I see the gender at 8 weeks?

No — it's far too early. Gender prediction via ultrasound isn't reliable until at least 16 weeks. At 8 weeks, external genitalia haven't developed.

### Will I get a good photo at 8 weeks?

Yes, most private clinics will give you a photo that shows a recognisable baby shape. It won't be detailed, but you'll be able to see the head, body, and limb buds. Some clinics offer digital images or short video clips.

### Is an abdominal scan possible at 8 weeks?

Yes, many clinics will try an abdominal scan at 8 weeks. If the view isn't clear, they'll switch to transvaginal. A full bladder can help with the abdominal view.
