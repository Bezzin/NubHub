# CRL (Crown-Rump Length) Explained

CRL stands for Crown-Rump Length. It is the measurement from the top of your baby's head (the crown) to the bottom of their buttocks (the rump), taken during an ultrasound scan. Because babies in the first trimester tend to curl up, the legs are not included. CRL is one of the most important early scan measurements because, in these early weeks, all babies grow at a very similar rate, making it a reliable guide to how far along you are.

## What CRL Tells You

Your sonographer uses CRL to work out several key things about your pregnancy:

- Determine your baby's gestational age (how many weeks pregnant you are)
- Confirm or adjust your estimated due date
- Check that your baby is growing as expected for this stage

CRL is taken by measuring your baby along their longest length while they are lying in a neutral, relaxed position. The sonographer may wait for your baby to move or take more than one measurement to get an accurate reading. The number is then compared against standard growth charts.

## Why CRL Matters for Your Due Date

In the first trimester, CRL is considered the most accurate way to date a pregnancy. This is because early growth is so consistent between babies that the measurement closely reflects gestational age. If your scan date differs from the date worked out from your last menstrual period, your due date is usually adjusted to match the CRL, as this is generally more reliable, particularly if your periods are irregular or you are unsure of your dates. Getting accurate dating early on also helps plan later scans and screening tests at the right time.

As pregnancy progresses beyond the first trimester, babies start to grow at slightly different rates, so CRL becomes less useful and sonographers switch to other measurements such as head circumference, abdominal circumference, and thigh bone length.

## What If the CRL Is Different From Expected?

A CRL that is a little ahead of or behind your expected dates is common and usually just means your due date is gently adjusted. Occasionally a sonographer may want to repeat a scan after a week or two to check growth, especially very early in pregnancy. Try not to read too much into a single number, as measurements have a normal margin and your maternity team will interpret it alongside everything else they see. If you have questions about your own scan report, your midwife or the sonographer can talk you through what your measurements mean for you.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What's a normal CRL at 12 weeks?

At around 12 weeks, the average CRL is approximately 55-60mm, although there is a normal range either side of this. Your sonographer will compare your baby's CRL to standard growth charts rather than rely on a single target figure, so a measurement slightly above or below is usually nothing to worry about.

### Can CRL predict the due date?

Yes. In the first trimester, CRL is the most accurate way to estimate your due date and is generally considered more reliable than calculating from your last menstrual period, especially if your cycle is irregular. If the scan date and your period date differ, your due date is usually based on the CRL.

### Is CRL used throughout pregnancy?

No. CRL is mainly used in the first trimester, when all babies grow at a similar rate. Later in pregnancy, babies grow at slightly different rates, so other measurements such as head, abdomen, and thigh bone length are used to monitor growth instead.

For advice tailored to your scan and circumstances, speak to your midwife or sonographer.
