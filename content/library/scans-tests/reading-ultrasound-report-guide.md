# Reading Your Ultrasound Report: A Guide for Mums

Your ultrasound report contains medical terms that can be confusing. Here's a plain English guide to what they mean.

## Common Terms on Your Scan Report

- **CRL (Crown-Rump Length)**: Measurement of your baby from head to bottom. Used to determine gestational age.
- **NT (Nuchal Translucency)**: Fluid measurement at the back of the baby's neck. Used for chromosomal screening.
- **GS (Gestational Sac)**: The sac that contains your baby and amniotic fluid.
- **YS (Yolk Sac)**: Provides nutrition to the embryo before the placenta takes over.
- **FH (Fetal Heart)**: Indicates whether a heartbeat was detected.
- **FHR (Fetal Heart Rate)**: The baby's heart rate in beats per minute.
- **EFW (Estimated Fetal Weight)**: Weight estimate (used later in pregnancy).

## FAQ

### What does 'intrauterine' mean on my report?

It means the pregnancy is in the correct location - inside the uterus. This rules out ectopic pregnancy.

### What does 'single live intrauterine pregnancy' mean?

This is good news. It means one baby, with a heartbeat, in the correct position in the uterus.
