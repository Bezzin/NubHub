# NIPT Blood Test: Complete Guide

NIPT (Non-Invasive Prenatal Testing) is a blood test that screens for chromosomal conditions and can determine your baby's sex. Here's everything you need to know.

## What Is NIPT? {#what-is-nipt}

NIPT analyses fragments of fetal DNA in your blood. It screens for Down syndrome (Trisomy 21), Edwards syndrome (Trisomy 18), and Patau syndrome (Trisomy 13). It can also determine fetal sex with 99%+ accuracy.

## When Can You Get NIPT? {#when}

NIPT can be done from 10 weeks of pregnancy. It's a simple blood test - no ultrasound needed for the test itself.

## How Accurate Is NIPT? {#accuracy}

NIPT is over 99% accurate for detecting Down syndrome. It's slightly less accurate for Edwards and Patau syndromes but still highly reliable. For sex determination, NIPT is 99%+ accurate.

## NIPT on the NHS {#nhs}

The NHS offers NIPT free for pregnancies identified as 'high risk' after combined screening. If you're low risk, you can still get NIPT privately.

## NIPT Cost UK {#cost}

Private NIPT costs between £150 and £500 in the UK. Common brands include Harmony, Panorama, MaterniT21, and SAFE NIPT. Some packages include sex determination; others focus only on chromosomal screening.

## NIPT vs Nub Theory {#vs-nub-theory}

NIPT is far more accurate than nub theory for gender (99%+ vs 80-95%). NIPT also screens for serious conditions, which nub theory doesn't. However, NIPT costs money while nub theory is free.

## Frequently Asked Questions {#faq}

### Is NIPT worth it if I just want to know the gender?

If your only goal is gender, nub theory at 12+ weeks is free and 80-95% accurate. NIPT at £150-500 for gender alone may not be worth it. However, NIPT also screens for serious chromosomal conditions, which many parents find valuable.

### How long do NIPT results take?

NIPT results typically take 7-10 working days. Some providers offer faster turnaround for an additional fee.
