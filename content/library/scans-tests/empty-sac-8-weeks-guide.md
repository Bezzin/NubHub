# Empty Sac at 8 Weeks: What It Means

An empty sac at 8 weeks is more likely to indicate a problem, as by 8 weeks a fetal pole and heartbeat should be visible.

## What It Means at 8 Weeks

At 8 weeks, an empty sac may indicate:
- A blighted ovum (anembryonic pregnancy)
- Missed miscarriage
- Less likely: incorrect dating

## What Happens Next

Your sonographer or doctor will arrange a follow-up scan to confirm. If the sac remains empty, you'll be given information about your options.

## FAQ

### Can an empty sac at 8 weeks still turn out OK?

It's possible but less likely than at 6-7 weeks. A follow-up scan will provide a definitive answer.

### What are my options if it's a blighted ovum?

You may choose natural management, medical management, or surgical management. Your doctor will explain all options.
