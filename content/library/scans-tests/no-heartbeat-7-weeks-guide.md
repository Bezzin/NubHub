# No Heartbeat at 7 Weeks: Should I Worry?

Finding out there's no heartbeat at your 7-week scan is frightening, but it doesn't always mean the worst. Here's what you need to know.

## Why There Might Be No Heartbeat at 7 Weeks

Common reasons for no heartbeat at 7 weeks:
- The dates may be wrong (you're earlier than 7 weeks)
- The baby is small and the heartbeat hasn't started yet
- The equipment or scan angle wasn't optimal
- In some cases, it may indicate a missed miscarriage

## What Happens Next

If no heartbeat is seen at 7 weeks, the sonographer will usually:
- Ask you to return in 1-2 weeks for a repeat scan
- Check your hCG levels with blood tests
- Possibly arrange a transvaginal scan for better visibility

## FAQ

### How common is no heartbeat at 7 weeks?

It's fairly common, especially if dates are uncertain. Many women who see no heartbeat at 7 weeks find one at their follow-up scan at 8-9 weeks.

### Should I prepare for the worst?

Not necessarily. The most common reason for no heartbeat at 7 weeks is incorrect dating. Wait for your follow-up scan before drawing conclusions.
