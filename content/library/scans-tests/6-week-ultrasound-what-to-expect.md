# What Does a 6 Week Ultrasound Look Like

Pregnancy brings countless questions, and understanding what each scan and sign means can help you feel more prepared. This guide breaks down the essentials in plain language so you know what to expect and what to ask your care team.

## What You Might See at 6 Weeks {#what-you-see}

At 6 weeks the baby is only a few millimetres long, so the scan focuses on confirming the pregnancy is in the right place and checking early development. You may see the gestational sac and yolk sac, and sometimes a tiny fetal pole. A heartbeat can sometimes be detected from around 6 weeks, though it is not always visible this early.

Understanding these basics helps you approach each appointment with realistic expectations and clearer questions for your sonographer or midwife.

## What Might Be Too Early to See {#too-early}

It is completely normal for a 6 week scan not to show a heartbeat yet, as timing can vary by a few days. If your dates are slightly off, the baby may measure smaller than expected. Missing details at 6 weeks does not mean something is wrong, and a follow up scan a week or two later usually gives a clearer picture.

Understanding these basics helps you approach each appointment with realistic expectations and clearer questions for your sonographer or midwife.

## FAQ {#faq}

### Should I see a heartbeat at 6 weeks?

Sometimes, but not always. It often appears by 6 to 7 weeks, and dates can shift this.

### Is a 6 week scan transvaginal?

Usually yes, as it gives a much clearer view at this early stage.

### What if the sac is empty at 6 weeks?

It can be too early to see anything inside. A repeat scan is normally recommended rather than assuming the worst.
