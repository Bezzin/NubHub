# NT Scan (Nuchal Translucency) Explained

The nuchal translucency (NT) scan is part of your 12-week screening. It measures fluid at the back of your baby's neck to assess the risk of chromosomal conditions. Here's what you need to know.

## What Is Nuchal Translucency? {#what-is-nt}

Nuchal translucency is a fluid-filled space at the back of every baby's neck. All babies have this fluid, but babies with chromosomal conditions (like Down syndrome, Edwards syndrome, or Patau syndrome) tend to have more fluid, making the measurement larger.

## What's a Normal NT Measurement? {#normal-nt}

A normal NT measurement is typically less than 3.5mm. Most babies measure between 1-3mm. A measurement over 3.5mm doesn't mean your baby definitely has a condition - it means further testing is recommended.

## What Happens If NT Is High? {#high-nt}

If your NT is above 3.5mm, you'll be offered: a consultation with a fetal medicine specialist; NIPT (non-invasive prenatal testing); or diagnostic tests (CVS or amniocentesis). Most babies with a high NT turn out to be perfectly healthy.

## Combined Screening Test {#combined-test}

The NT scan is combined with a blood test to give an overall risk assessment for Down syndrome, Edwards syndrome, and Patau syndrome. Results come as 'low risk' (less than 1 in 150) or 'high risk' (1 in 150 or higher).

## Frequently Asked Questions {#faq}

### What NT measurement is concerning?

A measurement above 3.5mm is considered increased. This doesn't mean your baby has a condition - it means further investigation is recommended. Many babies with increased NT are perfectly healthy.

### Can I refuse the NT scan?

Yes. All screening tests in pregnancy are optional. You can decline the NT scan and/or the combined screening. Your midwife will explain the options and respect your decision.
