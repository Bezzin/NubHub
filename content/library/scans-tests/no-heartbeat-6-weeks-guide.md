# No Heartbeat at 6 Weeks What It Means

Pregnancy brings countless questions, and understanding what each scan and sign means can help you feel more prepared. This guide breaks down the essentials in plain language so you know what to expect and what to ask your care team.

## Why a Heartbeat May Not Be Visible {#why-not-visible}

At 6 weeks a heartbeat is not guaranteed to be detectable. The most common reason is simply that the pregnancy is a few days less advanced than your dates suggest, especially if your cycles are irregular. Scan quality, baby position, and the type of probe used also affect whether a heartbeat can be seen.

Understanding these basics helps you approach each appointment with realistic expectations and clearer questions for your sonographer or midwife.

## What Usually Happens Next {#what-happens-next}

If no heartbeat is seen at 6 weeks, the usual advice is to wait and repeat the scan after about one to two weeks. This gives the baby time to grow and the heartbeat to become clearly visible. In most cases where dates were uncertain, a later scan shows a healthy heartbeat. Only after a repeat scan would further investigations be considered.

Understanding these basics helps you approach each appointment with realistic expectations and clearer questions for your sonographer or midwife.

## FAQ {#faq}

### Does no heartbeat at 6 weeks mean miscarriage?

Not usually. It often just means it is too early, and a repeat scan is the standard next step.

### When should the heartbeat definitely be visible?

By about 7 weeks a heartbeat is usually detectable, though dates can still vary.

### What should I do in the meantime?

Try not to panic. Follow your clinic's advice and attend the repeat scan as scheduled.
