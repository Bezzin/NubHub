# Private Scan vs NHS Scan: Key Differences Explained

When you discover you are pregnant in the UK, you will be offered routine scans on the NHS, but you may also see advertisements for private scans. Understanding the differences helps you decide whether to pay for an extra scan or stick with NHS care alone.

## What the NHS Provides

The NHS offers two routine ultrasound scans as standard: a **dating scan** between 8 and 14 weeks (usually around 12 weeks) and an **anomaly scan** at around 20 weeks. These are free at the point of use and are clinically focused, meaning they check your baby growth, development, and organs, and confirm your due date. You may also have additional NHS scans if your pregnancy is higher risk.

## What a Private Scan Offers

Private clinics offer scans at any point in pregnancy, including early reassurance scans from around 6 or 7 weeks, gender scans from 16 weeks, and 4D bonding scans later on. Appointments are typically longer (20-45 minutes), you can often bring family members, and you will usually receive printed photos or a USB video to take home. Some clinics offer weekend and evening slots.

## Cost Comparison

NHS scans are free. Private scans in the UK typically range from around 40 for a basic early scan to 150 or more for a 4D bonding package. Prices vary by location and what is included.

## Key Differences at a Glance

- **Timing**: NHS scans are fixed at roughly 12 and 20 weeks; private scans can happen any time.
- **Focus**: NHS scans are clinical and diagnostic; private scans often emphasise reassurance and bonding.
- **Duration**: NHS appointments are often 15-20 minutes; private appointments are usually longer.
- **Visitors**: NHS units often restrict companions to one; private clinics may welcome partners, children, and grandparents.
- **Output**: NHS may give one or two photos; private packages often include multiple prints, videos, and digital copies.
- **Reporting**: NHS scans feed directly into your maternity notes; private scans may advise you to share results with your midwife.

## Are Private Scans Safe?

Ultrasound is considered safe in pregnancy when used appropriately. Private clinics should be staffed by qualified sonographers, ideally registered with the **Health and Care Professions Council (HCPC)** or holding equivalent qualifications. Always check the clinic credentials before booking.

## When a Private Scan Makes Sense

A private scan can be worthwhile if you want early reassurance before your 12-week NHS scan, if you are anxious after a previous loss, if you want a gender reveal, or if you simply want a more relaxed, family-inclusive experience. Some women also use private scans to check growth between NHS appointments.

## When the NHS Is the Better Choice

For clinical screening, anomaly detection, and anything that may require follow-up care or referral, the NHS is the gold standard. A private scan cannot replace the diagnostic value and joined-up record-keeping of NHS maternity care.

## Key Takeaways

- NHS scans are free, clinical, and diagnostic at 12 and 20 weeks.
- Private scans offer flexibility, longer appointments, and bonding extras.
- Costs range from around 40 to 150+ for private packages.
- Always verify sonographer qualifications at private clinics.
- Many women use a mix of both for reassurance and clinical care.
