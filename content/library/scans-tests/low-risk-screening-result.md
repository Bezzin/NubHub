# What Does a Low Risk Screening Result Mean?

A lower risk screening result is reassuring news for most pregnant women. But what does it actually mean, and does it rule out all concerns? This guide explains what a lower risk result tells you, what it does not tell you, and what happens next.

## What a Lower Risk Result Means {#what-it-means}

A lower risk result means that, based on your screening test, the chance of your baby having a chromosomal condition such as Down syndrome, Edwards syndrome, or Patau syndrome is below the set threshold. In England, the threshold for Down syndrome is 1 in 150. A result of 1 in 5,000, for example, is considered lower risk. The higher the second number, the lower the chance. Most women receive a lower risk result.

## What It Does Not Rule Out {#not-ruled-out}

A lower risk result does not mean zero risk. Screening estimates a probability, it does not provide a yes or no answer. Rarely, a baby with a chromosomal condition can be missed by screening. A lower risk result also does not rule out other genetic conditions, structural problems, or conditions that develop later in pregnancy. The 20-week anomaly scan is an important check for many of these.

## Your Ongoing Care {#ongoing-care}

After a lower risk result, you continue with routine antenatal care. This includes your 16-week midwife appointment, the 20-week anomaly scan, and regular check-ups. If you had private NIPT and received a low chance result, the same applies. You do not need any further genetic testing unless a new concern arises.

## When to Seek Further Advice {#further-advice}

Contact your midwife if you notice reduced baby movements, unusual pain, bleeding, or any other concern — regardless of your screening result. These symptoms are not related to chromosomal conditions and need prompt assessment. If a structural concern is picked up at your 20-week scan, you may be offered further detailed scans and sometimes genetic testing.

## FAQ {#faq}

### Is a lower risk result completely accurate?

No screening test is 100% accurate. A lower risk result is very reassuring, but there is always a small chance a condition is missed. Your midwife can explain the detection rate for the test you had.

### Can I still have NIPT if my result is lower risk?

Yes, privately. Some women choose NIPT for additional reassurance even with a lower risk NHS result. Discuss the pros and cons with your midwife before paying for private testing.
