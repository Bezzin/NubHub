# When Is the Dating Scan in the UK

Pregnancy brings countless questions, and understanding what each scan and sign means can help you feel more prepared. This guide breaks down the essentials in plain language so you know what to expect and what to ask your care team.

## Standard Timing in the UK {#standard-timing}

In the UK the NHS dating scan is typically offered between 10 and 14 weeks of pregnancy, with most appointments falling around 12 weeks. The exact date depends on your midwife booking appointment, local trust availability, and how your due date is initially calculated from your last period.

Understanding these basics helps you approach each appointment with realistic expectations and clearer questions for your sonographer or midwife.

## What Can Affect Your Date {#affects-date}

Several things can shift your appointment earlier or later. If your cycles are irregular, your midwife may book the scan toward the later end of the window to be sure the baby is big enough to measure accurately. Local waiting times and clinic capacity also play a part, so some people are seen at 11 weeks and others closer to 14.

Understanding these basics helps you approach each appointment with realistic expectations and clearer questions for your sonographer or midwife.

## FAQ {#faq}

### Is 12 weeks the latest I can have it?

Most trusts aim to complete it by 14 weeks, as later measurements are less accurate for dating.

### What if I am not sure of my dates?

Your midwife will usually book toward the later end of the window and use the scan to confirm timing.

### Can I ask for an earlier date?

You can ask, but earlier slots are usually reserved for medical concerns.
