# Private Scan vs NHS Scan Differences

Pregnancy brings countless questions, and understanding what each scan and sign means can help you feel more prepared. This guide breaks down the essentials in plain language so you know what to expect and what to ask your care team.

## What NHS Scans Cover {#nhs-scans}

NHS scans in the UK are free and focus on medical screening. They include the dating scan, the combined screening test, and the 20 week anatomy scan. NHS sonographers prioritise checking your baby's health and development, and they do not routinely offer long bonding sessions, gender reveals, or keepsake photos beyond a basic image.

Understanding these basics helps you approach each appointment with realistic expectations and clearer questions for your sonographer or midwife.

## What Private Scans Add {#private-scans}

Private scans are paid and offer more flexibility. You can book early reassurance scans, gender scans from around 16 weeks, 4D bonding scans, and longer appointment times. Clinics often provide more photos, video clips, and a more relaxed setting. They do not replace NHS screening, but they can be a nice addition for parents who want extra scans.

Understanding these basics helps you approach each appointment with realistic expectations and clearer questions for your sonographer or midwife.

## FAQ {#faq}

### Do I still need NHS scans if I go private?

Yes. Private scans are extras and do not replace the medical screening the NHS provides.

### Are private scans medically checked?

Reputable clinics employ qualified sonographers, but always check their credentials before booking.

### How much do private scans cost?

Prices vary widely, from around 50 pounds for a basic early scan to several hundred for a 4D package.
