# What If Baby Measures Smaller Than Expected?

If your baby measures smaller than expected at your scan, it can be worrying. Here's what it means and what happens next.

## Why Baby Might Measure Small

Common reasons:
- Your dates are wrong (you're not as far along as you thought)
- Irregular menstrual cycles leading to miscalculated due date
- Small but healthy baby (normal variation)
- In some cases, growth issues

## What Happens Next

If baby measures small:
- The sonographer will adjust your due date based on the scan measurements
- You may be asked to return for a follow-up scan in 1-2 weeks
- In most cases, the baby catches up and is perfectly healthy

## FAQ

### Does measuring small mean miscarriage?

No. The most common reason for measuring small is incorrect dates. Your due date will be adjusted based on scan measurements.

### Will I need extra scans?

If your baby measures significantly small, you may have follow-up scans to monitor growth. In most cases, the baby is fine.
