# 8-Week Ultrasound: What to Expect

An 8-week ultrasound is a common time for an early scan. Here's what you can expect to see and what the sonographer will check.

## What You'll See at 8 Weeks

At 8 weeks, the ultrasound should show:
- A clear gestational sac and yolk sac
- The fetal pole with a visible heartbeat
- The baby measuring approximately 16-20mm
- Beginning of limb buds
- Facial features starting to form

## What the Sonographer Checks

The sonographer will:
- Confirm the heartbeat (normal rate 150-170 bpm)
- Measure CRL to confirm gestational age
- Check for multiples
- Confirm the pregnancy is intrauterine

## FAQ

### Is an 8-week scan internal?

It depends. Some clinics do transvaginal scans at 8 weeks for the clearest image. Others use abdominal ultrasound. Both are normal.

### What's the heart rate at 8 weeks?

Normal fetal heart rate at 8 weeks is approximately 150-170 bpm. This is much faster than an adult heart rate and is completely normal.
