# Nub Theory at Dating Scan vs NT Scan

Pregnancy brings countless questions, and understanding what each scan and sign means can help you feel more prepared. This guide breaks down the essentials in plain language so you know what to expect and what to ask your care team.

## The Dating Scan and Nub Theory {#dating-scan}

The dating scan usually happens between 10 and 14 weeks and is designed to confirm your due date and check basic development. Because it often falls within the 11 to 14 week window, many parents try to read the nub from these images. Readings at the earlier end of this range are less reliable, as the nub has not fully developed its angle yet.

Understanding these basics helps you approach each appointment with realistic expectations and clearer questions for your sonographer or midwife.

## The NT Scan and Nub Theory {#nt-scan}

The NT scan, which checks the nuchal translucency for chromosomal markers, typically happens between 11 and 14 weeks and tends to produce clearer side profile images. This often makes it a better source for nub theory readings than an earlier dating scan. The clearer the profile, the more meaningful the angle reading becomes.

Understanding these basics helps you approach each appointment with realistic expectations and clearer questions for your sonographer or midwife.

## FAQ {#faq}

### Which scan is better for nub theory?

Usually the NT scan, because it is timed within the ideal 12 to 14 week window and tends to give clearer side profile images.

### Can I use both scans?

Yes. Comparing readings from both can be fun, but remember neither is a confirmed gender result.

### Does the NHS let you know the nub angle?

No. NHS sonographers will not predict sex from the nub. Any reading is done by you or a private reader afterwards.
