# Feeling Hot in Early Pregnancy

Feeling unusually warm is a common early pregnancy symptom. Rising progesterone raises your core body temperature slightly, and increased blood flow adds to the sensation. For many it is one of the first hints before a positive test.

## Why You Feel Hot in Pregnancy {#why-you-feel-hot-in-pregnancy}

Progesterone raises the body's baseline temperature after ovulation, and it stays elevated in pregnancy. Increased blood volume and a faster metabolism also generate more heat. Blood vessels near the skin dilate, which is why you may feel flushed and warm.

## How to Stay Comfortable {#how-to-stay-comfortable}

Wear loose, breathable layers, stay hydrated, keep rooms cool, and avoid hot environments. Cool showers and a fan at night help. If you feel overheated with dizziness or a high fever, contact your GP, as fever in early pregnancy needs review.

## FAQ {#faq}

### Is feeling hot a sign of pregnancy?

It can be. A sustained raised temperature after ovulation is linked to progesterone in early pregnancy. But a missed period and test are more reliable.

### Does fever harm the baby?

A true fever above 38 degrees Celsius in early pregnancy may slightly raise certain risks, so always report a fever to your GP. Feeling warm without fever is normal.

### When does feeling hot stop?

Some people feel warm throughout pregnancy. It often eases in the second trimester as hormones stabilise, then may return late in pregnancy.
