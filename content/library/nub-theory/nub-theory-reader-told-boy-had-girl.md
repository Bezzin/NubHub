# Nub Theory Reader: Told Boy, Had a Girl

This is a real reader story where nub theory predicted a boy but the baby was a girl. Here's why it happened.

## Table of Contents

1. [The Story](#s1)
2. [Why It Was Wrong](#s2)
3. [FAQ](#faq)

## The Story {#s1}

Our reader had her 12-week scan and the nub looked angled up. Multiple people in nub theory groups said 'definitely boy.' At her 20-week scan, she was told she was having a girl.

## Why It Was Wrong {#s2}

The scan was at 11+5 (just before 12 weeks), the image quality was moderate, and the nub was at approximately 20 degrees — in the ambiguous zone. It was read as 'up' but was actually a girl nub that hadn't fully flattened yet.

## FAQ {#faq}

### How common is a wrong boy prediction?

Boy predictions are wrong approximately 10-15% of the time at 12 weeks. The most common reason is reading an ambiguous nub as 'up' when it's actually a girl nub that hasn't flattened yet.
