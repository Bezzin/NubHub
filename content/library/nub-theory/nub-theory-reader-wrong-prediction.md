## The Prediction {#the-prediction}

One mum shared that her 12 week scan image was sent to a nub theory reader who confidently predicted a boy, pointing to a steep upward angle. She bought boy clothes and told family the news early. At her 20 week anatomy scan, the sonographer confirmed she was expecting a girl. The prediction had been wrong, and it was a sharp reminder that nub theory is never certain.

Understanding these basics helps you approach each appointment with realistic expectations and clearer questions for your sonographer or midwife.

## Why Predictions Can Be Wrong {#why-wrong}

Wrong predictions usually come down to scan angle, image quality, or reading too early. The nub changes as it develops, and a steep angle at 11 weeks can settle by 13. Even experienced readers can misread a tilted or rotated profile. This is why nub theory should be treated as fun speculation, not fact.

## FAQ {#faq}

### How common are wrong nub predictions?

No formal statistics exist, but early readings are wrong often enough that they should never be relied on for certainty.

### Should I buy gender specific items after a nub reading?

It is wiser to wait until your anatomy scan or a later confirmed scan before spending money on gendered items.

### Can I trust a confident reader?

Confidence does not equal accuracy. Always treat any nub prediction as a guess.
