# Nub Theory Dating Scan Vs Nt Scan

In the UK, most pregnant women have two scans in the first trimester that tend to cause a lot of confusion: the dating scan and the NT (nuchal translucency) scan. The good news is that for nub theory purposes, they are almost always the same appointment — but there are some important differences worth understanding before you get your scan image read.

## What Is the Dating Scan?

The dating scan is your first routine NHS ultrasound, usually offered between 10 and 14 weeks of pregnancy. Its main purpose is to confirm how far along you are by measuring the baby from crown to rump, check the pregnancy is progressing in the uterus, and look for a heartbeat. The sonographer will also check whether you are carrying more than one baby.

The dating scan gives you your estimated due date (EDD) and is used to set the timing of all your future antenatal care.

## What Is the NT Scan?

The NT (nuchal translucency) scan is a screening test for chromosomal conditions such as Down's syndrome, Edwards' syndrome, and Patau's syndrome. It measures the fluid at the back of the baby's neck. To get an accurate measurement, the baby needs to be between 11+2 and 14+1 weeks of gestation and ideally in a profile position — which is also exactly the position needed for nub theory.

In most NHS trusts, the dating scan and NT scan are combined into a single appointment at around 11 to 13 weeks, commonly called the combined first trimester screening or the 12-week scan. A blood test is taken at the same visit to complete the screening.

## Which Scan Is Better for Nub Theory?

Because both scans are usually done together, asking whether one is "better" than the other is largely moot. What actually matters for nub theory accuracy is:

**Gestational age.** The nub (genital tubercle) looks the same in all babies until around 11 to 12 weeks, when it begins to angle upward in boys. Before 11 weeks, the nub is nearly impossible to read reliably even in an ideal image. From 12 weeks onwards, accuracy improves noticeably — most experienced nub theory readers put accuracy at around 80 to 90% at 12 weeks in a good, clear profile image.

**Image quality and position.** The baby needs to be lying in a true profile (side-on) position. Even at 13 weeks, a slightly rotated or angled image will make the nub difficult or impossible to read accurately. Private early pregnancy scans at dedicated sonography clinics sometimes capture better profile images because the sonographer has more time, but NHS scan images can be excellent too.

**Who is reading the image.** An experienced nub theory reader or community with a large reference image bank will generally give you a more considered opinion than a first-time guesser. No reading is guaranteed.

## Will the Sonographer Tell Me the Sex?

No. NHS sonographers do not reveal fetal sex at the 12-week scan, even if they can see the nub. Sex determination is only offered at the 20-week anomaly scan, and even then it is not a formal part of the NHS scan — some trusts offer it and some do not.

If you want a private gender prediction scan, these are typically offered from around 16 weeks onwards, though some private clinics offer nub theory reads from 12 weeks using a HD recorded scan image.

## What If My Scan Is Before 12 Weeks?

If your dating scan falls at 10 or 11 weeks, nub theory is possible but less reliable. The nub will be present in the image, but the angle of ascent that distinguishes boys from girls is still developing. Some readers will attempt a read at 11+3 or later, but many will flag the reading as lower confidence. A follow-up private scan at 16 weeks would give you a more definitive answer before the 20-week anomaly scan.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Are the dating scan and NT scan the same thing?

Usually yes, in the UK. Most NHS trusts combine the dating scan and nuchal translucency measurement into a single appointment at around 11 to 13 weeks. You will often hear it referred to simply as "the 12-week scan." Both happen in the same ultrasound session, so you will come away with one set of scan images that can be used for nub theory.

### What gestational age gives the best nub theory accuracy?

For the most reliable nub theory read, 12 weeks or later is recommended. At this point the genital tubercle angle has had time to diverge between male and female babies. Earlier than 11+2 weeks the nub looks essentially the same in both sexes, making any prediction little better than a guess. The clearest reads tend to come from scans at 12 to 13 weeks in a true side-on profile position.

### Can I ask the NHS sonographer to capture a nub theory image?

You can let the sonographer know you are interested in the baby's position for nub theory, but NHS sonographers are not able to spend extra time on gender prediction during a clinical appointment. The most reliable way to get a scan image suitable for nub theory is to book a private early pregnancy or reassurance scan at a dedicated clinic, where the sonographer has more time and can capture multiple angles.

Speak to your midwife or GP if you have any concerns about your scan results or antenatal care.
