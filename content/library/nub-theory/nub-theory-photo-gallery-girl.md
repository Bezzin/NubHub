## What Is the Nub and How Does It Predict Girl?

The nub — or genital tubercle — is the small protrusion visible on a 12-week ultrasound scan that eventually develops into either a penis and scrotum (boy) or a clitoris and labia (girl). At 11–13 weeks, before the sex organs have fully differentiated, the nub looks similar in both sexes. The key difference lies in its **angle relative to the spine**.

Nub theory holds that:

- A nub angled at **less than 30 degrees** from the spine (flat or parallel) is associated with a **girl**
- A nub angled at **30 degrees or more** above the spine is associated with a **boy**

For a girl prediction, you are looking for a nub that sits low, flat, and runs roughly parallel to the baby's spine — it does not tip upward at the end.

## What Does a Confirmed Girl Nub Look Like?

When reviewing confirmed girl examples, several consistent features appear:

**Flat angle to the spine.** The most reliable girl indicator is a nub that runs horizontally, level with or very slightly below the line of the spine. There is no upward tilt at the tip.

**Three short, flat lines.** On a clear girl scan, you may be able to see three distinct parallel lines rather than a single pointing structure. These represent the early development of female genitalia. This "three lines" appearance becomes clearer on second-trimester anatomy scans but can sometimes be seen at 12 weeks in a good-quality image.

**Short, flush appearance.** Girl nubs tend to sit close to the body and do not protrude noticeably. They look compact and low-slung rather than raised or elongated.

**No stacking or forking.** Boy nubs often show a stacked appearance — a thickening or raised tip — or a forked shape. Confirmed girl scans consistently lack this. If the nub tip looks blunt and flat, that is consistent with girl.

**No upward curve.** Even a subtle upward curve at the nub tip has historically been associated with boy. Girl nubs remain straight and flat to the very end.

## How to Read Your Own Scan for Girl Features

When comparing your scan to girl examples, the process works best when:

1. **You have a true profile view** — the baby is lying on their side, with the spine clearly visible and the nub visible between the legs
2. **The scan is taken at 12 weeks or later** — the earlier the scan, the harder the nub is to identify reliably
3. **The image is clear and in focus** — blurry or angled scans make the nub angle genuinely impossible to assess accurately

Even with a clear image, nub theory is a fun guide, not a diagnostic tool. The accuracy of nub theory at 12 weeks is debated, and even experienced enthusiasts misread scans. The only reliable way to confirm your baby's sex is via a medical test such as NIPT (non-invasive prenatal testing) from around 10 weeks, or at your 20-week anatomy scan.

## When Nub Theory Is Hard to Call

Not every scan produces a clear girl nub reading. If any of the following apply, the result is better described as unclear rather than girl:

- The nub is obscured or not visible
- The baby's position makes angle assessment unreliable
- The image quality is low
- The nub appears to have a very slight upward angle — this sits in an ambiguous zone

Posting your scan to a nub theory community (such as the Nub Hub community) can help you get a second opinion from people who have seen many examples.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What does a confirmed girl nub look like?

A confirmed girl nub is flat or parallel to the spine, with three short flat lines and no stacked or forked appearance. There is no upward angle at all. It sits close to the body, looks compact, and the tip does not curve or point upward. Girl nubs are generally described as lower and flatter than boy nubs, which tend to tip upward at 30 degrees or more.

### Can a flat nub still be a boy?

In theory, a nub that is genuinely flat and parallel to the spine at 12 weeks or later is unlikely to be boy — but nub theory is not perfectly reliable. Scan quality, angle, and the exact week of the scan all affect accuracy. A flat nub is a good girl indicator, but it cannot be treated as a certainty without confirmation from a medical test or anatomy scan.

### How accurate is nub theory for girl predictions?

Nub theory accuracy figures vary depending on the study and scan conditions. Results are more reliable after 12 weeks and when performed by experienced readers on clear images. Even in ideal conditions, it is an indicator rather than a guarantee. For certainty, NIPT blood testing or the 20-week anatomy scan are the reliable options.
