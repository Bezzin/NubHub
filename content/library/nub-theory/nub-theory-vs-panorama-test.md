# Nub Theory vs Panorama Test

If you are curious about your baby's sex early in pregnancy, you may have come across two very different options: nub theory and the Panorama NIPT (non-invasive prenatal test). They sit at opposite ends of the spectrum — one is a free, community-based method using your NHS scan photo, and the other is a laboratory blood test costing several hundred pounds. Understanding what each one actually involves will help you decide which, if either, is right for you.

## What Is the Panorama Test? {#section-1}

Panorama is a brand of non-invasive prenatal testing (NIPT) offered by Natera. Like other NIPTs, it analyses cell-free fetal DNA that circulates in your blood during pregnancy. The test screens for chromosomal conditions such as Down's syndrome (trisomy 21), Patau's syndrome (trisomy 13), and Edwards' syndrome (trisomy 18). As part of that analysis, it can also determine fetal sex with very high accuracy — typically reported at 99% or above.

Panorama is available from around nine weeks of pregnancy. You have a blood draw at a private clinic, the sample is sent to a laboratory, and results typically come back within one to two weeks. Costs in the UK vary by clinic and by which panel of conditions you choose to screen for, but you should expect to pay somewhere between £400 and £800 when combining the chromosome screening with the sex determination result.

It is worth noting that Panorama is not the same as diagnostic testing. A positive screening result for a chromosomal condition would need to be confirmed by an invasive diagnostic test such as CVS or amniocentesis before any clinical decisions are made.

## What Is Nub Theory? {#section-2}

Nub theory is a method of estimating fetal sex using the angle of the genital tubercle — the small projection visible on ultrasound images between roughly 11 and 14 weeks. At this stage, the external genitalia of male and female fetuses are still forming, but the tubercle's angle relative to the baby's spine can give an indication of sex. An angle greater than 30 degrees from the spine is associated with male development; a more parallel angle is associated with female.

Nub theory is not a clinical test. It is an interpretive method used by enthusiasts and some sonographers on a best-guess basis. Accuracy improves with scan quality and gestational age — at 12 weeks or later with a clear image, experienced readers often report accuracy in the range of 80–95%, though individual results vary significantly and the nub is not always visible.

There is no cost involved beyond having your scan. Many parents share their scan images in community forums or with dedicated services like Nub Hub to get a reading.

## How They Compare {#section-3}

| Feature | Panorama NIPT | Nub Theory |
|---|---|---|
| Accuracy (sex) | ~99% | 80–95% at 12+ weeks |
| Earliest available | ~9 weeks | ~11–12 weeks |
| Cost | £400–£800 | Free |
| Also screens for chromosomal conditions | Yes | No |
| Clinical test | Yes | No |
| Waiting time for result | 1–2 weeks | Hours to days |

The core trade-off is straightforward: Panorama is far more accurate and provides additional health information about chromosomal conditions, but it costs a significant amount of money. Nub theory costs nothing but is less reliable, highly dependent on image quality, and cannot tell you anything about your baby's health.

If you are primarily interested in sex determination and cannot afford NIPT, nub theory can be a fun way to get an early indication — just treat it as a guess rather than a certainty. If you have clinical reasons to want chromosomal screening, or if accuracy matters greatly to you, Panorama or another NIPT brand is the more appropriate route.

## Frequently Asked Questions {#faq}

### Is Panorama better than Harmony for finding out the sex?

Panorama and Harmony are both highly accurate NIPT brands with comparable sex determination accuracy of around 99%. The main practical difference is timing — Panorama can be done from around nine weeks, while Harmony is typically offered from ten weeks. Both screen for the major chromosomal conditions. Your choice will likely come down to which clinic near you offers which test, and the price at that particular clinic.

### Does the Panorama test always give a sex result?

In the vast majority of cases, yes. However, a small number of results may be inconclusive — for example if there is insufficient fetal DNA in the sample (known as a low fetal fraction), which can happen earlier in pregnancy or in some other circumstances. In that case, you would usually be offered a repeat blood draw.

### Can nub theory tell me anything about my baby's health?

No. Nub theory is purely an interpretation of scan images to estimate sex. It gives no information about chromosomal conditions, structural development, or any other aspect of your baby's health. For that kind of information you need appropriate clinical screening, either through the NHS (which offers combined screening at 11–14 weeks) or through a private NIPT.
