# Thrush in Early Pregnancy Guide

Thrush is a common yeast infection that often appears in early pregnancy. Hormonal changes make the vaginal environment friendlier to yeast, leading to itching, soreness, and discharge. It is usually easy to treat safely in pregnancy with the right guidance.

## What Causes Thrush in Pregnancy {#what-causes-thrush-in-pregnancy}

The Candida fungus naturally lives in the vagina, but higher oestrogen levels in pregnancy increase glycogen, which yeast feeds on. This allows overgrowth. Antibiotics, a weakened immune response, and tight clothing can contribute.

## Symptoms and Safe Treatment {#symptoms-and-safe-treatment}

Typical symptoms include itching, soreness, white discharge, and stinging during urination or sex. Treatment in pregnancy usually involves topical antifungal creams or pessaries. Oral tablets are generally avoided. Always confirm with your midwife or GP before treating.

## FAQ {#faq}

### Can thrush harm my baby?

Thrush is not usually harmful to the pregnancy. During birth it can pass to the baby as oral thrush, which is treatable.

### Can I use over-the-counter thrush treatments?

Some are safe in pregnancy, but always check with a pharmacist or midwife first. Oral fluconazole tablets are typically avoided.

### How can I prevent thrush in pregnancy?

Wear cotton underwear, avoid tight clothing, wash with water rather than perfumed products, and keep the area dry and breathable.
