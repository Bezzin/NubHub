# What Is a Forked Nub in Nub Theory?

A "forked" nub is another term you'll see frequently in nub theory discussions. Here's what it means and how to spot it.

## What Does "Forked" Mean? {#what-is-forked}

A "forked" nub has a Y-shaped or split appearance at the tip — like the tines of a fork. Instead of a single smooth line, the nub tip appears to split into two small prongs.

## Forked Nub = Boy? {#forked-boy}

A forked tip is **commonly associated with boys**. The fork represents the developing foreskin and glans of the penis. When combined with a 30+ degree upward angle, a forked nub is a strong boy indicator.

However, like stacking, forking is a **secondary indicator**. Always check the angle first.

## How to Spot a Fork {#how-to-spot}

Look at the very tip of the nub:
- **Forked**: The tip splits into two small prongs (Y-shape)
- **Not forked**: The tip is a single smooth line or bump

Forking can be subtle and is easier to see on clear, high-quality images.

## Forked vs Stacked {#forked-vs-stacked}

Both forked and stacked nubs are boy indicators:
- **Forked**: Y-shaped split at the tip (represents foreskin/glans)
- **Stacked**: Layered white lines at the tip (represents penis/scrotum)

Some boy nubs show both stacking and forking. Some show only one. Both are secondary to the angle.

## Frequently Asked Questions {#faq}

### Can a girl nub be forked?

In rare cases, image quality or shadows can create a fork-like appearance on a girl's nub. True forking (representing developing male anatomy) is a boy sign. Always verify with the angle.

### Is forking or stacking more reliable?

Both are equally reliable (or unreliable) as secondary indicators. Neither should be relied upon without checking the primary angle. If the angle is clearly 30+ degrees, forking and stacking just confirm the boy prediction.
