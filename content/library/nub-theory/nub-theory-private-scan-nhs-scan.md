# Nub Theory: Private Scan vs NHS Scan

Private scan vs NHS scan for nub theory — which is better for gender prediction?

If you are hoping to use nub theory to get an early hint at your baby's sex, the quality and content of your scan images matter a great deal. The nub — the small genital tubercle visible in profile ultrasound images between roughly 11 and 14 weeks — needs to be clearly visible at a true profile angle to be assessed. Not all scan photos show it, and the type of scan you have affects how useful your images will be.

## What Is the Nub?

The nub is the early genital structure that is present in all babies from around 11 weeks. In a true profile view, it appears as a small protrusion beneath the base of the spine. Nub theory holds that the angle of this structure relative to the spine can suggest whether the baby is male or female — angled up more steeply is often associated with a boy, while a parallel or downward angle is often associated with a girl. The nub becomes less visible and more differentiated after around 14 weeks.

## NHS Dating Scan

Your NHS dating scan usually takes place between 11 and 14 weeks and is carried out by a trained sonographer. Its purpose is clinical: to confirm your due date, check for a heartbeat, assess whether the pregnancy is progressing normally, and screen for chromosomal conditions if you opt in to combined screening.

**What this means for nub theory:**
- The sonographer is focused on clinical measurements, not gender prediction. They will not specifically angle the probe to capture a clear nub view.
- You will typically receive one to three printed or digital photos, chosen for clinical purposes (usually a face-on or partial profile view).
- Image quality is generally good, as NHS machines are well maintained — but whether your photos show a clear nub is largely down to luck: your baby's position, your body type, and how the sonographer frames the images.
- Nub theory using NHS scan photos is entirely possible if you happen to get a clear profile image showing the nub, but it is not guaranteed.

## Private Early Pregnancy Scans

Private scan clinics offer ultrasound appointments outside the NHS system. Scans specifically aimed at early gender prediction or reassurance are widely available from around 12 weeks and typically cost between £60 and £200 depending on location and the package chosen.

**What this means for nub theory:**
- The appointment is longer, giving the sonographer more time to find a clear profile view.
- Many private sonographers are familiar with nub theory and will actively try to capture the nub angle if asked.
- You typically receive more photos and often a short video clip, increasing the chance of getting a usable image.
- Some clinics offer a nub reading as part of the appointment or provide images specifically taken for this purpose.
- Image quality can vary between private clinics — ask whether the clinic uses equipment comparable to NHS standard before booking.

## Which Is Better for Nub Theory?

A private scan at or after 12 weeks gives you the best conditions for nub theory: more time, more photos, a cooperative sonographer, and images taken with your purpose in mind. If getting a clear nub image is your goal, a private scan is the more reliable route.

That said, many parents get perfectly usable nub images from their NHS dating scan and successfully submit them for community readings or AI prediction. The NHS scan is not designed for nub theory, but it absolutely can work if the stars align.

Important caveats to keep in mind regardless of scan type:
- Nub theory is not medically validated as a reliable method of sex prediction. It can be a fun way to speculate, but it should not be relied upon for planning or decision-making.
- No scan before the 20-week anomaly scan guarantees an accurate sex determination.
- Even at the anatomy scan (20 weeks), sex is sometimes inconclusive depending on the baby's position.

## Preparing for Your Scan

Whether you are attending an NHS dating scan or a private appointment, there are a few things you can do to improve your chances of a clear nub image:

- Drink water before your scan as a fuller bladder can improve image clarity in early pregnancy.
- Consider walking or having a light snack beforehand — gentle movement can encourage your baby into a profile position.
- If attending a private clinic, tell the sonographer in advance that you are hoping for a clear profile view for nub theory purposes.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Which is better for nub theory — a private scan or an NHS scan?

A private scan gives you better conditions for nub theory: more time, more photos, and a sonographer who can actively try to capture the nub angle. However, many NHS dating scan photos do show a clear nub, particularly if your baby cooperates with a profile position. Both can work; a private scan simply makes a clear image more likely.

### Is a private scan worth it for nub theory?

That depends on how much an early gender hint matters to you. Private early pregnancy scans typically cost £60–£200 and provide a longer, more relaxed appointment with more images. If you are eager to try nub theory before your 20-week scan, a private booking gives you the best chance of getting useful images. Many parents also find the reassurance of seeing their baby and hearing the heartbeat to be worth the cost independently of gender prediction.

### Can I submit my NHS scan photo for a nub reading?

Yes — if your NHS scan photo shows a clear profile of your baby with the spine and nub visible, it can be submitted to nub theory communities or AI reading tools. The quality of the image matters more than where it was taken. Look for a profile shot where the baby is lying on their side and the spine is clearly visible from the base of the skull to the base of the spine.

Always speak to your midwife or GP for personalised advice about your pregnancy and your scans.
