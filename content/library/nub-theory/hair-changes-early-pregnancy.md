# Hair Changes in Early Pregnancy

Hair changes are a common early pregnancy sign, thanks to shifting hormones. Many people notice thicker, fuller hair, while others experience dryness or unwanted growth in new places. These changes are temporary and usually settle after birth.

## Why Hair Changes in Pregnancy {#why-hair-changes-in-pregnancy}

Higher oestrogen levels extend the growing phase of hair and slow the shedding phase. This means less hair falls out each day, so hair often appears thicker and fuller. Some people also notice oilier or drier scalp conditions as hormones fluctuate.

## Common Hair Changes {#common-hair-changes}

Typical changes include thicker hair, slower shedding, changes in curl or texture, and increased body hair growth. A few people experience mild hair thinning early on. After birth, the shedding that was delayed can happen all at once, known as postpartum hair loss.

## FAQ {#faq}

### Will my hair go back to normal after pregnancy?

Yes. Most hair changes resolve within six to twelve months after birth as hormone levels settle. Postpartum shedding usually peaks around three to four months postpartum.

### Is hair growth a sign of pregnancy?

It can be, but it is not definitive. Many factors affect hair. A missed period and a pregnancy test are far more reliable signs.

### Can I dye my hair during pregnancy?

Most evidence suggests hair dye is safe after the first trimester. Always check with your midwife and ensure good ventilation.
