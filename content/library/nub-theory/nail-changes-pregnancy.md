# Nail Changes in Pregnancy

Nails often change during pregnancy due to hormones and increased blood flow. Some people enjoy stronger, faster-growing nails, while others notice brittle or ridged nails. These changes are normal and usually reverse after birth.

## Why Nails Change {#why-nails-change}

Hormonal shifts, particularly oestrogen, and increased circulation affect nail growth and strength. Some nails grow faster and feel harder, while others become dry, brittle, or develop ridges. Nutritional demands of pregnancy can also play a role.

## Common Nail Changes {#common-nail-changes}

Reported changes include faster growth, harder nails, brittleness and splitting, vertical ridges, and separation from the nail bed in rare cases. Keeping nails trimmed, moisturised, and protected from harsh chemicals helps minimise problems.

## FAQ {#faq}

### Are nail changes in pregnancy normal?

Yes. Most nail changes are harmless and linked to hormones and blood flow. They typically resolve postpartum.

### Can I get manicures while pregnant?

Generally yes, with good ventilation and safe products. Check with your midwife if unsure, and avoid certain chemicals in the first trimester.

### When should I see a doctor about my nails?

If nails separate from the bed, become discoloured, or you suspect infection, consult your GP. These may not be simple pregnancy changes.
