# Nub Theory Forums: Where to Get Your Scan Read

After your 12 week scan, it is very common to want someone to take a look at the image and offer a nub theory prediction. Online forums and communities have sprung up specifically for this purpose, and they can be a fun way to engage with other expectant parents — provided you go in with realistic expectations about accuracy.

## What Is a Nub Theory Forum Reading?

Nub theory is based on the angle of the genital tubercle (the "nub") visible on a scan image taken between around 11 and 13 weeks. The theory suggests that a nub angling upward at more than 30 degrees relative to the spine indicates a boy, while a nub that lies flat or angles downward suggests a girl. Community readers use this framework to offer informal predictions on your scan image.

These are entirely informal opinions from fellow parents or enthusiasts — not medical diagnoses. Even experienced readers can only offer a probability, not a certainty, and accuracy depends heavily on scan angle, image quality, and gestational age.

## Where People Go to Get Scans Read

Several online communities focus on nub theory readings. The landscape of specific forums changes over time, so the best approach is to search for active communities on platforms such as Facebook, Reddit, and dedicated pregnancy forums. Look for groups that are clearly active, have a large membership, and have clear rules about how to post images.

**What to look for in a good community:**
- Clear posting guidelines (they will usually ask for a side-profile image, gestational age, and scan date)
- Moderators who manage the tone and accuracy of discussion
- Readers who state their confidence level and acknowledge when an image is unreadable
- Transparency that predictions are for fun, not medical fact

**Red flags to watch for:**
- Anyone claiming 100% accuracy or offering guaranteed predictions
- Requests for payment in exchange for a "professional" reading — no community reader has clinical training in gender determination
- Communities that pressure you to share more images than you are comfortable with

## Tips for Getting the Best Reading

The quality of your scan image makes a huge difference to what any reader can tell you. Before posting, consider the following:

**Post a clear side profile.** Nub theory only works when the baby is in a clear lateral (side-on) position. If your image shows the baby face-on, from above, or at an angle, no reliable reading is possible.

**Include the gestational age.** Readers need to know how many weeks and days you were at the scan. Before 11 weeks the nub is often too small to assess; after 14 weeks the genitals may be more directly visible but the nub method is less relevant.

**Use an unedited, unzoomed image if possible.** Zooming and cropping can distort the angle, which is the most important element of any reading.

**Do not circle or annotate what you think you see.** This can bias readers toward confirming your guess rather than giving an independent opinion.

**Accept that "inconclusive" is a valid result.** If your image does not show the nub in the right position, no honest reader will be able to give you a useful prediction. An inconclusive result tells you nothing about your baby's sex — it simply means the image is not suitable.

## Managing Expectations

Nub theory readings from online communities are a source of fun and community connection, not a reliable gender prediction tool. Even under ideal conditions, predictions based on nub theory have a meaningful error rate, particularly when made by non-specialists from consumer-quality scan photos. Many parents enjoy the guessing game while fully accepting that the only reliable confirmation comes from a medical professional — either at an anatomy scan from around 16 to 20 weeks, or via a blood-based test such as NIPT.

If you are trying for a particular sex for medical reasons (for example, a sex-linked genetic condition in the family), please discuss this with your midwife or genetic counsellor rather than relying on community forum readings.

## Frequently Asked Questions {#faq}

### Are nub theory forum readings accurate?

Community nub theory readings are informal and variable. Accuracy depends on the quality of the scan image, the gestational age, and the experience of the reader. They are best treated as a fun prediction rather than reliable information.

### How do I post my scan for a nub theory reading?

Look for active Facebook groups or Reddit communities dedicated to nub theory. Most ask you to share a clear side-profile image, your gestational age in weeks and days, and sometimes the scan date. Read the group rules before posting.

### Can I get a more reliable prediction than a forum reading?

Yes. A private gender scan from a reputable clinic (usually available from around 16 weeks) is far more reliable than a nub theory forum reading. Blood-based tests such as NIPT, available privately from around 10 weeks, can determine foetal sex with high accuracy.
