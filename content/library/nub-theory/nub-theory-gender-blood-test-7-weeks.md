## Comparison {#section-1}

At 7 weeks, the only blood test that can predict gender is SneakPeek, which claims 95%+ accuracy. NIPT requires at least 9-10 weeks. SneakPeek is cheaper but less accurate.

## Which Should You Choose? {#section-2}

SneakPeek works by detecting male DNA in your blood. At 7 weeks, there may not be enough fetal DNA yet, which can lead to false results. Waiting until 9-10 weeks for NIPT is more reliable.

## FAQ {#faq}

### Can I find out my baby's gender at 7 weeks?

Only with SneakPeek (approximately 95% accurate at 7 weeks). NIPT requires 9-10 weeks. For best accuracy, wait for NIPT or nub theory at 12 weeks.

### Is SneakPeek accurate at 7 weeks?

SneakPeek claims 95% accuracy at 7 weeks, but early testing increases the risk of false results due to insufficient fetal DNA. Wait until 8+ weeks for better accuracy.
