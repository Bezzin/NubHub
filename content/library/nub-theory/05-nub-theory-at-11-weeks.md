# Nub Theory at 11 Weeks: Is It Too Early to Predict Gender?

If you're considering nub theory at 11 weeks, you might be wondering whether it's too early to get a reliable gender prediction. The honest answer is: **11 weeks is on the early side for nub theory**, and the accuracy is significantly lower than at 12+ weeks — but it's not impossible.

## Can You Use Nub Theory at 11 Weeks? {#can-you-use-it}

Technically, yes — you can attempt nub theory at 11 weeks. The genital tubercle (nub) is present and visible on ultrasound at this gestational age. However, the developmental differences between boys and girls are **less pronounced at 11 weeks** than at 12–13 weeks, making accurate prediction much harder.

## Accuracy at 11 Weeks {#accuracy}

At 11 weeks, nub theory accuracy is approximately **70%** — meaning roughly 3 out of 10 predictions will be wrong. Compare this to:

- 11 weeks: ~70% accuracy
- 12 weeks: ~80–85% accuracy
- 13 weeks: ~92–95% accuracy

At 70%, you're only slightly better than a coin flip (50%). This is why most nub theory experts recommend waiting until at least 12 weeks.

### Accuracy by Day at 11 Weeks

- 11+0 to 11+2: ~65% accuracy
- 11+3 to 11+5: ~70% accuracy
- 11+6: ~72% accuracy

Each day makes a small difference, and by 12+0 weeks, accuracy jumps significantly.

## Why 11 Weeks Is Difficult {#why-difficult}

At 11 weeks, the genital tubercle is still in the early stages of development:

- **In boys**: The nub is beginning to elongate and angle upward, but the angle may only be 15–20 degrees — not the clear 30+ degrees seen at 12+ weeks
- **In girls**: The nub is starting to flatten, but it may still have a slight upward angle that hasn't fully resolved yet

This means:
- A boy nub at 11 weeks might look **ambiguous** because the angle isn't steep enough yet
- A girl nub at 11 weeks might look **slightly raised** because it hasn't fully flattened yet
- The "ambiguous zone" (10–30 degrees) is very common at 11 weeks

## When Nub Theory at 11 Weeks Might Work {#when-it-works}

There are some situations where nub theory at 11 weeks can give a more confident result:

1. **The nub is very clearly angled at 30+ degrees**: If the angle is unmistakably steep, it's more likely to be a boy, even at 11 weeks
2. **The nub is completely flat**: A truly flat nub at 11 weeks is more likely to be a girl
3. **The image quality is excellent**: A very clear profile view gives you the best chance
4. **The baby is measuring ahead**: If the baby measures closer to 12 weeks, development may be further along

## What to Do Instead {#what-to-do-instead}

If you're at 11 weeks and desperate to know the gender:

1. **Wait 1–2 weeks**: The accuracy improvement from 11 to 12+ weeks is dramatic
2. **Consider NIPT**: NIPT blood tests can determine sex from 10 weeks with 99%+ accuracy, but cost £150–£500
3. **Book a private scan at 12+ weeks**: A private scan at 12+ weeks gives you a better image for nub theory
4. **Try Ramzi theory**: While less accurate, Ramzi theory uses the placenta position at 6–8 weeks — but be aware it's only ~50% accurate
5. **Accept the uncertainty**: Sometimes you just have to wait — and that's OK

## Frequently Asked Questions {#faq}

### Is 11 weeks too early for nub theory?

11 weeks is considered early for nub theory. The accuracy at 11 weeks is approximately 70%, compared to 80–85% at 12 weeks and 92–95% at 13 weeks. If you can wait until 12+ weeks, your prediction will be significantly more reliable.

### Can nub theory be accurate at 11 weeks?

Nub theory can be approximately 70% accurate at 11 weeks. This means roughly 7 out of 10 predictions will be correct. While better than chance (50%), it's not reliable enough for a confident prediction. By 12+ weeks, accuracy improves to 80–85%.

### Should I get a private scan at 11 weeks for nub theory?

If you're going to pay for a private scan specifically for nub theory, wait until at least 12+ weeks. Paying for a scan at 11 weeks gives you a much lower accuracy rate, and you'd be better off spending that money on a scan 1–2 weeks later or on an NIPT blood test.

### What if my nub theory at 11 weeks shows a clear boy angle?

If the nub is clearly angled at 30+ degrees at 11 weeks, it's more likely to be a boy — but still only about 70–75% certain. A clear upward angle at 11 weeks is a stronger indicator than a flat nub at 11 weeks, because a boy's nub develops earlier than a girl's flattens
