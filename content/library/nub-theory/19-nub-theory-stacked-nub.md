# What Is a Stacked Nub in Nub Theory?

You've probably seen people in nub theory groups talking about "stacked nubs" — but what does it actually mean? Here's a clear explanation.

## What Does "Stacked" Mean? {#what-is-stacked}

A "stacked" nub refers to a layered appearance at the tip of the genital tubercle on an ultrasound image. Instead of a single line, the nub appears to have multiple white lines stacked on top of each other — like layers of a cake.

## Stacked Nub = Boy? {#stacked-boy}

A stacked appearance is **commonly associated with boys**. The stacking represents the developing penis and the beginning of scrotal development. When you see stacking combined with a 30+ degree upward angle, it's a strong boy indicator.

However, stacking is a **secondary indicator**. The primary indicator is always the angle. Don't rely on stacking alone.

## How to Identify Stacking {#how-to-identify}

Look at the tip of the nub on your ultrasound image:
- **Stacked**: Multiple short white lines layered on top of each other at the nub tip
- **Not stacked**: A single line or smooth bump with no layered appearance

Stacking is easier to see on high-quality images. On blurry or overexposed images, it can be hard to tell.

## Stacked vs Forked {#stacked-vs-forked}

| Feature | Stacked | Forked |
|---------|---------|--------|
| **Appearance** | Layered lines | Y-shaped split |
| **Represents** | Penis + scrotum developing | Foreskin/glans developing |
| **Association** | Boy | Boy |
| **Reliability** | Secondary indicator | Secondary indicator |

Both stacking and forking are boy indicators, but neither is as reliable as the primary angle measurement.

## Frequently Asked Questions {#faq}

### Can a girl have a stacked nub?

In rare cases, image artefacts or shadows can create a stacked appearance on a girl's nub. However, true stacking (representing developing male anatomy) is a boy sign. Always verify with the angle.

### Is a stacked nub 100% boy?

No. Stacking is a strong boy indicator but not 100%. Always check the angle first — if the nub is flat but appears stacked, it could be an image artefact. If the nub is at 30+ degrees AND stacked, that's a very strong boy prediction.
