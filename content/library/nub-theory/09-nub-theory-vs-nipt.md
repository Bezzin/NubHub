# Nub Theory vs NIPT Blood Test: Which Is Better for Gender Prediction?

If you want to know your baby's gender in the first trimester, your two main options are nub theory (free, from your 12-week scan) and NIPT (paid blood test from 10 weeks). Here's how they compare.

## What Is NIPT? {#what-is-nipt)

NIPT (Non-Invasive Prenatal Testing) is a blood test that analyses fragments of fetal DNA in the mother's bloodstream. It's primarily used to screen for chromosomal conditions (Down syndrome, Edwards syndrome, Patau syndrome), but it can also determine fetal sex with 99%+ accuracy from 10 weeks.

Common NIPT brands include Harmony, Panorama, and MaterniT21. In the UK, NIPT costs £150–£500 privately, though some NHS trusts offer it free for high-risk pregnancies.

## Accuracy Comparison {#accuracy}

| Method | Accuracy | Earliest Use |
|--------|----------|-------------|
| Nub theory at 12 weeks | 80–85% | 11 weeks |
| Nub theory at 13 weeks | 92–95% | 11 weeks |
| NIPT (any brand) | 99%+ | 10 weeks |
| 20-week scan | 95–99% | 16 weeks |

NIPT is the clear winner for accuracy. At 99%+, it's nearly perfect. Nub theory at its best (13 weeks) reaches 92–95%, which is good but not definitive.

## Cost Comparison {#cost}

| Method | Cost |
|--------|------|
| Nub theory | Free (uses your NHS scan) |
| NIPT private | £150–£500 |
| NIPT on NHS | Free (only if high-risk) |
| Private 12-week scan for nub | £60–£200 |

Nub theory is free — you just need your 12-week scan photo. NIPT costs money, though some NHS trusts offer it free for high-risk pregnancies.

## Timing Comparison {#timing}

| Method | Earliest You Can Use |
|--------|----------------------|
| NIPT | 10 weeks |
| Nub theory | 11 weeks (but 12+ recommended) |

NIPT can be done earlier — from 10 weeks. Nub theory requires waiting until at least 12 weeks for reasonable accuracy.

## Pros and Cons {#pros-cons}

### Nub Theory
**Pros:** Free, uses existing scan, fun, no needles, instant result
**Cons:** Not definitive, depends on image quality, depends on baby position, only 80–95% accurate

### NIPT
**Pros:** 99%+ accurate, detects chromosomal conditions, works from 10 weeks, doesn't depend on baby position
**Cons:** Costs £150–£500, requires a blood draw, results take 7–10 days, may not be available on NHS

## Which Should You Choose? {#which-to-choose}

- **If cost is not an issue**: Get NIPT at 10 weeks for 99%+ accuracy, then use nub theory at 12 weeks as a fun confirmation
- **If you want free**: Use nub theory at 12+ weeks — it's 80–85% accurate and costs nothing
- **If you need certainty**: NIPT is the only non-invasive method that's nearly 100% accurate
- **If you're at high risk for chromosomal conditions**: NIPT is recommended regardless of gender interest

## Frequently Asked Questions {#faq}

### Can NIPT be wrong about gender?

NIPT is 99%+ accurate for gender, but it's not 100%. Rare errors can occur due to: vanishing twin (remaining male DNA), recent blood transfusion, bone marrow transplant, or laboratory errors. In practice, NIPT gender results are almost always correct.

### Is NIPT worth it just for gender?

If your only reason for NIPT is to find out the gender, it may not be worth £150–£500 when nub theory is free and 80–95% accurate. However, NIPT also screens for serious chromosomal conditions, which many parents find valuable regardless of the gender result.

### Can I use nub theory AND NIPT?

Absolutely. Many parents get NIPT at 10 weeks for definitive results, then try nub theory at 12 weeks for fun to see if the nub matches the NIPT result. It's a nice way to verify the nub theory worked.

## Frequently Asked Questions {#faq}

### How much does NIPT cost in the UK?

NIPT costs between £150 and £500 privately in the UK, depending on the brand and provider. Some NHS trusts offer NIPT free for high-risk pregnancies. Common brands include Harmony, Panorama, and MaterniT21.

### Does the NHS offer NIPT for gender?

The NHS offers NIPT free for pregnancies identified as high-risk for chromosomal conditions (following combined screening). The NHS does not offer NIPT solely for gender determination. Gender is confirmed at the 20-week anatomy scan on the NHS.
