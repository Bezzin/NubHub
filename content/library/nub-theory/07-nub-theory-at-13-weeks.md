# Nub Theory at 13 Weeks: Maximum Accuracy Gender Prediction

If you want the highest possible accuracy from nub theory, **13 weeks is the time to do it**. At 13 weeks, the genital tubercle has developed to its maximum differentiation point before it transitions into fully formed genitalia — giving you the clearest angle and the most confident prediction possible from ultrasound-based gender prediction.

## Why 13 Weeks Is the Most Accurate {#why-13-weeks}

At 13 weeks, the genital development has progressed to the point where:

- **Boy nubs** are at their most pronounced upward angle (30+ degrees) — the penis is beginning to form and the angle is unmistakable
- **Girl nubs** are completely flat with no residual upward angle — the clitoris is formed and the nub lies flush against the body
- The **ambiguous zone** (10–30 degrees) is rare at 13 weeks — most nubs are clearly one or the other

This makes 13 weeks the **peak accuracy time** for nub theory, with studies showing 92–95% accuracy.

## Accuracy at 13 Weeks {#accuracy}

| Exact Gestational Age | Accuracy |
|----------------------|----------|
| 13+0 to 13+2 | ~92% |
| 13+3 to 13+5 | ~94% |
| 13+6 | ~95% |

At 95% accuracy, nub theory at 13+6 weeks is nearly as reliable as some paid methods. Only NIPT (99%+) and invasive diagnostic tests (100%) are more accurate.

### Comparison Across Weeks
- 11 weeks: ~70%
- 12 weeks: ~80–85%
- 13 weeks: ~92–95%

The jump from 12 to 13 weeks is significant — accuracy improves by roughly 10 percentage points.

## What Changes at 13 Weeks {#what-changes}

By 13 weeks, the genital tubercle has completed its primary development phase:

### Male Development at 13 Weeks
- The nub has fully elongated and angled upward
- The penis is beginning to form
- The scrotum is starting to develop
- The upward angle is at its maximum — clearly 30+ degrees
- The "stacked" appearance is often very clear

### Female Development at 13 Weeks
- The nub has completely flattened
- The clitoris is formed
- The labia are beginning to develop
- The nub is parallel to the spine with no upward angle
- Three flat lines may be visible

### After 14 Weeks
After 14 weeks, the nub has essentially transitioned into formed genitalia. At this point, you're no longer looking at "nub theory" (angle-based) but rather direct visualization of genitalia — which is the standard method used at the 20-week scan.

## Is 13 Weeks Too Late for Nub Theory? {#too-late}

No — 13 weeks is actually the **optimal time**, not too late. Nub theory is valid from 11+0 to 13+6 weeks. Some people worry that 13 weeks is "too late" because the nub has developed too far, but in reality, the development at 13 weeks is what makes the angle most readable.

However, **after 14 weeks**, nub theory in the traditional sense is less applicable because the genitalia have developed beyond the "nub" stage. At that point, a sonographer may be able to see actual genital structures rather than just the angle.

## How to Get a 13-Week Scan {#how-to-get-a-scan}

The NHS dating scan is typically performed between 11+2 and 14+1 weeks. If your NHS scan is at 11–12 weeks, you may want to consider:

1. **Requesting a later NHS scan**: Some hospitals may accommodate a request for a scan closer to 13 weeks, but this isn't guaranteed
2. **Booking a private scan at 13 weeks**: Many private clinics offer 12–14 week scans specifically for gender prediction. Cost: £60–£200
3. **Combining with NT scan**: If your NT scan falls at 13 weeks, you're in luck — this is perfect for nub theory

### Private Scan Options at 13 Weeks
- **Gender scan clinics**: Specialise in gender determination from 12+ weeks
- **General private scan clinics**: Offer dating/NT scans and can check the nub on request
- **3D/4D scan clinics**: Some offer early gender scans using 3D imaging

## Frequently Asked Questions {#faq}

### Is 13 weeks the best time for nub theory?

Yes, 13 weeks is the most accurate time for nub theory, with accuracy rates of 92–95%. The genital tubercle has developed to its maximum differentiation, making the angle between boys and girls most distinct.

### Can nub theory be wrong at 13 weeks?

Yes, but it's rare. At 13 weeks, nub theory is approximately 92–95% accurate, meaning there's a 5–8% chance of an incorrect prediction. The most common reason for errors at 13 weeks is poor image quality or baby position, rather than developmental ambiguity.

### Should I wait until 13 weeks for nub theory?

If you can wait, 13 weeks gives you the best accuracy. However, most women have their NHS scan at 11–12 weeks. If your scan is at 12 weeks and shows a clear nub, that's still 80–85% accurate — which is reasonably reliable. If you want maximum accuracy and can afford a private scan, booking one at 13 weeks is ideal.

### What's the difference between nub theory at 13 weeks and gender at 20 weeks?

At 13 weeks, you're looking at the angle of the nub (indirect prediction). At 20 weeks, the sonographer can directly see the genitalia (direct visualization). The 20-week scan is more accurate (95–99%) because the genitalia are fully formed and visible. Nub theory at 13 weeks (92–95%) is an earlier but slightly less accurate method.
