# Single and Pregnant: Support and Advice

Being pregnant on your own is challenging but absolutely doable. Here's the support available and how to navigate it.

## Table of Contents

1. [Practical Support](#section-1)
2. [Emotional Support](#section-2)
3. [FAQ](#faq)

## Practical Support {#section-1}

- Tell your midwife you're going solo — they can offer extra support
- Ask friends or family to attend scans with you
- Look into single-parent support groups
- Check your entitlement to benefits and maternity pay
- Consider hiring a doula for birth support

## Emotional Support {#section-2}

- Connect with other single mums online
- Don't be afraid to ask for help
- Build a support network before the baby arrives
- Consider counselling if you're feeling overwhelmed

## FAQ {#faq}

### What benefits can I get as a single pregnant woman?

You may be entitled to Universal Credit, Maternity Allowance, Healthy Start vouchers, Sure Start Maternity Grant, free prescriptions, and housing support. Check with your local Jobcentre or Citizens Advice.

### Can I have someone at my birth if I'm single?

Yes. You can bring anyone you choose — a friend, family member, or doula. You don't need a partner to have support during birth.
