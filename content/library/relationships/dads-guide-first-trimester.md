# Dad's Guide to the First Trimester

The first trimester can feel like a waiting game, especially for partners who are watching rather than experiencing the physical changes. But your role is crucial, and there is plenty you can do to support your partner and stay involved from the very beginning.

## What Is Happening in the First Trimester {#section-1}

The first trimester spans weeks one to twelve and is a period of rapid development for your baby, even though the changes are largely invisible from the outside. Your partner may be experiencing nausea, extreme fatigue, breast tenderness, mood swings, and frequent trips to the toilet. All of these are normal signs that her body is working hard to sustain a new life.

As a dad or partner, you might feel a bit left out during this stage because the physical changes are happening to someone else. This is completely normal. Your role is not any less important; it is simply different. Understanding what your partner is going through physically and emotionally will help you provide the right kind of support at the right time.

## How You Can Help and Stay Involved {#section-2}

Practical help goes a long way. Take on extra household chores, especially cooking if smells trigger her nausea. Offer to pick up groceries, run errands, or simply make her a cup of ginger tea. Listen without trying to fix everything; sometimes she just needs to vent about feeling rubbish. Attend every appointment you can, including the booking appointment and the first scan, as these are milestones you can share together.

Educate yourself by reading up on pregnancy, birth, and early parenthood. The more you know, the more confident you will feel when decisions need to be made. Talk openly with your partner about your own feelings too, whether that is excitement, anxiety, or a mix of both. Sharing your experience strengthens your bond and reminds her she is not alone.

## FAQ {#faq}

### Is it normal for dads to feel anxious in the first trimester?

Yes, feeling anxious is completely normal. The first trimester is full of uncertainty, and it is natural to worry about the health of your partner and baby. Talking about your concerns helps.

### Should I attend the first scan?

If at all possible, yes. The first scan, usually around twelve weeks, is a special milestone and seeing the heartbeat for the first time is an unforgettable experience for both of you.

### What if I feel disconnected from the pregnancy?

Feeling disconnected early on is common because the physical changes are not happening to you. Attending scans, talking to your partner, and reading about foetal development can help you feel more connected.
