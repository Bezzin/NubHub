# Alcohol in Pregnancy: Is Any Amount Safe?

This article explores a clear explanation of the advice on alcohol in pregnancy, why no safe limit is given, the risks, and what to do if you drank before knowing. We cover what you need to know in plain language.

## The Official Advice {#section-1}

UK guidance is clear: the safest approach is not to drink alcohol at all during pregnancy or while trying to conceive. This advice changed from previous, more permissive guidance because no level of alcohol has been proven safe. Alcohol crosses the placenta, and a developing baby cannot process it as an adult can, so any alcohol you drink reaches them. Heavy drinking is known to cause fetal alcohol spectrum disorder, which can lead to lifelong physical, behavioural, and learning difficulties. Because we cannot identify a threshold below which harm is impossible, the guidance is precautionary. This does not mean a single drink causes harm, but that the safest choice is none. If you find it hard to cut down, ask your midwife or GP for confidential support, which may include referral to a specialist service.

## If You Drank Before You Knew {#section-2}

Many women drink in early pregnancy before they know they are pregnant, and the most common reaction is anxiety. The reassuring news is that the risks of harm from a few drinks in the first weeks are very low, particularly before the placenta is fully formed and begins to pass nutrients and alcohol to the baby around week 12. The organs develop in the first trimester, and the evidence suggests that low levels of alcohol in the very early weeks rarely cause problems. The important thing is to stop drinking as soon as you know you are pregnant and to tell your midwife about any concerns. They will not judge you and can give personalised reassurance. If you drank more heavily before knowing, mention it honestly so they can advise and monitor appropriately.

## FAQ {#faq}

### Is there a safe amount of alcohol in pregnancy?

UK guidance says the safest approach is not to drink at all. No level has been proven safe, so the advice is precautionary rather than based on a proven threshold.

### I drank before I knew I was pregnant, will it harm the baby?

Low levels of alcohol in the very early weeks rarely cause harm, especially before the placenta is fully formed. Stop drinking once you know you are pregnant and tell your midwife.

### Can one drink cause fetal alcohol spectrum disorder?

FASD is linked to heavy or frequent drinking. A single small drink is very unlikely to cause harm, but because no safe level is proven, the advice is to avoid alcohol entirely.
