# Vaping in Pregnancy: Is It Safe?

This article explores an honest look at vaping in pregnancy, how it compares to smoking, what we do and don't know, and advice for those trying to quit smoking. We cover what you need to know in plain language.

## What We Know and Don't Know {#section-1}

Vaping is often promoted as safer than smoking, and for cigarette smokers it can be a useful harm reduction tool. However, 'safer than smoking' is not the same as 'safe in pregnancy'. E-cigarettes do not produce tar or carbon monoxide, the two most harmful components of cigarette smoke, which is why UK guidance accepts vaping as a step down for smokers who cannot quit another way. But e-cigarette vapour does contain nicotine, which crosses the placenta, and flavouring chemicals whose effects on a developing baby are not fully understood. Long-term data on children exposed in pregnancy is limited because vaping is relatively recent. The current consensus is that vaping is likely less harmful than smoking but not risk-free, and that the safest option is to quit both. If you vape to stay off cigarettes, this is better than returning to smoking, but aim to reduce and stop if you can.

## Practical Advice for Pregnancy {#section-2}

If you smoked before pregnancy and are using vaping to quit, talk to your midwife or stop-smoking service about the best approach. NHS guidance suggests that licensed nicotine replacement, such as patches and gum, should generally be tried first because we know more about their safety. If you are already vaping and it is keeping you off cigarettes, do not switch back to smoking, but try to reduce the nicotine strength over time and aim to stop altogether. Avoid sharing devices, keep them clean, and buy from reputable suppliers to reduce the risk of contamination or harmful additives. Be wary of unregulated products, especially those bought online or from abroad, as their contents may not be as labelled. Whatever you use, behavioural support from a stop-smoking advisor increases your chances of quitting for good.

## FAQ {#faq}

### Is vaping safer than smoking in pregnancy?

Vaping is likely less harmful than smoking because it avoids tar and carbon monoxide, but it is not risk-free. Nicotine and flavouring chemicals may affect your baby.

### Should I switch from vaping back to cigarettes?

No. If vaping is keeping you off cigarettes, do not switch back. Smoking is more harmful. Aim to reduce and stop vaping over time instead.

### Can I use vaping to quit smoking in pregnancy?

It can be a step down, but NHS guidance suggests trying licensed nicotine replacement first. Talk to your midwife or stop-smoking service about the best approach for you.
