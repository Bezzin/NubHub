# Pets in Pregnancy: Cats and Toxoplasmosis

This article explores clear advice on keeping cats and other pets during pregnancy, including toxoplasmosis risks, safe handling of litter, and reassurance. We cover what you need to know in plain language.

## Understanding the Toxoplasmosis Risk {#section-1}

Toxoplasmosis is an infection caused by a parasite that can be found in cat faeces, undercooked meat, and soil. In pregnancy, if you catch it for the first time, it can pass to the baby and cause serious problems, which is why it features in pregnancy advice. The reassuring news is that the risk from pet cats is lower than many fear. Cats only shed the parasite in their faeces for a short period after first exposure, usually as kittens, and indoor cats fed commercial food are very unlikely to carry it. Most human infections come from undercooked or cured meat, not from cats. If you have had cats for years, you may already be immune, though testing is not routine. You do not need to give up your cat, but a few precautions in pregnancy are sensible.

## Safe Handling and Other Pets {#section-2}

To reduce any risk, ask someone else to clean the cat litter tray if possible, and change it daily, as the parasite becomes infectious only after one to five days. If you must clean it, wear gloves and wash your hands thoroughly afterwards. Keep cats off kitchen surfaces and do not feed them raw meat. Wear gloves when gardening, as soil may be contaminated, and wash your hands before eating. Wash fruit and vegetables, and ensure meat is cooked through with no pink. Other pets pose little risk: dogs are safe with basic hygiene, and there is no need to rehome animals. If you keep rodents or exotic pets, ask your vet about pregnancy-specific advice. Overall, a few simple habits let you enjoy your pets safely throughout pregnancy.

## FAQ {#faq}

### Do I need to give up my cat in pregnancy?

No. With basic precautions such as having someone else change the litter and washing your hands, you can keep your cat safely. Indoor cats fed commercial food rarely carry toxoplasmosis.

### How do I catch toxoplasmosis?

Most human infections come from undercooked or cured meat or contaminated soil, not from cats. Cats only shed the parasite briefly, usually as kittens, and indoor cats are very low risk.

### Can I clean the cat litter tray while pregnant?

If possible, ask someone else. If you must, wear gloves, change the tray daily, and wash your hands. Daily changing matters because the parasite becomes infectious only after one to five days.
