# How to Modify Workouts in Early Pregnancy

If you exercised before pregnancy, you can usually continue — with modifications. Here's what to change.

## Table of Contents

1. [Modifications](#s1)
2. [Warning Signs](#s2)
3. [FAQ](#faq)

## Modifications {#s1}

- Reduce intensity if you feel nauseous or dizzy
- Avoid lying flat on your back (after 16 weeks)
- Stay hydrated
- Avoid overheating
- Listen to your body — rest when needed
- Switch high-impact to low-impact if uncomfortable

## Warning Signs {#s2}

Stop exercising if you experience: bleeding, dizziness, chest pain, calf pain, or shortness of breath. Contact your midwife.

## FAQ {#faq}

### Can I keep running in early pregnancy?

If you ran before pregnancy, yes — at a reduced intensity. Listen to your body and slow down if you feel nauseous. If you didn't run before, start with walking instead.
