# Can I Take Cough Medicine When Pregnant?

Can you take cough medicine when pregnant? Here's what you need to know.

## Quick Answer

**Cough Medicine in pregnancy:** Most cough medicines should be used with caution in pregnancy. Simple linctus or honey and lemon is safest. Avoid cough medicines containing dextromethorphan or guaifenesin without checking with your pharmacist.

## Is Cough Medicine Safe in Pregnancy? {#is-it-safe}

Most cough medicines should be used with caution in pregnancy. Simple linctus or honey and lemon is safest. Avoid cough medicines containing dextromethorphan or guaifenesin without checking with your pharmacist.

Always consult your GP, midwife, or pharmacist before taking ANY medication during pregnancy. This includes over-the-counter medicines, herbal remedies, and supplements.

## NHS Guidance {#nhs-guidance}

The NHS advises that you should always check with a healthcare professional before taking medication in pregnancy. Some medicines can harm your baby, while others are perfectly safe.

## Alternatives {#alternatives}

If cough medicine is not recommended, ask your GP or pharmacist about pregnancy-safe alternatives. There are often alternative treatments that are safe during pregnancy.

## Frequently Asked Questions {#faq}

### What if I already took cough medicine before knowing I was pregnant?

Don't panic. Taking a small amount of most medications before you knew you were pregnant is unlikely to cause harm. Contact your GP or midwife for reassurance and mention it at your booking appointment.

### Can I buy cough medicine over the counter while pregnant?

Always tell the pharmacist you're pregnant before buying any medication. They can advise whether it's safe and suggest alternatives if needed.
