## Why Dental Health Matters in Pregnancy {#section-1}

Pregnancy hormones can affect your gums, making them more prone to inflammation and bleeding, a condition sometimes called pregnancy gingivitis. Good oral health during pregnancy is important not only for your comfort but also because gum disease has been linked to a higher risk of premature birth and low birth weight. Routine dental care, including check-ups and cleaning, is safe and encouraged during pregnancy.

## Safe Treatments During Pregnancy {#section-2}

Most routine dental treatments are safe during pregnancy. These include check-ups, scale and polish, fillings, crowns, and root canal treatment if needed. The second trimester is often considered the most comfortable time for dental work, as morning sickness has usually eased and lying back is still manageable. Local anaesthetic, including lidocaine, is safe for use during pregnancy. If you need treatment, let your dentist know you are pregnant so they can tailor your care.

## Treatments to Postpone {#section-3}

Elective or cosmetic procedures, such as teeth whitening, are usually postponed until after birth because there is limited evidence on their safety during pregnancy. Non-urgent treatments that can wait, particularly those requiring general anaesthetic or sedation, are typically deferred. If you have a dental emergency, your dentist and obstetric team will work together to provide the safest possible care.

## Managing Morning Sickness and Dental Health {#section-4}

Frequent vomiting from morning sickness exposes your teeth to stomach acid, which can erode enamel over time. To protect your teeth, rinse with water after being sick and wait at least 30 minutes before brushing, as enamel is softer immediately after acid exposure. A fluoride mouthwash can help strengthen enamel. Eating small, frequent meals and avoiding very acidic foods can also reduce acid contact with your teeth.

## FAQ {#faq}

### Is dental treatment safe during pregnancy?

Yes, routine treatments such as check-ups, fillings, and cleaning are safe. Tell your dentist you are pregnant so they can plan your care appropriately.

### Can I have a filling while pregnant?

Yes, fillings can be safely placed during pregnancy using local anaesthetic. The second trimester is often the most comfortable time for treatment.

### Should I whiten my teeth while pregnant?

Teeth whitening is usually postponed until after birth due to limited safety evidence. Focus on routine cleaning and check-ups instead.
