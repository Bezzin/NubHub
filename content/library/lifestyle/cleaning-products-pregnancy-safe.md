# Cleaning Products in Pregnancy: What Is Safe

## Why Cleaning Product Safety Matters {#section-1}

Many household cleaning products contain chemicals that can irritate the skin, eyes, and respiratory system. During pregnancy, your sense of smell may be heightened, and you may feel more sensitive to strong odours. Some chemicals, particularly in poorly ventilated spaces, can cause dizziness or nausea. While most everyday cleaning products are safe when used correctly, it is worth understanding which ones to use with care.

## Products to Use with Caution {#section-2}

Strong solvents, oven cleaners, and products containing ammonia or bleach should be used in well-ventilated areas. Never mix bleach with ammonia or other cleaning products, as this produces toxic fumes. Aerosol sprays can release fine particles into the air, so consider pump sprays instead. Mould removers often contain strong chemicals, so wear gloves and a mask, or ask someone else to tackle stubborn mould.

## Safer Cleaning Alternatives {#section-3}

Simple, natural cleaners are effective for most household tasks and reduce your exposure to harsh chemicals. A solution of white vinegar and water works well for glass and surfaces. Bicarbonate of soda is a gentle abrasive that can clean sinks and ovens. Lemon juice cuts through grease and leaves a fresh scent. Microfibre cloths lift dirt without the need for strong detergents. These alternatives are affordable, pregnancy-safe, and better for the environment too.

## Practical Tips for Pregnancy Cleaning {#section-4}

Wear gloves to protect your skin, even with natural products. Open windows or use an extractor fan to keep air circulating. Clean in short bursts rather than long sessions, and take breaks if you feel tired or nauseous. If a particular smell bothers you, switch products or ask a partner or family member to help. Remember that a reasonably clean home is perfectly adequate; you do not need to sterilise every surface during pregnancy.

## FAQ {#faq}

### Is bleach safe to use during pregnancy?

Bleach is generally safe when used in a well-ventilated area with gloves. Never mix bleach with ammonia or other cleaners, as this produces toxic fumes.

### Can I clean with vinegar while pregnant?

Yes, vinegar is a safe and effective natural cleaner for most household tasks. It is a good alternative to stronger chemical products.

### Are aerosol cleaning sprays safe in pregnancy?

Aerosols can release fine particles into the air. Consider pump sprays instead, and always use cleaning products in a well-ventilated space.
