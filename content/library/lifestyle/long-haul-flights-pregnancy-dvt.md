# Long-Haul Flights in Pregnancy: DVT Risk

Pregnancy increases your risk of blood clots (DVT), and long-haul flights increase it further. Here's how to stay safe.

## Table of Contents

1. [Reducing DVT Risk](#s1)
2. [Tips](#s2)
3. [FAQ](#faq)

## Reducing DVT Risk {#s1}

- Wear compression socks (graduated compression, 15-20mmHg)
- Get up and walk every 1-2 hours
- Do ankle circles and calf exercises while seated
- Stay hydrated — avoid alcohol and caffeine
- Consider an aisle seat for easy movement

## FAQ {#faq}

### Can I fly long-haul in the first trimester?

Yes, if your pregnancy is uncomplicated. Most airlines allow flying until 36 weeks (single baby) or 32 weeks (twins). Check your airline's policy and get a fitness-to-fly letter from your midwife.
