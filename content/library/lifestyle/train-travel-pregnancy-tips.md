# Train Travel in Pregnancy: Tips and Advice

Train travel is generally safe and comfortable throughout pregnancy, and for many people it is the easiest way to get around. You can get up and stretch your legs whenever you need to, use the toilet without waiting, and avoid the tiredness of long drives. With a little planning around comfort, hydration, and rest stops, the train can be one of the most relaxing ways to travel while you are expecting.

## Before You Travel {#s1}

A few simple steps make train journeys much more comfortable:

- Book a seat in advance, ideally near the toilet and with a little extra legroom
- Travel off-peak where you can, so there is more space and a better chance of a seat
- Pack plain snacks in case of nausea, plus a refillable water bottle
- Take your maternity notes with you in your hand luggage for any longer trip
- Arrange assistance if you would like it — UK rail operators offer help through their Passenger Assist service, which you can book ahead for help getting on and off the train and with luggage

Carrying a heavy bag is harder in pregnancy, so pack light and use a wheeled case or rucksack where possible. If you feel faint or unwell in a busy carriage, don't hesitate to ask staff or fellow passengers for a seat.

## On the Journey {#s2}

Sitting still for long periods raises the small risk of blood clots in pregnancy, so on longer journeys try to stand up and walk along the carriage every 30 minutes or so, and gently flex your ankles while seated. Keep sipping water to stay hydrated, and have your snacks within reach to steady any dips in blood sugar that can make nausea worse.

Wear loose, comfortable clothing and consider compression socks for journeys lasting several hours. If you start to feel hot, faint, or queasy, get some air at the next stop if you can, and let someone know how you are feeling.

It is normal to feel a little more tired or queasy when travelling. However, seek medical advice if you develop bleeding, persistent or severe tummy pain, a painful or swollen calf, breathlessness, a severe headache, or reduced baby movements later in pregnancy. Call 111 for advice if you are unsure, or 999 in an emergency.

## FAQ {#faq}

### Is train travel safe in early pregnancy?

Yes, train travel is safe throughout pregnancy, including the early weeks. It is often more comfortable than driving or flying because you can move around freely. Just stay hydrated, keep snacks handy, and walk about regularly on longer trips.

### How can I make a long train journey more comfortable?

Book a seat near the toilet, travel off-peak for more space, wear loose clothing and compression socks, and get up to stretch your legs every half hour. Keep water and plain snacks within reach, and book Passenger Assist in advance if you would like help with luggage or boarding.

### Can I book assistance for train travel when pregnant?

Yes. UK rail operators offer Passenger Assist, which you can arrange in advance or on the day, to help you board, find your seat, and manage luggage. This can be especially helpful later in pregnancy or at busy stations.

If you have any concerns about travelling at your stage of pregnancy, speak to your midwife or GP for personal advice.
