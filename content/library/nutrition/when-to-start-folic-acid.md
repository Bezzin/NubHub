# When to Start Taking Folic Acid in Pregnancy

Knowing when to start taking folic acid can make a real difference to your pregnancy. Because the neural tube forms in the first few weeks — often before a pregnancy test is even positive — starting early is key. This guide explains the ideal timeline and what to do if you start later than planned.

## The Ideal Start Date {#ideal-start}

The NHS recommends starting a daily 400mcg folic acid supplement before you start trying to conceive — ideally at least three months before you become pregnant. This builds up your folate stores so that, from the moment of conception, your body has enough available to support the rapid early development of the neural tube.

If your pregnancy is a surprise, begin taking folic acid the day you discover you are pregnant. The neural tube closes by about week six of pregnancy, so the earlier you start, the better, but beginning at any point in the first trimester still offers benefit.

## How Long Should You Take It? {#how-long}

For most women, folic acid is taken from before conception until the end of the 12th week of pregnancy. After the first trimester, the neural tube has fully formed, and the focus shifts to other nutrients such as iron, vitamin D, and omega-3. Some clinicians suggest continuing a multivitamin containing folic acid throughout pregnancy, but the dedicated high-dose supplement is most critical in those first weeks.

## Starting Late — What Now? {#starting-late}

If you find out you are pregnant at six, eight, or even ten weeks, do not panic. Start folic acid immediately and continue until 12 weeks. Although the neural tube has often closed by this stage, folate continues to support placental growth, red blood cell production, and your baby's overall development. Mention your late start to your midwife so she can offer tailored reassurance.

## Planning Ahead {#planning-ahead}

If you are planning a pregnancy, use the preconception period wisely. Start folic acid, review your diet, cut alcohol, and begin tracking your cycle. If you have a condition such as diabetes, epilepsy, or coeliac disease, speak to your GP before trying to conceive, as you may need the higher 5mg dose prescribed from the outset.

## FAQ {#faq}

### Should I take folic acid if I'm not trying to conceive?

Because around half of pregnancies are unplanned, the NHS advises all women who could become pregnant to take 400mcg daily as a precaution. If that feels excessive, at least begin a supplement the moment you stop using contraception.

### Can I start folic acid at week 10?

Yes — start immediately. Any folate you take in the first trimester contributes to your baby's development, even if the critical neural tube window has passed.
