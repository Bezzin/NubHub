# How Many Extra Calories Do You Need in the First Trimester?

Many women assume pregnancy means eating more from day one, but the first trimester has different rules. This guide explains how many extra calories you actually need in early pregnancy and what to focus on instead of simply eating more.

## First Trimester Calorie Needs

In the first trimester, most healthy women do not need any additional calories. Your baby is still very small, and your body energy demands have not yet increased significantly. The emphasis should be on the quality of your diet rather than the quantity. Folate, iron, and vitamin D are particularly important in these early weeks. If you feel hungrier than usual, adding a small nutritious snack is fine, but there is no need to increase your overall intake substantially. Every woman is different, so listen to your body and discuss any concerns with your midwife.

## What to Focus on Instead of Calories

Rather than worrying about extra calories, prioritise nutrient-rich foods that support your baby early development. Include plenty of fruit and vegetables, wholegrains, lean protein, and dairy or fortified alternatives. Foods high in folate, such as leafy greens and fortified cereals, help reduce the risk of neural tube defects. If morning sickness limits your intake, eat whatever you can manage in small, frequent amounts. Staying hydrated is essential, especially if you are vomiting. A prenatal vitamin helps fill any nutritional gaps during this crucial stage of development.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Do I need extra calories in the first trimester?

For most women, no extra calories are needed in the first trimester; focus on eating nutrient-rich foods instead.

### Why am I so hungry in early pregnancy if I do not need extra calories?

Hormonal changes can increase appetite, but a small healthy snack is usually enough rather than significantly increasing portions.

### What if morning sickness makes it hard to eat enough in the first trimester?

Eat small, frequent amounts of whatever you can tolerate and stay hydrated; your baby will still receive nutrients from your reserves.
