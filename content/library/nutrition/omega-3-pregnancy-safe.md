# Omega-3 in Pregnancy: Is It Safe?

Omega-3 fatty acids, particularly DHA, play a key role in your baby's brain and eye development. But with conflicting advice about fish, mercury, and supplements, many pregnant women are unsure what is safe. This guide explains the benefits, the safest sources, and what to avoid.

## Why Omega-3 Matters in Pregnancy {#why-omega-3}

Omega-3 fatty acids are a family of fats that include EPA and DHA. DHA is especially important in pregnancy because it is a major structural component of your baby's brain and retina. Your baby's demand for DHA rises sharply in the third trimester and continues through the first months of life. Studies suggest that adequate omega-3 intake may support cognitive development, reduce the risk of premature birth, and contribute to a healthy birth weight.

## Safe Fish to Eat {#safe-fish}

The NHS recommends eating two portions of fish a week during pregnancy, one of which should be oily fish. Safe oily fish include salmon, trout, mackerel, herring, sardines, and pilchards. White fish such as cod, haddock, and plaice are also safe and lower in fat. Canned tuna is fine in moderation — no more than four medium cans a week — because it is lower in mercury than fresh tuna. These fish provide good amounts of DHA and are low in mercury.

## Fish to Avoid {#avoid-fish}

Avoid shark, swordfish, and marlin entirely during pregnancy because they can contain high levels of mercury, which can harm your baby's developing nervous system. Limit fresh tuna to one steak or two cans per week. Avoid raw fish such as sushi, oysters, and cold smoked fish unless they have been frozen first or cooked thoroughly, due to the risk of listeria and other infections. Avoid fish from waters known to be polluted.

## Omega-3 Supplements {#supplements}

If you do not eat fish, an omega-3 supplement derived from fish oil or algae can help you meet your needs. Look for a supplement that provides at least 200mg of DHA daily. Avoid cod liver oil supplements in pregnancy, as they contain high levels of vitamin A (retinol), which can be harmful. Algae-based supplements are a good vegan option and provide DHA directly. As with any supplement, check the label and look for products made for pregnancy.

## FAQ {#faq}

### Can I take fish oil while pregnant?

Yes, if it is a purified fish body oil supplement designed for pregnancy, providing DHA and low in vitamin A. Avoid cod liver oil. If you are unsure, an algae-based DHA supplement is a safe alternative.

### How much omega-3 do I need in pregnancy?

There is no official UK daily target, but most guidelines suggest at least 200mg of DHA per day. Eating one portion of oily fish a week, or taking a suitable supplement, will cover this.
