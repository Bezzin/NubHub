# Choline in Pregnancy: The Underrated Nutrient

Choline is often overlooked but is critical for your baby's brain development. Many prenatal vitamins don't include enough.

## Table of Contents

1. [Why Choline Matters](#s1)
2. [Sources](#s2)
3. [FAQ](#faq)

## Why Choline Matters {#s1}

Choline supports: brain development, neural tube formation, and memory function. Requirements increase in pregnancy to 450mg/day. Studies link higher choline intake to better cognitive outcomes.

## Sources {#s2}

Rich sources: eggs (especially yolks), meat, fish, dairy, and soy. One egg contains about 147mg of choline.

## FAQ {#faq}

### Does my prenatal vitamin have enough choline?

Many don't. Check the label — some popular brands contain little or no choline. You may need to increase dietary choline (eggs are excellent) or take a separate supplement.
