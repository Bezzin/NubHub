Can you eat salmon when pregnant? The short answer: **Yes - fully cooked salmon is safe and nutritious**.

## Is Salmon Safe in Pregnancy? {#is-it-safe}

Salmon is a fish that is safe to eat during pregnancy when properly prepared and stored.

The main concerns with food in pregnancy are listeria (a bacteria that can cause serious illness), toxoplasmosis (a parasite), salmonella (a bacteria), and vitamin A toxicity.

## NHS Guidance on Salmon {#nhs-guidance}

The NHS advises that yes - fully cooked salmon is safe and nutritious. Always follow NHS food safety guidance during pregnancy.

## How to Eat Salmon Safely {#how-to-eat-safely}

Salmon is safe to eat during pregnancy. Follow general food hygiene: check use-by dates, store at correct temperature, and prepare cleanly.

## Frequently Asked Questions {#faq}

### What if I accidentally ate salmon while pregnant?

Don't panic. The risk of infection is low. Watch for symptoms like fever, nausea, vomiting, or diarrhoea. If you feel unwell, contact your GP or call 111.

### Is salmon safe in the first trimester?

The same rules apply throughout pregnancy. Yes - fully cooked salmon is safe and nutritious
