# Best Pregnancy Snacks: Nutritious Ideas for Every Trimester

When you are pregnant, hunger can strike at any moment. Choosing the right pregnancy snacks helps you stay energised, manage morning sickness, and provide your growing baby with essential nutrients. This guide covers the best pregnancy snacks that are both delicious and nourishing.

## Why Snacking Matters During Pregnancy

Pregnancy increases your body demand for energy, vitamins, and minerals. Rather than three large meals, eating smaller snacks throughout the day can help stabilise blood sugar, reduce nausea, and prevent energy dips. Aim for snacks that combine protein, complex carbohydrates, and healthy fats to keep you fuller for longer. Nutrient-dense options also support fetal development, supplying folate, iron, calcium, and omega-3 fatty acids that your baby needs to grow. Keeping healthy snacks within reach makes it easier to resist less nutritious choices when cravings hit.

## Top Pregnancy Snack Ideas

Some of the best pregnancy snacks include apple slices with nut butter, Greek yoghurt with berries, hummus with carrot sticks, wholegrain crackers with cheese, and a handful of unsalted nuts. Hard-boiled eggs, edamame beans, and avocado on wholemeal toast are also excellent choices. If you are battling nausea, try dry crackers, ginger biscuits, or plain rice cakes. For a sweet tooth, blend a banana with spinach and milk for a quick smoothie. Always wash fruit and vegetables thoroughly, avoid unpasteurised dairy, and steer clear of high-mercury fish. Variety ensures you receive a wide range of nutrients across the week.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How many snacks should I eat during pregnancy?

Most pregnant women benefit from two to three nutrient-rich snacks a day alongside balanced meals, though your appetite and trimester may shift this number.

### Are snacks safe in pregnancy if I have gestational diabetes?

Yes, but choose low-glycaemic options such as nuts, cheese, and vegetables with hummus, and consult your midwife or dietitian for a tailored plan.

### What snacks help with morning sickness?

Dry crackers, ginger-based snacks, plain toast, and bland foods eaten little and often can help reduce nausea, especially in the first trimester.
