# Pregnacare vs Other Pregnancy Vitamin Brands: A Comparison

Pregnacare is the most recognised pregnancy vitamin brand in the UK, but it is not the only option. In this comparison we look at how Pregnacare stacks up against Centrum, Seven Seas, Boots, and Superdrug, so you can choose with confidence.

## Pregnacare Range Overview {#pregnacare-range}

Pregnacare offers several products. Pregnacare Conception is designed for women trying to conceive and includes 400mcg folic acid. Pregnacare Original is the standard one-tablet-a-day pregnancy multivitamin. Pregnacare Plus adds an omega-3 capsule. Pregnacare Max includes higher levels of certain nutrients and comes in larger packs. All Pregnacare products are free from artificial colours, preservatives, and gluten, and are suitable for vegetarians (though the omega-3 capsule is not vegan).

## Pregnacare vs Centrum {#vs-centrum}

Centrum Pregnancy is a single daily tablet providing folic acid, vitamin D, iron, and iodine at NHS-recommended levels. It is usually cheaper per month than Pregnacare Original and is widely available. Pregnacare includes a broader range of trace minerals such as selenium and chromium, which may or may not be useful depending on your diet. If you want a straightforward, well-priced option, Centrum is a strong contender. If you want a wider nutrient spread, Pregnacare has the edge.

## Pregnacare vs Seven Seas {#vs-seven-seas}

Seven Seas Pregnancy is another long-standing UK brand. It provides 400mcg folic acid and 10mcg vitamin D in a small, easy-to-swallow tablet. It is competitively priced and often on promotion in supermarkets and pharmacies. Compared with Pregnacare Original, Seven Seas contains fewer additional minerals but covers all the NHS essentials. It is a good choice if you prefer a simpler formulation.

## Pregnacare vs Supermarket Brands {#vs-supermarket}

Boots and Superdrug own-label pregnancy vitamins, along with supermarket options from Tesco, Asda, and Sainsbury's, meet the same basic requirements: 400mcg folic acid and 10mcg vitamin D. They are typically the cheapest per tablet and are a sensible choice if you are on a budget. The main trade-off is a shorter ingredient list and less brand-specific research, but the core nutrients are what matter most in pregnancy.

## FAQ {#faq}

### Is Pregnacare worth the extra cost?

Pregnacare offers a comprehensive formulation and good quality assurance. If you value a wide nutrient range and brand reassurance, it is worth it. If you simply need folic acid and vitamin D, a cheaper brand will serve you just as well.

### Can I switch brands during pregnancy?

Yes. There is no need to stick with one brand throughout pregnancy. Switching is fine, but avoid doubling up on supplements — check that your new product does not overlap with anything else you are taking.
