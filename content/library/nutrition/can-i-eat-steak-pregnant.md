Can you eat steak when pregnant? The short answer: **Yes - but must be well-done (no pink)**.

## Is Steak Safe in Pregnancy? {#is-it-safe}

Steak is a meat that is safe to eat during pregnancy when properly prepared and stored.

The main concerns with food in pregnancy are listeria (a bacteria that can cause serious illness), toxoplasmosis (a parasite), salmonella (a bacteria), and vitamin A toxicity.

## NHS Guidance on Steak {#nhs-guidance}

The NHS advises that yes - but must be well-done (no pink). Always follow NHS food safety guidance during pregnancy.

## How to Eat Steak Safely {#how-to-eat-safely}

Steak is safe to eat during pregnancy. Follow general food hygiene: check use-by dates, store at correct temperature, and prepare cleanly.

## Frequently Asked Questions {#faq}

### What if I accidentally ate steak while pregnant?

Don't panic. The risk of infection is low. Watch for symptoms like fever, nausea, vomiting, or diarrhoea. If you feel unwell, contact your GP or call 111.

### Is steak safe in the first trimester?

The same rules apply throughout pregnancy. Yes - but must be well-done (no pink)
