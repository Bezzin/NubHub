# Best Foods for Morning Sickness

Morning sickness affects up to 80% of pregnant women, and while it's usually worst in the first trimester, it can strike at any time of day (despite the name). The right foods can make a real difference. Here's what to eat, what to avoid, and how to keep nourished when everything makes you feel queasy.

## What Causes Morning Sickness? {#causes}

Morning sickness is caused by the rapid rise in pregnancy hormones, particularly **hCG (human chorionic gonadotropin)** and **oestrogen**. It typically starts around week 6, peaks at weeks 8-10, and eases by weeks 12-14 — though some women suffer for longer.

While you can't change your hormone levels, you can manage the symptoms through what you eat and how you eat. The key principles are: keep your blood sugar stable, avoid strong smells, eat little and often, and choose bland, easy-to-digest foods.

## Foods That Help {#help}

### Ginger
Ginger is the most well-researched natural remedy for nausea. Studies show it can significantly reduce morning sickness symptoms.
- **Ginger tea**: Sip throughout the day
- **Ginger biscuits**: Keep some by your bed for mornings
- **Crystallised ginger**: A small piece can settle nausea
- **Ginger capsules**: Ask your pharmacist (don't exceed 1g per day)
- **Ginger ale**: Choose one with real ginger, not just flavouring

### Dry, Bland Carbohydrates
These are easy on the stomach and help absorb stomach acid:
- **Plain crackers** (Ritz, Jacob's, cream crackers)
- **Toast** (plain or with a little butter)
- **Plain biscuits** (Rich Tea, digestive)
- **Rice** (plain white rice is often well-tolerated)
- **Plain pasta** (with a tiny bit of butter or olive oil)
- **Plain boiled potatoes**

### Cold Foods
Cold foods have less smell than hot foods, which makes them easier to tolerate when you're sensitive to odours:
- **Sandwiches** (with pregnancy-safe fillings)
- **Salads** (washed thoroughly)
- **Cold pasta or rice dishes**
- **Fruit** (especially melon, which many women find refreshing)
- **Yoghurt** (pasteurised)
- **Smoothies** (if you can tolerate fruit)

### Protein-Rich Foods
Protein helps stabilise blood sugar, which can reduce nausea:
- **Lean chicken** (cold or plain)
- **Eggs** (cooked thoroughly — scrambled or boiled)
- **Cheese** (hard cheeses like cheddar)
- **Nut butters** (peanut butter on toast is a popular choice)
- **Beans and lentils** (in soup — mild and comforting)

### Foods High in Vitamin B6
Vitamin B6 has been shown to reduce nausea in some women:
- **Bananas** (easy to eat and gentle on the stomach)
- **Chicken and turkey**
- **Potatoes**
- **Sunflower seeds**
- **Fortified cereals**

### Citrus
Some women find citrus fruits and scents help:
- **Lemon in water** (sipping can ease nausea)
- **Orange segments** (fresh and easy to eat)
- **Lemon or orange scent** (sniffing can settle nausea)

### Mint
Mint can soothe the stomach:
- **Peppermint tea** (caffeine-free)
- **Mints or mint sweets** (to suck on)
- **Fresh mint leaves** (in water)

## Foods to Avoid {#avoid}

When you're suffering from morning sickness, avoid:

- **Strong-smelling foods**: Curries, spicy dishes, fish being cooked
- **Fatty or greasy foods**: Fried food, rich creamy dishes, fast food
- **Very sweet foods**: Can cause blood sugar spikes and crashes
- **Spicy foods**: Can irritate the stomach
- **Acidic foods**: Tomatoes, citrus, and vinegar can worsen nausea for some
- **Caffeine**: Can irritate the stomach — stick to decaf or herbal teas
- **Large meals**: Too much food at once overwhelms the digestive system

## General Eating Strategies {#strategies}

- **Eat before you get up**: Keep crackers by your bed. Eat 2-3 before standing up.
- **Eat little and often**: 5-6 small meals rather than 3 large ones. An empty stomach makes nausea worse.
- **Never let your stomach get completely empty**: Carry snacks everywhere.
- **Eat what you can, when you can**: If only one food works for you right now, eat it. Nutrition can wait.
- **Don't worry about variety**: If you can only face toast and crackers for a week, that's fine. Your baby will take nutrients from your stores.
- **Avoid cooking smells**: Ask your partner to cook, or use cold prepared meals. Food smells are a major trigger.
- **Try acupressure wristbands**: Some women find sea-bands (acupressure wristbands) help.
- **Take your folic acid at night**: If you can't keep it down in the morning, take it before bed with a small snack.

## Drinks That Help {#drinks}

Staying hydrated is crucial, especially if you're vomiting:
- **Water**: Sip constantly rather than gulping
- **Ice lollies or ice chips**: If you can't face drinks, sucking on ice helps
- **Ginger tea**: Soothes nausea naturally
- **Peppermint tea**: Caffeine-free and calming
- **Diluted fruit juice**: Apple or cranberry, diluted with water
- **Coconut water**: Natural electrolytes if you've been vomiting
- **Fennel or chamomile tea**: Mild and soothing

Avoid:
- **Coffee and strong tea**: Caffeine can irritate the stomach
- **Fizzy drinks**: Can bloat you and make nausea worse (except ginger ale, which some women tolerate)

## When to Seek Help {#seek-help}

Morning sickness is normal, but **hyperemesis gravidarum (HG)** is a severe form of pregnancy sickness that needs medical treatment. See your GP if:

- You can't keep any food or water down for 24 hours
- You're vomiting multiple times a day
- You're losing weight
- Your urine is dark and concentrated (sign of dehydration)
- You feel dizzy, weak, or confused
- You're vomiting blood

HG affects about 1-2% of pregnant women and can be treated with anti-sickness medication, IV fluids, and sometimes hospital admission. Don't suffer in silence — help is available.

## Frequently Asked Questions {#faq}

### How long does morning sickness last?

For most women, morning sickness peaks at 8-10 weeks and eases by 12-14 weeks. Some women have it for longer, and a small percentage have it throughout pregnancy.

### Can I take anti-sickness medication in pregnancy?

Yes. Several anti-sickness medications are safe in pregnancy, including cyclizine, prochlorperazine, and ondansetron. Your GP can prescribe these if dietary measures aren't enough.

### Will morning sickness harm my baby?

No. Normal morning sickness doesn't harm your baby — they take nutrients from your stores. Even if you can only eat a limited diet for a few weeks, your baby will be fine. Severe HG that causes dehydration and weight loss needs treatment to protect both of you.
