# Drinking Enough Water in Pregnancy: A Complete Guide

Staying hydrated is one of the most important things you can do during pregnancy. Water supports your baby development, helps form the amniotic fluid, and eases common pregnancy complaints. This complete guide covers how much to drink and how to make hydration easy.

## Why Hydration Matters in Pregnancy

During pregnancy, your blood volume increases significantly, and your body uses extra water to support the placenta and amniotic fluid. Adequate hydration helps prevent constipation, urinary tract infections, and swelling, while also reducing the risk of headaches and fatigue. Dehydration can trigger contractions and may contribute to complications, so drinking enough is essential. The NHS recommends aiming for around six to eight glasses or mugs of fluid per day, with water being the best choice. If you are exercising or it is hot, you may need more. Listen to your thirst and drink consistently throughout the day rather than all at once.

## Tips for Staying Hydrated

Carry a reusable water bottle so you can sip regularly wherever you are. If plain water feels unappealing, try adding slices of lemon, cucumber, or mint for flavour. Herbal teas such as peppermint or ginger count towards your intake and can help with nausea. Milk and fortified plant milks also contribute. Limit caffeinated drinks to 200mg per day and avoid sugary fizzy drinks. If morning sickness makes fluids difficult, try ice cubes, ice lollies, or cold water in small sips. Watch for signs of dehydration such as dark urine, dizziness, or a dry mouth, and contact your midwife if symptoms persist.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much water should I drink during pregnancy?

Aim for around six to eight glasses or mugs of fluid per day, with water as the main choice, increasing in hot weather or with exercise.

### Can drinking more water help with pregnancy swelling?

Staying hydrated can help reduce fluid retention, though some swelling is normal in pregnancy; mention severe swelling to your midwife.

### What counts towards my daily fluid intake in pregnancy?

Water, herbal teas, milk, fortified plant milks, and diluted fruit juice all contribute, though water should make up the majority.
