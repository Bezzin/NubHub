## Is Brie Safe in Pregnancy? {#is-it-safe}

Brie is a soft mould-ripened cheese that should be avoided during pregnancy due to the risk of food poisoning or other complications.

The main concerns with food in pregnancy are listeria (a bacteria that can cause serious illness), toxoplasmosis (a parasite), salmonella (a bacteria), and vitamin A toxicity.

## NHS Guidance on Brie {#nhs-guidance}

The NHS advises that no - avoid brie in pregnancy unless cooked thoroughly. Always follow NHS food safety guidance during pregnancy.

## How to Eat Brie Safely {#how-to-eat-safely}

Brie should be avoided during pregnancy. If you want a similar taste, try safer alternatives like hard cheeses, pasteurised products, or thoroughly cooked options.

## Frequently Asked Questions {#faq}

### What if I accidentally ate brie while pregnant?

Don't panic. The risk of infection is low. Watch for symptoms like fever, nausea, vomiting, or diarrhoea. If you feel unwell, contact your GP or call 111.

### Is brie safe in the first trimester?

The same rules apply throughout pregnancy. No - avoid brie in pregnancy unless cooked thoroughly
