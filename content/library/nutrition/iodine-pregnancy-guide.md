# Iodine in Pregnancy

Iodine is essential for your baby's brain development and thyroid function.

## Table of Contents

1. [Why Iodine Matters](#s1)
2. [Sources](#s2)
3. [FAQ](#faq)

## Why Iodine Matters {#s1}

Iodine supports thyroid hormone production, which is critical for baby's brain development. Deficiency in pregnancy can affect cognitive development. The NHS recommends 250mcg/day.

## Sources {#s2}

Good sources: dairy products, fish, eggs, and iodised salt. UK soils are low in iodine, so dietary intake may be insufficient. Check your prenatal vitamin includes iodine.

## FAQ {#faq}

### Should I take an iodine supplement in pregnancy?

If your prenatal vitamin contains iodine and you eat dairy and fish, you likely get enough. If you're vegan or avoid dairy, discuss supplementation with your GP.
