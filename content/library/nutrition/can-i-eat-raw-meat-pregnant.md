Can you eat raw meat when pregnant? The short answer: **No - avoid raw or undercooked meat in pregnancy**.

## Is Raw Meat Safe in Pregnancy? {#is-it-safe}

Raw Meat is a meat that should be avoided during pregnancy due to the risk of food poisoning or other complications.

The main concerns with food in pregnancy are listeria (a bacteria that can cause serious illness), toxoplasmosis (a parasite), salmonella (a bacteria), and vitamin A toxicity.

## NHS Guidance on Raw Meat {#nhs-guidance}

The NHS advises that no - avoid raw or undercooked meat in pregnancy. Always follow NHS food safety guidance during pregnancy.

## How to Eat Raw Meat Safely {#how-to-eat-safely}

Raw Meat should be avoided during pregnancy. If you want a similar taste, try safer alternatives like hard cheeses, pasteurised products, or thoroughly cooked options.

## Frequently Asked Questions {#faq}

### What if I accidentally ate raw meat while pregnant?

Don't panic. The risk of infection is low. Watch for symptoms like fever, nausea, vomiting, or diarrhoea. If you feel unwell, contact your GP or call 111.

### Is raw meat safe in the first trimester?

The same rules apply throughout pregnancy. No - avoid raw or undercooked meat in pregnancy
