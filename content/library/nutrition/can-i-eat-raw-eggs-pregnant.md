## Is Raw Eggs Safe in Pregnancy? {#is-it-safe}

Raw Eggs is a eggs that should be avoided during pregnancy due to the risk of food poisoning or other complications.

The main concerns with food in pregnancy are listeria (a bacteria that can cause serious illness), toxoplasmosis (a parasite), salmonella (a bacteria), and vitamin A toxicity.

## NHS Guidance on Raw Eggs {#nhs-guidance}

The NHS advises that no - avoid raw eggs unless they have the british lion mark. Always follow NHS food safety guidance during pregnancy.

## How to Eat Raw Eggs Safely {#how-to-eat-safely}

Raw Eggs should be avoided during pregnancy. If you want a similar taste, try safer alternatives like hard cheeses, pasteurised products, or thoroughly cooked options.

## Frequently Asked Questions {#faq}

### What if I accidentally ate raw eggs while pregnant?

Don't panic. The risk of infection is low. Watch for symptoms like fever, nausea, vomiting, or diarrhoea. If you feel unwell, contact your GP or call 111.

The same rules apply throughout pregnancy. No - avoid raw eggs unless they have the British Lion mark
