Can you eat tuna when pregnant? The short answer: **Yes - limit to 2 fresh tuna steaks or 4 cans per week**.

## Is Tuna Safe in Pregnancy? {#is-it-safe}

Tuna is a fish that is safe to eat during pregnancy when properly prepared and stored.

The main concerns with food in pregnancy are listeria (a bacteria that can cause serious illness), toxoplasmosis (a parasite), salmonella (a bacteria), and vitamin A toxicity.

## NHS Guidance on Tuna {#nhs-guidance}

The NHS advises that yes - limit to 2 fresh tuna steaks or 4 cans per week. Always follow NHS food safety guidance during pregnancy.

## How to Eat Tuna Safely {#how-to-eat-safely}

Tuna is safe to eat during pregnancy. Follow general food hygiene: check use-by dates, store at correct temperature, and prepare cleanly.

## Frequently Asked Questions {#faq}

### What if I accidentally ate tuna while pregnant?

Don't panic. The risk of infection is low. Watch for symptoms like fever, nausea, vomiting, or diarrhoea. If you feel unwell, contact your GP or call 111.

### Is tuna safe in the first trimester?

The same rules apply throughout pregnancy. Yes - limit to 2 fresh tuna steaks or 4 cans per week
