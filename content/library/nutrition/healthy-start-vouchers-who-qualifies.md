# Healthy Start Vouchers: Who Qualifies?

Healthy Start is an NHS scheme that provides prepaid cards to eligible pregnant women and families with young children, which can be spent on nutritious basics like fruit, vegetables, milk, and infant formula. If money is tight during your pregnancy, it is well worth checking whether you qualify — many people who are entitled to the support do not apply for it.

## What Is Healthy Start?

Healthy Start is a government-funded scheme available in England, Wales, and Northern Ireland. It is designed to help low-income families afford nutritious food during pregnancy and early childhood. Eligible recipients receive a prepaid Healthy Start card that is loaded with funds each week. The card works like a payment card and can be used at supermarkets and other participating retailers.

Alongside the food card, Healthy Start also provides free vitamins — for pregnant women and for children under four — through participating health centres, children's centres, and some pharmacies.

## Who Qualifies

You may be eligible for Healthy Start vouchers if you are pregnant and receiving one of the following benefits:

- **Universal Credit** (where your household earned income is £408 or less per month net)
- **Income Support**
- **Income-related Employment and Support Allowance (ESA)**
- **Child Tax Credit** (but not Working Tax Credit, and with a household income under £16,190 per year)

You are also automatically eligible if you are **under 18 and pregnant**, regardless of whether you receive benefits.

If you have a child under four and you receive any of the benefits listed above, you may also qualify for ongoing support after your baby is born.

Eligibility rules can change, so it is worth checking the official NHS Healthy Start website (healthystart.nhs.uk) for the most up-to-date criteria, or asking your midwife who can advise you directly.

## What You Get

Eligible recipients receive funds loaded onto a prepaid Healthy Start card. The amount varies depending on your circumstances:

- If you are pregnant (with no other qualifying children), you receive a set amount per week
- If you have a child under one, you receive a higher weekly amount
- If you have a child aged one to four, you receive a lower weekly amount
- If you are pregnant and have a child under four, you receive payments for both

The card can be spent on:

- Plain liquid cow's milk (including long-life)
- Fresh, frozen, or tinned fruit and vegetables (plain, with no added sugar, salt, or sauce)
- Infant formula based on cow's milk
- Plain fresh, frozen, or tinned pulses (such as lentils, chickpeas, and beans)

The card cannot be used for other groceries, alcohol, or non-food items. Vitamin supplements are provided separately through the scheme via local health services.

## Free Healthy Start Vitamins

In addition to the food card, the scheme provides free vitamins:

- **Healthy Start Women's Vitamins**: Contain folic acid, vitamin C, and vitamin D — all of which are recommended during pregnancy. These are free for eligible women.
- **Healthy Start Children's Vitamins**: Contain vitamins A, C, and D for children from birth to four years old.

You can collect these vitamins from many children's centres, some GP surgeries, health visiting teams, and participating pharmacies. Ask your midwife where to collect them in your area.

## How to Apply

You can apply online at **healthystart.nhs.uk** — the application takes around ten minutes. You will need:

- Your National Insurance number
- Details of the benefits you receive
- Your midwife's name or GP surgery details (to confirm your pregnancy)

Your midwife can also give you a paper application form if you prefer, or help you apply if you are unsure about the process. Once approved, the card is posted to you and loaded automatically each week.

Apply as soon as you know you qualify — the scheme does not backdate payments, so the earlier you apply, the sooner you start receiving support.

## Frequently Asked Questions {#faq}

### How much are Healthy Start vouchers worth?

The weekly amount depends on your circumstances — pregnant women receive a set amount per week, and the rate is higher if you also have a child under one. You receive a separate amount for each qualifying pregnancy or child. Check the current rates at healthystart.nhs.uk, as amounts are reviewed periodically. The card can be spent on milk, fruit, vegetables, infant formula, and pulses.

### When can I apply for Healthy Start?

You can apply from 10 weeks pregnant, provided you meet the benefit eligibility criteria (or are under 18). Apply as early as possible — the scheme does not backdate payments, so you will miss out on support if you delay. You will need your National Insurance number, benefit details, and your midwife or GP's information.

### Can I get Healthy Start vitamins even if I don't get the food card?

The free vitamins are only automatically available through the Healthy Start scheme to those who qualify for the card. However, if you do not qualify for Healthy Start, NHS vitamin supplements are sometimes available at low or no cost through children's centres and some health visiting services. Ask your midwife what is available in your area, as provision varies locally.

*Speak to your midwife or GP if you are unsure whether you qualify for Healthy Start or need help with your application.*
