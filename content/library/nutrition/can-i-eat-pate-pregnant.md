## Is Pate Safe in Pregnancy? {#is-it-safe}

Pate is a meat product that should be avoided during pregnancy due to the risk of food poisoning or other complications.

The main concerns with food in pregnancy are listeria (a bacteria that can cause serious illness), toxoplasmosis (a parasite), salmonella (a bacteria), and vitamin A toxicity.

## NHS Guidance on Pate {#nhs-guidance}

The NHS advises that no - avoid all pate in pregnancy (liver content + listeria risk). Always follow NHS food safety guidance during pregnancy.

## How to Eat Pate Safely {#how-to-eat-safely}

Pate should be avoided during pregnancy. If you want a similar taste, try safer alternatives like hard cheeses, pasteurised products, or thoroughly cooked options.

## Frequently Asked Questions {#faq}

### What if I accidentally ate pate while pregnant?

Don't panic. The risk of infection is low. Watch for symptoms like fever, nausea, vomiting, or diarrhoea. If you feel unwell, contact your GP or call 111.

### Is pate safe in the first trimester?

The same rules apply throughout pregnancy. No - avoid all pate in pregnancy (liver content + listeria risk)
