# Salmonella Pregnancy Guide

Salmonella food poisoning in pregnancy — symptoms, risks, and prevention.

## Overview

Salmonella is a foodborne bacteria that can cause food poisoning in pregnancy. Here's what to know.

## FAQ

### Salmonella causes: diarrhoea, vomiting, fever, and abdominal cramps. It's found in raw eggs, undercooked poultry, and raw meat. In pregnancy, salmonella can cause dehydration and, rarely, complications. Prevention: cook eggs and meat thoroughly, wash hands, and avoid raw egg products (unless British Lion stamped).

Can salmonella harm my baby?

### Salmonella rarely crosses the placenta, so it's unlikely to directly harm your baby. However, severe dehydration from vomiting and diarrhoea can cause complications. Stay hydrated and contact your GP if symptoms are severe.
