## Is Coca-Cola Safe in Pregnancy? {#is-it-safe}

Coca-Cola is a drinks that is safe to eat during pregnancy when properly prepared and stored.

The main concerns with food in pregnancy are listeria (a bacteria that can cause serious illness), toxoplasmosis (a parasite), salmonella (a bacteria), and vitamin A toxicity.

## NHS Guidance on Coca-Cola {#nhs-guidance}

The NHS advises that yes - but limit due to caffeine (about 32mg per can). Always follow NHS food safety guidance during pregnancy.

## How to Eat Coca-Cola Safely {#how-to-eat-safely}

Coca-Cola is safe to eat during pregnancy. Follow general food hygiene: check use-by dates, store at correct temperature, and prepare cleanly.

## Frequently Asked Questions {#faq}

### What if I accidentally ate coca-cola while pregnant?

Don't panic. The risk of infection is low. Watch for symptoms like fever, nausea, vomiting, or diarrhoea. If you feel unwell, contact your GP or call 111.

### Is coca-cola safe in the first trimester?

The same rules apply throughout pregnancy. Yes - but limit due to caffeine (about 32mg per can)
