# Toxoplasmosis Pregnancy How Avoid

Toxoplasmosis in pregnancy — what it is, how to avoid it, and when to get tested.

## Overview

Toxoplasmosis is a parasitic infection that can harm your baby. Here's how to avoid it.

## FAQ

### Toxoplasmosis is caused by a parasite found in: undercooked meat, unwashed vegetables, cat faeces, and soil. In pregnancy, it can cause serious harm to the baby. Prevention: cook meat thoroughly, wash all fruit and vegetables, wear gloves when gardening, have someone else change cat litter, and wash hands after handling raw meat.

How common is toxoplasmosis in pregnancy?

### Toxoplasmosis is uncommon in the UK. About 0.5-1% of pregnant women become infected. However, the consequences can be serious, so prevention is important.
