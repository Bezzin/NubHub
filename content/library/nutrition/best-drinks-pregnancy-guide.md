# Best Drinks for Pregnancy: What to Sip and What to Avoid

Choosing the right drinks during pregnancy is just as important as eating well. This guide covers the best pregnancy-friendly beverages, what to limit, and which drinks to avoid entirely so you can stay hydrated and nourished safely.

## The Best Drinks for Pregnancy

Water is the top choice for pregnancy hydration, supporting blood volume, amniotic fluid, and digestion. Milk and fortified plant milks provide calcium and protein, while herbal teas such as ginger, peppermint, and rooibos can be soothing and count towards your fluid intake. Fresh fruit smoothies made with pasteurised ingredients offer vitamins, though they should be enjoyed in moderation due to natural sugars. Coconut water is a good option for electrolytes, particularly if you have been vomiting. Aim for six to eight glasses of fluid daily, with water forming the bulk of your intake.

## Drinks to Limit or Avoid

Caffeine should be limited to 200mg per day, roughly one to two cups of coffee, as high intakes are linked to pregnancy risks. Avoid alcohol entirely, as no safe level has been established. Steer clear of unpasteurised juices and milk, which can harbour harmful bacteria. Energy drinks are best avoided due to high caffeine and sugar content. Sugary fizzy drinks contribute to excess weight gain and offer little nutritional value. Herbal teas are generally safe in moderation, but some herbs, such as those containing licorice root, should be avoided. When in doubt, check with your midwife.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the best drink during pregnancy?

Water is the best drink for pregnancy, as it hydrates without caffeine, sugar, or unnecessary additives.

### Can I drink herbal tea while pregnant?

Most herbal teas like ginger, peppermint, and rooibos are safe in moderation, but avoid blends with licorice root or unverified herbs.

### How much caffeine is safe in pregnancy?

Up to 200mg per day is generally considered safe, equivalent to about one to two cups of instant coffee or two to three cups of tea.
