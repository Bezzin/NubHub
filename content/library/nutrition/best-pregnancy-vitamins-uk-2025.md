# Best Pregnancy Vitamins in the UK for 2025

Choosing a pregnancy vitamin can feel overwhelming, with shelves full of brightly coloured boxes all promising the best for your baby. In this guide we cut through the marketing and explain what to look for in a UK pregnancy multivitamin in 2025, how to compare products, and which nutrients actually matter.

## What to Look For {#what-to-look-for}

A good pregnancy multivitamin should provide at least 400mcg of folic acid (or 5mg if prescribed), 10mcg of vitamin D, and a sensible level of iron, iodine, and vitamin C. Avoid supplements containing vitamin A (retinol) in high amounts, as excess vitamin A can harm your baby. Look for the UK Informed Sport or GMP quality marks, and check that the dose is one or two tablets a day — anything requiring four or five tablets can be hard to sustain.

## Key Nutrients and Why They Matter {#key-nutrients}

Folic acid protects against neural tube defects in the first 12 weeks. Vitamin D supports bone health for you and your baby throughout pregnancy. Iron helps your blood carry more oxygen as your volume increases. Iodine supports your baby's brain development, and omega-3 DHA contributes to brain and eye development, especially in the third trimester. Calcium and magnesium are useful but are often better sourced from food.

## Comparing Popular UK Brands {#comparing-brands}

The most widely available brands in the UK include Pregnacare, Centrum Pregnancy, Seven Seas Pregnancy, and Boots and Superdrug own-label options. Pregnacare offers a wide range including Conception, Original, Plus, and Max. Centrum Pregnancy is a straightforward one-tablet option. Seven Seas is often lower priced and easy to swallow. Boots and Superdrug own-brand vitamins are usually the cheapest and meet the same basic NHS recommendations. We compare these in detail in our dedicated brand comparison guide.

## Budget vs Premium Options {#budget-vs-premium}

Premium products often include extras such as omega-3, ginger for nausea, or probiotics, but the core NHS-recommended nutrients are the same across price points. If you are on a tight budget, a supermarket own-brand pregnancy tablet plus a separate vitamin D supplement will meet your needs at a fraction of the cost. If you want a single convenient product, the mid-range options are a sensible middle ground.

## FAQ {#faq}

### Do I need a pregnancy vitamin if I eat well?

Even with a balanced diet, the NHS recommends folic acid and vitamin D supplements in pregnancy. A multivitamin is a convenient way to cover everything, but you can take individual supplements if you prefer.

### Are gummy pregnancy vitamins as good as tablets?

Gummies can be easier to take if you have nausea, but they often contain less iron and more sugar. Check the label and consider switching to tablets once your nausea eases.
