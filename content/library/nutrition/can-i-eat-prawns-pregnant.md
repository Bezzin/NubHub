## Is Prawns Safe in Pregnancy? {#is-it-safe}

Prawns is a shellfish that is safe to eat during pregnancy when properly prepared and stored.

The main concerns with food in pregnancy are listeria (a bacteria that can cause serious illness), toxoplasmosis (a parasite), salmonella (a bacteria), and vitamin A toxicity.

## NHS Guidance on Prawns {#nhs-guidance}

The NHS advises that yes - cook thoroughly until pink and hot. Always follow NHS food safety guidance during pregnancy.

## How to Eat Prawns Safely {#how-to-eat-safely}

Prawns is safe to eat during pregnancy. Follow general food hygiene: check use-by dates, store at correct temperature, and prepare cleanly.

## Frequently Asked Questions {#faq}

### What if I accidentally ate prawns while pregnant?

Don't panic. The risk of infection is low. Watch for symptoms like fever, nausea, vomiting, or diarrhoea. If you feel unwell, contact your GP or call 111.

### Is prawns safe in the first trimester?

The same rules apply throughout pregnancy. Yes - cook thoroughly until pink and hot
