# What to Do If You Think You're Miscarrying

If you think you're miscarrying, here's exactly what to do, step by step.

## Step 1: Don't Panic

Bleeding doesn't always mean miscarriage. Up to 25% of women bleed in early pregnancy and many have healthy babies.

## Step 2: Contact the Right Service

- Call your GP during surgery hours
- Call your midwife or EPU directly
- Call 111 out of hours
- Go to A&E only if bleeding is very heavy or pain is severe

## Step 3: What Will Happen

You'll be seen by the EPU. They'll do: an ultrasound to check the baby, blood tests to check hCG levels, and possibly a vaginal exam. You'll get a diagnosis and a plan.

## FAQ

### Should I go to A&E if I'm bleeding?

For light bleeding, call your GP or EPU. For heavy bleeding (soaking a pad in an hour) or severe pain, go to A&E or call 999.

### What should I bring to the EPU?

Bring your maternity notes, a pad (not a tampon), and a support person if you'd like one. The EPU will arrange a scan to check the baby.
