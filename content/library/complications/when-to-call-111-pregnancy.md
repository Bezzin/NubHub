# When to Call 111 in Pregnancy: Warning Signs

Knowing when to call 111 in pregnancy can be confusing. This guide covers the warning signs that need medical attention and when to seek emergency help.

## When to Call 111 {#call-111}

Call 111 if you experience: heavy bleeding; severe abdominal pain; fever (38C+); persistent vomiting (can't keep fluids down 24+ hours); signs of UTI (painful urination, blood in urine); sudden swelling; severe headache that won't go away; visual disturbances.

## When to Go to A&E {#go-ae}

Go to A&E or call 999 if: you're bleeding heavily (soaking a pad in an hour); you have severe, doubling-over pain; you collapse or feel very faint; you have chest pain or shortness of breath; you pass out.

## When to Call Your Midwife {#call-midwife}

Call your midwife for: mild bleeding or spotting; mild cramping; questions about medications; general pregnancy concerns; changes in symptoms; to arrange appointments.

## Red Flag Symptoms {#red-flags}

Always seek urgent help for: severe pain; heavy bleeding; high fever; inability to keep fluids down; shoulder pain with bleeding (possible ectopic); sudden severe headache; blurred vision.

## Frequently Asked Questions {#faq}

### Should I call 111 or my midwife first?

For non-emergency concerns, call your midwife first. For urgent symptoms that aren't emergencies, call 111. For emergencies (heavy bleeding, severe pain, collapse), call 999 or go to A&E.

### Is it OK to call 111 for pregnancy concerns?

Yes. NHS 111 is available 24/7 and can direct you to the right service. They're used to pregnancy-related calls and can advise whether you need to see a GP, go to the EPU, or go to A&E.
