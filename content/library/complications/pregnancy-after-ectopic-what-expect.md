# Pregnancy After Ectopic What Expect

Pregnancy after an ectopic pregnancy — chances of success, monitoring, and support.

## Overview

Getting pregnant after an ectopic pregnancy is possible. Here's what to expect.

## FAQ

### Most women who have had an ectopic pregnancy can conceive again. Your chances of a successful pregnancy are approximately 65%. You'll be offered extra monitoring — an early scan at 6-7 weeks to confirm the pregnancy is in the right place. If you had one fallopian tube removed, you can still conceive with the other.

What are my chances of a normal pregnancy after ectopic?

### Approximately 65% of women who have had an ectopic pregnancy go on to have a successful pregnancy. Your chances depend on the condition of your remaining fallopian tube(s).
