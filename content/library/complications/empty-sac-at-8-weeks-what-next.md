# Empty Sac at 8 Weeks: What Next?

At 8 weeks, an ongoing pregnancy should clearly show an embryo with a heartbeat on a transvaginal scan. Finding an empty sac at this stage is highly likely to indicate a missed miscarriage, also known as an anembryonic pregnancy or blighted ovum. It is a devastating moment, but understanding what it means and what comes next can help you feel more in control.

## Confirming the Diagnosis

By 8 weeks, dating uncertainty is much less likely to be the explanation. NICE guidelines state that a missed miscarriage can be diagnosed if the mean sac diameter is 25mm or more with no embryo, or if a previously seen embryo has no heartbeat and has not grown. If there is any ambiguity or you are unsure of your dates, a second scan a week later may still be offered for certainty, but at 8 weeks a single scan is often diagnostic. Ask the sonographer to explain the measurements so you understand the basis for the diagnosis.

## Moving Forward After the Diagnosis

Once confirmed, you will discuss management options with your EPU team. Expectant management allows natural passage over one to two weeks but can be emotionally draining due to waiting. Medical management with mifepristone and misoprostol offers more control over timing and is often completed within 24 hours. Surgical management (manual vacuum aspiration or ERPC) provides the quickest resolution and is useful if you want to avoid seeing the process. Take time to decide, and ask about follow-up support and trying again.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### 1. Can an empty sac at 8 weeks still be a viable pregnancy?

It is very unlikely. By 8 weeks, an embryo with a heartbeat should be clearly visible.

### 2. Will I need a second scan to confirm?

Often not at 8 weeks if measurements are clear, but some units offer one if there is any uncertainty.

### 3. When can I try to conceive again?

Most women can try after one normal period, but take the emotional time you need and discuss with your team.
