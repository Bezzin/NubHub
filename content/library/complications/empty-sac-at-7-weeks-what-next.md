# Empty Sac at 7 Weeks: What Next?

By 7 weeks, most ongoing pregnancies show an embryo with a visible heartbeat on a transvaginal scan. Finding an empty sac at this gestation is more concerning than at 6 weeks, but it still does not automatically mean the pregnancy has failed: dating uncertainty and late ovulation remain possible explanations that a follow-up scan will clarify.

## Interpreting the Findings at 7 Weeks

At 7 weeks, a transvaginal scan should typically show a yolk sac and fetal pole with cardiac activity. If only a gestational sac is seen, the sonographer will measure its mean sac diameter and compare it to your dating. If the sac is large enough that an embryo should be visible (typically 25mm mean sac diameter with no embryo), guidelines indicate a failed pregnancy. However, if the sac is smaller and your dates are uncertain, a repeat scan in 7 to 14 days is standard practice before a diagnosis is made.

## Your Options After a Confirmed Diagnosis

If the follow-up scan confirms the pregnancy is not viable, you will be offered three management options. Expectant management lets your body miscarry naturally over the following weeks. Medical management uses tablets to bring on the miscarriage, usually at home or in hospital. Surgical management, often via manual vacuum aspiration under local or general anaesthetic, is the quickest option. All three are safe and your choice depends on your circumstances and preferences.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### 1. Is an empty sac at 7 weeks always a miscarriage?

Not always. If dating is uncertain, a repeat scan may reveal a developing pregnancy.

### 2. What if my sac has grown but still has no embryo?

Growth alone is not reassuring. The presence of an embryo of the expected size with a heartbeat is the key marker.

### 3. Which miscarriage management option is best?

There is no single best option. Each has different timelines and experiences, and you should choose what feels right for you.
