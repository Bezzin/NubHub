# Miscarriage Support Groups in the UK

This article explores find miscarriage support groups and services in the UK, including charities, helplines, online communities, and how to access counselling. We cover what you need to know in plain language.

## National Charities and Helplines {#section-1}

Several UK charities offer support after miscarriage. The Miscarriage Association provides a helpline, email support, and a website with resources written by and for those affected. Tommy's runs a pregnancy information line and funds miscarriage research, with practical advice online. SANDS supports anyone affected by pregnancy loss and the death of a baby, including later losses. The Lullaby Trust focuses on safer sleep but offers bereavement support. Cruse Bereavement Support helps with grief more broadly and is free to access. These services are confidential, and many offer evening helplines for those struggling at night, often the hardest time. You do not need a referral and can contact them as many times as you need.

## Local and Online Support {#section-2}

Beyond national charities, many NHS trusts run local pregnancy loss support groups or can signpost you to nearby services. Ask your GP or early pregnancy unit what is available in your area. Online communities can be invaluable, especially for those without local access or who prefer anonymity. The Miscarriage Association runs a private Facebook group, and forums such as those hosted by Tommy's allow you to read others' experiences and share your own. Some people find writing about their loss helpful, while others prefer to listen. Free counselling may be available through your GP, and specialist perinatal mental health services can be accessed for persistent or complex grief. There is no right way to grieve, and support can help whenever you feel ready.

## FAQ {#faq}

### Is miscarriage support free in the UK?

Yes. Charity helplines, online forums, and GP-referred counselling are free. NHS perinatal mental health services are also free, though waiting times vary.

### Do I need a referral to use these services?

No. Most charity helplines and online groups accept self-contact. For NHS counselling, ask your GP, who can refer you to local services.

### Can I get counselling after one miscarriage?

Yes. Support is available after a single loss, not only after recurrent miscarriage. If grief is affecting your daily life, ask your GP about counselling options.
