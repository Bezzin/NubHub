# Should You Go to A&E or the EPU?

Knowing where to go when something's wrong in early pregnancy can be confusing. Here's a guide.

## When to Go to the EPU

The Early Pregnancy Unit (EPU) is the best place for pregnancy-related concerns: bleeding, cramping, suspected miscarriage, or ectopic pregnancy symptoms. They can scan you quickly and give a diagnosis.

## When to Go to A&E

Go to A&E or call 999 for: very heavy bleeding (soaking a pad in an hour), severe abdominal pain, collapse or fainting, shoulder pain with bleeding, or fever with bleeding.

## When to Call 111

Call 111 if you're unsure where to go. They can direct you to the right service — EPU, GP, or A&E — and are available 24/7.

## FAQ

### Can I self-refer to the EPU?

Some EPUs accept self-referrals. Others require a GP or midwife referral. Check your local EPU's policy. If in doubt, call your GP or 111.

### What's the difference between A&E and EPU?

The EPU specialises in early pregnancy problems and can do ultrasounds quickly. A&E handles emergencies. For pregnancy bleeding, the EPU is usually better if available. A&E is for severe emergencies.
