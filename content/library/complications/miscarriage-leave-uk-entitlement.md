# Miscarriage Leave UK: What You're Entitled To

Understanding your leave entitlements after a miscarriage can be confusing. Here's a clear guide.

## Before 24 Weeks

Miscarriage before 24 weeks is treated as illness. You're entitled to: sick leave through your employer, Statutory Sick Pay if you qualify (£109.40/week), and GP sick notes for as long as needed.

## After 24 Weeks

Loss after 24 weeks is classified as stillbirth. You're entitled to: full Statutory Maternity Pay (SMP), 39 weeks of maternity leave, and additional support from SANDS.

## FAQ

### How much time off can I take after a miscarriage?

As much as you need. Your GP can sign you off for as long as necessary. There's no limit on sick leave for miscarriage recovery — both physical and emotional.

### Do I need a sick note after a miscarriage?

For the first 7 days, you can self-certify. After 7 days, you need a sick note from your GP. Tell your GP what's happened and they'll sign you off for as long as you need.
