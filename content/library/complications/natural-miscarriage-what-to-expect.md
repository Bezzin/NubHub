# Natural Miscarriage: What to Expect

This article explores a practical guide to natural or expectant management of miscarriage, including what happens physically, how long it takes, and when to seek help. We cover what you need to know in plain language.

## What Happens Physically {#section-1}

Natural, or expectant, management means allowing your body to pass the pregnancy without surgery or medication. The process usually begins with cramping and bleeding, similar to a heavy period, which intensifies over hours or days. You may pass clots and tissue, and the bleeding can be heavier than you expect. Pain is usually managed with paracetamol and ibuprofen, though stronger pain relief may be needed and your GP can prescribe it. The bleeding typically lasts one to two weeks, tapering to spotting. It is helpful to have maternity pads, loose clothing, and someone with you for support. Knowing what to expect can reduce fear, though the emotional impact is often harder to prepare for than the physical.

## Timeline, Risks and When to Seek Help {#section-2}

If the loss is diagnosed at a scan, natural passing can take one to three weeks to begin, and the whole process may take longer. If bleeding has not started within two weeks, or the miscarriage is incomplete with ongoing bleeding, your team may suggest medical or surgical management. Complications are uncommon but include infection, heavy bleeding requiring transfusion, or retained tissue. Seek urgent help if you soak through a pad in an hour, feel dizzy or faint, develop a high temperature or unpleasant discharge, or have severe pain not eased by painkillers. A follow-up scan checks the miscarriage is complete. If you are trying again, most clinicians advise waiting until you have had one period, though there is no need to wait longer if you feel ready.

## FAQ {#faq}

### How long does a natural miscarriage take?

Once bleeding starts, it usually lasts one to two weeks. If the loss was diagnosed at a scan, natural passing can take one to three weeks to begin.

### How much bleeding is too much?

Seek urgent help if you soak through a maternity pad in an hour, feel dizzy or faint, or pass very large clots. These may indicate heavy bleeding needing treatment.

### When can I try to conceive again after a natural miscarriage?

Most clinicians advise waiting until you have had one period so dating is easier, but there is no medical need to wait longer if you feel emotionally ready.
