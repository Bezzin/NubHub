# What Causes Miscarriage in the First Trimester?

This article explores explore the main causes of first trimester miscarriage, from chromosomal problems to lifestyle factors, and learn what is and isn't to blame. We cover what you need to know in plain language.

## The Main Cause: Chromosomal Problems {#section-1}

Around half to two thirds of first trimester miscarriages are caused by chromosomal abnormalities in the embryo. When an egg or sperm carries too many or too few chromosomes, the embryo cannot develop normally and the pregnancy ends. These errors are usually random and not inherited from either parent. They become more common as a woman ages, which is why miscarriage rates rise after 35. Importantly, chromosomal problems are nobody's fault: they happen at conception and cannot be prevented by diet, rest, or supplements. Other genetic factors, such as balanced translocations in one parent, can cause recurrent loss, but these are rare. Understanding that most first trimester losses are random chromosomal events can help reduce guilt, which is a common and painful response after miscarriage.

## Other Causes and Risk Factors {#section-2}

Beyond chromosomal issues, several medical and lifestyle factors can raise miscarriage risk. These include uncontrolled diabetes or thyroid disease, blood clotting disorders such as antiphospholipid syndrome, structural problems with the uterus or cervix, and infections like listeria or toxoplasmosis. Smoking, heavy alcohol use, and high caffeine intake are also linked to higher risk. Being significantly overweight or underweight can affect fertility and pregnancy outcomes. Some medications are unsafe in pregnancy, so always review yours with a clinician. Despite this long list, in many cases no clear cause is found, and that uncertainty can be hard to live with. If you have had two or three losses, ask your GP about referral for recurrent miscarriage testing, which can identify treatable factors.

## FAQ {#faq}

### Can stress cause a miscarriage?

No. There is no good evidence that everyday stress, work stress, or a single upsetting event causes miscarriage. Most losses are due to chromosomal problems that occur at conception.

### Does lifting or exercise cause miscarriage?

No. Normal activity, exercise, and lifting everyday objects do not cause miscarriage. Heavy, very high-impact exercise may carry some risk, but moderate exercise is safe and recommended in pregnancy.

### Can I prevent a first trimester miscarriage?

In most cases, no. Most first trimester miscarriages are caused by random chromosomal problems that cannot be prevented. Managing conditions like diabetes and avoiding smoking and alcohol can reduce risk, but cannot eliminate it.
