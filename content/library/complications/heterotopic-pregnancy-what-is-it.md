# Heterotopic Pregnancy What Is It

Heterotopic pregnancy — a rare condition where one baby is in the uterus and one is ectopic.

## Overview

A heterotopic pregnancy is extremely rare but important to know about.

## FAQ

### A heterotopic pregnancy is when there is both a normal intrauterine pregnancy AND an ectopic pregnancy simultaneously. It's very rare (1 in 30,000 natural pregnancies, higher with IVF). Symptoms include abdominal pain and bleeding despite a confirmed intrauterine pregnancy. It requires urgent surgical treatment.

How common is heterotopic pregnancy?

### Very rare — about 1 in 30,000 natural pregnancies. The risk is higher with IVF (up to 1 in 100). If you have IVF and experience pain despite seeing a heartbeat in the uterus, your doctor should check for heterotopic pregnancy.
