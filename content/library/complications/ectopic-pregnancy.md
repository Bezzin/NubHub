# Ectopic Pregnancy: Symptoms, Treatment & Recovery

An ectopic pregnancy is a serious condition where the embryo implants outside the uterus, usually in a fallopian tube. It affects approximately 1 in 90 pregnancies. Here's what you need to know.

## What Is an Ectopic Pregnancy? {#what-is-it}

An ectopic pregnancy occurs when a fertilised egg implants outside the uterus - most commonly in a fallopian tube. The pregnancy cannot survive and, if untreated, can be life-threatening.

## Symptoms {#symptoms}

Symptoms typically appear between weeks 4 and 10 and include: severe abdominal pain (often one-sided); vaginal bleeding; shoulder tip pain; weakness or dizziness; bowel pain or pain on going to the toilet. If you experience these, seek urgent medical help.

## Diagnosis {#diagnosis}

Diagnosis involves: blood tests to check hCG levels; ultrasound (transvaginal is usually needed); possibly laparoscopy. If ectopic is confirmed, treatment is needed urgently.

## Treatment {#treatment}

Treatment options include: medication (methotrexate) to stop the pregnancy if caught early; surgery (laparoscopy) to remove the pregnancy, sometimes with the fallopian tube; expectant management (watch and wait) in some cases.

## Future Fertility {#fertility}

Most women who have had an ectopic pregnancy can conceive again. If one fallopian tube was removed, you can still conceive with the other. IVF is an option if both tubes are affected.

## Frequently Asked Questions {#faq}

### How do I know if my pain is ectopic?

If you have one-sided abdominal pain, bleeding, and dizziness, especially between weeks 4-10, seek urgent medical attention. Ectopic pregnancy is a medical emergency. Call 111 or go to A&E.

### Can I get pregnant again after an ectopic?

Yes. Most women who have had an ectopic pregnancy go on to have successful pregnancies. You'll be offered extra monitoring in future pregnancies.
