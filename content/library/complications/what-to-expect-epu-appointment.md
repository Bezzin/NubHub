# What to Expect at Your EPU Appointment

Being referred to the early pregnancy unit (EPU) can feel daunting, but knowing what to expect makes the visit less stressful. EPUs see women in the first trimester for bleeding, pain, previous loss, or reassurance scans, and they are set up to provide answers quickly and compassionately.

## What Happens During the Appointment

You will usually be asked about your symptoms, medical history, and last period. A nurse or doctor may take blood tests to check your hCG levels and blood group, and a transvaginal ultrasound is often performed to locate the pregnancy and check the heartbeat. Depending on your gestation, you may also be offered a follow-up scan one to two weeks later if the pregnancy is too early to assess fully. Staff understand that attending the EPU is anxiety-inducing, and you can bring a partner or friend for support.

## How to Prepare and What to Bring

Wear loose, comfortable clothing as you may need to undress from the waist down. Bring your pregnancy notes if you have them, a list of medications, and details of your last period. Try to have a fairly full bladder if you think an abdominal scan may be offered, though you will be asked to empty it for a vaginal scan. Write down any questions in advance so you do not forget them in the moment.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### 1. Will I definitely get a scan at the EPU?

Most women do, but if you are very early in pregnancy a blood test may be done first with a scan booked later.

### 2. Can my partner come with me?

Yes, you can bring one support person. Check your local unit's current policy on visitor numbers.

### 3. How long does the appointment take?

Expect to be there for one to three hours, as blood results and scan reviews can take time.
