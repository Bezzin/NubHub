# Pregnant And Self Employed Rights Complete

Complete guide to rights and support for self-employed pregnant women in the UK.

## Overview

Self-employed and pregnant? Here's your complete guide to rights and support.

## FAQ

### Self-employed women can claim Maternity Allowance (£172.48/week for 39 weeks) instead of Statutory Maternity Pay. You need 26 weeks of NI contributions. You may also get: Universal Credit, Healthy Start vouchers, free prescriptions, Sure Start Maternity Grant. You have no employer obligations but should plan your workload and client communications.

How do I claim Maternity Allowance as self-employed?

### Claim through the Jobcentre Plus. You need form MA1, available online or from your midwife. You need 26 weeks of NI contributions in the 66 weeks before your due date. Payments start up to 11 weeks before your due date.
