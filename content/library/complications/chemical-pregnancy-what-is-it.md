## What Is a Chemical Pregnancy? {#section-1}

A chemical pregnancy is a very early pregnancy loss that occurs shortly after implantation, usually before five or six weeks. It is called a chemical pregnancy because it is detected by a positive pregnancy test, which measures the hormone hCG, but an ultrasound would not show a gestational sac or embryo. Many women who experience a chemical pregnancy never realised they were pregnant, while others may have been actively trying and testing early.

## Symptoms and Signs {#section-2}

A chemical pregnancy often feels like a normal period, sometimes slightly heavier or later than usual. You may notice mild cramping and bleeding a few days to a week after your expected period. If you had a positive test, you may notice it fading or a follow-up test coming back negative. Some women experience no symptoms at all. Because the loss happens so early, physical recovery is usually straightforward, though the emotional impact can still be significant.

## Why Chemical Pregnancies Happen {#section-3}

Most chemical pregnancies are caused by chromosomal abnormalities in the fertilised egg, which prevent it from developing beyond the earliest stage. Other factors can include hormonal imbalances, issues with the uterine lining, or underlying health conditions. These losses are common and are not caused by anything you did. They are not linked to exercise, stress, sex, or lifting. The body naturally stops a pregnancy that cannot develop normally, even before most women would know they are pregnant.

## Coping and Looking Ahead {#section-4}

Even a very early loss can bring genuine grief, especially if you were trying to conceive or had begun to imagine a future with this baby. Your feelings are valid, whatever they are. Talking to your partner or a trusted friend can help, and support groups for early loss offer understanding from others who have been there. Most women who experience a chemical pregnancy go on to conceive again, often without difficulty. If you have repeated early losses, your GP can arrange tests to check for any underlying causes.

## FAQ {#faq}

### What is a chemical pregnancy?

A chemical pregnancy is a very early loss, usually before five or six weeks, detected by a positive pregnancy test but not visible on a scan. It often feels like a slightly late or heavy period.

### Can I get pregnant again after a chemical pregnancy?

Yes. Most women who experience a chemical pregnancy conceive again and go on to have healthy pregnancies. If you have repeated early losses, speak to your GP about investigations.

### Did I do something to cause it?

No. Chemical pregnancies are almost always caused by chromosomal abnormalities in the egg and are not linked to anything you did or could have prevented.
