# Pregnancy and OCD: How to Manage It

OCD can worsen during pregnancy or appear for the first time. Perinatal OCD is more common than people realise.

## Table of Contents

1. [Perinatal OCD Symptoms](#section-1)
2. [Getting Help](#section-2)
3. [FAQ](#faq)

## Perinatal OCD Symptoms {#section-1}

- Intrusive thoughts about harm coming to the baby
- Compulsive checking (monitor, temperature, etc.)
- Excessive cleaning or hand washing
- Reassurance-seeking from healthcare providers
- Avoidance of certain situations

These thoughts are distressing but they do NOT mean you will act on them.

## Getting Help {#section-2}

- Tell your GP or midwife — this is common and treatable
- Ask about CBT (specifically ERP therapy)
- Some medications are safe in pregnancy for OCD
- Don't suffer in silence — perinatal OCD responds well to treatment

## FAQ {#faq}

### Can pregnancy trigger OCD?

Yes. Hormonal changes can trigger OCD or worsen existing symptoms. Perinatal OCD is well-recognised and treatable.

### Are intrusive thoughts normal in pregnancy?

Intrusive thoughts are common in perinatal OCD. They're distressing but they don't mean you're a danger to your baby. Getting help is the right thing to do.
