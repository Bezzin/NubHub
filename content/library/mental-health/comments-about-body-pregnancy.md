# Coping with Comments About Your Body in Pregnancy

"You're huge!" "Are you sure there's only one in there?" "You barely look pregnant — is the baby OK?" Pregnancy turns your body into public property, and the comments can start before you've even announced. Learning to handle them protects your mental health and your relationship with your changing body.

## Why people feel entitled to comment
Pregnancy is culturally treated as a shared event. Many people genuinely think they're being affectionate or funny. But intent doesn't erase impact — and repeated remarks about size, shape, or weight can chip away at body image, especially if you've struggled with it before.

## Common comment patterns
- **Size remarks.** "Too big" or "too small" — both feel like judgment.
- **Weight commentary.** "You're all bump" versus "You've put on weight everywhere."
- **Comparisons.** "My sister was much smaller at that stage."
- **Warnings.** "Just wait, it gets worse." or "You'll never get your body back."

## Why they hurt
Your body is changing rapidly and not always in ways you control. Stretch marks, swelling, and a shifting centre of gravity can already feel unfamiliar. Comments amplify the sense that your body is being watched and graded.

## How to respond
- **The neutral redirect.** "Bodies do what they need to in pregnancy. How are you?"
- **The honest line.** "I'm trying not to focus on size, so I'd rather not talk about it."
- **The humour deflection.** "Yep, housing a whole human — it's a big job!"
- **The firm stop.** "I'm not comfortable with comments about my body. Thanks for understanding."

## Protecting yourself
- **Curate your environment.** Limit time with serial commentators.
- **Focus on function.** Remind yourself what your body is doing: growing a baby, adapting, preparing.
- **Unfollow comparison content.** "Bump influencers" can distort your sense of normal.
- **Talk to someone.** If body image distress is affecting your mood or eating, tell your midwife or GP.

## A reminder
Your body is doing something remarkable and demanding. It doesn't exist for commentary. You get to decide what's said about it — and to you.
