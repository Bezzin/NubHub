# Anxiety in Early Pregnancy: How Common Is It?

If you feel anxious in the first few weeks of pregnancy, you are in good company. Research suggests that anxiety in early pregnancy is one of the most common emotional changes expectant mothers report, yet it is rarely talked about openly. Understanding how widespread it is can help reduce the shame that often surrounds it.

## How common is anxiety in early pregnancy {#section-1}

UK studies estimate that around one in five women experience significant anxiety during pregnancy, with rates highest in the first trimester. The uncertainty of early pregnancy, combined with physical symptoms and hormone shifts, makes this period particularly anxiety-provoking. Most women never report these feelings, meaning the true figure is likely higher.

## Why early pregnancy triggers anxiety {#section-2}

Common triggers include fear of miscarriage, worries about the health of the baby, concerns about how life will change, relationship pressures, and previous pregnancy loss. Even planned and wanted pregnancies can bring anxiety, because the early weeks involve so much that is outside your control.

## FAQ {#faq}

### Is anxiety in early pregnancy normal?

Yes. It is one of the most common experiences reported by pregnant women, especially in the first trimester.

### Will anxiety harm my baby?

Everyday anxiety does not harm your baby. Severe or prolonged anxiety can affect your wellbeing, so speak to your midwife if you are struggling.

### When should I seek help for pregnancy anxiety?

If anxiety interferes with daily life, sleep, or eating, or you experience panic attacks, contact your GP or midwife.
