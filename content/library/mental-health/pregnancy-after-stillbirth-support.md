# Pregnancy After Stillbirth: Support and Coping

Pregnancy after stillbirth is one of the most emotionally complex experiences a mother can go through. You're grieving one baby while carrying another.

## Table of Contents

1. [The Emotional Complexity](#section-1)
2. [Getting Support](#section-2)
3. [FAQ](#faq)

## The Emotional Complexity {#section-1}

You may feel:
- Guilt for being pregnant again
- Fear that lightning will strike twice
- Inability to bond with the new baby
- Grief that intensifies rather than fades
- Pressure to be happy when you're terrified

## Getting Support {#section-2}

- Ask for extra monitoring — your team should offer this
- Consider a specialist perinatal mental health referral
- Connect with Sands (stillbirth charity) support groups
- Consider counselling specific to pregnancy after loss
- Create a memory box or ritual for your lost baby

## FAQ {#faq}

### Is pregnancy after stillbirth high risk?

You'll be offered enhanced monitoring throughout your pregnancy. Your care team will create a personalised plan. Many women go on to have successful pregnancies after stillbirth.

### How do I cope with the fear?

Take it one day at a time. Accept that fear is normal. Lean on your support network and professional help. Each scan and milestone is a step forward.
