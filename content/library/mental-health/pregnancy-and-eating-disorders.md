# Pregnancy and Eating Disorders: Support Guide

If you have an eating disorder history, pregnancy body changes can be challenging. Here's how to get support.

## Table of Contents

1. [The Challenges](#section-1)
2. [Getting Support](#section-2)
3. [FAQ](#faq)

## The Challenges {#section-1}

- Weight gain can trigger eating disorder thoughts
- Body changes may cause distress
- Morning sickness can feel like a 'reward' for not eating
- Pressure to 'bounce back' after birth

## Getting Support {#section-2}

- Tell your midwife about your history
- Ask for a perinatal mental health referral
- Consider working with an eating disorder specialist
- Focus on nutrition for your baby
- Avoid weighing yourself if it's triggering

## FAQ {#faq}

### Can pregnancy trigger an eating disorder relapse?

Yes. Body changes, weight gain, and hormonal shifts can trigger eating disorder thoughts. Professional support during pregnancy is important.

### Is it safe to be pregnant with an eating disorder?

With proper support, many women with eating disorder histories have healthy pregnancies. Tell your healthcare team so they can support you.
