# Coping with the Wait Between Scan Appointments

## Why the Wait Feels Long {#section-1}

Between pregnancy scans, especially in the early weeks, the wait can feel endless. Scans offer reassurance that your baby is growing well, and the gap between them can feel like a long stretch of uncertainty. This is particularly true after the dating scan and before the 20-week anomaly scan, when several weeks pass without visual confirmation. It is natural to want reassurance, and feeling anxious during these gaps is very common.

## What Fuels Scan Anxiety {#section-2}

Anxiety between scans can be driven by previous losses, a difficult earlier pregnancy, or simply the natural wish to know your baby is okay. If you have had bleeding, pain, or a previous complicated pregnancy, the wait can carry extra weight. Social media and forums can amplify anxiety if you read about others' difficult experiences. Sometimes anxiety increases as a scan approaches rather than easing, because the appointment itself feels like a moment of judgement.

## Practical Ways to Cope {#section-3}

Mark the halfway point of the wait with something small, such as a treat or a walk, so the time feels more manageable. Focus on milestones you can track yourself, such as feeling movement once you reach that stage. Limit checking symptoms, as day-to-day changes are normal and do not necessarily indicate a problem. Plan something enjoyable for the day of the scan so you have a positive focus regardless of the outcome. Share how you feel with someone supportive.

## When to Seek Extra Support {#section-4}

If anxiety between scans is affecting your sleep, mood, or daily life, it is worth seeking support. Your midwife can offer reassurance, and in some cases may arrange an extra check if there is a clinical reason. Perinatal mental health services are available for pregnancy-related anxiety and can provide strategies tailored to you. You do not have to wait until things feel overwhelming; reaching out early is a sign of strength, not weakness.

## FAQ {#faq}

### Why do I feel anxious between scans?

Scans provide reassurance, and the gaps between them can feel uncertain. Previous losses, complications, or reading worrying stories can add to this anxiety. It is very common.

### How can I cope with the wait between scans?

Mark the halfway point with something positive, track milestones like movement, limit symptom-checking, plan something for scan day, and talk to someone supportive about how you feel.

### Can I have an extra scan if I am anxious?

Your midwife may arrange an extra check if there is a clinical reason. If anxiety is affecting your wellbeing, perinatal mental health support can also help you manage the emotional side.
