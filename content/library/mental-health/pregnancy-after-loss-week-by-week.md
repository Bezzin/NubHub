# Pregnancy After Loss: Week by Week Coping

Pregnancy after loss can feel like an emotional obstacle course, with each week bringing new hopes and new fears. This week-by-week guide offers coping strategies specific to weeks 0-12, the period most commonly associated with anxiety after miscarriage or loss.

## Weeks 0-4: The unknown and the wait {#section-1}

Before a positive test or in the earliest days, you may feel caught between hope and protective detachment. It is okay to feel nothing or everything. Avoid obsessively testing. Once pregnant, contact your midwife early and mention your previous loss so appropriate care can be arranged. Limit symptom-spotting, as symptoms vary widely and are not reliable indicators of viability.

## Weeks 5-12: Scans, milestones, and managing fear {#section-2}

Each week that passes can bring relief and renewed fear. Around weeks 7-9 you may be eligible for an early scan if you have had previous losses. The dating scan around 12 weeks is a major milestone. Plan something comforting for scan days. Ask for continuity of carer where possible. If anxiety is overwhelming, request a perinatal mental health referral. Celebrate each week quietly if it helps.

## FAQ {#faq}

### When can I have an early scan after loss?

Many NHS trusts offer scans from around 7 weeks after recurrent loss. Ask your midwife about local policy.

### How do I cope with the wait between scans?

Distraction, routine, talking to trusted people, and limiting forum use can help. Some women book private reassurance scans.

### Is it normal not to feel excited after loss?

Yes. Protective detachment is a common and valid response. It does not mean you will not bond later.
