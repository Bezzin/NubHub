# Pregnancy and PTSD: A Guide

If you have PTSD from a previous trauma, pregnancy can trigger symptoms. Here's how to manage it.

## Table of Contents

1. [How PTSD Affects Pregnancy](#section-1)
2. [Getting Support](#section-2)
3. [FAQ](#faq)

## How PTSD Affects Pregnancy {#section-1}

- Previous birth trauma can cause birth-related flashbacks
- Medical procedures may trigger anxiety
- Loss of control during labour can be triggering
- Pregnancy body changes may cause distress

## Getting Support {#section-2}

- Tell your midwife about your PTSD
- Ask for trauma-informed care
- Consider a perinatal mental health referral
- Birth plans can help you feel more in control
- A doula can provide continuous support

## FAQ {#faq}

### Can pregnancy make PTSD worse?

Yes. Hormonal changes, medical procedures, and the emotional weight of pregnancy can trigger PTSD symptoms. Professional support is available.

### Is trauma-informed maternity care available?

Yes. Tell your midwife about your trauma and they should adapt your care to be trauma-informed. You can request changes to examinations, appointments, and birth plans.
