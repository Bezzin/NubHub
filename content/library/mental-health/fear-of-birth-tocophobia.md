# Fear of Birth (Tocophobia): How to Overcome It

Tocophobia is an intense fear of childbirth that affects up to 14% of women. It's a real condition that deserves real support.

## Table of Contents

1. [What Tocophobia Looks Like](#section-1)
2. [Getting Help](#section-2)
3. [FAQ](#faq)

## What Tocophobia Looks Like {#section-1}

- Intense anxiety about the birth
- Avoidance of thinking or talking about birth
- nightmares about labour
- Requesting a caesarean out of fear
- Physical anxiety symptoms when birth is mentioned

## Getting Help {#section-2}

- Tell your midwife — they're trained to support this
- Ask for a referral to a perinatal mental health team
- Consider CBT specifically for birth fear
- Look into hypnobirthing courses
- Explore your birth options (you may be offered a planned c-section)

## FAQ {#faq}

### Can I request a c-section because of fear?

Yes. Under NICE guidelines, if you have a severe fear of birth (tocophobia), you can request a planned caesarean. Your team should support your choice.

### Does hypnobirthing help with tocophobia?

Many women find hypnobirthing helpful for reducing birth anxiety. It teaches relaxation techniques and helps you feel more in control.
