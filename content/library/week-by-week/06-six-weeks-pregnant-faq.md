## Section 1: Common Symptoms at 6 Weeks

- Understand that nausea, fatigue, and sore breasts are driven by rising hCG and progesterone.
- Know that some women feel no symptoms at all, which is also completely normal.
- Track how often nausea affects meals so you can describe it accurately to your midwife.
- Watch for headaches and bloating, both common as your blood volume increases.

## Section 2: When to Seek Medical Advice

- Contact your GP or early pregnancy unit if you experience heavy bleeding or severe abdominal pain.
- Seek help if you cannot keep any food or fluids down for 24 hours, as you may need treatment for hyperemesis.
- Report a sudden disappearance of all pregnancy symptoms alongside spotting, as this can occasionally signal a problem.
- Call 111 if you are unsure whether your symptoms are urgent and need same-day advice.

## FAQ

**Can I have a scan at 6 weeks?**

An NHS scan is not routinely offered at 6 weeks. If you have bleeding or pain, your GP may refer you for an early scan. Private reassurance scans are available from around 6 to 7 weeks.

**Is it normal to feel cramps at 6 weeks?**

Mild cramps similar to period pain are common as your uterus begins to stretch. Sharp or one-sided pain should be assessed quickly to rule out ectopic pregnancy.

**What should I do if I spot at 6 weeks?**

Light spotting can be normal, but you should still contact your GP or early pregnancy unit for advice. They may examine you or arrange an early scan to check that all is well.
