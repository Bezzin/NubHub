# 10 Weeks Pregnant FAQ

At 10 weeks pregnant your dating scan is nearly here and you may be feeling more pregnant than ever. This FAQ answers the most common questions about scans, exercise, and symptoms at this stage.

## Section 1: Questions About Your Dating Scan

- Know that the scan happens between 11 and 14 weeks and estimates your due date.
- Understand that the sonographer measures your baby from crown to rump.
- Ask whether you need a full bladder, as guidance varies by hospital.
- Remember that most scans are quick and painless, usually lasting around 15 minutes.

## Section 2: Safe Exercise and Weight Gain

- Aim for 150 minutes of moderate activity weekly, such as brisk walking or swimming.
- Avoid contact sports, hot yoga, and exercises with a high fall risk.
- Understand that weight gain in the first trimester is usually small, often 0 to 1.5 kilograms.
- Focus on nutrient-rich foods rather than counting calories, and discuss any concerns with your midwife.

## Key Takeaways

Here are the most important points to remember from this guide:

- Questions About Your Dating Scan: Know that the scan happens between 11 and 14 weeks and estimates your due date.
- Safe Exercise and Weight Gain: Aim for 150 minutes of moderate activity weekly, such as brisk walking or swimming.
- Will the dating scan hurt? No.
- Can I go swimming at 10 weeks pregnant? Yes, swimming is safe and excellent for pregnancy fitness.

Take each step at your own pace and reach out to your midwife whenever you need reassurance. Every pregnancy is unique, so trust your instincts and keep a note of anything that feels unusual.

## FAQ

**Will the dating scan hurt?**

No. The sonographer uses a small probe and warm gel on your abdomen. You may feel pressure as they get a clear view, but it should not be painful. If it is uncomfortable, tell the sonographer.

**Can I go swimming at 10 weeks pregnant?**

Yes, swimming is safe and excellent for pregnancy fitness. It supports your joints and eases back strain. Avoid very hot saunas or steam rooms afterwards.

**How much weight should I have gained by 10 weeks?**

Most women gain little in the first trimester, often between 0 and 1.5 kilograms. If you are gaining more or less, mention it at your next midwife appointment so she can reassure you.
