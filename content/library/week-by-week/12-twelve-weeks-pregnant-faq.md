# 12 Weeks Pregnant FAQ

At 12 weeks pregnant your dating scan is usually behind you and you may be ready to share your news. This FAQ answers the questions we hear most about scan results, announcing, and what comes next.

## Section 1: Understanding Your Scan Results

- Know that the sonographer will confirm your due date based on your baby's measurements.
- Ask about the nuchal translucency measurement if you opted for combined screening.
- Understand that screening results give a chance, not a diagnosis, and further tests are offered if needed.
- Remember that most babies are healthy and most scans are reassuring.

## Section 2: Telling People and Accessing Benefits

- Plan who to tell first, whether parents, siblings, or close friends.
- Use your scan photo if you want a simple way to announce the news.
- Claim free dental care and prescriptions by asking your midwife for a Maternity Exemption Certificate.
- Tell your employer in writing if you have not already, to trigger your risk assessment and maternity rights.

Here are the most important points to remember from this guide:

- Understanding Your Scan Results: Know that the sonographer will confirm your due date based on your baby's measurements.

- How accurate is my due date from the 12-week scan? The dating scan is the most accurate way to estimate your due date, with a margin of around 5 days at 12 weeks.

Take each step at your own pace and reach out to your midwife whenever you need reassurance. Every pregnancy is unique, so trust your instincts and keep a note of anything that feels unusual.

## FAQ

**How accurate is my due date from the 12-week scan?**

The dating scan is the most accurate way to estimate your due date, with a margin of around 5 days at 12 weeks. Your midwife will use this date rather than your last period if they differ significantly.

**Is 12 weeks the right time to announce?**

Many people announce at 12 weeks because miscarriage risk drops significantly. However, the right time is whenever you feel comfortable, whether earlier or later.

**When can I claim free dental care in pregnancy?**

You can claim from the start of your pregnancy once your midwife signs your Maternity Exemption Certificate. The benefit lasts until one year after your baby's due date.
