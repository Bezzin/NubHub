# 8 Weeks Pregnant FAQ

At 8 weeks pregnant your booking appointment is likely on the horizon. This FAQ answers the questions we hear most about that first major antenatal visit and the symptoms you may be feeling.

## Section 1: Your Booking Appointment Explained

- Expect the visit to last around an hour as your midwife takes a detailed history.
- Bring a list of medications and allergies so your records are accurate from day one.
- Ask about screening tests so you can decide in time for your combined screening window.
- Use the visit to discuss mental health, as your midwife will ask about anxiety and past experiences.

## Section 2: Managing Changing Symptoms

- Know that symptoms can come and go throughout the first trimester without signalling a problem.
- Keep snacks and water handy for nausea, and try cold foods if hot smells trigger aversions.
- Wear a supportive bra and loose clothing to ease breast tenderness and bloating.
- Rest when fatigue peaks, and accept that your energy may vary day to day.

Take each step at your own pace and reach out to your midwife whenever you need reassurance. Every pregnancy is unique, so trust your instincts and keep a note of anything that feels unusual.

## FAQ

**What should I bring to my booking appointment?**

Bring your medications list, any previous medical records, your partner or a friend if you wish, and a list of questions. Your midwife will also need your NHS number and the first day of your last period.

**Are blood tests at the booking visit painful?**

Blood tests involve a quick needle prick and are over in under a minute. You may feel a brief sting. Tell your midwife if you are nervous or prone to fainting so she can help you stay comfortable.

**My symptoms disappeared at 8 weeks, is that normal?**

Symptoms can fluctuate and a temporary easing is often nothing to worry about. If you also have bleeding or pain, contact your early pregnancy unit for advice.
