# 13 Weeks Pregnant FAQ

At 13 weeks pregnant you are stepping into the second trimester. This FAQ covers the questions we hear most about easing symptoms, the anomaly scan, travel, and what to expect as your energy returns.

## Section 1: Second Trimester Symptoms and Comfort

- Expect nausea and fatigue to ease for many women as hormone levels stabilise.
- Watch for new symptoms such as round ligament pain as your uterus grows.
- Start using a pregnancy pillow to improve sleep comfort on your side.
- Keep doing pelvic floor exercises daily to build strength for later pregnancy and birth.

## Section 2: Looking Ahead to the Anomaly Scan and Travel

- Note the anomaly scan window of 18 to 21 weeks and wait for your appointment letter.
- Decide whether you want to find out your baby's sex at the scan.
- If you plan to travel, do so before 28 weeks if possible and check airline policies.
- Book a pre-travel consultation if you are going abroad to discuss vaccines and insurance.

## Key Takeaways

Here are the most important points to remember from this guide:

- Second Trimester Symptoms and Comfort: Expect nausea and fatigue to ease for many women as hormone levels stabilise.
- Looking Ahead to the Anomaly Scan and Travel: Note the anomaly scan window of 18 to 21 weeks and wait for your appointment letter.
- When will my symptoms ease at 13 weeks? Many women notice improvement from around weeks 12 to 14 as the placenta takes over hormone production.
- Can I travel in the second trimester? Yes, the second trimester is often the most comfortable time to travel.

Take each step at your own pace and reach out to your midwife whenever you need reassurance. Every pregnancy is unique, so trust your instincts and keep a note of anything that feels unusual.

## FAQ

**When will my symptoms ease at 13 weeks?**

Many women notice improvement from around weeks 12 to 14 as the placenta takes over hormone production. If symptoms persist, they usually ease further by week 16.

**Can I travel in the second trimester?**

Yes, the second trimester is often the most comfortable time to travel. Before 28 weeks is ideal. Stay hydrated, move regularly, and ensure your travel insurance covers pregnancy.

**When is my anomaly scan booked?**

Your anomaly scan is arranged by your maternity service and takes place between 18 and 21 weeks. You should receive an appointment letter after your dating scan. Contact your midwife if you have not heard by week 16.
