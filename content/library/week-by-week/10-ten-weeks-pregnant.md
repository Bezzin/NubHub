## Your Baby's Development at 10 Weeks {#baby-development}

**Key milestone: Baby is now a fetus**

Bones start to harden, nails begin to form. Your baby is now approximately The fetus is the size of a strawberry.

At this stage, your baby is growing rapidly. Every week brings significant changes as organs form, limbs develop, and the tiny body takes shape.

## Your Body at 10 Weeks {#your-body}

Your body is going through enormous changes to support your growing baby. Hormone levels are surging, which affects almost every organ in your body.

## Symptoms at 10 Weeks {#symptoms}

Common symptoms at 10 weeks include:

- Morning sickness may ease
- round ligament pain
- visible bump may start

Remember: every pregnancy is different. Some women have many symptoms, others have none. Both are normal.

## What to Do This Week {#what-to-do}

Prepare for your 12-week scan. Keep taking folic acid. Start thinking about telling people about your pregnancy. Consider antenatal classes.

## Tips for This Week {#tips}

1. Rest as much as you need - your body is working hard
2. Eat small, frequent meals if you have nausea
3. Stay hydrated - aim for 8-10 glasses of water a day
4. Take your folic acid supplement daily
5. Be kind to yourself - growing a baby is exhausting work

## Frequently Asked Questions {#faq}

### Is it normal to have no symptoms at 10 weeks?

Yes. Some women experience very few symptoms throughout the first trimester. While symptoms like nausea and fatigue are common, their absence doesn't necessarily mean anything is wrong. If you're worried, contact your midwife.

### Can I have a scan at 10 weeks?

You can have a private early scan at 10 weeks. The NHS dating scan is typically done between 11+2 and 14+1 weeks.

### What should I be eating at 10 weeks?

Focus on a balanced diet with plenty of fruit, vegetables, whole grains, lean protein, and calcium-rich foods. Take folic acid daily. Avoid raw meat, soft cheeses, unpasteurised dairy, and high-mercury fish. Stay hydrated.
