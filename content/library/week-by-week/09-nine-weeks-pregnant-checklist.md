# 9 Weeks Pregnant Checklist

At 9 weeks pregnant you are approaching the end of the first trimester. This checklist helps you understand screening options, build a support network, and prepare for your upcoming dating scan.

## Table of Contents

## Section 1: Understand Your Screening Options

- Read about the NHS combined screening test, offered between 11 and 14 weeks, which assesses the chance of Down's syndrome, Edwards' syndrome, and Patau's syndrome.
- Decide whether you want screening, as it is optional and your midwife will respect your choice.
- Ask about NIPT (non-invasive prenatal testing) if you want a more accurate private option.
- Note the window for the dating scan and nuchal translucency measurement so you do not miss it.

## Section 2: Build Your Pregnancy Support Network

- Tell trusted family or friends so you have emotional and practical support.
- Join a local or online antenatal group to connect with other parents-to-be due around the same time.
- Discuss birth partner plans with your partner or chosen support person.
- Consider whether you want a doula and research local options if so.

## FAQ

**When will my dating scan be booked?**

Your dating scan is usually arranged at your booking appointment and takes place between 11 and 14 weeks. You should receive a letter with the date and time.

**Is NIPT available on the NHS at 9 weeks?**

NIPT is not routinely offered on the NHS to all women, but may be available if your combined screening result shows a higher chance. Private NIPT is widely available from around 10 weeks.

**Can I start pelvic floor exercises at 9 weeks?**

Yes. Pelvic floor exercises are safe throughout pregnancy and starting early helps prevent leaks later. Aim for three sets of 10 slow and 10 fast squeezes daily.
