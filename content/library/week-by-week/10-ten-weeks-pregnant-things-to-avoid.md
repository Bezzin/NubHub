# Things to Avoid at 10 Weeks Pregnant

At 10 weeks pregnant, your baby is developing rapidly and your body is going through significant changes. Knowing what to avoid during this stage of your pregnancy helps protect your growing baby and keeps you feeling your best. This guide covers the key things to steer clear of at 10 weeks.

## Foods and Drinks to Avoid

At 10 weeks, your baby is developing rapidly. Avoid raw or undercooked meat, poultry, and fish, as they can harbour harmful bacteria like listeria and salmonella. Steer clear of unpasteurised dairy products, soft mould-ripened cheeses such as brie and camembert, and pate, which can carry listeria. Limit caffeine to under 200mg per day, roughly one to two cups of coffee, and avoid alcohol entirely, as no amount is considered safe during pregnancy.

Certain fish should also be avoided. Shark, swordfish, and marlin contain high levels of mercury that can affect your developing baby. Raw shellfish, including oysters and sushi-grade seafood, poses a risk of food poisoning. Always wash fruit and vegetables thoroughly and avoid raw or partially cooked eggs to reduce the risk of salmonella.

## Lifestyle Factors to Avoid

Beyond diet, several lifestyle habits are best avoided at 10 weeks. Smoking and recreational drugs are strictly off-limits, as they significantly increase the risk of miscarriage, low birth weight, and developmental issues. If you need support quitting, speak to your GP or midwife, who can offer tailored help.

Avoid hot tubs, saunas, and very hot baths, particularly in early pregnancy, as raising your core body temperature can be harmful during these critical weeks. High-impact or contact sports should be swapped for pregnancy-safe exercise such as swimming, walking, or prenatal yoga. Avoid heavy lifting and activities with a high risk of falling.

Be cautious with over-the-counter medications and herbal remedies. Many common medicines, including ibuprofen and certain decongestants, are not recommended in pregnancy. Always check with your pharmacist or midwife before taking anything. Similarly, avoid handling cat litter trays to reduce the risk of toxoplasmosis, a parasitic infection that can affect your baby.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I drink coffee at 10 weeks pregnant?

Yes, but limit your intake to under 200mg of caffeine per day, which is about one to two cups of instant coffee. Be mindful that tea, chocolate, energy drinks, and some medications also contain caffeine.

### Is it safe to exercise at 10 weeks?

Moderate exercise is encouraged and beneficial during pregnancy. Avoid contact sports, scuba diving, and activities with a high risk of falling. Swimming, brisk walking, and prenatal yoga are excellent choices. Speak to your midwife if you have any concerns.

### Should I avoid skincare products at 10 weeks?

Some skincare ingredients, such as retinoids and high-dose salicylic acid, are best avoided in pregnancy. Check product labels and consult your GP or dermatologist if you are unsure about specific ingredients.
