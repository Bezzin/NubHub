# 10 Weeks Pregnant Checklist

At 10 weeks pregnant your dating scan is only a week or two away. This checklist helps you prepare for that milestone, keep active safely, and get your maternity notes in order.

## Table of Contents

## Section 1: Prepare for Your Dating Scan

- Confirm the date and time of your scan and whether you need a full bladder.
- Decide who will come with you and check your hospital's policy on attendance.
- Write down any questions about screening results or due date calculation.
- Plan how you will share the news after the scan, if you have been keeping it private.
- Bring your maternity notes if you already have them.

## Section 2: Keep Fit and Active Safely

- Aim for 150 minutes of moderate exercise per week, such as brisk walking, swimming, or stationary cycling.
- Avoid hot yoga, contact sports, and any activity with a fall risk.
- Add pelvic floor and gentle core exercises to your routine.
- Listen to your body and slow down if you feel dizzy or breathless.
- Stay hydrated before, during, and after exercise.

## Key Takeaways

Here are the most important points to remember from this guide:

- Prepare for Your Dating Scan: Confirm the date and time of your scan and whether you need a full bladder.

Take each step at your own pace and reach out to your midwife whenever you need reassurance. Every pregnancy is unique, so trust your instincts and keep a note of anything that feels unusual.

## FAQ

**Do I need a full bladder for the dating scan?**

A partially full bladder can help the sonographer get a clearer image in early pregnancy. Drink about a pint of water 30 to 45 minutes before your appointment, but do not overfill.

**Can the dating scan tell me my exact due date?**

Yes. The sonographer measures your baby from head to bottom (crown-rump length) to estimate your due date. This estimate is most accurate between 11 and 14 weeks.

**Is it safe to swim in a public pool at 10 weeks?**

Yes. Swimming is an excellent low-impact exercise in pregnancy. The chlorine in properly maintained pools poses no risk to your baby.
