# 11 Weeks Pregnant Checklist

At 11 weeks pregnant your dating scan and combined screening may happen any day now. This checklist covers what to expect from screening, your maternity pay basics, and how to start telling people your news.

## Section 1: Get Ready for Combined Screening

- Attend your dating scan appointment, which includes the nuchal translucency ultrasound measurement.
- Expect a blood test alongside the scan to calculate the chance of chromosomal conditions.
- Ask the sonographer to explain results before you leave so you understand what the numbers mean.
- Remember that screening shows a chance, not a diagnosis. Further tests are offered if needed.

## Section 2: Know Your Maternity Pay Basics

- Check whether you qualify for Statutory Maternity Pay (SMP) through your employer.
- If you are self-employed or recently changed jobs, look into Maternity Allowance.
- Note that you must tell your employer by the 15th week before your baby is due, but earlier is fine.
- Request the MAT B1 form from your midwife, usually issued around 20 weeks, to claim your pay.
- Keep payslips and any HR correspondence in one folder for easy reference.

## Key Takeaways

Here are the most important points to remember from this guide:

- Get Ready for Combined Screening: Attend your dating scan appointment, which includes the nuchal translucency ultrasound measurement.
- Know Your Maternity Pay Basics: Check whether you qualify for Statutory Maternity Pay (SMP) through your employer.
- What happens at the combined screening appointment? You will have a dating scan and a blood test.
- When do I need to tell my employer I am pregnant? Legally you must inform your employer by the 15th week before your due week.

Take each step at your own pace and reach out to your midwife whenever you need reassurance. Every pregnancy is unique, so trust your instincts and keep a note of anything that feels unusual.

## FAQ

**What happens at the combined screening appointment?**

You will have a dating scan and a blood test. The sonographer measures fluid at the back of your baby's neck and combines this with your blood results and age to estimate the chance of certain chromosomal conditions.

**When do I need to tell my employer I am pregnant?**

Legally you must inform your employer by the 15th week before your due week. Telling them earlier means you can access risk assessments and maternity rights sooner.

**What if my screening result shows a higher chance?**

You will be offered further testing, which may include NIPT or diagnostic tests such as CVS or amniocentesis. A specialist midwife or doctor will discuss the options and support you.
