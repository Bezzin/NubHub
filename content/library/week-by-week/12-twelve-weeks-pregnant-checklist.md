# 12 Weeks Pregnant Checklist

Reaching 12 weeks pregnant is a milestone many parents look forward to. After your dating scan you may feel ready to share your news. This checklist covers who to tell, claiming free dental care, and preparing for the second trimester.

## Table of Contents

- [FAQ](#faq)

## Section 1: Tell Family, Friends, and Work

- Plan how you will tell close family and friends, whether in person, by phone, or with a photo from the scan.
- Arrange a meeting with your manager or HR to discuss your pregnancy, risk assessment, and maternity leave plans.
- Give written notice to your employer if you have not already, ideally including your due date and intended leave start.
- Decide whether and when to announce publicly on social media, there is no right or wrong time.

## Section 2: Claim Free Dental Care and Prepare for Trimester Two

- Request a Maternity Exemption Certificate (FW8) from your midwife so you can claim free NHS dental care and prescriptions.
- Book a dental check-up, as pregnancy hormones can affect gum health.
- Review your first trimester symptom diary and note what is improving as you approach week 13.
- Start planning your maternity wardrobe as your waistband may soon feel snug.
- Begin researching antenatal classes and book early, popular courses fill up fast.

## Key Takeaways

Here are the most important points to remember from this guide:

- Tell Family, Friends, and Work: Plan how you will tell close family and friends, whether in person, by phone, or with a photo from the scan.
- Claim Free Dental Care and Prepare for Trimester Two: Request a Maternity Exemption Certificate (FW8) from your midwife so you can claim free NHS dental care and prescriptions.
- Is dental care really free in pregnancy? Yes.
- Is it safe to announce at 12 weeks? The risk of miscarriage drops significantly after 12 weeks, which is why many people choose to share their news now.

Take each step at your own pace and reach out to your midwife whenever you need reassurance. Every pregnancy is unique, so trust your instincts and keep a note of anything that feels unusual.

## FAQ

**Is dental care really free in pregnancy?**

Yes. Once your midwife signs your Maternity Exemption Certificate, NHS dental treatment and prescriptions are free until one year after your baby is due. Keep the certificate safe and show it at appointments.

**Is it safe to announce at 12 weeks?**

The risk of miscarriage drops significantly after 12 weeks, which is why many people choose to share their news now. However, the right time is whenever feels comfortable to you.

**When should I book antenatal classes?**

Aim to book around weeks 12 to 20 for classes starting in your third trimester. NHS classes are free and private options offer smaller groups and more flexibility.
