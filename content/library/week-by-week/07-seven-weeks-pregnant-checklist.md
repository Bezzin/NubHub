# 7 Weeks Pregnant Checklist

Now at 7 weeks pregnant, your baby is growing rapidly and your body is working hard. This checklist focuses on nutrition, workplace comfort, and keeping on top of changing symptoms.

## Section 1: Eat a Balanced First Trimester Diet

- Include protein at each meal, such as eggs, beans, lean meat, or tofu, to support steady energy.
- Add leafy greens and citrus fruits for folate and vitamin C alongside your folic acid supplement.
- Choose wholegrain carbohydrates to help reduce blood sugar swings that can worsen nausea.
- Stay hydrated with water, herbal teas, or diluted fruit juice, aiming for around 1.6 litres a day.
- Avoid raw or undercooked meats, soft cheeses, and high-mercury fish.

## Section 2: Make Your Workday More Comfortable

- Take short breaks every hour to stand, stretch, or walk, especially if you sit at a desk.
- Keep snacks and a water bottle within reach to manage nausea during meetings.
- Discuss flexible start times with your manager if commuting in rush hour worsens fatigue.
- Request a workplace risk assessment if your job involves lifting, chemicals, or long shifts.

Here are the most important points to remember from this guide:

Take each step at your own pace and reach out to your midwife whenever you need reassurance. Every pregnancy is unique, so trust your instincts and keep a note of anything that feels unusual.

## FAQ

**Can I travel at 7 weeks pregnant?**

Yes, travel is generally safe in early pregnancy. If flying, stay hydrated and move regularly. Check your travel insurance covers pregnancy and avoid destinations requiring mandatory vaccines unless approved by your GP.

**Is it safe to dye my hair at 7 weeks?**

Most research suggests hair dye is safe after the first 12 weeks. If you must colour your hair now, consider highlights or a semi-permanent option and ensure good ventilation.

**How much weight should I gain by 7 weeks?**

Weight gain in early pregnancy is usually minimal, often 0 to 1 kilogram. Focus on nutritious food rather than the scale, and discuss any concerns at your booking appointment.
