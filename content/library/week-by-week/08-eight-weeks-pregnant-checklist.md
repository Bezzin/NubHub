# 8 Weeks Pregnant Checklist

At 8 weeks pregnant your booking appointment is likely just around the corner. This is one of the most important visits of your pregnancy, so preparation pays off. Use this checklist to get ready and manage your changing body.

## Table of Contents
- [Section 1](#section-1)
- [Section 2](#section-2)
- [FAQ](#faq)

## Section 1: Prepare for Your NHS Booking Appointment

- Bring a list of your current medications, supplements, and allergies.
- Have your family medical history to hand, including any genetic conditions.
- Be ready to discuss your mental health, as your midwife will ask about anxiety and mood.
- Ask about screening tests offered on the NHS, including blood tests and the combined screening scan.
- Confirm whether you will see the same midwife throughout, known as continuity of care.

## Section 2: Keep on Top of First Trimester Symptoms

- If nausea is severe, ask your GP about anti-sickness medication suitable for pregnancy.
- Wear loose clothing to ease bloating and breast tenderness.
- Practice gentle stretches for your lower back if aching begins.
- Track your weight and blood pressure at home if you have a history of concerns, but do not obsess over daily numbers.
- Plan easy meals for days when fatigue hits hardest, such as batch-cooked soups or freezer-ready dinners.

## Key Takeaways

Here are the most important points to remember from this guide:

- Prepare for Your NHS Booking Appointment: Bring a list of your current medications, supplements, and allergies.
- Keep on Top of First Trimester Symptoms: If nausea is severe, ask your GP about anti-sickness medication suitable for pregnancy.
- What blood tests are done at the booking appointment? Your midwife will offer a full blood count, blood group and Rh status, and screening for infections such as HIV, hepatitis B, and syphilis.
- Can I request a different midwife if I am not comfortable? Yes.

Take each step at your own pace and reach out to your midwife whenever you need reassurance. Every pregnancy is unique, so trust your instincts and keep a note of anything that feels unusual.

## FAQ

**What blood tests are done at the booking appointment?**

Your midwife will offer a full blood count, blood group and Rh status, and screening for infections such as HIV, hepatitis B, and syphilis. These are routine and help keep you and your baby safe.

**Can I request a different midwife if I am not comfortable?**

Yes. You can ask to change midwives if you do not feel supported. Continuity of care matters, and maternity services will try to accommodate your preference where possible.

**Is it normal to have no symptoms at 8 weeks?**

Yes. Some women feel fine at 8 weeks while others feel rough. Symptoms vary widely and a lack of nausea does not mean something is wrong.
