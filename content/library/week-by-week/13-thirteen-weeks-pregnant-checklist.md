# 13 Weeks Pregnant Checklist

At 13 weeks pregnant you are on the cusp of the second trimester, often called the honeymoon phase of pregnancy. This checklist helps you plan ahead, build a gentle fitness routine, and prepare for your 20-week anomaly scan.

## Section 1: Plan for Your 20-Week Anomaly Scan

- Note the expected date of your anomaly scan, which falls between 18 and 21 weeks.
- Decide whether you want to find out your baby's sex at this scan.
- Write down any questions about your baby's growth or organs that the sonographer can address.
- Plan childcare or time off work for the appointment, as scans can run late.

## Section 2: Build a Sustainable Pregnancy Fitness Routine

- Set a weekly exercise goal of 150 minutes spread across most days.
- Mix aerobic activity such as swimming or walking with strength and flexibility work like pregnancy yoga.
- Add daily pelvic floor exercises if you have not already started.
- Track how exercise affects your energy and mood, and adjust intensity as your bump grows.
- Consider booking a session with a pregnancy fitness instructor if you are unsure where to start.

Take each step at your own pace and reach out to your midwife whenever you need reassurance. Every pregnancy is unique, so trust your instincts and keep a note of anything that feels unusual.

## FAQ

**When will my 20-week anomaly scan be booked?**

Your anomaly scan is arranged by your maternity service and takes place between 18 and 21 weeks. You should receive an appointment letter after your dating scan.

**Can I find out my baby's sex at 13 weeks?**

Sex determination is more reliable at the 20-week anomaly scan. Some private clinics offer early sex scans from around 16 weeks, but the NHS does not routinely offer this before 20 weeks.

**Is it normal to feel better at 13 weeks?**

Yes. As you enter the second trimester, nausea and fatigue often ease. Enjoy the energy boost and use it to prepare for the months ahead.
