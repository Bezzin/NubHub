# Welsh Baby Names: Beautiful Ideas

Welsh baby names carry a rich heritage, lyrical sound and meanings rooted in nature, faith and folklore. Whether you have Welsh roots, love the language, or simply find the names beautiful, there's a wonderful range to choose from. Below you'll find ideas for girls and boys, along with tips on pronunciation and choosing a name that travels well.

## Popular Welsh Girl Names {#s1}

Welsh girls' names are often soft and melodic, with meanings drawn from the natural world:

- **Cerys** — love
- **Eleri** — named after a river and a saint
- **Ffion** — foxglove
- **Nia** — brightness or radiance
- **Seren** — star
- **Bethan** — a Welsh form of Elizabeth
- **Carys** — love
- **Anwen** — very fair or beautiful

These range from well-known choices to gentler, less common names, so there's plenty of scope whether you want something familiar or more distinctive.

## Popular Welsh Boy Names {#s2}

Welsh boys' names often have strong, characterful meanings tied to history and legend:

- **Dylan** — sea, the son of the wave
- **Ieuan** — God is gracious (a Welsh form of John)
- **Rhys** — enthusiasm or ardour
- **Tomos** — twin (a Welsh form of Thomas)
- **Owain** — young warrior
- **Gareth** — gentle
- **Emrys** — immortal
- **Cai** — rejoice

Many of these work beautifully alongside non-Welsh middle names and surnames, which can be helpful if you want a nod to heritage without an unfamiliar spelling.

## Choosing a Welsh Name

A few things are worth thinking about. Some Welsh names use letters and sounds that look unfamiliar in English — for example, "dd" is pronounced like the "th" in "this", "f" sounds like "v", and "ll" has a soft breathy sound that's unique to Welsh. Saying a name aloud, and checking how it might be pronounced outside Wales, can help you decide.

It's also worth considering how the name pairs with your surname, whether you like any natural nicknames, and how the initials look together. There's no right or wrong choice — pick the name that feels right to you and your family.

## Frequently Asked Questions {#faq}

### What do Welsh names mean?

Many Welsh names relate to nature, qualities or faith. For example, Seren means star, Dylan means sea, and Cerys and Carys both mean love. The meanings are part of what makes them so popular.

### Are Welsh names hard to pronounce?

Some can look tricky at first because Welsh uses certain letters differently from English, but most follow consistent rules once you know them. Saying a name aloud a few times usually makes it feel natural, and shorter names like Nia, Cai and Rhys are very easy to use.

### Do Welsh names work outside Wales?

Many do, especially shorter names such as Dylan, Seren, Rhys and Nia, which are widely recognised. If you love a less familiar name, pairing it with a more common middle name can give a nice balance.

For tips on choosing and sharing your baby's name, your midwife or health visitor can also point you to local resources.
