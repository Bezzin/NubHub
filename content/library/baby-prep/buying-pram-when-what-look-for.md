# Buying a Pram: When and What to Look For

Choosing a pram is one of the biggest baby purchases you'll make. Here's our guide.

## Table of Contents

1. [When to Buy](#s1)
2. [What to Look For](#s2)
3. [FAQ](#faq)

## When to Buy {#s1}

Start researching in the second trimester. Buy by the third trimester. Don't buy too early — you want to be sure the pregnancy is progressing well first.

## What to Look For {#s2}

Weight and foldability (will it fit in your car?), suspension (for rough surfaces), storage basket size, adjustable handle height, compatibility with car seats, and your budget.

## FAQ {#faq}

### How much should I spend on a pram?

Prams range from £100 to £1,500+. A mid-range pram (£300-600) is fine for most families. Consider second-hand — many are barely used. The most expensive isn't necessarily the best.
