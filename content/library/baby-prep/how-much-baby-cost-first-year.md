# How Much Does a Baby Cost in the First Year?

The cost of a baby in the first year can range from £1,500 to £10,000+ depending on your choices. Here's a breakdown.

## Table of Contents

1. [Essential Costs](#s1)
2. [Money-Saving Tips](#s2)
3. [FAQ](#faq)

## Essential Costs {#s1}

Nappies: £400-800/year. Formula (if not breastfeeding): £400-700/year. Clothes: £200-500/year. Medical/health: mostly free on NHS. Childcare: £0-£12,000+ depending on your situation.

## Money-Saving Tips {#s2}

Buy second-hand (prams, cots, clothes). Use cloth nappies. Breastfeed if possible. Accept hand-me-downs. Buy in bulk. Don't overbuy newborn clothes (they grow fast).

## FAQ {#faq}

### How much should I budget for a baby's first year?

Budget £1,500-£3,000 for essentials (nappies, clothes, equipment) if you buy second-hand and breastfeed. Budget £5,000-£10,000+ if you buy new and use formula and childcare. Plan ahead.
