# What To Pack Hospital Bag First Trimester

It is completely normal to feel keen to organise your hospital bag the moment you see a positive test. The honest answer is that you do not need a packed bag in the first trimester, but this is a lovely time to start a list and gather ideas while you have the energy and headspace. Most midwives suggest having the bag actually packed and by the door by around 36 weeks, since labour can begin before your due date. Treating the early weeks as the planning stage takes the last-minute panic out of the third trimester.

## When To Start And When To Pack

In the first trimester, keep it simple. Jot down a running list on your phone, note anything you spot that looks useful, and spread the cost by buying a few bits each month rather than all at once. There is no need to wash baby clothes or fill a bag yet.

As a rough guide:

- First trimester (weeks 1 to 12): start a list, research what your hospital or birth centre provides, and budget.
- Second trimester (weeks 13 to 27): buy essentials gradually so nothing is rushed.
- Third trimester (from around 28 weeks): wash baby clothes and pack, aiming to be ready by 36 weeks.

It is worth checking with your own maternity unit, as some provide nappies, pads or a cot while others ask you to bring your own. Your midwife can tell you what your local hospital expects.

## What To Pack (For Later)

When the time comes, a typical UK hospital bag includes:

- Your maternity notes or details to access your digital record
- A few sets of loose, comfortable clothes and a dressing gown
- Maternity pads, dark old underwear, and basic toiletries
- A going-home outfit for you and for baby
- Baby vests, sleepsuits, a hat, and a muslin or two
- A phone charger with a long cable, and snacks and drinks
- A car seat fitted ready for the journey home
- A separate small bag of essentials for your birth partner

Many parents find it easier to pack two smaller bags, one for labour and one for after the birth, so nothing important gets buried.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### When should I pack my hospital bag?

Aim to have it packed and ready by around 36 weeks, as some babies arrive earlier than expected. Start planning in the first trimester and buying items through the second trimester so you are not rushing near the end.

### What goes in a hospital bag?

The core items are your maternity notes, comfortable clothes, toiletries, maternity pads, baby clothes and nappies, a going-home outfit, a phone charger, snacks, and a few essentials for your birth partner. Add a fitted car seat for the journey home.

### Do I need to buy everything now?

No. The first trimester is for planning, not packing. Spreading purchases over several months is kinder to your budget, and your needs may change as your pregnancy progresses.

Always speak to your midwife or GP for advice tailored to your circumstances.
