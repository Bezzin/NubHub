# Scottish Baby Names: Beautiful Ideas

Scottish baby names carry deep roots, strong meanings, and a lovely connection to Scotland's landscape, history, and Gaelic heritage. Whether you want something traditional that has been used for generations or a softer, more modern choice, there is a wealth of beautiful names to consider. Below you will find popular girls' and boys' names with their meanings, plus a few tips on choosing and pronouncing them.

## Popular Scottish Girl Names {#s1}

Isla (island), Maisie (pearl), Skye (island), Bonnie (beautiful), Kirsty (follower of Christ), Ailsa (fairy rock), Elspeth (God is my oath).

A few more loved choices include Eilidh (a Gaelic form of Helen, meaning bright or shining), Mhairi and Mairi (forms of Mary), Iona (named after the peaceful Hebridean island), Catriona (a Scottish form of Catherine), Rhona (associated with the island of Rona), Senga, and Effie. Many of these draw on nature and place names, which is a hallmark of Scottish naming.

## Popular Scottish Boy Names {#s2}

Callum (dove), Hamish (supplanter), Finlay (fair warrior), Lachlan (from the fjords), Ruaraidh (red king), Alasdair (defender of men).

Other strong options include Angus (one strength), Fraser (from a Scottish clan name), Euan and Ewan (forms linked to youth), Rory (an anglicised form of Ruaraidh, meaning red king), Struan (stream), Kenneth (handsome or born of fire), and Malcolm (devotee of Saint Columba). Clan and surname-style first names such as Fraser, Stuart, and Cameron are also popular.

## Choosing and Pronouncing Scottish Names

Some Scottish names are spelled very differently from how they sound, which is part of their charm but worth thinking about. Eilidh is usually said "AY-lee", Mhairi as "VAH-ree", and Ruaraidh as "ROO-a-ree". If you love a name with a less obvious spelling, it can help to say it aloud, imagine it being read out at school or work, and consider whether you are happy to explain the pronunciation now and then.

It is also worth checking the meaning and any family or clan associations, especially if you have Scottish heritage you would like to honour. Pairing a distinctive Scottish first name with a simpler middle name can give you flexibility later on.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Are Scottish names popular in the UK?

Yes. Names such as Isla, Callum, and Maisie are popular right across the UK, not only in Scotland. Scottish names often appeal because they have strong meanings and connections to nature, history, and heritage.

### How do you pronounce names like Eilidh and Ruaraidh?

Eilidh is generally pronounced "AY-lee" and Ruaraidh "ROO-a-ree", though there can be slight regional variations. If a spelling is unfamiliar to most people, it is a good idea to settle on the pronunciation you prefer and be ready to share it.

### Do I need Scottish heritage to use a Scottish name?

Not at all. Many parents choose Scottish names simply because they love the sound or meaning. If you do have Scottish roots, you might enjoy choosing a name with a family or clan connection to make it feel more personal.
