# Antenatal Classes: When to Book

Antenatal classes help you prepare for birth, feeding, and early parenthood. Knowing when to book ensures you get a place on the right course at the right stage of your pregnancy, before classes fill up.

## When to start looking {when-to-start-looking}

Start researching antenatal classes in your second trimester, around 16-20 weeks. Popular courses, especially NCT and private hypnobirthing, fill up months in advance. Booking early gives you the best choice of dates, formats, and locations. NHS classes are usually free but may have limited availability in your area.

## When classes typically run {when-classes-run}

Most antenatal classes are timed to finish around 36 weeks, so they usually run in the last 8-10 weeks of pregnancy. If you are expecting in summer, book early as demand peaks. Some providers offer early-bird classes from 25 weeks that cover pregnancy wellbeing, while intensive weekend courses are available closer to your due date.

## NHS vs private classes {nhs-vs-private}

NHS antenatal classes are free and run by midwives or health visitors, often at children's centres or hospitals. They cover the basics of labour, feeding, and baby care. Private classes like NCT, hypnobirthing, or active birth are fee-paying but smaller, more in-depth, and a great way to make friends. Many parents combine both.

## What to consider when booking {consider-when-booking}

Think about class size, format (in-person, online, weekend vs weekly), cost, and the topics covered. Check the teacher's qualifications and whether your partner can attend. If you want a social group, NCT classes are popular for meeting other local parents. Book a course that finishes by 36 weeks so you are prepared if baby arrives early.

## FAQ {#faq}

### Are NHS antenatal classes enough?

For many parents, yes. They cover the essentials. Private classes add depth, relaxation techniques, and social connection if budget allows.

### Can my partner come to antenatal classes?

Most private classes include partners. NHS classes vary, so check with your local provider.

### What if I miss the booking window?

Some providers offer late-start or intensive weekend courses. Online classes are also flexible if you book later in pregnancy.
