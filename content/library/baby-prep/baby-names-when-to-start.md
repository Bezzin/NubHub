# Baby Names: When to Start Thinking About Them

Choosing a baby name is one of the most enjoyable parts of pregnancy, but it can also be surprisingly tricky. Knowing when to start and how to narrow down your options takes the pressure off and helps you and your partner find a name you both love.

## When to start discussing names {when-to-start-discussing}

Many parents start chatting about names in the second trimester, once the initial shock has passed and the pregnancy feels more real. There is no right time, but starting around 16-20 weeks gives you plenty of room to test names, get used to them, and avoid last-minute panic. Some parents wait until after the 20-week scan when they know the sex, if they choose to find out.

## How to build a shortlist {how-to-shortlist}

Start with individual lists: each partner writes 10-20 names they like. Compare lists and highlight overlaps or near-matches. Research meanings, origins, and popularity in the UK using the ONS baby names data. Consider how the name sounds with your surname, initials, and potential nicknames. Aim for a shortlist of 3-5 by the third trimester.

## Things to consider {things-to-consider}

Think about popularity (do you mind if your child shares a name with three classmates?), initials (avoid embarrassing combos), and how the name ages. Consider family traditions, cultural heritage, and whether the name is easy to spell and pronounce. Say the name out loud with your surname and imagine calling it across a playground.

## Keeping options open {keeping-options}

Even with a shortlist, many parents wait until birth to decide, sometimes meeting their baby before choosing. This is completely normal. Pack your shortlist in your hospital bag. If you do not know the sex, have shortlists for both. Remember you have 42 days after birth to register the name in the UK, so there is no rush.

## FAQ {#faq}

### Do we have to decide before birth?

No. Many parents meet their baby first. You have 42 days after birth to register the name in the UK.

### How do I check if a name is popular in the UK?

Use the ONS baby names tool, which publishes annual data on the most popular names in England and Wales.

### What if we can't agree on a name?

Try each partner picking a veto-proof favourite, making a combined shortlist, or each taking a first and middle name. Compromise is key.
