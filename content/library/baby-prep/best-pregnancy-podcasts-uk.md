# Best Pregnancy Podcasts UK

Podcasts are a flexible way to absorb pregnancy information while commuting, walking, or nesting. Whether you want reassuring birth stories, expert interviews, or week-by-week updates, here are some of the best pregnancy podcasts for UK listeners.

## UK pregnancy podcasts {#uk-podcasts}

The Midwives' Cauldron is a UK-based podcast from practising midwives covering evidence, birth culture, and the realities of NHS maternity care. It is particularly useful if you want an insider perspective on how decisions get made during labour and what your options genuinely are. The Parenting Podcast from the creators of Parenting Hell offers a humorous, relatable take on pregnancy and family life, which many listeners find a relief from the more earnest corners of the pregnancy internet. Both are free on Spotify and Apple Podcasts.

The Happy Mum Happy Baby podcast, hosted by TV presenter Giovanna Fletcher, includes honest conversations about pregnancy, birth, and motherhood from a wide range of guests — including celebrities who share experiences that are not always heard publicly. The accessible tone works well if you want company and warmth rather than clinical detail.

## Birth stories and positivity {#birth-stories}

The Birth Hour is dedicated to birth stories across a wide range of experiences: home births, hospital births, caesarean sections, VBACs, multiple pregnancies, and more. Listening to varied, honest accounts can reduce fear and help you feel less alone, whatever your birth plan looks like. The key value of birth story podcasts is exposure to outcomes you have not considered — which can help you feel prepared rather than caught off guard.

The Birthful podcast with Adriana Lozada mixes personal birth stories with expert interviews on labour, infant feeding, and postnatal wellbeing. While US-produced, the content translates well to a UK context.

If you are anxious about birth, curated positive birth story podcasts can be genuinely helpful — but be selective. Some women find that too many birth stories increase rather than reduce anxiety, particularly if the stories are dramatic or detailed about complications. Trust your instincts about what is helping.

## Expert-led podcasts {#expert-led}

Pregnancy, Birth and Beyond — produced in association with midwifery organisations — offers evidence-based episodes from UK midwives and other perinatal professionals. Topics range from physiological birth to postnatal mental health, presented in accessible language without being dumbed down.

The Evidence Based Birth podcast, while US-focused, covers research topics highly relevant to UK parents: induction, monitoring in labour, cord clamping, and informed consent. It is particularly useful if you want to understand the evidence behind common maternity recommendations before making decisions.

Dr Midwife and similar practitioner-led shows feature qualified professionals discussing current practice and debunking common pregnancy myths. These are useful for fact-checking things you have read online.

## Choosing the right podcast {#choosing}

Not every podcast will be right for every listener, and your needs may change across the trimesters. A few pointers:

- **If you want reassurance**, start with birth story podcasts that prioritise positive experiences. Many are curated specifically to avoid traumatic content.
- **If you want facts and evidence**, try expert-led shows from midwives or researchers. These help you understand your options and ask informed questions at appointments.
- **If you want company and humour**, look for podcasts hosted by parents with a light touch. Feeling less alone in the strange experience of pregnancy has real value.
- **If a podcast makes you more anxious**, stop listening to it. There is no obligation to consume content that does not serve you.

## Tips for listening well {#listening-tips}

Podcasts are a supplement to, not a replacement for, your NHS antenatal appointments and midwife's advice. Use them for background learning, emotional support, and inspiration — but take anything that conflicts with your midwife's guidance with caution, and raise any questions at your next appointment rather than acting on them independently.

Be aware that some pregnancy podcasts are produced outside the UK and may reference practices, medications, or healthcare structures that differ from NHS care. US or Australian episodes on topics like Group B Strep testing, fundal height measurement, or induction guidelines may not directly apply to your care pathway.

Limit your listening if you find yourself going down rabbit holes of worst-case scenarios. A varied playlist — stories, expert interviews, lighter content — tends to work better than intense, back-to-back listening on a single worrying topic.

## FAQ {#faq}

### Are pregnancy podcasts free?

Most are free with occasional adverts on Spotify and Apple Podcasts. Some shows offer ad-free tiers or bonus episodes through Patreon or paid subscriptions, but the core content is almost always freely available.

### Can podcasts replace antenatal classes?

They complement but do not replace structured classes. Antenatal courses — whether NHS or independent — give you the chance to ask questions in real time, practise practical skills, and meet other parents due at the same time. Podcasts are excellent for background learning and reassurance, but they work best alongside, not instead of, in-person preparation.

### Which podcast is best for first-time parents?

The Birth Hour and The Midwives' Cauldron are both widely recommended for accessible, balanced content. First-time parents often benefit from a mix: one expert-led show for information and one birth story show for emotional preparation.
