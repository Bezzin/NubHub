# When to Start Buying Baby Things

Figuring out when to start buying baby things can feel overwhelming, especially in the early weeks of pregnancy. This guide walks you through a sensible UK timeline so you can spread the cost and avoid last-minute panic buying.

## Why timing matters {why-timing-matters}

Many parents are superstitious about buying baby items too early, but modern UK guidance suggests there is no medical reason to wait beyond the first trimester. The main reason to pace yourself is practical: spreading the cost over several months is easier on your budget, and waiting until after your 12-week scan means you can feel more confident about your pregnancy before investing in big-ticket items.

## First trimester: research, don't rush {first-trimester}

During weeks 0-12, focus on researching rather than purchasing. Use this time to browse reviews, compare prams and cots, and start a wishlist. You might begin buying small neutral items like vests or muslins if you see a good sale, but most parents hold off on furniture and pushchairs until after the first scan.

## Second trimester: the sweet spot {second-trimester}

Weeks 13-27 are ideal for big purchases. The sickness may have eased, you have more energy, and you still have time to assemble furniture and wait for deliveries. Aim to have your pram, cot, Moses basket, and car seat sorted by around 28 weeks. This is also when many parents start a baby registry or share a wishlist with family.

## Third trimester: finishing touches {third-trimester}

From 28 weeks onwards, pack your hospital bag, stock up on nappies in a couple of sizes, and add the finishing touches like bedding and a changing bag. Having everything ready by 36 weeks means you are prepared if baby arrives early. Keep receipts and tags on bigger items in case plans change.

## FAQ {#faq}

### Is it bad luck to buy baby things before 12 weeks?

There is no evidence that buying early affects pregnancy outcomes. The tradition is cultural, not medical. Buy when you feel comfortable.

### How much should I budget for baby essentials in the UK?

A realistic first-year budget for essentials (pram, cot, car seat, clothing, feeding) ranges from 800 to 2,500 pounds depending on whether you buy new or second-hand.

### Should I wait for Black Friday sales?

Black Friday can be a great time to buy pushchairs and furniture, but only if the timing aligns with your pregnancy. Don't delay essential items if you are past 36 weeks.
