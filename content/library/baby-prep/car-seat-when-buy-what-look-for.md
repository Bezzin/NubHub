# Car Seat: When to Buy and What to Look For

A car seat is one of the most important purchases you'll make. Here's what you need to know.

## When to Buy

Buy your car seat by 36 weeks at the latest. Some babies come early, and you'll need it for the journey home from hospital.

## What to Look For

Look for: i-Size (R129) compliance, rear-facing for as long as possible, a 5-point harness, side impact protection, and compatibility with your car. Get it professionally fitted.

## FAQ

### When should I buy a car seat?

By 36 weeks at the latest. You need it to take your baby home from hospital. Consider buying earlier to allow time for fitting and practice.

### Should my baby be rear-facing?

Yes. Rear-facing is much safer for babies and toddlers. The NHS recommends keeping your child rear-facing for as long as possible — ideally until at least 4 years old.
